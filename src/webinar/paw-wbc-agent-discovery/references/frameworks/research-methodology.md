# Research Methodology

## Overview

This framework guides comprehensive webinar research that produces actionable insights for hook generation. The goal is depth over breadth — thoroughly exploring relevant angles rather than surface-level coverage of everything.

## Research Domains

### 1. Published Content Search

**Purpose:** Understand what's already been said on the topic to find gaps and differentiation opportunities.

**Sources:**
- Blog posts and articles (industry publications, Medium, LinkedIn)
- YouTube videos and TED talks
- Podcasts and interview transcripts
- White papers and industry reports
- Conference presentations (SlideShare, speaker decks)

**Search Strategies:**
- Use multiple search tools (Exa, Web Search) with varied query formulations
- Search both broad terms and specific subtopics
- Include "webinar", "guide", "framework", "best practices" modifiers
- Look for content targeting the same audience

**What to Capture:**
- Common themes and repeated advice (what everyone says)
- Unique angles or contrarian positions (differentiation opportunities)
- Gaps in coverage (what's NOT being discussed)
- Quality assessment: which content is actually good?

### 2. Data and Statistics

**Purpose:** Provide credibility and weight to arguments. Numbers make hooks more compelling.

**Sources:**
- Industry research reports (Gartner, Forrester, McKinsey)
- Government statistics and census data
- Academic research and journals
- Company reports and case studies
- Survey data ( Pew Research, industry surveys)

**What to Capture:**
- Statistics that validate the problem's importance
- Trend data showing growth or change
- Comparative data (before/after, with/without)
- Counter-intuitive data that challenges assumptions

**Quality Criteria:**
- Source credibility: Is the source reputable?
- Recency: Is the data current enough to be relevant?
- Specificity: Exact numbers are better than ranges
- Relevance: Does this directly support the topic?

### 3. Competitive Landscape

**Purpose:** Identify who else covers this topic and what angles they take.

**Analysis Framework:**
- Direct competitors: Same product/service, same topic
- Adjacent competitors: Different product, overlapping topic
- Thought leaders: Influential voices in the space
- Content saturation: How much content exists?

**What to Assess:**
- Positioning: How do others frame this topic?
- Depth: Surface-level or deep dives?
- Quality: What's the production value?
- Engagement: Comments, shares, views — what resonates?
- Gaps: What angles are underserved?

### 4. Audience Pain Points

**Purpose:** Ground the webinar in real problems the audience faces.

**Discovery Methods:**
- Community forums (Reddit, Stack Overflow, industry forums)
- Social media discussions (LinkedIn, Twitter threads)
- Q&A sites (Quora, industry-specific)
- Customer feedback and reviews
- Support tickets and common questions (if available)

**What to Uncover:**
- Specific problems they articulate in their own words
- Emotional language revealing urgency and frustration
- Workarounds they've tried (and why they failed)
- Success stories they share
- Knowledge gaps they admit

### 5. User Expertise Extraction

**Purpose:** Incorporate the user's unique perspective and knowledge into research.

**When User Has Existing Content:**
- Read blog posts, articles, or previous presentations
- Watch videos or listen to podcasts they've created
- Review case studies or client work
- Note their unique frameworks or methodologies

**Questions to Ask:**
- What do you know that most people don't?
- What's your contrarian view on this topic?
- What stories or examples do you use?
- What mistakes have you seen others make?
- What would you wish you knew when starting?

**What to Capture:**
- Unique perspectives not found in published research
- Personal stories and examples
- Proprietary frameworks or methodologies
- Client success stories (anonymized if needed)

## Time Management

**Target: ~30 minutes of active research**

### Time Allocation Guide

| Activity | Time | Cumulative |
|----------|------|------------|
| Published content search | 8 min | 8 min |
| Data and statistics | 5 min | 13 min |
| Competitive landscape | 5 min | 18 min |
| Audience pain points | 7 min | 25 min |
| User expertise extraction | 5 min | 30 min |

### Efficiency Techniques

1. **Start broad, go deep selectively** — Quick scan first, deep dive on promising angles
2. **Set a timer** — Don't rabbit-hole on one source
3. **Capture, don't analyze** — Note findings; analyze during compression
4. **Use multiple tools in parallel** — Run searches while reading other content
5. **Good enough is good enough** — Not every source needs full analysis

### When to Extend Research

Extend beyond 30 minutes when:
- Topic is highly complex or technical
- Competitive landscape is unusually crowded
- User is entering a new domain they don't know well
- Initial research reveals surprising gaps worth exploring

## Source Quality Criteria

### High Quality Sources
- Reputable industry publications
- Academic peer-reviewed research
- Government or official statistics
- Recognized thought leaders
- Primary research (surveys, studies)

### Medium Quality Sources
- Industry blogs with good reputation
- Company case studies
- Conference presentations
- Well-researched long-form content

### Use with Caution
- Opinion pieces without data
- Anonymous content
- Outdated content (check dates)
- Competitor marketing materials

### Avoid
- Content farms and SEO spam
- Unattributed statistics
- Obviously biased sources
- Gated content you cannot access

## Research Output Format

After completing research, organize findings into these categories for compression:

1. **Key Statistics** — Top 5-10 most compelling numbers
2. **Common Themes** — What everyone says (3-5 items)
3. **Differentiation Opportunities** — Gaps and underserved angles
4. **Audience Pain Points** — Specific problems in their words
5. **Competitive Landscape** — Who covers this, their angles
6. **User's Unique Perspective** — Their expertise and stories
7. **Hook Seeds** — Initial angle ideas from research

This output feeds directly into research compression.

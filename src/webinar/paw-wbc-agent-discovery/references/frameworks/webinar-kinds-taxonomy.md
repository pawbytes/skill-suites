# Webinar Kinds Taxonomy

## Overview

Webinars serve different purposes and audiences. Identifying the correct webinar kind early shapes research focus, hook generation, and ultimately the production approach. This taxonomy helps detect the right kind from brief signals.

## Webinar Kinds

### 1. Thought Leadership Webinar

**Definition:** Positions the presenter as an expert voice on a topic, sharing insights, frameworks, or new perspectives.

**Primary Goals:**
- Build authority and credibility
- Shape industry conversation
- Generate speaking opportunities
- Attract high-value relationships

**Audience Characteristics:**
- Decision-makers and leaders
- Peers in the industry
- People seeking strategic insights
- Higher tolerance for complexity

**Detection Signals:**
- Topic is a methodology, framework, or perspective
- User has unique expertise or contrarian view
- Goal mentions "authority", "influence", "positioning"
- Not tied to specific product launch
- User is founder, executive, or consultant

**Research Focus:**
- Industry trends and conversations
- What thought leaders are saying
- Contrarian positions available
- Data supporting new perspectives
- Historical context on how thinking evolved

**Hook Tendencies:**
- Contrarian angles work well
- New frameworks or models
- "The future of X" perspectives
- Challenge conventional wisdom

### 2. Product Demo Webinar

**Definition:** Showcases a product or feature, demonstrating value through live or recorded walkthroughs.

**Primary Goals:**
- Convert trial users to customers
- Reduce friction in adoption
- Answer product questions
- Showcase specific features

**Audience Characteristics:**
- Already aware of product
- Evaluating or onboarding
- Need practical guidance
- Want to see "how it works"

**Detection Signals:**
- Topic is product-specific
- Goal mentions "demo", "adoption", "features"
- Timeline tied to product launch
- User is product manager or sales
- Audience is existing prospects

**Research Focus:**
- User questions and friction points
- Competitive alternatives
- Feature comparison landscape
- Use case scenarios
- Success stories and case studies

**Hook Tendencies:**
- Problem-solution angles
- "See it in action" appeals
- Before/after transformations
- Efficiency and time savings

### 3. Lead Generation Webinar

**Definition:** Educational content designed to attract new leads into the sales funnel.

**Primary Goals:**
- Capture contact information
- Begin nurture sequence
- Qualify leads by interest
- Fill pipeline

**Audience Characteristics:**
- Top of funnel
- Problem-aware but not solution-aware
- Seeking education
- Diverse roles and companies

**Detection Signals:**
- Goal mentions "leads", "pipeline", "list building"
- Topic addresses common industry problem
- Call-to-action is signup/registration
- User is marketing or sales
- Broad audience appeal

**Research Focus:**
- Common problems in target industry
- What content attracts the right leads
- Competitor lead magnets
- Keywords and topics with search volume
- Audience job titles and roles

**Hook Tendencies:**
- Problem-first angles
- "How to" educational framing
- Free value before ask
- Quick wins and actionable takeaways

### 4. Internal Training Webinar

**Definition:** Educates employees or team members on processes, tools, or skills.

**Primary Goals:**
- Transfer knowledge
- Standardize processes
- Upskill team
- Ensure compliance or consistency

**Audience Characteristics:**
- Internal team members
- Required attendance typically
- Varying skill levels
- Need practical applicability

**Detection Signals:**
- Audience is internal team
- Goal mentions "training", "onboarding", "skills"
- Topic is process or tool-specific
- Not for external distribution
- User is manager or L&D

**Research Focus:**
- Best practices for the skill/tool
- Common mistakes to avoid
- Step-by-step processes
- Resources and references
- Assessment criteria

**Hook Tendencies:**
- Competency and confidence angles
- "Master this skill" framing
- Practical applicability focus
- Team success stories

## Detection Process

### Step 1: Analyze Brief Signals

Look for keywords in brief that map to kinds:

| Keyword Examples | Suggests Kind |
|------------------|---------------|
| "authority", "positioning", "expert", "industry" | Thought Leadership |
| "demo", "features", "adoption", "product" | Product Demo |
| "leads", "pipeline", "generate", "list" | Lead Generation |
| "training", "team", "internal", "onboarding" | Internal Training |

### Step 2: Consider Audience

| Audience Type | Suggests Kind |
|---------------|---------------|
| Industry peers, executives | Thought Leadership |
| Prospects, trial users | Product Demo |
| Top-of-funnel, broad market | Lead Generation |
| Employees, team members | Internal Training |

### Step 3: Consider User's Role

| User Role | Typical Priority |
|-----------|------------------|
| Founder, Executive, Consultant | Thought Leadership |
| Product Manager, Sales | Product Demo |
| Marketing | Lead Generation |
| Manager, L&D | Internal Training |

### Step 4: Propose and Confirm

Present the detected kind with reasoning:

> "Based on your goal of building authority in the email automation space and your audience of marketing leaders, I'm detecting this as a **Thought Leadership Webinar**. This means our research will focus on industry trends, contrarian positions, and what other thought leaders are saying. Does this feel right?"

If user disagrees, adjust and continue.

## Research Focus by Kind

| Kind | Primary Research Focus | Secondary Focus |
|------|------------------------|-----------------|
| Thought Leadership | Industry trends, contrarian positions, thought leader landscape | Audience pain points, data support |
| Product Demo | User questions, competitive alternatives, use cases | Feature documentation, success stories |
| Lead Generation | Common problems, search trends, competitor lead magnets | Audience roles, industry context |
| Internal Training | Best practices, process documentation, resources | Common mistakes, skill assessment |

## Implications for Production

The detected kind influences how the Producer agent structures content:

| Kind | Structure Tendency | Hook Style | Call-to-Action |
|------|-------------------|------------|----------------|
| Thought Leadership | Framework/insight-driven | Contrarian or novel | Connect, follow, discuss |
| Product Demo | Feature-benefit driven | Problem-solution | Try, buy, upgrade |
| Lead Generation | Problem-tutorial driven | Educational value | Download, subscribe, contact |
| Internal Training | Step-by-step driven | Competency building | Apply, practice, certify |

## Hybrid Situations

Some webinars blend kinds. Handle these by:

1. **Identifying primary kind** — What's the main goal?
2. **Acknowledging secondary elements** — "This is primarily lead gen, but with thought leadership elements"
3. **Prioritizing research accordingly** — Primary kind gets 70% of focus

Example hybrids:
- Product launch webinar (Product Demo + Thought Leadership)
- Educational sales webinar (Lead Generation + Product Demo)
- Executive briefing (Thought Leadership + Internal Training)

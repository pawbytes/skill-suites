# Sales Deck Capability

**Outcome:** Build a 10-12 slide story arc deck that positions the prospect as hero and product as the winning tool.

---

## When to Use

Creating or revising the main sales deck. Use for new product launches, vertical-specific versions, or annual refresh.

---

## Story Arc Framework

A sales deck is not a feature catalog. It's a story where the prospect is the hero.

| Slide | Title | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | The Problem | Industry-wide pain, quantified. Make them feel seen. |
| 2 | Cost of Inaction | Financial/time/reputation cost of the problem today. |
| 3 | Market Shift | Why now? (regulation, market shift, tech change, competitive pressure) |
| 4 | Our Solution | One clear statement, max 15 words. |
| 5 | Product Walkthrough | 3-4 screens/features mapped to Slide 1 pains. |
| 6 | Proof | Customer results, metrics, logos (with permission). |
| 7 | Case Study | One deep before/after/result story with numbers. |
| 8 | How It Works | Implementation overview. Reduce switching fear. |
| 9 | ROI Summary | Quantify value using prospect's numbers. |
| 10 | Pricing | Starting price or "plans start at" — no surprises. |
| 11 | Next Steps | One low-friction ask: trial, pilot, technical call. |

---

## Slide Content Standards

| Slide | Key Rule |
|---|---|
| 1 — The Problem | Lead with stat they recognize; frame as industry problem; use customer language |
| 2 — Cost of Inaction | Translate pain into money; ask "Does this resonate?" |
| 3 — Market Shift | Name specific trigger (regulation, competitor, tech) |
| 4 — Our Solution | "We help [persona] [outcome] by [mechanism], without [drawback]" |
| 5 — Product Walkthrough | 3-4 real UI screens tied to Slide 1 pains; presenter notes talk track |
| 6 — Proof | 3-5 metrics + verbatim quote; use logos with permission |
| 7 — Case Study | Before/after/result with role, company size, timeframe |
| 8 — Implementation | 3-step diagram; time-to-value, migration story |
| 9 — ROI Summary | "Based on what you shared" — customize per meeting |
| 10 — Pricing | Lead with most popular plan; show annual discount |
| 11 — Next Steps | Specific date; ask "What would you need to feel confident?" |

---

## Design Principles

- Max 7 words per headline
- One idea per slide
- Visuals over text — show product, show outcomes, show data
- Presenter notes with full talk track
- 16:9 ratio in Google Slides or PowerPoint
- Follow brand colors/fonts from brand-context.md

# One-Pagers Capability

**Outcome:** Create scannable single-page assets for specific moments in the deal cycle and buyer personas.

---

## When to Use

Post-meeting leave-behinds, champion selling tools, executive summaries for late-entering buyers, technical briefs for IT review.

---

## One-Pager Types

| Type | When to Use | Audience | Length |
|---|---|---|---|
| Product one-pager | Post-discovery recap, champion tool | All buyers | 1 page |
| Executive summary | Economic buyer enters late | C-suite | 1 page |
| Battle card | Competitive shortlist | Sales rep only (internal) | 1 page |
| Technical brief | Security/IT review | Technical buyer | 1-2 pages |
| ROI summary sheet | Proposal support, board approval | Economic buyer | 1 page |

---

## Product One-Pager Structure

Scannable in 30 seconds. Post-meeting recap or champion selling tool.

**Sections:**
- Header: product name + one-line value prop (max 12 words)
- Problem: 2-3 sentences in customer language
- Solution: 3 outcome-focused bullets
- Key Differentiators: 3 bullets, each ending with proof
- Proof Point: one metric or quote
- Logos
- CTA: single action

**Format:** Single page, two-column preferred. Bullets only, no paragraphs. Min 10pt font. PDF output, editable source.

---

## Executive Summary Structure

For economic buyer joining late. 90 seconds to read.

**Sections:**
- Headline: "[Outcome] for [Company Name]"
- Business Problem: 2 sentences, quantified
- Proposed Solution: 1 sentence, no jargon
- Expected Outcomes: 3 bullets with metrics/timeframes
- Investment and Return: price, ROI, payback period
- Implementation: timeline, team hours
- Next Step: specific action + date

---

## Battle Card Structure

Internal-only. Never share with prospects.

**Sections:**
- Their Honest Strengths: 2-3 strengths prospect has seen
- Our Advantages: 2-3 with proof points and talk tracks
- When They Come Up: Discovery questions to ask first
- Common Objections: Price, feature, switching responses
- Leave-Behind Proof: Customer story from same competitive eval
- What Not to Do: Guardrails against badmouthing

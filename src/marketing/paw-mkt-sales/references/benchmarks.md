# Sales Enablement Benchmarks

**Purpose:** Reference data for setting realistic expectations and measuring sales enablement effectiveness. Use these benchmarks when building collateral, setting KPIs, or advising on sales process optimization.

---

## Sales Cycle Benchmarks by Deal Size

| Deal Size (ACV) | Avg Sales Cycle | Typical Stakeholders | Decision Complexity |
|---|---|---|---|
| < $5K | 14-30 days | 1-2 (end user + manager) | Low — often self-serve or single call |
| $5K-$25K | 30-60 days | 2-4 (manager, director, procurement) | Medium — demo + evaluation + approval |
| $25K-$100K | 60-120 days | 4-8 (VP, C-suite, IT, legal, procurement) | High — multi-stakeholder, formal evaluation |
| $100K+ | 120-270 days | 6-12+ (executive sponsor, buying committee) | Very High — RFP, security review, legal |

## Win Rate Benchmarks

| Stage | Average Win Rate | Top Performers |
|---|---|---|
| Lead → Qualified Opportunity | 13-20% | 25-30% |
| Qualified → Proposal/Demo | 40-50% | 55-65% |
| Proposal → Closed Won | 20-30% | 35-45% |
| Overall Pipeline → Closed Won | 15-25% | 30-40% |

## Collateral Usage and Impact

| Asset Type | % Sales Reps Who Use Regularly | Avg Impact on Win Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Customer case studies | 65-75% | +15-25% when included in deal |
| ROI calculators | 30-40% | +20-30% when used in enterprise |
| Competitive battle cards | 55-65% | +12-18% in competitive deals |
| One-pagers / leave-behinds | 70-80% | Hard to isolate; supports multi-threading |
| Demo scripts / talk tracks | 45-55% | +10-15% for new reps specifically |
| Objection handling guides | 50-60% | +8-12% overall; higher for complex objections |

## Proof Point Effectiveness

Not all proof formats carry equal weight. Effectiveness depends on deal stage and buyer persona:

| Proof Format | Best For | Trust Level |
|---|---|---|
| Named logo + quote | Early stage awareness, social proof | Medium |
| Metrics-driven case study (named) | Mid-funnel evaluation, ROI justification | High |
| Anonymous metrics case study | When named customers won't participate | Medium |
| Third-party validation (G2, Gartner) | Enterprise credibility, shortlist decisions | Very High |
| Live customer reference call | Final evaluation, de-risking | Very High |
| Video testimonial | Emotional connection, executive buy-in | High |
| ROI calculator with customer data | CFO/finance approval, budget justification | High |

## Deal Influence by Stakeholder Role

Understanding who influences vs who decides helps target collateral:

| Role | Influence Level | What They Care About | Best Asset |
|---|---|---|---|
| End User / Champion | High influence, low authority | Ease of use, daily workflow improvement | Product demo, quick-start guide |
| Manager / Director | High influence, medium authority | Team productivity, measurable outcomes | Case study, ROI calculator |
| VP / C-Suite | Medium influence, high authority | Strategic alignment, competitive advantage | Executive summary, board-ready deck |
| IT / Security | Veto power | Compliance, integration, security posture | Security whitepaper, architecture doc |
| Procurement | Veto power | Pricing, terms, vendor risk | Comparison sheet, compliance docs |
| Finance / CFO | Approval power | ROI, payback period, budget fit | ROI model, TCO comparison |

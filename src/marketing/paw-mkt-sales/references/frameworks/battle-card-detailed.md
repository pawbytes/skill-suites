# Battle Card Detailed Template

Full competitive battle card template for internal sales use. Customize per competitor.

---

```
# Battle Card -- [COMPETITOR NAME]
**Internal use only. Do not share with prospects.**
Last updated: [DATE] | Updated by: [NAME]

------------------------------------------------------------
WHEN THEY COME UP
------------------------------------------------------------

Ask before claiming anything:
1. "What did [COMPETITOR] show you that you liked?" -- Listen. Understand what
   the prospect values, not just who they're talking to.
2. "What specifically are you comparing on?" -- Get their evaluation criteria
   before you make a single claim.
3. "Have you seen a demo yet, or are you still exploring?" -- Stage of evaluation
   determines response.

------------------------------------------------------------
THEIR HONEST STRENGTHS
(Know these -- prospects have already seen them)
------------------------------------------------------------

- [STRENGTH 1 -- be specific and honest]
- [STRENGTH 2]
- [STRENGTH 3 -- brand recognition, funding, market share if relevant]

------------------------------------------------------------
OUR ADVANTAGES (for this specific competitive situation)
------------------------------------------------------------

| Our Advantage | Proof Point | How to Raise It |
|---|---|---|
| [ADVANTAGE 1] | [Customer X achieved Y] | "Where [COMPETITOR] focuses on [X], we focus on [Y]. For teams that care about [Y]..." |
| [ADVANTAGE 2] | [G2 review or metric] | "[Question that surfaces this advantage organically]" |
| [ADVANTAGE 3] | [Specific feature or outcome] | "[Reframe that shifts the evaluation criteria]" |

------------------------------------------------------------
COMMON OBJECTIONS IN THIS COMPETITIVE SITUATION
------------------------------------------------------------

**"[COMPETITOR] has [FEATURE WE LACK OR THEY THINK WE LACK]"**
> [Honest response: either "You're right, we handle that differently by..."
   or "Actually, here's how we do that..."]
> Advance: "[Question that refocuses on what matters to them]"

**"[COMPETITOR] is cheaper."**
> "Price is usually a proxy for ROI uncertainty. Let me show you what similar
   teams realized in 90 days -- and then we can talk about whether the gap
   justifies the difference."
> Advance: "What would make the value case clear for you?"

**"We already use [COMPETITOR]."**
> "That's helpful context. What made you look at alternatives today?"
> [Find the gap -- there is always one. Build response around that specific gap.]

------------------------------------------------------------
LEAVE-BEHIND PROOF
------------------------------------------------------------

"[Customer in same situation as the prospect -- same size, same use case -- was
 evaluating [COMPETITOR] and chose us because [SPECIFIC REASON WITH RESULT]."

Reference customer available: [Yes -- coordinate with CS first / No]

------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT NOT TO DO
------------------------------------------------------------

- Never badmouth [COMPETITOR] directly. It makes you look insecure.
- Never claim features you don't have. Prospects talk to each other.
- Never ignore their genuine strengths -- pretending they don't exist loses trust.
```

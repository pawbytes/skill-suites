# Sales Deck Slide Templates

Ready-to-customize slide templates with headline formulas, body structures, and presenter note scripts for a 10-11 slide sales deck story arc.

---

### Slide 1 — The Problem

**Headline template:**
> Teams like [TARGET AUDIENCE] lose [QUANTIFIED COST — hours/week, $ amount, % efficiency] to [SPECIFIC PAIN POINT].

**Body (3 bullets max):**
- [Industry-wide problem framed as recognized trend, not the prospect's fault]
- [Second consequence or dimension of the problem]
- [Why this is getting worse, not better — market pressure, regulation, competition]

**Visual direction:** Data visualization or striking statistic displayed large. Industry report citation preferred.

**Presenter note template:**
> "Before I show you what we built, I want to make sure I'm describing the right problem. [STAT OR STORY ABOUT THE PAIN]. Does that resonate with what your team is experiencing? Tell me if I'm off."

---

### Slide 2 — Cost of Inaction

**Headline template:**
> Every [TIME UNIT], [SPECIFIC PROBLEM] costs [AUDIENCE] approximately [DOLLAR AMOUNT or HOURS].

**Body structure:**
```
Time cost:     [HOURS/WEEK] × [TEAM SIZE] × [HOURLY RATE] = [ANNUAL LABOR COST]
Revenue cost:  [MISSED DEALS/CHURN/ERRORS] × [AVERAGE VALUE] = [ANNUAL REVENUE IMPACT]
Risk cost:     [COMPLIANCE, REPUTATION, COMPETITIVE] impact if unaddressed
```

**Presenter note template:**
> "I want to make this concrete. Based on what similar teams tell us, this problem is costing you somewhere in the range of [ESTIMATE]. Does that feel roughly accurate, or would you adjust the numbers?"

---

### Slide 3 — Market Shift (Why Now)

**Headline template:**
> [SPECIFIC CHANGE — regulation / technology / customer expectation / competitive pressure] is making [OLD APPROACH] unsustainable.

**Body (2-3 bullets):**
- [Specific trigger event or trend — name it, date it if possible]
- [How this affects the prospect's competitive position]
- [What early movers are already doing]

**Presenter note template:**
> "The reason I'm bringing this up now, rather than six months ago or six months from now, is [SPECIFIC TRIGGER]. The teams that move on this in [TIMEFRAME] are positioned to [OUTCOME]. Those that wait until [LAGGING INDICATOR] are playing catch-up."

---

### Slide 4 — Our Solution

**Headline template (choose one):**
- > We help [PERSONA] [ACHIEVE OUTCOME] by [MECHANISM], without [COMMON DRAWBACK].
- > [PRODUCT NAME]: [ONE-LINE CUSTOMER LANGUAGE DESCRIPTION].
- > [OUTCOME FOR CUSTOMER] — without [WHAT THEY HATE ABOUT CURRENT SOLUTIONS].

**Body:** One sentence maximum. This is not the feature slide. The transformation is the message.

**Presenter note template:**
> "Before I show you the product, I want to anchor on what we're actually trying to do. [READ HEADLINE ALOUD]. Everything I'm about to show you connects back to that."

---

### Slide 5 — Product Walkthrough

**Structure (3-4 screens maximum):**

```
Screen 1: [FEATURE NAME]
  → "The problem you mentioned was [PAIN FROM SLIDE 1]. Here's how we handle that."
  → [SCREENSHOT OF RELEVANT FEATURE]
  → "This means your team [SPECIFIC OUTCOME — never has to X again / can now Y in Z minutes]."
  → Check-in: "How does that compare to how you handle this today?"

Screen 2: [FEATURE NAME]
  → [Same structure]

Screen 3: [FEATURE NAME]
  → [Same structure]
```

**Design direction:** Real product UI, not marketing illustrations. Annotate with callout boxes, not wall-of-text overlay.

**Presenter note template per screen:**
> "Remember the problem of [PAIN]. Watch what happens when [TRIGGER ACTION]. [DESCRIBE WHAT HAPPENS]. For a team your size, that typically means [QUANTIFIED IMPACT]."

### Slide 6 — Proof

**Headline template:**
> [NUMBER] [AUDIENCE TYPE] use [PRODUCT NAME] to [PRIMARY OUTCOME].

**Body structure:**
```
[METRIC 1]: [Company/Role] reduced [X] by [%] in [timeframe]
[METRIC 2]: [Company/Role] increased [Y] from [before] to [after]
[METRIC 3]: [Company/Role] saved [hours/$ amount] per [time period]

"[Short verbatim customer quote — 1-2 sentences, named, role, company size]"
— [Name], [Title], [Company]
```

**Logo placement:** 4-6 recognizable customer logos below proof points. If logos cannot be shared, use "[50-person SaaS team]" style descriptors.

### Slide 7 — Case Study

**Headline template:**
> How [COMPANY TYPE / ANONYMIZED DESCRIPTION] [ACHIEVED OUTCOME] in [TIMEFRAME].

**Body structure:**
```
BEFORE
  "[Description of their world before: what they were doing, how it was working,
   what was frustrating. Use their own words if available.]"

THE SWITCH
  "[What triggered them to look for a solution. What they evaluated.
   Why they chose us — the real reason, not the marketing reason.]"

AFTER
  "[Specific, quantified results with timeframe.]"
  "[Secondary benefit they didn't expect.]"

THEIR WORDS
  "[Customer quote — specific and attributed to role/company size if name can't be used]"
```

### Slide 8 — How It Works / Implementation

**Headline template:**
> From sign-up to [FIRST VALUE MILESTONE] in [TIME — days/weeks].

**Body structure (3-step diagram):**
```
Step 1: [SETUP ACTION] — [TIME REQUIRED] — [WHO DOES IT]
  → "This is where we connect to [EXISTING TOOLS]. It takes [X minutes]."

Step 2: [CONFIGURATION ACTION] — [TIME REQUIRED] — [WHOSE TEAM]
  → "We assign a dedicated [ONBOARDING SPECIALIST / CSM] to this phase."

Step 3: [LIVE / VALUE MILESTONE] — [DATE FROM START]
  → "By [DAY X], your team is [DOING THE THING THEY CARE ABOUT]."
```

**Risk reduction line:** "If anything unexpected comes up during implementation, [SPECIFIC SUPPORT COMMITMENT — dedicated Slack channel / 24h SLA / rollback option]."

### Slide 9 — ROI Summary

**Headline template:**
> Based on what you shared, [PRODUCT NAME] delivers approximately [NET ROI] in year one.

**Body (fill in from discovery conversation):**
```
Time savings:
  [HOURS/WEEK] × [TEAM SIZE] × [HOURLY RATE] × 52 weeks = [ANNUAL LABOR SAVINGS]

[ADDITIONAL VALUE DRIVER — error reduction / revenue impact / cost avoidance]:
  [FORMULA VISIBLE] = [AMOUNT]

Total annual value:           [SUM]
Annual investment:            [PRICE]
Net ROI:                      [VALUE MINUS PRICE]
Payback period:               [INVESTMENT / (VALUE/12)] months
```

**Presenter note:**
> "I want to be clear that I used conservative assumptions here — I used [X%] efficiency gain rather than the [Y%] we see in best cases. The actual number is likely higher. And critically, these are your numbers, not mine."

### Slide 10 — Pricing

**Headline template:**
> [MOST POPULAR TIER NAME] — [PRICE]. Everything your team needs to [CORE OUTCOME].

**Body structure:**
```
[TIER NAME]          [TIER NAME]          [TIER NAME]
[PRICE/mo annual]    [PRICE/mo annual]    [Contact sales]
                     ★ Most Popular

Key inclusions:      Key inclusions:      Custom inclusions:
[FEATURE 1]          [Everything in X]    [Enterprise features]
[FEATURE 2]          [ADDED FEATURE 1]    [Security/compliance]
[FEATURE 3]          [ADDED FEATURE 2]    [Dedicated CSM]
[LIMIT]              [HIGHER LIMIT]       [Custom limit]
```

**Framing line:** "Based on the ROI we just calculated, the [RECOMMENDED TIER] pays for itself in approximately [PAYBACK PERIOD] months."

### Slide 11 — Next Steps

**Headline template:**
> One next step: [SPECIFIC LOW-FRICTION ACTION].

**Options by deal stage:**

| Situation | Recommended Next Step |
|---|---|
| Early evaluation | "A 30-minute technical call with [their IT/technical lead] to answer the hard questions" |
| Active evaluation | "A two-week pilot with [specific team or use case] — we handle setup" |
| Near decision | "A proposal for [their specific situation] by [specific date]" |
| Budget approval needed | "A 20-minute call including [economic buyer] — I'll prepare an executive summary" |

**Closing question (end every deck with this):**
> "What would you need to feel confident moving forward?"

# Champion Kits Capability

**Outcome:** Arm internal champions with materials to sell on your behalf when they present to boss/committee/board.

---

## When to Use

When champion says "I need to present to [boss/committee]", deal stalled at internal approval, budget conversation needed, or economic buyer hasn't been in any meeting.

---

## Kit Contents

### 1. Executive Summary (1 page)

Written for economic buyer, not champion.

**Sections:**
- Headline: "[Outcome] for [Company Name]"
- Business problem (quantified)
- Proposed solution (1 sentence)
- Expected outcomes (their numbers)
- Investment and ROI
- Implementation overview
- Recommended next step

### 2. Competitive Comparison (1-2 pages)

**Format:**
- Objective table: criteria rows, vendor columns (include status quo column)
- Checkmarks/ratings per cell
- Keep factual — no unverified claims
- Bottom: "This is why we recommend [Your Product] for [Company]"

### 3. Risk Mitigation Page

Address "What if this doesn't work?"

**Sections:**
- What if adoption is low?
- What if implementation takes longer?
- What if we need to exit?

For each: risk, mitigation, proof that mitigation works.

Include: pilot/POC offer, SLA commitments, CS resources, migration support, contract terms.

### 4. Internal FAQ (1 page)

Pre-answer questions champion will get.

**Common questions:**
- Why now?
- Why not [Competitor]?
- What does implementation require from our team?
- What happens if we want to leave?
- Can we see references?

### 5. Reference Customers

1-3 customers to reference.

**Include:** company size, industry, outcome achieved.
**For live calls:** coordinate with CS first, always ask reference customer.

---

## Champion Coaching

Give champion talk track for internal meeting:

- Opening: Frame problem in their words
- ROI case: Walk through numbers, let them do work
- Objection prep: Pre-script answers for 2-3 expected objections
- Close: Specific ask tied to business milestone
- Your availability: Offer to join call or standby

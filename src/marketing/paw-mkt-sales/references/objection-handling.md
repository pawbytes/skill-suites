# Objection Handling Capability

**Outcome:** Build structured response frameworks for the six objection categories that cover 90%+ of B2B sales pushback.

---

## When to Use

Preparing reps for calls, building objection guides, coaching on specific competitive or price objections.

---

## ARFA Framework

Every response follows this structure:

1. **Acknowledge** — Validate concern without agreeing with premise
2. **Reframe** — Shift from their framing to yours
3. **Respond** — Deliver counter with proof
4. **Advance** — End with forward-moving question

---

## Objection Categories

| Category | Common Variants | Core Reframe |
|---|---|---|
| Price/Budget | "Too expensive", "No budget", "Looking for cheaper" | Shift from price to cost-of-inaction and ROI |
| Timing | "Not right time", "In middle of project", "Waiting for event" | Design phased approach or pre-event evaluation |
| Competition | "Evaluating [Competitor]", "Already use [Competitor]" | Surface criteria, address 2-3 you win on |
| Authority/Buy-In | "Need buy-in", "Boss needs to approve" | Arm champion with internal meeting materials |
| Status Quo | "Happy with current solution", "We use spreadsheets" | Probe hidden friction; quantify current cost |
| Technical/Risk | "Security?", "How hard to implement?" | Lead with certifications and implementation timelines |

---

## Response Structure per Objection

For each objection variant, provide:
- What they say (verbatim)
- Why they say it (real concern underneath)
- How to respond (ARFA script)
- Evidence to share (proof point)

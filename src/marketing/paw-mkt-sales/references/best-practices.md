# Sales Enablement Best Practices

**Purpose:** Operating principles and proven patterns for creating sales collateral that reps actually use and that moves deals forward. Reference when building any sales asset.

---

## Core Principle: Build for the Deal Stage, Not the Asset Type

Sales collateral fails most often because it's generic — built to "describe the product" rather than to advance a specific deal at a specific stage. Every asset should answer: "What objection, question, or decision does this help the buyer resolve right now?"

| Deal Stage | Buyer State of Mind | Collateral Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | "I have a problem but don't know solutions exist" | Educate on the problem, not the product |
| Consideration | "I'm evaluating options" | Differentiate, provide social proof, reduce risk |
| Decision | "I need to justify this internally" | Arm the champion, provide ROI evidence |
| Expansion | "Should we do more with this vendor?" | Show untapped value, new use cases |

## Writing Principles

### 1. Lead with the Customer's Words, Not Yours

Pull language directly from customer interviews, G2 reviews, support tickets, and sales call transcripts. Marketing speak creates distance; customer language creates resonance.

**Instead of:** "Our AI-powered platform leverages machine learning to optimize workflows."
**Write:** "Teams tell us they used to spend 3 hours a week on [task] — now it takes 15 minutes."

### 2. One Asset, One Job

Every piece of collateral should do exactly one thing well. A case study proves ROI. A battle card handles competitive objections. A one-pager summarizes the value prop for a specific persona. When a single asset tries to do everything, it does nothing well and nobody uses it.

### 3. Scannable Over Comprehensive

Sales reps have 30 seconds to find what they need mid-call. Buyers scan, they don't read. Design for scanning:
- Bold the key numbers and takeaways
- Use headers that answer questions ("Why do teams switch from [Competitor]?")
- Put the most important information in the first sentence of every section
- Keep one-pagers to genuinely one page

### 4. Always Include a "So What"

Every stat, feature, and claim needs a "so what" — the business impact. Features without outcomes are meaningless to buyers.

**Weak:** "Real-time dashboards with 50+ integrations"
**Strong:** "Real-time dashboards mean your ops team spots issues before customers do — reducing escalations by 40%"

### 5. Make Proof Specific and Verifiable

Vague proof is worse than no proof because it signals that you're hiding something.

**Weak:** "Customers see significant ROI"
**Strong:** "Acme Corp reduced their response time from 4 hours to 12 minutes in the first 90 days (verified by VP of Ops Sarah Chen)"

## Asset-Specific Best Practices

### Sales Decks

- **10-12 slides max** for a 30-minute meeting (2-3 min per slide)
- First 3 slides: the problem, cost of inaction, and your approach — no product features yet
- Include a "This is for you if..." slide early to confirm fit and build trust
- End with clear next steps, not a generic "Questions?" slide
- Build modular: reps should be able to skip or reorder slides for different audiences
- Include presenter notes — the deck alone is not the presentation

### One-Pagers

- Genuinely one page — if it's two pages, it's a brief
- Organize as: headline stat → problem → solution → proof → CTA
- Persona-specific: a CTO one-pager is different from a VP Sales one-pager
- Design for both screen and print (avoid thin fonts, low-contrast colors)

### Battle Cards

- Structure: Competitor overview → Their pitch → Their weaknesses → Our differentiators → Landmines to plant → Objection responses
- Update quarterly at minimum — stale battle cards are worse than none
- Include "what they'll say about us" with prepared responses
- Keep to 2 pages max — reps need this mid-call

### Case Studies

- Follow the "Situation → Challenge → Solution → Results" framework
- Lead with the result in the headline: "How [Customer] Reduced [Metric] by [%] in [Timeframe]"
- Include at least 3 specific, quantified outcomes
- Add a pull quote from the decision-maker (not just the user)
- Make the customer the hero, not your product

### Demo Scripts

- Open with a discovery recap, not a feature tour
- Build around 3 "aha moments" — the points where buyers typically say "oh, that's what I need"
- Include talk tracks for each major feature: what to say, what to click, what to skip
- Add branching paths for different personas (technical vs business)
- Time each section — demos should respect the buyer's time

### Objection Handling Guides

- Structure each objection as: What they say → Why they say it → How to respond → Evidence to share
- Categorize: price, competition, timing, internal resistance, technical, security
- Include "bridge" phrases that acknowledge the concern before redirecting
- Add real examples of how reps have successfully handled each objection

### ROI Calculators

- Keep inputs to 5-7 fields maximum — complexity kills adoption
- Pre-fill with industry benchmarks so the buyer sees value immediately
- Show conservative, moderate, and aggressive scenarios
- Include time-to-value (when they'll see results, not just final ROI)
- Make it easy to export/screenshot for internal sharing

## Distribution and Adoption

The best collateral in the world is worthless if reps can't find it or don't use it. Common failure modes:

| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| Reps can't find assets | Organize by deal stage + persona, not asset type |
| Reps use outdated versions | Version-control everything, add "Last updated" dates |
| Reps don't know assets exist | Announce new assets in sales standups, not just email |
| Reps don't trust the content | Involve top reps in creation; peer-validated content gets used |
| Assets don't match real conversations | Shadow sales calls before writing; audit call recordings quarterly |

## Measurement

Track whether enablement content is actually working:

| Metric | How to Measure | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Asset utilization rate | CRM tracking / content platform analytics | > 60% of reps using within 30 days |
| Influence on pipeline | Deals where asset was shared before close | Track correlation with win rate |
| Ramp time for new hires | Time from start to first closed deal | Reduce by 20-30% |
| Rep feedback score | Quarterly survey: "How useful is this asset?" (1-5) | > 4.0 average |
| Content freshness | % of assets updated in last 90 days | > 80% |

# Influencer Campaign Lifecycle Workflow

This reference defines the standard workflow, phase structure, diagnostic questions, tier-based approaches, ethical guidelines, benchmarks, and deliverable format for all influencer marketing work.

---

## Workflow Overview

The Influencer Campaign Lifecycle is a 6-phase workflow that takes influencer campaigns from strategic planning through discovery, contracting, execution, and performance measurement. Every phase has entry conditions, key activities, deliverables, and exit conditions.

The core principle: **Authenticity over control.** The best influencer partnerships give creators freedom within guardrails. Overly scripted content underperforms because audiences detect inauthenticity instantly.

### Phase Summary

| Phase | Name | Purpose | Typical Duration |
|-------|------|---------|-----------------|
| 1 | Strategy & Budget | Define campaign objective, audience, budget, platform focus | 1-2 weeks |
| 2 | Discovery & Vetting | Identify influencers, check audience quality, assess brand fit | 1-3 weeks |
| 3 | Outreach & Negotiation | Personalized outreach, rate negotiation, deliverable agreement | 1-2 weeks |
| 4 | Contracting | Usage rights, FTC disclosure, payment terms, timelines | 3-7 days |
| 5 | Campaign Management | Brief delivery, content review, posting schedule, amplification | 2-6 weeks |
| 6 | Performance Measurement | EMV, engagement, conversions, attribution, ROI calculation | 1-2 weeks post-campaign |

## Phase 1: Strategy & Budget

### Purpose
Define what the influencer campaign needs to achieve, who it targets, how much is available to spend, and which platforms deserve focus. Strategy-first prevents the most common failure: choosing influencers based on follower count instead of business fit.

### Entry Conditions
- Brand context or SOSTAC plan available (or user provides equivalent context)
- User has articulated an influencer marketing need or campaign objective

### Key Activities
1. **Define the campaign objective** (choose one primary objective):
   - **Awareness**: Brand reach, impressions, video views, follower growth
   - **Engagement**: Comments, saves, shares, UGC generation
   - **Traffic**: Website visits, landing page clicks, app downloads
   - **Conversions**: Sales, signups, demo bookings, lead generation
   - **Content production**: Generate high-quality branded content for reuse across channels
   - **Authority**: Position the brand as a category leader through expert endorsements

2. **Map the target audience**:
   - Demographics: Age, gender, location, income level
   - Psychographics: Interests, values, lifestyle, content consumption habits
   - Platform behavior: Where do they spend time? What creators do they follow?
   - Purchase behavior: How do they discover and buy products in this category?

3. **Set the budget** and allocate across tiers:

   | Budget Range | Recommended Tier Mix | Expected Reach |
   |-------------|---------------------|----------------|
   | $1K-5K | 100% nano/micro | 50K-500K impressions |
   | $5K-25K | 70% micro, 30% mid-tier | 500K-2.5M impressions |
   | $25K-100K | 50% mid-tier, 30% micro, 20% macro | 2.5M-15M impressions |
   | $100K+ | 40% macro, 30% mid-tier, 30% micro | 15M+ impressions |

4. **Choose platform focus** (1-2 platforms maximum per campaign):
   - **TikTok**: Best for awareness, viral potential, younger demographics (18-34). Short-form video.
   - **Instagram**: Best for lifestyle brands, visual products, engagement. Reels + Stories + Feed.
   - **YouTube**: Best for in-depth reviews, tutorials, SEO value, long-term discovery. Long-form video.
   - **LinkedIn**: Best for B2B, thought leadership, professional services. Text + carousel posts.
   - **Twitter/X**: Best for tech, news, real-time commentary. Thread format.
   - **Twitch**: Best for gaming, tech, live interaction. Live streams.

5. **Define campaign timeline**:
   - One-off activation: 4-8 weeks total (strategy through measurement)
   - Multi-month partnership: 3-12 months with monthly content cadence
   - Always-on program: Ongoing with quarterly review and roster rotation

6. **Set success metrics** aligned to the primary objective:
   - Awareness: CPM, total impressions, brand mention volume, share of voice
   - Engagement: Engagement rate, cost per engagement, UGC volume, sentiment
   - Traffic: CPC, CTR, sessions from influencer referrals, bounce rate
   - Conversions: CPA, ROAS, attributed revenue, discount code redemptions
   - Content: Cost per asset, content quality score, reuse rate across channels

### Deliverables
- Influencer campaign strategy (objective, audience, budget, platform, timeline, success metrics)
- Budget allocation by tier and platform

### Exit Conditions
- Single primary campaign objective defined
- Budget allocated with tier breakdown
- Platform(s) selected with rationale
- Success metrics defined with specific targets
- Timeline established with key milestones

## Phase 2: Discovery & Vetting

### Purpose
Build a shortlist of influencers who are genuine fits for the brand, have authentic audiences, and can deliver on the campaign objective. This phase prevents the most expensive mistake in influencer marketing: paying for fake or misaligned audiences.

### Entry Conditions
- Campaign strategy from Phase 1 is complete
- Budget, platform, and audience targets are defined

### Key Activities
1. **Influencer identification** (build a longlist of 30-50 candidates):
   - Platform native search: hashtags, keywords, explore pages
   - Influencer platforms: AspireIQ, Grin, CreatorIQ, Upfluence, Heepsy, Modash
   - Competitor monitoring: Who are competitors working with?
   - Community mining: Who are existing customers already following and trusting?
   - Manual discovery: Industry events, podcast guests, conference speakers, community leaders

2. **Audience quality check** (eliminate fakes and mismatches):

   | Quality Signal | Red Flag | Green Flag |
   |---------------|----------|------------|
   | Follower growth | Sudden spikes (purchased followers) | Steady, organic growth pattern |
   | Engagement rate | Below tier average (see benchmarks) | At or above tier average |
   | Comment quality | Generic ("Nice!" "Great post!") or bot patterns | Substantive, conversational replies |
   | Audience demographics | Misaligned geography, age, or interests | Matches target audience profile |
   | Audience authenticity | High % of suspicious/inactive accounts | Majority real, active accounts |
   | Content consistency | Wildly inconsistent topics and quality | Consistent niche focus and quality |

3. **Brand fit assessment** (score 1-10 on each):
   - **Content quality**: Production value, storytelling, creativity
   - **Audience alignment**: How closely their audience matches the target buyer
   - **Brand safety**: History of controversial content, competitor partnerships, brand risks
   - **Authenticity**: Do they genuinely use and recommend products in this category?
   - **Engagement quality**: Do followers actually interact meaningfully?
   - **Professionalism**: Response time, content reliability, partnership track record

4. **Build the shortlist** (narrow to 10-20 candidates):
   - Score each candidate across all dimensions
   - Rank by composite score weighted toward audience alignment and engagement quality
   - Ensure tier diversity matches budget allocation from Phase 1
   - Document rationale for each shortlisted creator

### Tier-Based Discovery Approach

| Tier | Follower Range | Discovery Method | Vetting Depth |
|------|---------------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Nano** (1K-10K) | Platform search, community mining, customer lists | Light vetting: engagement rate, content review, audience geography. Volume-based selection. |
| **Micro** (10K-100K) | Influencer platforms, competitor monitoring, hashtag search | Standard vetting: full audience quality check, brand safety review, content quality assessment |
| **Mid-Tier** (100K-500K) | Influencer platforms, agency databases, industry events | Deep vetting: audience authenticity audit (tool-based), past brand partnership review, reference check |
| **Macro** (500K-1M) | Agency representation, direct outreach, talent management | Full vetting: audience audit report, media kit review, past campaign performance data, legal review |
| **Mega** (1M+) | Talent agencies, management companies | Full vetting + agency negotiation, detailed performance projections, exclusivity review |

### Deliverables
- Influencer longlist (30-50 candidates with tier, platform, follower count, engagement rate)
- Vetting scorecards for shortlisted candidates
- Final shortlist (10-20 candidates) with rationale

### Exit Conditions
- Shortlist has 10-20 vetted candidates across target tier mix
- Each candidate has audience quality check and brand fit score
- No candidates with unresolved brand safety concerns remain on the shortlist
- Shortlist covers the platform and tier allocation from Phase 1

## Phase 3: Outreach & Negotiation

### Purpose
Make contact with shortlisted influencers, negotiate rates and deliverables, and reach agreement on the partnership terms. Personalized outreach dramatically outperforms generic pitches.

### Entry Conditions
- Vetted shortlist from Phase 2 (10-20 candidates)
- Budget allocation per tier confirmed
- Campaign timeline and content requirements defined

### Key Activities
1. **Personalized outreach** (response rate benchmarks: 15-30% for warm, 5-15% for cold):
   - Reference specific content they have created that you genuinely appreciate
   - Explain why their audience is a fit (be specific, not flattering)
   - State the opportunity clearly: brand name, campaign type, expected deliverables, general compensation range
   - Keep it concise: 150-250 words maximum
   - Send via their preferred contact method (DM, email from media kit, management contact)

2. **Rate negotiation** (use benchmark data, not arbitrary discounting):

   | Tier | Instagram Post | Instagram Reel | TikTok Video | YouTube Integration | YouTube Dedicated |
   |------|---------------|----------------|-------------|--------------------|--------------------|
   | Nano (1K-10K) | $50-250 | $75-300 | $100-400 | $200-500 | $500-1,000 |
   | Micro (10K-100K) | $250-1,000 | $300-1,500 | $400-2,000 | $500-2,500 | $1,000-5,000 |
   | Mid-Tier (100K-500K) | $1,000-5,000 | $1,500-7,500 | $2,000-10,000 | $2,500-10,000 | $5,000-20,000 |
   | Macro (500K-1M) | $5,000-15,000 | $7,500-20,000 | $10,000-25,000 | $10,000-30,000 | $20,000-50,000 |
   | Mega (1M+) | $15,000+ | $20,000+ | $25,000+ | $30,000+ | $50,000+ |

   Note: Rates vary significantly by niche, platform, engagement rate, and production value. Creators in finance, tech, and B2B command premium rates. High-engagement creators justify premium over follower count alone.

3. **Deliverable agreement** (clarify before contract):
   - Number and type of content pieces (posts, stories, reels, videos, blog posts)
   - Content exclusivity period (how long before they can work with competitors)
   - Usage rights scope (organic only, paid amplification, website use, email use)
   - Revision rounds included (1-2 is standard; more than 3 suggests misalignment)
   - Posting timeline and any coordination requirements
   - Whitelisting/spark ads: Will the brand run paid ads through the creator's account?

4. **Negotiation principles**:
   - Lead with value, not price: explain the partnership benefits beyond money (exposure, product, early access, long-term relationship)
   - Bundle for better rates: multi-post packages, long-term partnerships, or cross-platform deals
   - Offer product/experience compensation for nano/micro creators when appropriate (but never as a substitute for fair pay on commercial work)
   - Be transparent about budget constraints rather than haggling aggressively
   - Walk away from creators who do not meet quality standards, even at discounted rates

### Deliverables
- Outreach messages sent (tracked with response status)
- Rate and deliverable agreements for each confirmed creator
- Partnership terms summary

### Exit Conditions
- Sufficient creators confirmed to meet campaign objectives (minimum 80% of planned roster)
- Rates negotiated within budget allocation
- Deliverables, timeline, and usage rights agreed in writing (even if informal at this stage)
- Any creators who declined have documented reasons

## Phase 4: Contracting

### Purpose
Formalize the partnership with clear legal terms that protect both the brand and the creator. This phase is non-negotiable for any paid partnership.

### Entry Conditions
- Rate and deliverable agreement from Phase 3
- Legal/compliance team available for review (or standard contract template approved)

### Key Activities
1. **Contract essentials** (every influencer contract must include):
   - **Parties**: Brand legal entity and creator legal entity or full name
   - **Deliverables**: Exact content types, quantities, formats, and specifications
   - **Timeline**: Content submission deadlines, review periods, posting dates
   - **Compensation**: Amount, payment method, payment schedule (50% upfront / 50% on delivery is standard)
   - **Usage rights**: Scope (organic, paid, website, email), duration (6 months, 1 year, perpetual), territory
   - **Exclusivity**: Category exclusivity period (30-90 days is standard; 6+ months commands premium)
   - **FTC/ASA disclosure requirements**: Specific disclosure language and placement requirements
   - **Content approval**: Review and revision process, turnaround times, approval authority
   - **Cancellation terms**: Conditions for termination by either party, compensation for completed work
   - **Confidentiality**: NDA terms for unreleased products, campaign details, or strategy

2. **FTC disclosure requirements** (non-negotiable):
   - Material connections must be disclosed clearly and conspicuously
   - Acceptable: #ad, #sponsored, "Paid partnership with [Brand]" (platform built-in tools)
   - Not acceptable: Buried in hashtag lists, abbreviated (#sp), or only in stories that disappear
   - Disclosure must be in the content itself, not just in the bio or a separate post
   - Both the brand and the creator are liable for non-disclosure
   - International campaigns: Check local regulations (ASA in UK, ARPP in France, etc.)

3. **Payment terms**:
   - Standard: 50% on contract signing, 50% within 30 days of content going live
   - Alternative: Net 30 after content goes live (larger creators may require this)
   - Nano/micro: Full payment on content going live (simpler administration)
   - Performance bonus: Additional compensation tied to exceeding engagement or conversion targets
   - Product compensation: Document retail value, do not substitute for cash on commercial work

4. **Content rights management**:
   - **Organic only**: Creator posts on their channels; brand can repost/share with credit
   - **Paid amplification**: Brand can run ads using the creator's content (whitelisting/spark ads)
   - **Full usage**: Brand can use content across all owned channels (website, email, ads, print)
   - **Perpetual vs. time-limited**: Perpetual rights cost 2-3x more than 6-month rights
   - **Derivative works**: Can the brand edit, crop, or remix the content? Must be specified.

### Tier-Based Contracting Approach

| Tier | Contract Complexity | Payment Structure | Typical Terms |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Nano** | Simple agreement (email confirmation or 1-page brief with terms) | Product + flat fee or product-only for gifting | 30-day exclusivity, organic rights only |
| **Micro** | Standard contract (2-3 pages) | Flat fee per deliverable, 50/50 split | 60-day exclusivity, organic + whitelisting rights |
| **Mid-Tier** | Full contract (3-5 pages) | Flat fee + performance bonus option | 90-day exclusivity, organic + paid + website rights |
| **Macro/Mega** | Comprehensive contract (5+ pages, legal review both sides) | Flat fee + usage fees + performance bonus | 90-180 day exclusivity, full usage with time limits, detailed approval process |

### Deliverables
- Signed contracts for all confirmed creators
- FTC compliance documentation
- Payment schedule and tracking

### Exit Conditions
- All confirmed creators have signed contracts
- FTC disclosure requirements are documented and communicated to each creator
- Payment terms are clear and payment schedule is set
- Usage rights are defined and time-limited where appropriate

## Phase 5: Campaign Management

### Purpose
Execute the campaign by delivering briefs, reviewing content, coordinating posting schedules, and amplifying top-performing content. This is where strategy meets execution.

### Entry Conditions
- Contracts signed with all participating creators
- Campaign timeline confirmed
- Tracking infrastructure ready (UTM links, promo codes, pixels)

### Key Activities
1. **Deliver the creative brief**:
   - **Campaign overview**: Brand story, campaign theme, key messages (2-3 maximum)
   - **Content guidelines**: Do's and don'ts, brand assets, required mentions, hashtags
   - **Key talking points**: What must be communicated (product benefits, offer details, CTA)
   - **Creative freedom**: What the creator can decide (format, hook, personal angle, shooting style)
   - **Technical requirements**: Aspect ratio, minimum duration, caption requirements, link placement
   - **Tracking requirements**: Unique UTM link, promo code, or landing page URL
   - **FTC disclosure reminder**: Exact disclosure language and placement
   - **Deadline**: Content submission date, review window, posting date

   Important: Briefs should be 1-2 pages maximum. Over-briefing kills authenticity. Define outcomes, not scripts.

2. **Content review process**:
   - First draft review within 48 hours of submission
   - Check for: brand safety, factual accuracy, FTC disclosure, key message inclusion, technical requirements
   - Do NOT review for: creator's personal style, phrasing, humor, or aesthetic (this is why you chose them)
   - Maximum 2 revision rounds (more suggests brief failure, not creator failure)
   - Approval authority must be a single person to avoid conflicting feedback

3. **Posting schedule coordination**:
   - Stagger posts across creators to maintain campaign momentum (avoid all posts on one day)
   - Align with brand's own content calendar for cross-promotion
   - Time posts to the creator's optimal engagement window (they know their audience best)
   - Coordinate with paw-mkt-social for brand reshares and engagement

4. **Real-time engagement**:
   - Brand account engages with creator content within 1 hour of posting (comment, like, share)
   - Respond to audience questions about the brand in creator's comments (coordinate with creator)
   - Monitor sentiment: positive, negative, or off-topic conversations

5. **Paid amplification** (for top-performing content):
   - Whitelisting/spark ads: Run paid ads through the creator's account for native feel
   - Boost criteria: Content exceeding 2x average engagement rate in first 24 hours
   - Budget allocation: Reserve 20-30% of campaign budget for amplification of winners
   - Targeting: Expand reach to lookalike audiences of the creator's followers

### Tier-Based Management Approach

| Tier | Brief Depth | Review Rigor | Communication Cadence |
|------|------------|--------------|----------------------|
| **Nano** | Light brief (brand kit + key messages + tracking link) | Quick review: brand safety + disclosure only | Batch communication via email or DM group |
| **Micro** | Standard brief (1 page) | Standard review: all checkpoints | Weekly check-in, available via DM |
| **Mid-Tier** | Detailed brief (1-2 pages) | Full review with creative feedback | Bi-weekly call + ongoing DM |
| **Macro/Mega** | Comprehensive brief + mood board + reference examples | Full review with stakeholder approval | Dedicated point of contact, regular calls |

### Deliverables
- Creative briefs delivered to all creators
- Content review log (submissions, feedback, approvals)
- Posting schedule (confirmed dates and times)
- Amplification decisions and budget allocation

### Exit Conditions
- All contracted content pieces are posted and live
- FTC disclosure verified on every published piece
- Tracking links and promo codes are active and verified
- Brand engagement on all creator posts completed

## Phase 6: Performance Measurement

### Purpose
Measure campaign results against objectives, calculate ROI, identify top-performing creators and content, and make decisions about ongoing partnerships. Measurement that does not lead to decisions is wasted analysis.

### Entry Conditions
- Campaign content has been live for the planned duration
- All tracking data is accessible (platform analytics, UTM data, promo code redemptions, pixel data)
- Minimum 7 days post-last-post for engagement data to stabilize

### Key Activities
1. **Earned Media Value (EMV) calculation**:
   - EMV = Total impressions x CPM equivalent for the platform and audience
   - Industry standard CPM benchmarks:
     | Platform | Average CPM | Premium Niche CPM |
     |----------|------------|-------------------|
     | Instagram | $5-15 | $15-35 |
     | TikTok | $3-10 | $10-25 |
     | YouTube | $10-30 | $30-75 |
     | LinkedIn | $15-40 | $40-80 |
   - Note: EMV is an approximation, not a precise measure. Use alongside harder metrics.

2. **Engagement analysis**:
   - **Engagement rate**: (Likes + Comments + Shares + Saves) / Impressions
   - **Cost per engagement (CPE)**: Total spend / total engagements
   - **Sentiment analysis**: Positive, neutral, negative comment ratio
   - **Comment quality**: Substantive conversations vs. generic reactions
   - Compare each creator's performance to their historical average (outperform = strong brand fit)

3. **Conversion attribution**:
   - **Direct attribution**: Promo code redemptions, unique UTM link clicks, dedicated landing page conversions
   - **Assisted attribution**: Post-purchase survey ("How did you hear about us?"), brand search volume lift during campaign, direct traffic increase
   - **Halo effects**: Social follower growth, email list growth, organic mention increase, branded search volume

4. **ROI calculation**:
   - **Basic ROI**: (Revenue attributed to campaign - Total campaign cost) / Total campaign cost x 100
   - **Blended CPA**: Total campaign cost / total conversions attributed
   - **Lifetime value consideration**: If influencer-acquired customers have higher LTV, adjust ROI accordingly
   - **Content value**: If content is reused across channels, add the production cost savings to ROI

5. **Creator performance ranking**:
   | Score Dimension | Weight | Measurement |
   |----------------|--------|-------------|
   | Engagement rate vs. average | 25% | How much above/below their usual engagement |
   | Conversion rate | 25% | Promo code / UTM attributed conversions |
   | Content quality | 20% | Subjective assessment: production value, brand alignment, storytelling |
   | Professionalism | 15% | Brief adherence, timeline compliance, communication quality |
   | Audience response | 15% | Comment sentiment, follower growth, saves/shares ratio |

6. **Partnership decisions**:
   | Creator Score | Decision |
   |--------------|----------|
   | Top 20% (A-tier) | Extend to long-term partnership (3-12 months). Priority for future campaigns. Consider ambassador program. |
   | Next 30% (B-tier) | Re-engage for next campaign. Adjust brief or deliverables based on learnings. |
   | Middle 30% (C-tier) | Evaluate: Was the issue the brief, the audience, or the creator? Test once more with adjustments or replace. |
   | Bottom 20% (D-tier) | Do not re-engage. Document learnings for future vetting improvement. |

### Tier-Based Measurement Approach

| Tier | Primary Metrics | Attribution Method | Reporting Depth |
|------|----------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Nano** | Engagement rate, content quality, UGC volume | UTM link clicks, promo code redemptions | Aggregate (batch reporting across all nano creators) |
| **Micro** | Engagement rate, conversions, CPE | UTM + promo code + post-purchase survey | Individual creator scorecard |
| **Mid-Tier** | EMV, conversions, CPA, ROAS | Full attribution stack (UTM + promo + pixel + survey) | Detailed individual report |
| **Macro/Mega** | EMV, reach, brand lift, conversions, ROAS | Full attribution + brand lift study (if budget allows) | Comprehensive campaign report with brand impact |

### Deliverables
- Campaign performance report (EMV, engagement, conversions, ROI)
- Creator performance rankings with partnership decisions
- Attribution analysis
- Learnings document (what worked, what failed, what to change)
- Recommendations for next campaign

### Exit Conditions
- All creator content measured against campaign objectives
- ROI calculated with documented methodology
- Creator performance ranked and partnership decisions made
- Learnings documented for future campaign improvement
- Budget reconciliation complete (actual spend vs. planned)

## Diagnostic Questions

Ask these before producing any influencer marketing recommendation. Recommendations without this context are guesses.

1. **Campaign objective**: What is the primary goal? (Awareness, engagement, traffic, conversions, content production?)
2. **Target audience**: Who is the ideal customer? Demographics, interests, platforms, purchase behavior?
3. **Budget**: What is the total influencer marketing budget? Is this a one-time campaign or ongoing program?
4. **Platform**: Where does the target audience spend the most time? Which platform is the primary focus?
5. **Product/service**: What is being promoted? Is it easy to demonstrate visually? Does it require explanation?
6. **Prior experience**: Has the brand worked with influencers before? What worked? What did not? Any existing relationships?
7. **Timeline**: When does this need to launch? Are there external deadlines (product launch, seasonal event, competitor activity)?
8. **Brand voice**: How casual or formal should creator content feel? Are there strict brand guidelines?
9. **Content rights**: Does the brand need to reuse creator content? For how long? Across which channels?
10. **Measurement**: What tracking is in place? Can you track from influencer post to conversion? What attribution model?

If the user cannot answer questions 1-3, recommend defining campaign objectives and audience before identifying influencers. Choosing creators without a strategy is paying for vanity metrics.

## Ethics: Authentic Partnerships Without Deception

Influencer marketing operates on audience trust. When that trust is violated, both the creator and the brand suffer permanent damage. This section defines the ethical line.

### Ethical Influencer Practices

- **Mandatory disclosure**: Every sponsored post must be clearly disclosed per FTC/ASA guidelines. No exceptions. No shortcuts. Both the brand and creator share legal liability.
- **Authentic endorsement**: Only partner with creators who genuinely align with the brand. Forced fits damage both parties' credibility.
- **Fair compensation**: Pay creators fairly for their work. Exposure is not payment for professional content production. Product-only compensation is only appropriate for genuine gifting with no content obligation.
- **Creative freedom**: Respect the creator's voice and style. Audiences follow creators for their authenticity, not for brand-approved scripts.
- **Honest claims**: Creators must not make claims about the product that are not factually supported. Health claims, financial promises, and performance guarantees require substantiation.
- **Audience respect**: Do not ask creators to deceive their audience about the nature of the partnership or the product's capabilities.

### Practices That Are Always Off-Limits

| Practice | Why It Is Banned |
|----------|-----------------|
| Undisclosed paid partnerships | FTC/ASA violation; legal liability for both parties; trust destruction |
| Fake engagement (buying likes/comments on sponsored posts) | Fraud against the brand; platform violations; metric corruption |
| Requiring creators to hide or minimize disclosure | FTC violation; liable to enforcement action; unethical |
| Astroturfing (paid creators posing as organic customers) | Deception; FTC enforcement risk; reputational catastrophe |
| Working with creators who have purchased followers | Wasted budget; inflated metrics; no real reach |
| Requiring creators to make unsubstantiated claims | Legal liability; FTC enforcement; health/safety risk if medical/financial claims |
| Targeting children without parental consent considerations | COPPA violations; ethical boundary; reputational risk |
| Pressuring creators to post against their genuine opinion | Destroys authenticity; creator relationship damage; audience trust violation |

### The Influencer Ethics Test

Before launching any influencer partnership, ask:

1. **If the FTC reviewed this campaign, would every post be compliant?**
2. **Does this creator genuinely use or believe in this product category?**
3. **If the creator's audience discovered the full terms of this deal, would they feel respected?**
4. **Would we be comfortable if this partnership was featured in a trade publication?**

If any answer raises doubt, address the issue before proceeding.

Use these benchmarks to set realistic expectations and evaluate campaign performance.

### Engagement Rate Benchmarks by Tier and Platform

| Tier | Instagram | TikTok | YouTube | LinkedIn |
|------|-----------|--------|---------|----------|
| Nano (1K-10K) | 4-8% | 8-15% | 4-8% | 5-10% |
| Micro (10K-100K) | 2-5% | 5-10% | 3-6% | 3-7% |
| Mid-Tier (100K-500K) | 1.5-3% | 3-7% | 2-4% | 2-5% |
| Macro (500K-1M) | 1-2% | 2-5% | 1.5-3% | 1.5-3% |
| Mega (1M+) | 0.5-1.5% | 1-3% | 1-2% | 1-2% |

### Campaign Performance Benchmarks

| Metric | Below Average | Average | Good | Excellent |
|--------|--------------|---------|------|-----------|
| Campaign CPM | > $25 | $10-25 | $5-10 | < $5 |
| Cost per engagement | > $1.00 | $0.25-1.00 | $0.10-0.25 | < $0.10 |
| Promo code redemption rate | < 0.5% | 0.5-2% | 2-5% | 5%+ |
| Landing page CTR (from influencer) | < 1% | 1-3% | 3-7% | 7%+ |
| Campaign ROAS | < 1x | 1-3x | 3-6x | 6x+ |
| Creator response rate (outreach) | < 5% | 5-15% | 15-25% | 25%+ |
| Content approval rate (first draft) | < 50% | 50-70% | 70-90% | 90%+ |

### Long-Term Partnership vs. One-Off ROI

| Approach | Average ROAS | Engagement Lift | Content Quality | Brand Affinity Impact |
|----------|-------------|----------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| One-off activation | 1-3x | Baseline | Standard | Minimal |
| 3-month partnership | 2-4x | +20-40% | Improved (creator learns brand) | Moderate |
| 6-month partnership | 3-6x | +30-60% | High (deep brand understanding) | Strong |
| 12-month ambassador | 4-8x | +40-80% | Excellent (authentic advocacy) | Very strong |

## Path Resolution: Campaign vs Standalone

**Campaign mode** -- working within a named campaign:
  -> Save to `./.pawbytes/marketing-suites/brands/{brand-slug}/campaigns/{type}-{campaign-slug}/channels/influencer/content/`
  -> Read campaign strategy at `./.pawbytes/marketing-suites/brands/{brand-slug}/campaigns/{type}-{campaign-slug}/strategy.md`

**Standalone mode** -- evergreen or independent work:
  -> Save to `./.pawbytes/marketing-suites/brands/{brand-slug}/channels/influencer/content/`

**Legacy fallback** -- old directory structure detected:
  -> Suggest migration to new structure

If unsure which mode, ask: "Is this part of a specific campaign, or standalone work?"

## Response Protocol

When the user requests influencer marketing work:

1. **Read brand context and SOSTAC** when available; otherwise proceed from user-provided context, competitor data, or existing deliverables.
2. **Clarify scope**: Strategy, discovery, outreach, campaign management, UGC program, affiliate program, or performance review?
3. **Ask diagnostic questions** if the user has not already provided campaign objective, audience, budget, and platform.
4. **Identify the influencer phase** and load the appropriate reference:
   - Strategy -> `./references/influencer-strategy.md`
   - Discovery and vetting -> `./references/influencer-identification.md`
   - Outreach -> `./references/outreach-frameworks.md`
   - Campaign types -> `./references/campaign-types.md`
   - Compensation -> `./references/compensation.md`
   - UGC programs -> `./references/ugc-programs.md`
   - Affiliate programs -> `./references/affiliate-programs.md`
   - Campaign management -> `./references/campaign-management.md`
   - Performance measurement -> `./references/performance-measurement.md`
5. **Apply tier-based approaches** throughout -- nano/micro/mid-tier/macro/mega require different tactics at every phase.
6. **Deliver actionable output**: Strategies, shortlists, briefs, templates, scorecards, reports.
7. **Save deliverables** to the resolved path (see Path Resolution).
8. **Recommend next steps**: Which creators to prioritize, what to test, when to review performance.

## 1. Engagement Rates by Platform and Tier

### Instagram Engagement Rates

| Tier | Followers | Avg Engagement Rate | Typical Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nano | 1K-10K | 4.8% | 3.5-7.0% |
| Micro | 10K-100K | 2.4% | 1.8-3.5% |
| Mid-Tier | 100K-500K | 1.7% | 1.2-2.5% |
| Macro | 500K-1M | 1.2% | 0.8-1.8% |
| Mega | 1M+ | 0.9% | 0.5-1.5% |

- Engagement formula: (Likes + Comments + Saves + Shares) / Reach x 100
- Reels consistently outperform static posts by 2-3x in reach and engagement.
- Carousels generate highest save rates; Stories generate highest link click rates.
- Below 1% engagement rate on Instagram is a red flag regardless of tier.

### TikTok Engagement Rates

| Tier | Followers | Avg Engagement Rate | Typical Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nano | 1K-10K | 10.3% | 7.0-15.0% |
| Micro | 10K-100K | 8.1% | 5.0-12.0% |
| Mid-Tier | 100K-500K | 6.8% | 4.0-10.0% |
| Macro | 500K-1M | 5.5% | 3.5-8.0% |
| Mega | 1M+ | 4.9% | 3.0-7.0% |

- TikTok engagement rates are significantly higher than Instagram across all tiers.
- Algorithm-driven distribution means even small creators can achieve viral reach.
- TikTok affiliate content achieves 5.2% engagement rate, 160% higher than Instagram affiliate content.
- Creators with under 50K followers achieve 30.1% engagement rates on TikTok affiliate content specifically.
- Below 2% engagement rate on TikTok is a red flag.

### YouTube Engagement Rates

| Tier | Subscribers | Avg Engagement Rate | Typical Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nano | 1K-10K | 3.5% | 2.0-6.0% |
| Micro | 10K-100K | 2.8% | 1.5-4.5% |
| Mid-Tier | 100K-500K | 2.0% | 1.0-3.5% |
| Macro | 500K-1M | 1.5% | 0.8-2.5% |
| Mega | 1M+ | 1.1% | 0.5-2.0% |

- YouTube engagement includes likes, comments, shares, and subscribe actions relative to views.
- Long-form content (8-20 minutes) generates highest watch time and subscriber conversion.
- YouTube Shorts engagement rates are comparable to TikTok but with lower average view counts.
- Evergreen content accumulates engagement over months and years, unlike Instagram/TikTok.

## 3. ROAS by Influencer Tier

| Tier | Avg ROAS | Typical Range | Best Performers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nano (1K-10K) | 5.0x | 3-8x | 10-15x |
| Micro (10K-100K) | 4.0x | 2-6x | 8-12x |
| Mid-Tier (100K-500K) | 2.5x | 1.5-4x | 6-8x |
| Macro (500K-1M) | 1.8x | 1-3x | 4-6x |
| Mega (1M+) | 1.2x | 0.5-2x | 3-5x |

- Average influencer marketing ROI: $5.78 per dollar spent (Influencer Marketing Hub 2025).
- ROAS varies significantly by product price point, niche, and campaign type.
- Higher-priced products tend to show higher absolute ROAS due to per-sale revenue.
- DTC brands report highest influencer ROAS; B2B shows lower ROAS but higher lifetime value per conversion.
- Long-term partnerships (3-12 months) deliver 2-3x higher ROAS than one-off activations.
- Creators on Aspire drove $52 million in attributed affiliate sales in 2025, a 45% year-over-year growth.

## 7. Attribution Benchmarks

### Measurability by Method

| Attribution Method | Accuracy | Coverage | Ease of Setup | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Unique discount codes | High | 30-50% of conversions | Easy | Direct response campaigns |
| UTM-tagged links (GA4) | High | 20-40% of conversions | Medium | Website traffic and conversion |
| Affiliate platform tracking | High | 40-60% of conversions | Medium | Commission-based programs |
| Post-purchase surveys | Medium-High | 15-30% of conversions | Easy | First-party attribution |
| Brand search lift | Medium | Indirect measurement | Complex | Awareness campaigns |
| Platform-native insights | Medium | Platform-specific only | Easy | Content performance |
| Revenue correlation analysis | Low-Medium | Broad trends only | Complex | Overall program evaluation |
| Unique landing pages | High | Per-creator tracking | Medium | High-value partnerships |

### Attribution Gap

- Expect 20-40% of influencer-driven impact to be unmeasurable through direct response methods.
- Influencer marketing has strong "dark social" effects: screenshots, word-of-mouth, DM sharing that cannot be tracked.
- Multi-touch attribution is essential: a consumer may see influencer content, search on Google, then purchase from a retargeting ad. Only one channel gets credit in last-click models.
- Best practice: combine multiple attribution methods (discount codes + UTM links + post-purchase surveys + brand search lift) for the most complete picture.
- Influencer-driven orders during Cyber Week 2025 nearly doubled year-over-year (Impact.com), demonstrating growing influence on purchase decisions even when attribution is indirect.

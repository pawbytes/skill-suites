# Paid Advertising Benchmarks by Platform & Industry (2025-2026)

Industry benchmark data for paid advertising performance metrics across major platforms. Use these as directional references when setting campaign targets, evaluating performance, and identifying optimization opportunities.

**Important notes on using benchmarks:**
- Benchmarks represent medians and averages; top performers significantly exceed these numbers
- Your results will vary based on offer strength, creative quality, landing page experience, and competitive landscape
- Always compare against your own historical data first, then industry benchmarks
- Benchmarks shift quarterly -- use these as starting references, then calibrate to your actual data

### 6.4 Scaling Rules of Thumb

| Rule | Details |
|---|---|
| Budget increase cadence | 15-20% every 3-5 days (never more than 2x at once) |
| CPA tolerance during scaling | Allow 15-20% CPA increase during scaling; recalibrate if it exceeds 30% |
| Creative-to-spend ratio | Produce 1 new creative per $1,000 of monthly spend (minimum) |
| Learning phase budget | Daily budget should be 5-10x the target CPA |
| Audience saturation signal | Frequency exceeds 2.5 in prospecting; CPM rises 30%+ without audience change |
| Platform diversification trigger | When primary platform CPA increases 20%+ despite optimization, test a new platform |
| Diminishing returns threshold | Typically hits at 70-80% impression share (search) or 3.0 frequency (social) |
| Minimum test budget | $500 or 50 conversions (whichever comes first) per test variant |

## 8. Seasonal Trends and CPM Fluctuations

### 8.1 Quarterly CPM Index (baseline = Q1)

| Quarter | Meta CPM Index | Google CPC Index | TikTok CPM Index | LinkedIn CPM Index |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 (Jan-Mar) | 1.00x (baseline) | 1.00x | 0.90x | 1.05x |
| Q2 (Apr-Jun) | 1.10x | 1.05x | 1.00x | 1.15x |
| Q3 (Jul-Sep) | 1.05x | 1.00x | 0.95x | 0.95x |
| Q4 (Oct-Dec) | 1.40x - 1.80x | 1.15x - 1.30x | 1.20x - 1.50x | 1.10x |

**Notes:**
- Q4 is the most expensive quarter across all platforms (holiday competition, Black Friday, year-end budgets)
- Meta CPMs can spike 40-80% in November/December
- LinkedIn is most expensive in Q2 (budget planning season) and Q1 (new year initiatives)
- January is often the cheapest month on Meta and TikTok (post-holiday advertiser pullback)
- Plan budgets with seasonal multipliers to maintain consistent impression volume year-round

### 8.2 Day-of-Week Performance Patterns

| Day | B2C Social (Meta/TikTok) | B2B (LinkedIn/Google) | E-commerce (All) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monday | Average | Strong (week start) | Below average |
| Tuesday | Above average | Strongest | Average |
| Wednesday | Above average | Strong | Above average |
| Thursday | Average | Strong | Above average |
| Friday | Below average | Below average | Average |
| Saturday | Strong | Weak | Strongest |
| Sunday | Strongest | Weak | Strong |

**Notes:**
- B2C social campaigns perform best on weekends when users have leisure time
- B2B campaigns perform best Tuesday through Thursday during business hours
- E-commerce peaks on Saturday as consumers browse and shop
- Use dayparting and day-of-week bid adjustments to optimize spend allocation

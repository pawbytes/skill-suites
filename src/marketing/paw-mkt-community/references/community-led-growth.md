# Community-Led Growth

Community-Led Growth (CLG) is a go-to-market strategy where the community itself becomes the primary engine for acquisition, retention, and expansion.

## The Community Flywheel

The CLG flywheel operates in four stages:

1. **Attract**: Community content, member stories, and organic search draw new prospects.
2. **Activate**: Onboarding converts visitors into participating members within the first 48 hours.
3. **Engage**: Consistent value delivery (events, content, peer support) deepens member investment.
4. **Advocate**: Engaged members refer others, create content, and defend the brand -- feeding back into attraction.

Each stage should have measurable conversion rates. The flywheel stalls when any stage underperforms.

## Referral from Community

Design community mechanics that naturally produce new members:

- **Invite incentives**: Members who invite others earn perks (exclusive access, points, badges, recognition). Two-sided rewards: the inviter and invitee both benefit.
- **Shareable moments**: Create content and experiences within the community that members naturally want to share externally. Challenge results, achievements, event highlights.
- **Referral tracking**: Unique invite links per member. Dashboard showing who invited whom. Monthly recognition of top inviters.

Top-performing communities generate 15-25% of new members through member referrals.

## Community as Social Proof

- **Public wins**: Showcase member success stories on your website, social media, and sales pages. "Our community helped 500 marketers land their first client."
- **Testimonial mining**: Community conversations are a goldmine of authentic quotes. Ask permission, then use in marketing materials.
- **Community size as proof**: "Join 10,000 marketers" is social proof. Display member count on landing pages and in ads.

## Member-Get-Member Programs

Structure a formal program:
1. Every member gets a unique referral link upon joining.
2. Track referrals and display a personal dashboard.
3. Tiered rewards: 1 referral = badge, 5 = exclusive channel access, 10 = free month, 25 = lifetime membership or special title.
4. Monthly leaderboard and recognition for top referrers.
5. Seasonal referral campaigns with boosted rewards.

## Community Content as Marketing

Repurpose community activity into public-facing content:
- **Discussion highlights**: "Best of the community this week" newsletter or social post.
- **Member case studies**: In-depth stories of member success, published as blog posts.
- **Curated wisdom**: Compile community answers into guides, resource lists, or FAQ pages.
- **Event recordings**: Edit community events into podcast episodes, YouTube videos, or social clips.

## Advocacy Programs

Turn top community members into formal brand advocates.

For detailed ambassador program structure, look up `ambassador-program` in `./references/frameworks-index.csv` and read the referenced file.

**Ambassador program basics**:
- Application-based. 10-30 ambassadors.
- Benefits include early access, direct team contact, swag, co-creation opportunities, and public recognition.
- Responsibilities: Share brand content, represent the brand at events, provide feedback, recruit new members, create content.

**Certification programs**: Members who complete training or demonstrate expertise earn a public credential. Certified members become evangelists who carry the brand into other communities.

## Owned vs. Rented Audiences

| Factor | Owned (Circle, Discourse, custom) | Rented (Facebook, Reddit, Discord) |
|--------|----------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Data ownership | Full control, exportable | Limited or no export |
| Algorithm control | You control visibility | Platform algorithm decides reach |
| Monetization | Full flexibility | Platform restrictions apply |
| Branding | White-label possible | Platform branding dominates |
| Risk | Self-hosted reliability | Platform can change rules, suspend accounts |
| Discovery | Must drive traffic yourself | Built-in distribution and discoverability |

**2026 trend**: Hub-and-spoke model -- owned primary platform supplemented by presence on rented platforms that funnel members to the owned hub.

# Onboarding Flow Design

## The First 48 Hours

The first 48 hours determine whether a member stays or ghosts. Members who complete at least one action within 48 hours are 3-5x more likely to be retained at 90 days.

**Onboarding sequence**:

**Step 1: Welcome message (within 5 minutes of joining)**
- Automated DM or email
- Include: what this community is about (1 sentence), 2-3 channels/spaces to visit first, how to introduce themselves
- Tone: warm, specific, actionable. Not a wall of text.

**Step 2: Introduction post (within first session)**
- Direct prompt to post an introduction
- Provide a template: "Name, what you do, what you hope to get from this community, one fun fact"
- Community manager and/or moderators respond to every introduction within 4 hours

**Step 3: First meaningful action (within 24 hours)**
- Guide member to one low-friction engagement action
- Options: answer a discussion prompt, react to a post, join an upcoming event, share a resource
- The goal is to break the "lurker barrier" with the smallest possible action

**Step 4: First connection (within 48 hours)**
- Facilitate a member-to-member connection
- Tools: Donut bot (Slack), introduction threads, accountability pairs, welcome buddy system
- One human connection in the first 48 hours significantly increases retention

**Step 5: Day 7 check-in**
- Automated or personal follow-up
- If they have posted: highlight recent discussions they might enjoy, invite to upcoming event
- If they have not posted: send a personal nudge with a specific, easy prompt to respond to

## Onboarding Optimization Benchmarks

| Metric | Target | Action if Below Target |
|---|---|---|
| Welcome message open/read rate | 70%+ | Shorten message, improve subject line, change timing |
| Introduction post rate (within 7 days) | 40-60% | Simplify template, add moderator responses faster, reduce friction |
| First action completion (within 7 days) | 50-70% | Lower the barrier -- try reactions before posts |
| Return visit within 48 hours | 30-50% | Improve content quality, add daily notification digest |
| 30-day retention (post-onboarding) | 40-55% | Review the full onboarding flow for gaps |

## Reducing Onboarding Friction

Common friction points and solutions:
- **Too many steps before access**: Minimize gates. One screening question maximum for free communities.
- **Overwhelming information on day one**: Drip information over 7 days, not all at once.
- **No immediate value**: Ensure the first thing a member sees is a high-value discussion or resource, not an empty feed.
- **No human response**: If introductions go unacknowledged, members disengage. Staff and moderators must respond.
- **Unclear expectations**: Tell members exactly what to do first, second, and third.

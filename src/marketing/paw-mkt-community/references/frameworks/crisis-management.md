# Community Crisis Management

## Crisis Types and Response Protocols

**Type 1: Internal Drama (member conflicts, moderator issues)**
- Severity: Low to Medium
- Response time: Within 4 hours
- Protocol: Move to private, hear both sides, apply rules consistently, document, follow up. Public statement only if the community is broadly aware.

**Type 2: Mass Exodus (significant member departure)**
- Severity: High
- Response time: Within 24 hours
- Protocol: Identify cause (platform issue, controversy, competitor, value decline). Address root cause publicly with transparency. Reach out to departing members individually for feedback. Implement changes visibly and quickly. Communicate action plan to remaining members.

**Type 3: Platform Outage or Data Loss**
- Severity: High
- Response time: Immediately
- Protocol: Communicate on backup channel (email, social media). Provide regular updates. Document what was lost. Restore from backups where possible. Post-mortem with prevention plan.

**Type 4: PR Crisis (brand controversy affecting community)**
- Severity: High to Critical
- Response time: Within 2 hours
- Protocol: Coordinate with PR/comms team. Acknowledge the situation honestly in community. Do not delete or suppress discussion. Provide facts, not spin. Follow up with concrete actions. Moderators monitor for escalation.

**Type 5: Toxic Takeover (coordinated disruption, brigading)**
- Severity: Critical
- Response time: Immediately
- Protocol: Lock new member registration temporarily. Increase moderation presence. Ban coordinated bad actors. Restrict posting in affected channels. Communicate with trusted members. Document everything for potential legal action.

## Crisis Communication Principles

1. **Speed**: Acknowledge within hours, not days. Silence breeds speculation.
2. **Transparency**: Share what you know, what you do not know, and what you are doing about it.
3. **Empathy**: Acknowledge member feelings before defending positions.
4. **Accountability**: Own mistakes. Do not deflect or minimize.
5. **Action**: Every crisis communication should include what you are doing to fix it.
6. **Follow-through**: Update the community as you make progress. Close the loop.

## Post-Crisis Recovery

After any significant crisis:
- **Debrief**: Internal review of what happened, response effectiveness, and prevention measures.
- **Community survey**: Check in with members on how they feel, what they need, and whether trust is intact.
- **Visible improvements**: Implement at least one concrete change that members can see.
- **Re-engagement campaign**: Extra programming, events, or content in the 2-4 weeks post-crisis to rebuild momentum.
- **Documentation**: Add the crisis and response to an internal playbook for future reference.

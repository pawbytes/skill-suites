# Scaling Playbook

## When to Hire

| Community Size (Active) | Staffing Recommendation | Budget Range |
|---|---|---|
| Under 200 | Part-time community manager (0.5 FTE) | $25-40K (part-time) |
| 200-500 | Full-time community manager | $50-80K |
| 500-1,000 | CM + 2-3 volunteer moderators | $50-80K + volunteer program |
| 1,000-2,500 | CM + community coordinator + 5-10 volunteer mods | $100-160K |
| 2,500-5,000 | Community team lead + CM + coordinator + volunteer program | $150-250K |
| 5,000-10,000 | Head of community + 2-3 CMs + moderator program | $250-400K |
| 10,000+ | Full community department | $400K+ |

## Volunteer Moderator Program

**Recruitment**:
- Recruit from active, constructive members (not just the most vocal)
- Application process: short form + interview
- Target ratio: 1 volunteer moderator per 200-500 active members

**Training**:
- Written moderator handbook (guidelines, escalation, tone, tools)
- Shadow period: 1-2 weeks observing before active moderation
- Weekly moderator sync call (30 min) for alignment and support

**Retention**:
- Public recognition (special role, badge, community shoutout)
- Private perks (direct team access, early feature access, swag)
- Regular check-ins to prevent burnout
- Maximum 10-15 hours/week commitment recommendation
- Quarterly review with clear feedback and appreciation

## Community-Led Moderation

As communities scale beyond 5,000 active members, transition from staff-led to community-led moderation:
- Empower trusted members with moderation permissions
- Establish clear moderation guidelines that members enforce
- Community voting on borderline content decisions
- Moderation council (5-10 senior members) for complex cases
- Staff moderators handle only escalations and edge cases

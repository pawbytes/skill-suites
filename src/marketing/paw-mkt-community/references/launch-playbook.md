## 1. 90-Day Launch Plan

Nearly 70% of online communities fail in their first year (Gartner). The primary causes are unclear goals, no strategic alignment, and inconsistent execution. This playbook provides a week-by-week plan to avoid those failures.

### Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1-30)

**Week 1: Strategic Foundation**
| Action | Owner | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|
| Define community purpose (1-2 sentences) | Community lead | Community promise statement |
| Identify primary community type (customer, industry, interest, practice) | Community lead + leadership | Type decision documented |
| Define target member persona (who are the first 50 people?) | Community lead + marketing | Member persona document |
| Set 90-day success metrics and targets | Community lead | Metrics dashboard setup |
| Audit existing audience touchpoints (email list, social, customers) | Marketing team | Audience inventory with sizes |
| Review brand SOSTAC plan for community alignment | Community lead | Alignment notes |

**Week 2: Platform and Infrastructure**
| Action | Owner | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|
| Select community platform (see platform decision framework) | Community lead | Platform decision rationale |
| Set up platform account with channel/space structure | Community lead | Configured platform |
| Configure roles, permissions, and access tiers | Community lead | Roles and permissions document |
| Set up moderation tools (bots, filters, reporting) | Community lead | Moderation tools configured |
| Write community guidelines and code of conduct | Community lead | Published guidelines |
| Design onboarding flow (welcome message, intro prompt, first action) | Community lead | Onboarding sequence documented |

**Week 3: Content Seeding and Recruitment Prep**
| Action | Owner | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|
| Write 10-15 seed discussion posts (varied categories) | Content team | Pre-written posts scheduled |
| Create 3-5 resource threads (guides, FAQs, tools) | Content team | Published resource threads |
| Write welcome guide for new members | Community lead | Welcome guide published |

| Action | Owner | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|

| Gather founding member feedback on platform and structure | Community lead | Feedback log |
| Assign "Founding Member" badges or roles | Community lead | Badges assigned |

**Week 7: Public Launch**
| Action | Owner | Deliverable |

| Open community to public with launch event | Community lead | Community publicly open |
| Run 48-hour welcome challenge for all new members | Community lead | Challenge active |

**Week 8: Post-Launch Stabilization**
| Action | Owner | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|

## 2. Founding Member Recruitment Playbook

### 2.1 Who to Recruit

Founding members set the culture, norms, and tone for the entire community. They are the most important community decision.

**Ideal founding member sources** (in priority order):
1. Power users or top customers who already engage with your product actively
2. Vocal customers who give unsolicited feedback (positive or constructive)
3. Social media followers who comment and engage regularly
4. Email subscribers who reply to newsletters
5. Beta testers from previous product launches
6. Industry contacts who share your target audience
7. Existing network connections who match the member persona

**Founding member qualities to look for**:
- Already active in relevant online spaces (proves willingness to engage)
- Shares or creates content related to your topic area
- Has given you feedback before (shows investment in the relationship)
- Has a track record of being constructive and collaborative
- Represents the diversity of your target audience

### 2.2 Outreach Strategy

**Personal invitation (highest conversion, 40-60%)**:
- Individual email or DM, not a mass blast
- Reference a specific interaction or contribution they have made
- Explain what makes them specifically valuable as a founding member
- Template: "Hi [Name], I am building [community name] for [audience] to [value proposition]. I thought of you because [specific reason]. As a founding member, you would get [benefits]. Would you like to join the first group? I am inviting just [number] people to start."

**Waitlist application (medium conversion, 15-30%)**:
- Landing page with community promise and founding member benefits
- Short application form (3-5 questions: who are you, why join, what will you contribute)
- Creates scarcity and selectivity signals
- Review applications and send personal acceptance messages

**Existing channel promotion (lower conversion, 5-15%)**:
- Newsletter announcement with exclusive founding member offer
- Social media posts teasing the community with an application link
- In-product notification for eligible users
- Less personal, but broader reach

### 2.3 Founding Member Benefits

| Benefit | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| "Founding Member" title/badge (permanent) | Identity and status -- visible recognition that persists |
| Direct input on community direction | Ownership and voice -- members feel invested |
| Early access to features, content, or events | Exclusivity -- tangible value for being first |
| Direct access to the team (founders, product, leadership) | Proximity to power -- high-value perk for engaged members |
| Locked-in pricing (if paid) at 20-40% discount forever | Financial incentive with long-term lock-in |
| Public recognition as the community grows | Status that compounds -- becomes more valuable over time |

## 3. Onboarding Flow Design

### 3.1 The First 48 Hours

The first 48 hours determine whether a member stays or ghosts. Members who complete at least one action within 48 hours are 3-5x more likely to be retained at 90 days.

**Onboarding sequence**:

**Step 1: Welcome message (within 5 minutes of joining)**
- Automated DM or email
- Include: what this community is about (1 sentence), 2-3 channels/spaces to visit first, how to introduce themselves
- Tone: warm, specific, actionable. Not a wall of text.

**Step 2: Introduction post (within first session)**
- Direct prompt to post an introduction
- Provide a template: "Name, what you do, what you hope to get from this community, one fun fact"
- Community manager and/or moderators respond to every introduction within 4 hours

**Step 3: First meaningful action (within 24 hours)**
- Guide member to one low-friction engagement action
- Options: answer a discussion prompt, react to a post, join an upcoming event, share a resource
- The goal is to break the "lurker barrier" with the smallest possible action

**Step 4: First connection (within 48 hours)**
- Facilitate a member-to-member connection
- Tools: Donut bot (Slack), introduction threads, accountability pairs, welcome buddy system
- One human connection in the first 48 hours significantly increases retention

**Step 5: Day 7 check-in**
- Automated or personal follow-up
- If they have posted: highlight recent discussions they might enjoy, invite to upcoming event
- If they have not posted: send a personal nudge with a specific, easy prompt to respond to

### 3.2 Onboarding Optimization Benchmarks

| Metric | Target | Action if Below Target |
|---|---|---|
| Welcome message open/read rate | 70%+ | Shorten message, improve subject line, change timing |
| Introduction post rate (within 7 days) | 40-60% | Simplify template, add moderator responses faster, reduce friction |
| First action completion (within 7 days) | 50-70% | Lower the barrier -- try reactions before posts |
| Return visit within 48 hours | 30-50% | Improve content quality, add daily notification digest |
| 30-day retention (post-onboarding) | 40-55% | Review the full onboarding flow for gaps |

### 3.3 Reducing Onboarding Friction

Common friction points and solutions:
- **Too many steps before access**: Minimize gates. One screening question maximum for free communities.
- **Overwhelming information on day one**: Drip information over 7 days, not all at once.
- **No immediate value**: Ensure the first thing a member sees is a high-value discussion or resource, not an empty feed.
- **No human response**: If introductions go unacknowledged, members disengage. Staff and moderators must respond.
- **Unclear expectations**: Tell members exactly what to do first, second, and third.

## 5. Ambassador and Champion Program Design

### 5.1 Three-Tier Framework

Structured ambassador programs with clear tiers show 33% better tracking accuracy and double the conversion rates of unstructured programs (Shopify/Roster research).

**Tier 1: Supporters (Entry Level)**
| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| Criteria to enter | Active for 30+ days, 10+ posts or comments, positive engagement history |
| Responsibilities | Welcome new members, answer basic questions, share community content externally |
| Rewards | Supporter badge, recognition in community, early access to new content |
| Number of people | Unlimited (anyone who qualifies) |
| Progression | Automatic qualification based on activity metrics |

**Tier 2: Champions (Mid Level)**
| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| Criteria to enter | Supporter for 60+ days, 50+ posts/comments, hosted or co-hosted an event, referred 3+ members |
| Responsibilities | Lead discussions, moderate threads, mentor new members, create original content, represent community at events |
| Rewards | Champion badge, direct team access, swag, exclusive channel/space, input on community direction |
| Number of people | 10-30 (curated, application-based) |
| Progression | Application + review by community team |

**Tier 3: Ambassadors (Top Level)**
| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| Criteria to enter | Champion for 90+ days, created 5+ content pieces, referred 10+ members, demonstrated leadership |
| Responsibilities | Speak on behalf of community externally, create case studies, co-create content with team, advise on strategy, recruit champions |
| Rewards | Ambassador title, co-creation opportunities, annual summit invitation, product input, revenue share or compensation |
| Number of people | 5-15 (highly selective, invitation-based) |
| Progression | Invitation from community team based on track record |

### 5.2 Ambassador Program Operations

- **Monthly ambassador sync**: 30-minute video call with all ambassadors. Share updates, gather feedback, align on priorities.
- **Quarterly ambassador review**: Evaluate each ambassador's activity, impact, and tier status. Promote, maintain, or (rarely) off-board.
- **Ambassador toolkit**: Brand guidelines, content templates, talking points, referral links, swag kit.
- **Recognition calendar**: Public recognition monthly (social shoutout, community spotlight). Private recognition quarterly (personal thank you from leadership).

## 6. Gamification Framework

### 6.1 Points System

| Action | Points | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Post a discussion | 10 | Original content creation |
| Reply to a discussion | 5 | Conversation contribution |
| Receive a reaction/like | 2 | Quality signal from peers |
| Complete profile | 15 | One-time onboarding action |
| Post an introduction | 20 | One-time activation action |
| Attend an event | 25 | Live participation |
| Host or co-host an event | 50 | Leadership action |
| Refer a new member | 30 | Growth contribution |
| Complete a challenge | 40 | Sustained engagement |
| Create a resource or guide | 50 | High-value content contribution |
| Get featured in member spotlight | 75 | Recognition milestone |

### 6.2 Level System

| Level | Name | Points Required | Unlocked Perks |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Newcomer | 0 | Basic community access |
| 2 | Member | 50 | Access to resource library, ability to create polls |
| 3 | Contributor | 200 | Exclusive channel/space access, ability to host threads |
| 4 | Expert | 500 | Direct team access channel, early feature access, custom badge |
| 5 | Elder | 1,000 | Advisory board eligibility, co-creation invitations, annual summit invite |
| 6 | Legend | 2,500 | Permanent recognition, lifetime benefits, ambassador invitation |

### 6.3 Badges

| Badge | Criteria | Category |
|---|---|---|
| Founding Member | Joined during founding period | Identity |
| First Post | Made first discussion post | Activation |
| Conversation Starter | Started 10 discussions that each received 3+ replies | Engagement |
| Helpful Hand | Received 25 "helpful" reactions on answers | Quality |
| Event Regular | Attended 5+ community events | Participation |
| Event Host | Hosted or co-hosted a community event | Leadership |
| Challenge Champion | Completed 3+ community challenges | Commitment |
| Recruiter | Referred 5+ new members | Growth |
| Content Creator | Published 10+ original resources or guides | Contribution |
| Milestone Member | Active for 12+ consecutive months | Loyalty |

### 6.4 Leaderboard Design

- **Monthly reset**: Leaderboards reset each month to keep competition accessible. New members can compete immediately.
- **Top 3 recognition**: Monthly recognition for the top 3 members. Prize options: exclusive access, swag, 1:1 call with team, feature in community spotlight.
- **Category leaderboards**: Separate leaderboards for different activities (most helpful, most active, most referrals) so different behavior types are rewarded.
- **Opt-out option**: Allow members to opt out of public leaderboard if they prefer privacy.

**Important caveat**: Gamification amplifies existing engagement. It cannot fix a community with no inherent value. If members are not engaged without points, points will not save it.

## 8. Scaling Playbook

### 8.1 When to Hire

| Community Size (Active) | Staffing Recommendation | Budget Range |
|---|---|---|
| Under 200 | Part-time community manager (0.5 FTE) | $25-40K (part-time) |
| 200-500 | Full-time community manager | $50-80K |
| 500-1,000 | CM + 2-3 volunteer moderators | $50-80K + volunteer program |
| 1,000-2,500 | CM + community coordinator + 5-10 volunteer mods | $100-160K |
| 2,500-5,000 | Community team lead + CM + coordinator + volunteer program | $150-250K |
| 5,000-10,000 | Head of community + 2-3 CMs + moderator program | $250-400K |
| 10,000+ | Full community department | $400K+ |

### 8.2 Volunteer Moderator Program

**Recruitment**:
- Recruit from active, constructive members (not just the most vocal)
- Application process: short form + interview
- Target ratio: 1 volunteer moderator per 200-500 active members

**Training**:
- Written moderator handbook (guidelines, escalation, tone, tools)
- Shadow period: 1-2 weeks observing before active moderation
- Weekly moderator sync call (30 min) for alignment and support

**Retention**:
- Public recognition (special role, badge, community shoutout)
- Private perks (direct team access, early feature access, swag)
- Regular check-ins to prevent burnout
- Maximum 10-15 hours/week commitment recommendation
- Quarterly review with clear feedback and appreciation

### 8.3 Community-Led Moderation

As communities scale beyond 5,000 active members, transition from staff-led to community-led moderation:
- Empower trusted members with moderation permissions
- Establish clear moderation guidelines that members enforce
- Community voting on borderline content decisions
- Moderation council (5-10 senior members) for complex cases
- Staff moderators handle only escalations and edge cases

## 9. Platform Migration Playbook

### 9.1 When to Migrate

Reasons to consider migration:
- Platform no longer supports community size or needs
- Cost has become prohibitive
- Critical features are missing (courses, monetization, branding)
- Data ownership concerns
- Platform announced deprecation or major changes

### 9.2 Migration Timeline (8-12 Weeks)

| Phase | Duration | Actions |
|---|---|---|
| Assessment | Week 1-2 | Evaluate new platform options, test top 2-3 candidates, check data export capabilities |
| Decision | Week 3 | Select new platform, secure budget approval, announce to moderator team |
| Setup | Week 4-5 | Configure new platform, migrate content (where possible), set up structure |
| Beta migration | Week 6-7 | Move moderators and champions to new platform first, test all workflows |
| Announcement | Week 8 | Announce migration to full community, explain why, set timeline |
| Parallel run | Week 9-10 | Run both platforms simultaneously, direct all new activity to new platform |
| Full migration | Week 11 | Make old platform read-only, final member migration push |
| Cleanup | Week 12 | Archive old platform, resolve migration issues, gather feedback |

### 9.3 Migration Communication Template

**Announcement message**:
"We are moving [community name] to [new platform] starting [date]. Here is why: [2-3 bullet points on benefits for members]. Here is what you need to do: [specific steps with link]. Here is the timeline: [key dates]. Your content and history matter to us -- here is how we are preserving it: [migration details]. Questions? Ask in [dedicated thread]."

### 9.4 Migration Risks and Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Member loss (10-30% typical) | Communicate early and often, make migration easy, offer incentives for early movers |
| Content loss | Export everything possible before migration, screenshot or archive what cannot be exported |
| Engagement dip (4-8 weeks post-migration) | Plan intensive programming during transition, extra events, contests, re-engagement campaigns |
| Technical issues | Beta test thoroughly, have support ready, maintain old platform as read-only backup |
| Community culture shift | Move moderators and champions first to establish culture on new platform before general population |

## 10. Community Crisis Management

### 10.1 Crisis Types and Response Protocols

**Type 1: Internal Drama (member conflicts, moderator issues)**
- Severity: Low to Medium
- Response time: Within 4 hours
- Protocol: Move to private, hear both sides, apply rules consistently, document, follow up. Public statement only if the community is broadly aware.

**Type 2: Mass Exodus (significant member departure)**
- Severity: High
- Response time: Within 24 hours
- Protocol: Identify cause (platform issue, controversy, competitor, value decline). Address root cause publicly with transparency. Reach out to departing members individually for feedback. Implement changes visibly and quickly. Communicate action plan to remaining members.

**Type 3: Platform Outage or Data Loss**
- Severity: High
- Response time: Immediately
- Protocol: Communicate on backup channel (email, social media). Provide regular updates. Document what was lost. Restore from backups where possible. Post-mortem with prevention plan.

**Type 4: PR Crisis (brand controversy affecting community)**
- Severity: High to Critical
- Response time: Within 2 hours
- Protocol: Coordinate with PR/comms team. Acknowledge the situation honestly in community. Do not delete or suppress discussion. Provide facts, not spin. Follow up with concrete actions. Moderators monitor for escalation.

**Type 5: Toxic Takeover (coordinated disruption, brigading)**
- Severity: Critical
- Response time: Immediately
- Protocol: Lock new member registration temporarily. Increase moderation presence. Ban coordinated bad actors. Restrict posting in affected channels. Communicate with trusted members. Document everything for potential legal action.

### 10.2 Crisis Communication Principles

1. **Speed**: Acknowledge within hours, not days. Silence breeds speculation.
2. **Transparency**: Share what you know, what you do not know, and what you are doing about it.
3. **Empathy**: Acknowledge member feelings before defending positions.
4. **Accountability**: Own mistakes. Do not deflect or minimize.
5. **Action**: Every crisis communication should include what you are doing to fix it.
6. **Follow-through**: Update the community as you make progress. Close the loop.

### 10.3 Post-Crisis Recovery

After any significant crisis:
- **Debrief**: Internal review of what happened, response effectiveness, and prevention measures.
- **Community survey**: Check in with members on how they feel, what they need, and whether trust is intact.
- **Visible improvements**: Implement at least one concrete change that members can see.
- **Re-engagement campaign**: Extra programming, events, or content in the 2-4 weeks post-crisis to rebuild momentum.
- **Documentation**: Add the crisis and response to an internal playbook for future reference.

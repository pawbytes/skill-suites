## 1. Core Engagement Benchmarks

### 1.1 Monthly Active Users (MAU)

| Health Level | MAU as % of Total Members | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Critical | Below 10% | Ghost town. Revisit value proposition and onboarding. |
| Needs attention | 10-19% | Common in large, loosely managed communities. |
| Healthy | 20-30% | Standard benchmark for well-managed communities. |
| Strong | 30-40% | Active programming and consistent engagement. |
| Excellent | 40%+ | Top-tier communities with strong rituals and member investment. |

Industry context: Most association communities see 15-25% MAU. Brand and creator communities with paid tiers typically achieve 30-50% MAU because members have financial commitment.

### 1.2 Engagement Rate

| Metric | Benchmark | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly engagement rate | 20-40% | Members who posted, commented, or reacted / total members |
| Contributor ratio | 10-30% | Members who create content / total members |
| Lurker-to-contributor conversion | 5-15% annually | Lurkers who become contributors over 12 months |
| Post-to-reply ratio | 3-8 replies per post | Healthy discussion depth indicator |
| Average time to first reply | Under 4 hours | Faster indicates active, responsive community |

The 90-9-1 rule (also called the 1% rule) remains directionally accurate in 2025-2026: approximately 90% of community members are lurkers, 9% contribute occasionally, and 1% are power contributors. Well-designed communities shift this to roughly 70-20-10 through structured engagement programs.

### 1.3 Daily Activity Benchmarks

| Community Size | Healthy Daily Posts | Healthy Daily Comments | Daily Active Members |
|---|---|---|---|
| Small (under 500) | 3-10 | 10-30 | 25-75 |
| Medium (500-2,000) | 10-30 | 30-100 | 75-300 |
| Large (2,000-10,000) | 30-100 | 100-500 | 300-1,500 |
| Enterprise (10,000+) | 100+ | 500+ | 1,500+ |

### 2.3 Activation and First-Action Benchmarks

| Metric | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Profile completion rate | 40-60% | Higher with guided onboarding |
| First post within 7 days | 40-60% | Below 40% = broken onboarding |
| First reply/comment within 7 days | 50-70% | Lower-friction action; should be higher than first post |
| Return visit within 48 hours | 30-50% | Strongest predictor of long-term retention |
| Onboarding sequence completion | 50-70% | Multi-step onboarding (intro, profile, first action) |

Key finding: Members who complete at least one action (post, comment, or reaction) within their first 48 hours are 3-5x more likely to be retained at 90 days than those who remain passive.

## 3. Response Time Standards

### 3.1 Response Time Benchmarks

| Community Tier | Expected Response Time | Best Practice |
|---|---|---|
| Free community | Under 24 hours | Acceptable for most free communities |
| Paid community (standard) | Under 12 hours | Members paying expect faster responses |
| Paid community (premium) | Under 4 hours | Premium tiers require near-real-time engagement |
| VIP / high-touch | Under 1 hour | White-glove service for top-tier members |

### 3.2 Response Source Distribution

In healthy communities, the response workload should distribute across multiple sources:
| Response Source | Healthy Distribution | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Community manager(s) | 20-30% | Should not be the primary responder |
| Volunteer moderators | 15-25% | Trained, recognized community champions |
| Peer members | 40-60% | Sign of a self-sustaining community |
| AI / automated | 5-15% | FAQ responses, onboarding, simple queries |

If the community manager answers more than 50% of questions, the community is too dependent on staff and will not scale.

## 4. Community ROI Benchmarks

### 4.1 Support Ticket Deflection

| Metric | Benchmark Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Ticket deflection rate | 20-40% | Percentage of would-be tickets resolved in community |
| Average cost per support ticket | $15-25 | Industry average; varies by complexity |
| Annual savings (1,000 member community) | $30,000-100,000 | Depends on ticket volume and deflection rate |
| Annual savings (10,000 member community) | $200,000-800,000 | Scales significantly with community size |
| Self-service resolution rate | 30-50% | Members finding answers via search without posting |

Calculation: Monthly deflected tickets x cost per ticket x 12 = annual savings. Example: 200 deflected tickets/month x $20/ticket x 12 = $48,000 annual savings.

### 4.2 NPS and Satisfaction Impact

| Metric | Benchmark | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| NPS lift (community members vs. non-members) | +10 to +30 points | Consistent across industries |
| CSAT improvement | +15-25% | Customer satisfaction scores for community members |
| Customer effort score reduction | 20-35% improvement | Self-service reduces effort to find answers |
| Brand affinity increase | 25-40% | Measured through surveys and sentiment analysis |

### 4.3 Revenue and Retention Impact

| Customer retention lift | 15-25% higher | Community members vs. non-members |

## 6. Community Manager Benchmarks

### 6.1 Staffing Ratios

| Community Size (Active Members) | Recommended CM Staff | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Under 200 | 0.5 FTE (part-time) | Often one person wearing multiple hats |
| 200-500 | 1 FTE | Full-time community manager |
| 500-1,000 | 1-2 FTEs | CM + part-time support or volunteer mods |
| 1,000-5,000 | 2-3 FTEs | CM + moderators + content creator |
| 5,000-10,000 | 3-5 FTEs | Community team with specialized roles |
| 10,000+ | 5+ FTEs | Full community team + volunteer program |

Rule of thumb: 1 community manager per 500-1,000 active members. Add volunteer moderators at a ratio of 1 per 200-500 active members.

As of 2025, 41% of brands now have a dedicated community manager. 33% run more than one active community.

### 6.2 Community Manager Time Allocation (Healthy)

| Activity | % of Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Member engagement (responding, posting) | 25-30% | Direct community interaction |
| Content creation and curation | 15-20% | Discussion prompts, resources, programming |
| Moderation | 10-15% | Rule enforcement, conflict resolution |
| Events and programming | 15-20% | Planning and hosting events |
| Analytics and reporting | 10-15% | Weekly and monthly reporting |
| Strategy and planning | 10-15% | Long-term community roadmap |

If moderation consumes more than 25% of time, investigate root causes (unclear rules, toxic subgroups, platform issues) rather than hiring more moderators.

## 9. Community Health Scoring Model

### 9.1 Health Score Components (0-100)

| Component | Weight | Score 0-20 | Measurement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Member growth rate | 15 points | Monthly net growth as % of total | 5-15% monthly = max score |
| Engagement rate | 25 points | Monthly active participants / total members | 30%+ = max score |
| Retention rate | 25 points | Month-over-month active member retention | 70%+ = max score |
| Activation rate | 15 points | New members completing first action within 7 days | 60%+ = max score |
| Sentiment score | 10 points | Qualitative review or automated sentiment analysis | Positive/neutral ratio 80%+ = max score |
| Response quality | 10 points | Average time to first meaningful reply | Under 4 hours = max score |

### 9.2 Health Score Interpretation

| Score Range | Status | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 80-100 | Thriving | Optimize and scale. Focus on advanced programs. |
| 60-79 | Healthy | Stable foundation. Identify weakest component and improve. |
| 40-59 | Needs attention | Multiple areas underperforming. Prioritize retention and engagement. |
| 20-39 | At risk | Significant intervention needed. Re-evaluate strategy and value proposition. |
| 0-19 | Critical | Community may need to be relaunched or restructured entirely. |

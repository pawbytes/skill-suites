## Video Hook Formulas (First 3 Seconds)

The hook is everything. 70-85% of viewers who stay past the first 3 seconds will continue watching. These are proven hook formulas ranked by effectiveness.

### Hook Formula 1: The Question Hook
**Structure**: Ask a question that the viewer desperately wants answered.

**Examples**:
- "What if everything you know about [topic] is wrong?"
- "Why are 90% of [professionals] doing [thing] completely backwards?"
- "Want to know the #1 mistake [audience] makes with [topic]?"
- "How did this small brand go from zero to $1M in 6 months?"

**When to use**: Educational, how-to, mythbusting content.
**Why it works**: Opens a curiosity loop that viewers need closed.

### Hook Formula 2: The Bold Claim
**Structure**: Make a surprising, specific, or counterintuitive claim immediately.

**Examples**:
- "This one change doubled our conversion rate overnight."
- "[Popular advice] is actually destroying your [results]."
- "I made $50,000 from a single video. Here's exactly how."
- "Stop using [common tool/tactic]. Here's what works instead."

**When to use**: Results-driven content, controversial takes, authority building.
**Why it works**: Specific claims create credibility and curiosity simultaneously.

### Hook Formula 3: The Pattern Interrupt
**Structure**: Start with something visually or verbally unexpected.

**Examples**:
- Start mid-sentence or mid-action (no intro, no greeting)
- Unexpected visual (zoomed in on result, then pull back)
- Sound effect or music drop that breaks the scroll pattern
- Dramatic pause: "[silence]...okay, this is insane."
- Physical movement toward camera or sudden frame change

**When to use**: Social media shorts, entertainment, trend content.
**Why it works**: Breaks the viewer's autopilot scroll behavior.

### Hook Formula 4: The Curiosity Gap
**Structure**: Reveal partial information that can only be completed by watching.

**Examples**:
- "There are 3 types of [thing]. #2 will surprise you."
- "I tested [X] for 30 days. The results were NOT what I expected."
- "The secret behind [impressive result] is simpler than you think."
- "Here's what [expert/company] doesn't want you to know about [topic]."

**When to use**: Listicles, experiments, reveal-style content.
**Why it works**: Creates an information gap the brain feels compelled to close.

### Hook Formula 5: The Relatable Problem
**Structure**: Call out a problem or frustration your audience already feels.

**Examples**:
- "If you're struggling with [problem], you need to hear this."
- "Tired of [frustrating thing]? I found a fix."
- "POV: You've tried everything to [goal] and nothing works."
- "Nobody talks about how hard [thing] actually is."
- "This is for everyone who [common frustration]."

**When to use**: Solution-oriented content, product demos, empathy-driven content.
**Why it works**: Instant identification; viewer feels seen and stays for the solution.

### Hook Formula 6: Behind the Scenes
**Structure**: Pull back the curtain on something viewers don't normally see.

**Examples**:
- "Here's what actually happens when [process]..."
- "I'm about to show you something most [role] would never share."
- "Let me walk you through exactly how we [did thing]."
- "This is what [impressive result] actually looks like behind the scenes."

**When to use**: Process content, day-in-the-life, transparency content.
**Why it works**: Exclusivity creates value; people love seeing how things really work.

### Hook Formula 7: The Contrarian Take
**Structure**: Disagree with popular advice or common wisdom.

**Examples**:
- "Unpopular opinion: [widely accepted advice] is bad advice."
- "Everyone says [common advice]. They're wrong. Here's why."
- "I stopped [popular practice] and here's what happened."
- "The [industry] doesn't want you to know this."

**When to use**: Thought leadership, debate-style content, authority positioning.
**Why it works**: Controversy triggers emotional engagement; agree or disagree, they watch.

### Hook Formula 8: The Social Proof Hook
**Structure**: Lead with a result, testimonial, or proof point.

**Examples**:
- "[Customer name] went from [before] to [after] in [time]."
- "This strategy is used by [impressive company/person]."
- "Over [number] people have already tried this. Here's why."
- "[Screenshot of result] -- Let me show you how."

**When to use**: Case studies, product marketing, proof-driven content.
**Why it works**: Results create credibility; viewers want the same outcome.

### Hook Combination Matrix
| Hook Type          | + Question | + Bold Claim | + Curiosity Gap | + Relatable Problem |
|--------------------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Question           | -         | Strong      | Very Strong     | Very Strong         |
| Bold Claim         | Strong    | -           | Strong          | Strong              |
| Pattern Interrupt  | Good      | Very Strong | Good            | Good                |
| Behind the Scenes  | Good      | Good        | Very Strong     | Good                |

## Script Frameworks

### Framework 1: Hook-Story-Offer (HSO)
Best for: Sales videos, product content, conversion-focused videos.

**Structure**:
1. **Hook** (0-5s): Grab attention with one of the formulas above
2. **Story** (5s-80%): Tell a relatable story that illustrates the problem and journey
3. **Offer** (final 20%): Present your solution/product as the natural resolution

**Template**:
```
[HOOK]: "I was spending 4 hours a day on [task] until I discovered this."
[STORY]: "Three months ago, I was [struggling with problem]. I tried [common solution 1], [common solution 2], nothing worked. Then I found [discovery]..."
[TRANSITION]: "Here's what changed everything..."
[OFFER]: "That's why I created [product/solution]. It does [benefit 1], [benefit 2], and [benefit 3]. [CTA]."
```

### Framework 2: PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)
Best for: Educational content, product demos, pain-point content.

**Structure**:
1. **Problem** (0-10s): State the problem clearly and specifically
2. **Agitate** (10s-40%): Make the problem feel urgent and painful
3. **Solve** (40%-end): Present the solution step by step

**Template**:
```
[PROBLEM]: "Most [audience] waste [time/money] on [ineffective thing]."
[AGITATE]: "And the worst part? [Consequence 1]. [Consequence 2]. Meanwhile, [competitor/alternative] is [getting ahead]."
[SOLVE]: "Here's the 3-step fix. Step 1: [action]. Step 2: [action]. Step 3: [action]. The result? [Outcome]."
```

### Framework 3: AIDA for Video
Best for: Ads, landing page videos, conversion content.

**Structure**:
1. **Attention** (0-3s): Hook that stops the scroll
2. **Interest** (3-15s): Why this matters to the viewer
3. **Desire** (15s-70%): Show the transformation or result
4. **Action** (final 30%): Clear CTA

**Template**:
```
[ATTENTION]: "Stop scrolling if you [audience identifier]."
[INTEREST]: "I found a way to [desirable outcome] without [common pain]."
[DESIRE]: "Imagine [future state]. [Proof point]. [Social proof]. [Demonstration]."
[ACTION]: "[Specific CTA]. Link in bio / Click below / Comment [word] for [offer]."
```

### Framework 4: List Framework
Best for: Tips, hacks, tools, mistakes-to-avoid content.

**Structure**:
1. **Hook + Promise** (0-5s): "X [tips/mistakes/tools] that will [outcome]"
2. **Items** (5s-90%): Deliver each item with a mini-hook, explanation, and example
3. **Bonus + CTA** (final 10%): Extra tip + call to action

**Retention technique**: Tease the best item. "Save #[last] for the end -- it's the most important."

### Framework 5: Before-After-Bridge (BAB)
Best for: Transformations, case studies, testimonial content.

**Structure**:
1. **Before** (0-20%): Show the "before" state (the problem, the struggle)
2. **After** (20-50%): Show the "after" state (the result, the transformation)
3. **Bridge** (50-end): Explain exactly how to get from before to after

**Template**:
```
[BEFORE]: "6 months ago, our [metric] was [bad number]."
[AFTER]: "Today, it's [impressive number]."
[BRIDGE]: "Here are the 3 exact changes we made. Change 1: [explanation]. Change 2: [explanation]. Change 3: [explanation]."
```

## Thumbnail Design Principles

### The 3-Second Thumbnail Test
If a viewer cannot understand what the video is about in 3 seconds at mobile size, the thumbnail fails.

### Core Thumbnail Elements

#### 1. Face (Primary Element)
- **Expression**: Exaggerated emotion (surprise, excitement, concern)
- **Eye direction**: Looking at text or key element in thumbnail
- **Size**: Face takes up 30-50% of the thumbnail
- **Contrast**: Face should pop against the background
- **Rule**: Thumbnails with faces get 30%+ more clicks on average

#### 2. Text (2-4 Words Maximum)
- **Font**: Bold, sans-serif, high contrast
- **Size**: Readable at 120px width (mobile thumbnail size)
- **Content**: Complement the title (do NOT repeat the title)
- **Placement**: Upper-left or upper-right (not overlapping face)
- **Color**: High contrast against background; often yellow, white, or red

#### 3. Background
- **Simplify**: Remove clutter; use solid colors or blurred backgrounds
- **Contrast**: Background should make face and text pop
- **Color psychology**: Red (urgency), blue (trust), green (growth), yellow (attention)
- **Avoid**: Busy patterns, low-contrast, dark/muddy colors

#### 4. Composition Rules
- **Rule of thirds**: Place key elements at intersection points
- **Visual hierarchy**: Eye goes to face > text > supporting elements
- **Negative space**: Leave breathing room; do not overcrowd
- **Consistency**: Use a recognizable template across your channel (brand recognition)
- **Mobile-first**: Design at 1280x720 but check at thumbnail size (120x90)

### Thumbnail A/B Testing
- YouTube native A/B testing: Available for channels with 10K+ subscribers
- External tools: TubeBuddy, VidIQ thumbnail analyzer
- Test one variable at a time: face vs. no face, text vs. no text, color scheme
- Minimum run time: 7 days or 10,000 impressions per variation
- Winner criteria: Higher CTR with maintained or improved watch time

## Video SEO Checklist

### Title Optimization
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 5-6 words
- [ ] Under 60 characters (avoids truncation)
- [ ] Includes a hook element (number, question, bold claim)
- [ ] Matches search intent (informational, transactional, navigational)
- [ ] Does NOT keyword-stuff; reads naturally
- [ ] Complements thumbnail (together they tell the full story)

### Description Optimization
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 1-2 sentences
- [ ] First 150 characters are compelling (shown in search results)
- [ ] Include 2-3 relevant secondary keywords naturally
- [ ] Add timestamps/chapters (improves SEO and UX)
- [ ] Include relevant links (website, socials, mentioned resources)
- [ ] Add a brief summary paragraph (100-200 words)
- [ ] Include a CTA in the description

### Tags & Hashtags
- [ ] Primary keyword as first tag
- [ ] 5-10 relevant tags (mix of broad and specific)
- [ ] Include brand name as a tag
- [ ] Include common misspellings of key terms
- [ ] 3-5 hashtags in description (shown above title on YouTube)

### Chapters (Timestamps)
- [ ] Add timestamps for all major sections
- [ ] First timestamp starts at 0:00
- [ ] Minimum 3 chapters, maximum 10-15
- [ ] Chapter titles include relevant keywords
- [ ] Format: `0:00 Introduction` / `2:15 Step 1: [Keyword-rich title]`

### Cards & End Screens
- [ ] Add 1-3 cards at relevant moments (link to related content)
- [ ] Place cards when viewer might search for more info (not at hook)
- [ ] End screen (last 20 seconds): Subscribe button + best next video
- [ ] End screen video: Choose your best-performing related video
- [ ] Script the end screen: "If you enjoyed this, watch THIS next..."

### Closed Captions / Subtitles
- [ ] Upload accurate captions (auto-generated are 80-90% accurate)
- [ ] Review and fix auto-captions for key terms and brand names
- [ ] Consider adding captions for additional languages (top viewer demographics)
- [ ] Captions are indexed by YouTube for search (SEO benefit)

### Engagement Optimization
- [ ] Pin a comment with a question or CTA
- [ ] Reply to comments within first 1-2 hours (boosts algorithm)
- [ ] Ask a question in the video to encourage comments
- [ ] Include a verbal subscribe CTA mid-video (not just intro/outro)

## Platform-Specific Video Optimization

### YouTube Algorithm Optimization
- **Session time is king**: YouTube rewards videos that keep viewers ON YouTube longer
- **CTR x Watch Time**: The core equation; both must be strong
- **Posting schedule**: Consistent schedule (same day/time each week)
- **First 48 hours**: Critical window; maximize initial impressions
- **Playlists**: Group related videos; auto-play increases session time
- **Community tab**: Use for polls, teasers, and engagement between uploads
- **Shorts-to-Long pipeline**: Use Shorts to drive viewers to long-form content

### TikTok FYP Optimization
- **Hook within 1 second**: Faster than any other platform
- **Trending audio**: Use trending sounds for algorithmic boost (even at low volume)
- **Text on screen**: Adds watch time (viewers read + watch)
- **Loop potential**: End the video in a way that flows into the beginning
- **Hashtag strategy**: 3-5 hashtags; mix niche + trending + broad
- **Post time**: Test different times; TikTok is less time-sensitive than others
- **Engagement bait (ethical)**: Ask questions, prompt comments, create debate
- **Series content**: Use TikTok's series feature to encourage follows
- **Reply to comments with video**: Algorithm loves this engagement signal

### Instagram Reels Optimization
- **Cover image**: Design a custom cover image (grid aesthetic matters)
- **Caption matters**: Write a compelling caption (Instagram shows it below Reel)
- **Hashtags**: 5-10 relevant hashtags in caption
- **Share to Stories**: Post your Reel to Stories for additional reach
- **Collab feature**: Use Collaborator tag for cross-audience reach
- **Audio trends**: Use trending audio, but ensure it fits your brand
- **Save-worthy content**: Educational or reference content drives saves (top signal)
- **Cross-promote**: Share Reel in DMs, embed on website

### YouTube Shorts Optimization
- **Vertical format**: 9:16 aspect ratio, under 3 minutes
- **Title optimization**: Shorts titles appear differently; keep under 40 characters
- **Loop structure**: Design for seamless replay (major Shorts signal)
- **Subscribe CTA**: "Subscribe for more" is especially effective on Shorts
- **Hashtag #Shorts**: No longer required but can help categorization
- **Funnel to long-form**: End with "Full video on my channel" or pin comment with link
- **Thumbnail**: Shorts auto-generate thumbnails; design your start frame accordingly

## Retention Patterns & Techniques

### The Retention Curve
A healthy retention curve looks like a gradual decline that levels off, not a cliff drop.

| Time in Video      | Healthy Retention | Problem Sign   |
|--------------------|--------------------|----------------|
| 0-3 seconds        | 85-95%             | Below 70%      |
| 3-30 seconds       | 70-85%             | Below 60%      |
| Mid-video          | 50-65%             | Below 40%      |
| Final 20%          | 35-55%             | Below 25%      |

### Retention Techniques

#### Open Loops
- Tease upcoming content: "In a minute, I'll show you the biggest mistake..."
- Number-based promises: "5 tips, and #3 changed everything for me"
- Visual teasers: Flash a result/screenshot early, explain it later

#### Pattern Interrupts (Mid-Video)
- Change camera angle every 15-30 seconds
- Add B-roll footage to break up talking head
- Use text overlays, graphics, and sound effects
- Change background music at transition points
- Insert a joke, aside, or unexpected moment

#### Pacing
- Faster pace = better retention (especially for short-form)
- Cut dead air, filler words, and long pauses in editing
- Jump cuts keep energy high (standard practice on YouTube)
- Vary sentence length and speaking cadence

#### Progress Indicators
- Number each tip/step: "Step 1 of 5..."
- On-screen progress bar or section headers
- Verbal signposting: "Now for the most important part..."

## CTA Frameworks for Video

### The Natural CTA
Integrate the CTA into the content rather than tacking it on.

**Weak**: "Like and subscribe, hit the bell notification."
**Strong**: "If you found this useful, subscribe so you don't miss part 2 where I cover [teaser]."

### CTA Placement
| Placement          | Effectiveness  | Best For                          |
|--------------------|---------------|-----------------------------------|
| Opening (0-10s)    | Low           | Brand mention only; too early for ask |
| Mid-roll (40-60%)  | High          | Subscribe CTA when viewer is engaged |
| End (final 10%)    | Medium        | Follow-up content CTA, link click |
| Pinned comment     | High          | Links, resources, discussion prompt |
| On-screen overlay  | Medium-High   | Throughout; subtle but visible    |
| Description        | Medium        | Links, products, resources        |

### CTA Types for Video
1. **Engagement CTA**: "Comment [word] below if you agree"
2. **Subscribe CTA**: "Subscribe and hit the bell so you don't miss [next video teaser]"
3. **Share CTA**: "Share this with someone who needs to hear it"
4. **Link CTA**: "Link in bio / description for [resource]"
5. **Next video CTA**: "Watch this video next for [related topic]" (session time boost)
6. **Save CTA**: "Save this for later when you need it" (algorithm signal)
7. **DM CTA**: "DM me [word] for [free resource]" (Instagram, TikTok)

# Video Production Pipeline Workflow

This reference defines the standard workflow, diagnostic questions, benchmark data, deliverables, and the "hook-first" methodology that applies to all video marketing work.

---

## Core Principle: Hook-First

Every video lives or dies in its opening 1-3 seconds. Before evaluating any other aspect of a video -- script, production quality, SEO, distribution -- assess the hook first. If the hook fails, nothing else matters.

**Hook assessment protocol:**
1. Watch only the first 3 seconds. Would you stop scrolling?
2. Does it create a pattern interrupt (visual, audio, or text)?
3. Does it promise a specific outcome or provoke genuine curiosity?
4. Does it match the platform's native style (not an ad on a content feed)?

If the hook does not pass this 4-point check, fix it before proceeding to any other optimization. A well-produced video with a weak hook will underperform a rough video with a strong hook every time.

---

## Diagnostic Questions

Ask these before producing any video recommendation. Recommendations without this context are guesses.

1. **Objective**: What is the business goal of this video? (Awareness, consideration, conversion, retention, education, entertainment?)
2. **Platform**: Where will this be published? (YouTube, TikTok, Instagram Reels, LinkedIn, website, ads?) Each platform demands a different format, length, and pacing.
3. **Audience**: Who is the viewer? What do they already know? What is their emotional state when they encounter this video?
4. **Format**: Short-form (<60 sec), mid-form (1-10 min), or long-form (10+ min)? Is this a series or standalone?
5. **Current state**: Does the brand have existing video content? What has performed well or poorly? Are analytics available (retention curves, CTR)?
6. **Resources**: What production resources are available? (In-house team, freelance, AI tools, stock footage, talent, budget?)
7. **Success metrics**: How will success be measured? (Views, watch time, retention rate, CTR, conversions, subscriber growth?)

If the user cannot answer questions 2 and 5, recommend a platform audit and competitive analysis before producing video content. Platform-native content requires platform-specific strategy.

## Phase 1: Strategy & Brief

### Purpose
Define the video's purpose, target audience, platform, format, and success metrics before a single frame is planned.

### Entry Conditions
- Brand context and SOSTAC plan available (or brand intake completed)
- Business objective identified
- Target platform(s) selected

### Key Activities
1. **Define the video objective** with a measurable outcome:
   - Awareness: target X views, X% average view duration
   - Consideration: target X% CTR, X watch time minutes
   - Conversion: target X% click-through to landing page, X signups
   - Retention: target X% completion rate, X return viewers
   - Education: target X% retention at key learning moments
   - Community: target X comments, X shares, X saves

2. **Platform-format matrix** -- select the right format for the platform:

   | Platform | Primary Format | Ideal Length | Aspect Ratio | Key Algorithm Signal |
   |---|---|---|---|---|
   | YouTube (search) | Long-form tutorial/how-to | 8-15 min | 16:9 | Watch time, CTR |
   | YouTube (browse) | Mid-form entertainment/value | 5-12 min | 16:9 | CTR, retention curve |
   | YouTube Shorts | Vertical short-form | 30-60 sec | 9:16 | Replay rate, engagement |
   | TikTok | Vertical short-form | 15-60 sec | 9:16 | Watch-through rate, shares |
   | Instagram Reels | Vertical short-form | 15-30 sec | 9:16 | Saves, shares, replay |
   | LinkedIn | Professional / thought leadership | 30-90 sec | 1:1 or 16:9 | Comments, dwell time |
   | Website / landing page | Product demo / explainer | 60-120 sec | 16:9 | Play rate, completion |
   | Paid ads (Meta) | UGC-style / direct response | 15-30 sec | 9:16, 1:1, 4:5 | Hook rate (3-sec views) |
   | Paid ads (YouTube) | Pre-roll / mid-roll | 15-30 sec (skippable) | 16:9 | View-through rate, conversions |

3. **Competitive audit** -- analyze 5-10 top-performing videos in the space:
   - What hooks are used?
   - What format and length dominates?
   - What production quality level sets the floor?
   - What content gaps exist that you can fill?

4. **Create the video brief**:
   - Title (working)
   - Platform and format
   - Objective and success metrics
   - Target audience and emotional state
   - Key message (one sentence)
   - CTA (what the viewer should do after watching)
   - Competitive references (3-5 examples of effective videos)

### Deliverables
- Video strategy brief: objective, platform, format, audience, metrics, competitive analysis
- Content calendar slot (if part of an ongoing program)

### Exit Conditions
- Clear, measurable objective documented
- Platform and format selected
- Competitive audit complete
- Brief approved by stakeholder

## Phase 2: Scripting

### Purpose
Write the script with hook-first methodology. The hook earns the first 3 seconds; the structure earns the next 3 minutes; the CTA earns the click.

### Entry Conditions
- Phase 1 complete: strategy brief finalized
- Key message and CTA defined
- Platform-specific constraints understood

### Key Activities
1. **Write the hook (first 1-3 seconds)** -- this is the single most important creative decision:
   - Load `./references/hook-frameworks.md` for platform-specific hook formulas
   - Write 3-5 hook variants for testing
   - Hook types: bold claim, question, visual shock, pattern interrupt, curiosity gap, controversy, relatable moment

   | Hook Type | Example | Best For |
   |---|---|---|
   | Bold claim | "This one change doubled our revenue" | Tutorial, case study |
   | Question | "Why do 90% of startups fail at this?" | Educational, thought leadership |
   | Visual shock | Unexpected visual in first frame | Short-form, entertainment |
   | Pattern interrupt | Break expected format (text overlay, zoom) | TikTok, Reels |
   | Curiosity gap | "I tested this for 30 days. Here's what happened." | YouTube, story-driven |
   | Controversy | "Stop doing [common practice]" | Thought leadership, debate |
   | Relatable moment | Depict a universal frustration or experience | UGC, brand awareness |

2. **Write the body structure** (platform-dependent):
   - **Short-form (15-60 sec)**: Hook -> Key point -> Proof/Example -> CTA. Every second earns its place.
   - **Mid-form (1-10 min)**: Hook -> Promise -> Point 1 (with retention bump) -> Point 2 -> Point 3 -> CTA -> End screen
   - **Long-form (10+ min)**: Hook -> Credibility -> Roadmap ("3 things") -> Deep section 1 -> Retention bump -> Deep section 2 -> Bridge -> Deep section 3 -> Summary -> CTA -> End screen

3. **Write retention bumps** -- moments designed to re-engage viewers who are about to leave:
   - "But here's where it gets interesting..."
   - "The third one is the one most people miss..."
   - Tease upcoming content: "In a minute I'll show you the exact template..."
   - Pattern interrupts: change camera angle, add graphic, shift tone

4. **Write the CTA** -- specific and immediate:
   - YouTube: "Subscribe and click the bell" is weak. "Download the free template in the description" is strong.
   - Short-form: One clear next action (follow, comment, share, visit link in bio)
   - Ads: Direct to landing page with tracking

5. **Include shooting notes in the script**:
   - Camera direction (angle, movement, framing)
   - B-roll suggestions
   - Text overlay timing
   - Sound effects or music cues
   - Talent direction (energy level, delivery style)

### Deliverables
- Complete script with: hook (3-5 variants), body structure, retention bumps, CTA, shooting notes
- Script timing estimate (words per minute: 130-150 for conversational delivery)

### Exit Conditions
- Hook passes the 4-point hook assessment (see Core Principle above)
- Script structure matches platform format requirements
- CTA is specific and trackable
- **Human review gate**: Script reviewed and approved before production begins

## Phase 3: Production Planning

### Purpose
Plan the physical or virtual production -- shot list, equipment, talent, location, and B-roll needs -- so production day runs efficiently.

### Entry Conditions
- Phase 2 complete: script finalized and approved
- Budget and resource constraints known

### Key Activities
1. **Create the shot list** -- every shot planned before production begins:
   - A-roll (primary footage): talking head, product demo, screen recording
   - B-roll (supplementary footage): product close-ups, environment, process shots, stock footage
   - Text overlays and graphics: plan placement and timing
   - Transitions: match platform style (cuts for YouTube, jump cuts for TikTok)

2. **Equipment requirements by production level**:

   | Level | Camera | Audio | Lighting | Cost Estimate |
   |---|---|---|---|---|
   | Smartphone | iPhone 14+ / Samsung S23+ | Wireless lav mic ($30-100) | Ring light or window light | $50-200 |
   | Prosumer | Sony ZV-E10, Canon R50 | Rode VideoMic, lav mic | 2-point key + fill | $500-2,000 |
   | Professional | Sony A7IV, Canon R6 II | Shotgun + wireless lav | 3-point lighting kit | $2,000-10,000 |
   | Studio | Cinema camera or multi-cam | Boom + lav + backup | Full studio lighting | $5,000-50,000+ |

3. **Talent preparation**:
   - Teleprompter setup or bullet-point cards
   - Delivery coaching: energy, pacing, eye contact with lens
   - Wardrobe: avoid fine patterns (moire), bright white, or all black
   - Practice runs before recording

4. **Location scouting**:
   - Audio environment: avoid echo, HVAC noise, traffic
   - Visual background: on-brand, uncluttered, appropriate depth of field
   - Lighting conditions: consistent, no mixed color temperatures
   - Permits if shooting in public spaces

5. **B-roll library**:
   - Plan B-roll for every key point in the script
   - Stock footage sources: Pexels (free), Artgrid, Storyblocks
   - Screen recordings: clean desktop, hide notifications, high resolution
   - Product shots: multiple angles, in-use context

6. **Production schedule**:
   - Allocate 2-3x the estimated recording time for setup, retakes, and breaks
   - Short-form: 1-2 hours per video (including setup)
   - Long-form: 2-4 hours per video
   - Batch production: shoot multiple videos in one session to maximize efficiency

### Deliverables
- Shot list: every shot with description, framing, and duration
- Equipment and resource list
- Production schedule with time blocks
- B-roll requirements document

### Exit Conditions
- Shot list complete and mapped to script
- Equipment and talent confirmed
- Production schedule set
- All logistics resolved (location, permits, wardrobe)

## Phase 4: Post-Production

### Purpose
Edit the raw footage into a finished video optimized for the target platform, including captions, thumbnails, and aspect ratio variants.

### Entry Conditions
- Phase 3 complete: raw footage captured
- All B-roll and assets available

### Key Activities
1. **Edit specifications by platform**:

   | Platform | Resolution | Frame Rate | Aspect Ratio | Max File Size |
   |---|---|---|---|---|
   | YouTube | 1080p-4K | 24-60fps | 16:9 | 256GB |
   | YouTube Shorts | 1080x1920 | 30-60fps | 9:16 | 60 sec max |
   | TikTok | 1080x1920 | 30fps | 9:16 | 10 min max |
   | Instagram Reels | 1080x1920 | 30fps | 9:16 | 90 sec max |
   | Instagram Feed | 1080x1080 | 30fps | 1:1 or 4:5 | 60 sec max |
   | LinkedIn | 1080p | 30fps | 1:1 or 16:9 | 10 min max |
   | Website embed | 1080p | 24-30fps | 16:9 | Optimize for load speed |

2. **Editing priorities** (in order):
   - Trim the hook to be as tight as possible -- no dead frames before the pattern interrupt
   - Cut dead space and pauses (especially for short-form)
   - Add retention bumps at drop-off points (graphics, B-roll changes, text)
   - Add text overlays for key points (essential for sound-off viewing)
   - Color grade for consistency
   - Add music and sound effects (match platform energy)
   - Export in platform-native specs

3. **Captions** (non-negotiable for all social video):
   - 85% of Facebook/Instagram video is watched without sound
   - Auto-generate captions, then manually review for accuracy
   - Style captions for readability: 2-3 words per line, bold keywords, high-contrast background
   - Caption placement: center-bottom for most platforms, avoid overlap with platform UI elements
   - Tools: CapCut, Descript, Captions app, Rev

4. **Thumbnail creation** (YouTube and website):
   - Thumbnail is responsible for 50%+ of a video's CTR
   - Design rules: high contrast, readable text at mobile size (3-5 words max), expressive face, clear subject
   - Test at mobile size (120x67px) -- if it is not readable at that size, redesign
   - Create 2-3 thumbnail variants for A/B testing (YouTube allows thumbnail testing natively)
   - Tools: Canva, Figma, Photoshop

5. **Aspect ratio variants** -- create platform-specific versions from master footage:
   - 16:9 master -> 9:16 (reframe), 1:1 (crop), 4:5 (crop)
   - Do not simply crop -- reframe to keep the subject centered and key visual elements visible
   - Plan for multi-platform in production (shoot with safe zones for all ratios)

### Deliverables
- Final edited video in platform-native specifications
- Caption file (.srt or burned in)
- Thumbnail (2-3 variants for testing)
- Aspect ratio variants as needed
- Project file backup

### Exit Conditions
- Video passes hook assessment on first watch
- Captions reviewed and accurate
- Thumbnail readable at mobile size
- All platform variants exported
- **Human review gate**: Final video reviewed and approved before publishing

## Phase 5: SEO & Metadata

### Purpose
Optimize the video for discovery -- both within platform search and external search engines. Metadata is how algorithms understand and recommend your content.

### Entry Conditions
- Phase 4 complete: final video ready
- Target keywords and topic identified

### Key Activities
1. **Title optimization**:
   - Primary keyword in the first 40 characters
   - Power structure: "How to [outcome]" or "[Number] Ways to [outcome]" or "[Result] in [Timeframe]"
   - Avoid clickbait that the video does not deliver on -- this destroys retention and algorithm trust
   - Title length: 50-70 characters (full visibility on desktop and mobile)
   - Write 3-5 title variants; pick the one with highest specificity and curiosity balance

2. **Description optimization**:
   - First 2 lines (above the fold): primary keyword + compelling summary + CTA with link
   - Include timestamps / chapters (YouTube rewards this with search features)
   - Secondary keywords naturally integrated
   - Links: landing page, related videos, social profiles
   - Description length: 200-500 words for YouTube; shorter for social platforms

3. **Tags and hashtags**:
   - YouTube: 5-10 relevant tags (primary keyword first, then long-tail variations)
   - TikTok/Reels: 3-5 hashtags (mix of broad and niche)
   - LinkedIn: 3-5 hashtags in the post copy
   - Tags are a minor ranking factor -- do not over-invest here

4. **Chapters (YouTube)**:
   - Add timestamps in the description starting at 0:00
   - Each chapter should be a distinct topic or section
   - Chapters appear in search results and improve watch time by letting viewers navigate

5. **Schema markup** (for website embeds):
   - VideoObject schema: name, description, thumbnailUrl, uploadDate, duration, contentUrl
   - Helps videos appear in Google Video search results and rich snippets
   - Route to paw-mkt-seo for implementation if needed

6. **Platform-specific SEO**:

   | Platform | Primary Discovery | SEO Priority |
   |---|---|---|
   | YouTube | Search + Browse + Suggested | Title, thumbnail, retention, keywords |
   | TikTok | For You page (algorithm) | Hook, engagement signals, hashtags |
   | Instagram Reels | Explore + Reels tab | Engagement signals, audio trends |
   | LinkedIn | Feed algorithm | Post copy, comments, dwell time |
   | Website | Google search | Schema markup, page SEO, embed |

### Deliverables
- Optimized title (3-5 variants for testing)
- Description with keywords, chapters, and links
- Tags and hashtags
- Schema markup specification (for website embeds)

### Exit Conditions
- Title contains primary keyword in first 40 characters
- Description includes chapters and above-the-fold CTA
- Schema markup documented for website embeds
- All metadata reviewed for accuracy and keyword alignment

## Phase 6: Distribution

### Purpose
Publish the video to reach the target audience through platform-native publishing, cross-promotion, and paid amplification.

### Entry Conditions
- Phase 5 complete: video and metadata ready
- Distribution channels identified
- Budget for paid amplification confirmed (if applicable)

### Key Activities
1. **Platform-native publishing**:
   - Upload natively to each platform (never share cross-platform links -- algorithms penalize external links)
   - Schedule for optimal posting times:

   | Platform | Best Posting Times (general) | Best Days |
   |---|---|---|
   | YouTube | 2-4 PM (viewer's timezone) | Thursday-Saturday |
   | TikTok | 7-9 AM, 12-3 PM, 7-11 PM | Tuesday-Thursday |
   | Instagram Reels | 9 AM, 12 PM, 7 PM | Tuesday-Friday |
   | LinkedIn | 7-8 AM, 12 PM | Tuesday-Thursday |

   - Adapt content for each platform (do not just repost -- adjust captions, hashtags, aspect ratio)

2. **Cross-promotion strategy**:
   - Email list: embed video or link to full video in newsletter
   - Blog post: embed video with supporting written content (SEO compounding)
   - Social teaser: create 15-second teaser clip to drive traffic to full video
   - Community: share in relevant Discord, Slack, Reddit communities (value-first, not promotional)
   - Internal: share with team for employee advocacy

3. **Paid amplification** (route to paw-mkt-paid-ads for budget and targeting):
   - Boost top-performing organic videos (proven hooks + proven engagement)
   - Retarget video viewers with next-step content or offers
   - Use platform audience tools: YouTube Custom Intent, TikTok Spark Ads, Meta Advantage+
   - Budget guidance: allocate 20-30% of video marketing budget to amplification

4. **Collaboration and features**:
   - YouTube: collaborate with other creators (collabs feature)
   - TikTok: duets, stitches, and trends
   - Instagram: collaborative posts, Reels remix
   - LinkedIn: tag relevant people, ask for reshares from team

5. **First 48 hours are critical**:
   - Engage with every comment in the first 2 hours
   - Share across all owned channels immediately
   - Activate paid amplification if the hook metrics are strong
   - Monitor retention curve and CTR in real-time (YouTube Studio, TikTok Analytics)

### Deliverables
- Publishing schedule: platform, date, time, caption, hashtags
- Cross-promotion plan
- Paid amplification brief (if applicable)
- First 48-hour engagement plan

### Exit Conditions
- Video published on all target platforms
- Cross-promotion executed
- Paid amplification launched (if applicable)
- First 48-hour engagement plan active

## Phase 7: Performance Review

### Purpose
Analyze video performance against benchmarks, identify optimization opportunities, and feed learnings back into the next video's strategy.

### Entry Conditions
- Phase 6 complete: video published and distributed
- Minimum 7 days of performance data (30 days for long-form YouTube)

### Key Activities
1. **Retention curve analysis** (the single most valuable video metric):
   - **First 3 seconds**: Hook effectiveness. Target: >70% retention at 3 seconds for short-form, >80% for long-form.
   - **First 30 seconds**: Opening strength. Major drop-off here means the hook-to-body transition is weak.
   - **Mid-video dips**: Identify where viewers leave. These are the moments that need retention bumps.
   - **End-of-video**: Completion rate. Target: >40% for short-form, >30% for long-form.
   - Compare retention curve to channel average -- outperformance signals algorithm-friendly content.

2. **Performance metrics by platform**:

   | Metric | YouTube (Long) | YouTube Shorts | TikTok | Reels | LinkedIn |
   |---|---|---|---|---|---|
   | View threshold | 30 sec | Loop / replay | Full watch | Full watch | 3 sec |
   | Good CTR | 4-10% | N/A | N/A | N/A | N/A |
   | Good retention (30 sec) | >60% | >80% | >70% | >70% | >50% |
   | Good engagement rate | >5% | >5% | >5% | >4% | >3% |
   | Good completion rate | >30% | >50% | >40% | >40% | >30% |

3. **Algorithm feedback loop** -- what the platform's behavior tells you:
   - Increasing impressions over time = algorithm is promoting the video (strong signal)
   - High impressions but low CTR = thumbnail/title problem
   - High CTR but low retention = hook/content mismatch
   - High retention but low engagement = content is good but not shareable/saveable
   - Suggested/browse traffic increasing = algorithm trust is growing

4. **Benchmarks by video type**:

   | Video Type | Views (30 days) | Avg View Duration | CTR | Engagement Rate |
   |---|---|---|---|---|
   | Tutorial / How-to | 1-10x subscriber count | 5-8 min | 5-10% | 4-7% |
   | Product demo | 0.5-2x subscriber count | 2-4 min | 3-7% | 3-5% |
   | Thought leadership | 0.5-3x subscriber count | 3-6 min | 4-8% | 5-10% |
   | Short-form (TikTok/Reels) | 2-20x follower count | 80-100% watch | N/A | 5-15% |
   | Video ad | Reach-dependent | 50-75% of length | 0.5-2% | 1-3% |
   | Explainer | 1-5x subscriber count | 60-80% of length | 4-8% | 3-6% |

5. **Performance review cadence**:
   - **24 hours**: Hook metrics (3-sec view rate, initial retention), engagement velocity
   - **7 days**: Full retention curve, CTR, engagement rate, traffic sources
   - **30 days**: Final views, subscriber impact, conversion tracking, algorithm behavior
   - **Quarterly**: Channel-level trends, content pillar performance, audience growth

6. **Iteration triggers** -- act when these thresholds are breached:
   - Retention drop >30% in first 3 seconds -> hook failure, test new hook style
   - CTR below 4% (YouTube) -> thumbnail or title problem
   - Engagement rate below 3% -> content not resonating, review audience fit
   - Completion rate below 20% (long-form) -> structure or pacing issue
   - Views declining video-over-video -> content fatigue, review content pillars

### Deliverables
- Performance report: metrics vs benchmarks, retention curve analysis, algorithm signals
- Learnings document: what worked, what did not, specific improvements for next video
- Updated content calendar with strategy adjustments

### Exit Conditions
- Performance reviewed against benchmarks
- Retention curve analyzed with specific improvement recommendations
- Learnings documented and fed into next video's Phase 1 brief

## Ethics: Video Marketing Integrity

Video is a high-trust medium. Viewers invest their time and attention. Respect both.

### Ethical Video Practices

- **Honest hooks**: The hook must be delivered on in the video. Clickbait that does not deliver destroys audience trust and long-term channel performance.
- **Authentic production**: UGC-style ads should be genuine user experiences, not scripted to appear organic while being paid placements without disclosure.
- **Disclosure**: Sponsored content, paid partnerships, and affiliate relationships must be clearly disclosed per FTC guidelines and platform policies.
- **Accessible content**: Captions are not optional -- they are required for accessibility and they improve engagement for all viewers.
- **Respectful representation**: Avoid stereotypes, misleading before/after claims, and manipulated results.
- **Data claims**: Any statistics or data cited in video must be sourced and verifiable.

### Dark Patterns to Avoid

| Pattern | Description | Why It Harms |
|---|---|---|
| Misleading thumbnails | Thumbnail shows something not in the video | Destroys trust; tanks retention; algorithm penalizes |
| Fake urgency | "Watch before it's deleted" (it will not be) | Erodes credibility |
| Undisclosed sponsorship | Paid content without FTC-required disclosure | Legal liability; audience betrayal |
| Engagement bait | "Comment YES if you agree" without substance | Short-term metric manipulation; no real engagement |
| View manipulation | Purchased views, view bots, view exchanges | Platform penalties; fake metrics mislead strategy |
| Stolen content | Reposting others' content without credit | Copyright violation; reputation damage |

Before publishing any video, ask: **If the viewer understood exactly how this was made and why, would they still feel well-served?** If not, revise.

## Response Protocol

When the user requests video marketing work:

1. **Route the starting context** (see `./references/shared-patterns.md` for Starting Context Router). Decide whether this is strategy, production, or optimization work.
2. **Read the strongest available context** (Pre-Flight): brand and SOSTAC first when available; otherwise use existing channel analytics.
3. **Ask the diagnostic questions** if the user has not already provided this information. Do not produce recommendations without knowing the platform, audience, and objective.
4. **Identify the workflow phase**. Determine where in the pipeline the work begins:
   - New video from scratch: start at Phase 1
   - Script needed for defined concept: start at Phase 2
   - Existing footage needing editing guidance: start at Phase 4
   - Published video needing optimization: start at Phase 5 or Phase 7
5. **Apply hook-first methodology** at every relevant phase. Assess the hook before any other element.
6. **Execute the relevant phases** sequentially, producing deliverables at each phase.
7. **Save deliverables** to the resolved path (see Path Resolution).
8. **Recommend next steps**: what to produce, what to test, when to review performance.

### When to escalate

- Paid video ad campaign management -> route to paw-mkt-paid-ads
- Social media strategy beyond video -> route to paw-mkt-social
- Written content repurposing -> route to paw-mkt-content
- Influencer collaboration strategy -> route to paw-mkt-influencer
- Technical SEO for video embeds -> route to paw-mkt-seo
- Email sequences for video promotion -> route to paw-mkt-email

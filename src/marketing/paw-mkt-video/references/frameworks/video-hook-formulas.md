# Video Hook Formulas

The hook is everything. 70-85% of viewers who stay past the first 3 seconds will continue watching. These are proven hook formulas ranked by effectiveness.

### Hook Formula 1: The Question Hook
**Structure**: Ask a question that the viewer desperately wants answered.

**Examples**:
- "What if everything you know about [topic] is wrong?"
- "Why are 90% of [professionals] doing [thing] completely backwards?"
- "Want to know the #1 mistake [audience] makes with [topic]?"
- "How did this small brand go from zero to $1M in 6 months?"

**When to use**: Educational, how-to, mythbusting content.
**Why it works**: Opens a curiosity loop that viewers need closed.

### Hook Formula 2: The Bold Claim
**Structure**: Make a surprising, specific, or counterintuitive claim immediately.

**Examples**:
- "This one change doubled our conversion rate overnight."
- "[Popular advice] is actually destroying your [results]."
- "I made $50,000 from a single video. Here's exactly how."
- "Stop using [common tool/tactic]. Here's what works instead."

**When to use**: Results-driven content, controversial takes, authority building.
**Why it works**: Specific claims create credibility and curiosity simultaneously.

### Hook Formula 3: The Pattern Interrupt
**Structure**: Start with something visually or verbally unexpected.

**Examples**:
- Start mid-sentence or mid-action (no intro, no greeting)
- Unexpected visual (zoomed in on result, then pull back)
- Sound effect or music drop that breaks the scroll pattern
- Dramatic pause: "[silence]...okay, this is insane."
- Physical movement toward camera or sudden frame change

**When to use**: Social media shorts, entertainment, trend content.
**Why it works**: Breaks the viewer's autopilot scroll behavior.

### Hook Formula 4: The Curiosity Gap
**Structure**: Reveal partial information that can only be completed by watching.

**Examples**:
- "There are 3 types of [thing]. #2 will surprise you."
- "I tested [X] for 30 days. The results were NOT what I expected."
- "The secret behind [impressive result] is simpler than you think."
- "Here's what [expert/company] doesn't want you to know about [topic]."

**When to use**: Listicles, experiments, reveal-style content.
**Why it works**: Creates an information gap the brain feels compelled to close.

### Hook Formula 5: The Relatable Problem
**Structure**: Call out a problem or frustration your audience already feels.

**Examples**:
- "If you're struggling with [problem], you need to hear this."
- "Tired of [frustrating thing]? I found a fix."
- "POV: You've tried everything to [goal] and nothing works."
- "Nobody talks about how hard [thing] actually is."
- "This is for everyone who [common frustration]."

**When to use**: Solution-oriented content, product demos, empathy-driven content.
**Why it works**: Instant identification; viewer feels seen and stays for the solution.

### Hook Formula 6: Behind the Scenes
**Structure**: Pull back the curtain on something viewers don't normally see.

**Examples**:
- "Here's what actually happens when [process]..."
- "I'm about to show you something most [role] would never share."
- "Let me walk you through exactly how we [did thing]."
- "This is what [impressive result] actually looks like behind the scenes."

**When to use**: Process content, day-in-the-life, transparency content.
**Why it works**: Exclusivity creates value; people love seeing how things really work.

### Hook Formula 7: The Contrarian Take
**Structure**: Disagree with popular advice or common wisdom.

**Examples**:
- "Unpopular opinion: [widely accepted advice] is bad advice."
- "Everyone says [common advice]. They're wrong. Here's why."
- "I stopped [popular practice] and here's what happened."
- "The [industry] doesn't want you to know this."

**When to use**: Thought leadership, debate-style content, authority positioning.
**Why it works**: Controversy triggers emotional engagement; agree or disagree, they watch.

### Hook Formula 8: The Social Proof Hook
**Structure**: Lead with a result, testimonial, or proof point.

**Examples**:
- "[Customer name] went from [before] to [after] in [time]."
- "This strategy is used by [impressive company/person]."
- "Over [number] people have already tried this. Here's why."
- "[Screenshot of result] -- Let me show you how."

**When to use**: Case studies, product marketing, proof-driven content.
**Why it works**: Results create credibility; viewers want the same outcome.

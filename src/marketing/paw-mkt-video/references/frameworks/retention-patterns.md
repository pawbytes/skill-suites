# Retention Patterns & Techniques

### The Retention Curve
A healthy retention curve looks like a gradual decline that levels off, not a cliff drop.

| Time in Video      | Healthy Retention | Problem Sign   |
|--------------------|--------------------|----------------|
| 0-3 seconds        | 85-95%             | Below 70%      |
| 3-30 seconds       | 70-85%             | Below 60%      |
| Mid-video          | 50-65%             | Below 40%      |
| Final 20%          | 35-55%             | Below 25%      |

### Retention Techniques

#### Open Loops
- Tease upcoming content: "In a minute, I'll show you the biggest mistake..."
- Number-based promises: "5 tips, and #3 changed everything for me"
- Visual teasers: Flash a result/screenshot early, explain it later

#### Pattern Interrupts (Mid-Video)
- Change camera angle every 15-30 seconds
- Add B-roll footage to break up talking head
- Use text overlays, graphics, and sound effects
- Change background music at transition points
- Insert a joke, aside, or unexpected moment

#### Pacing
- Faster pace = better retention (especially for short-form)
- Cut dead air, filler words, and long pauses in editing
- Jump cuts keep energy high (standard practice on YouTube)
- Vary sentence length and speaking cadence

#### Progress Indicators
- Number each tip/step: "Step 1 of 5..."
- On-screen progress bar or section headers
- Verbal signposting: "Now for the most important part..."

## Platform Engagement Rate Benchmarks

Engagement rate = (likes + comments + shares + saves) / followers x 100.

### Instagram
- **Overall average**: 1.2-2.0%
- **Reels**: 2.5-4.5% (highest engagement format)
- **Carousels**: 1.8-3.2%
- **Single image**: 0.8-1.5%
- **Stories**: 2-5% reach rate, 0.5-1.0% reply rate
- **Micro-influencers (10K-50K)**: 2.5-4.0%
- **Mid-tier (50K-500K)**: 1.5-2.5%
- **Macro (500K+)**: 0.8-1.5%

### TikTok
- **Overall average**: 3.0-6.0%
- **View-through rate**: 15-25% (watched full video)
- **Share rate**: 0.5-2.0%
- **Comment rate**: 0.5-1.5%
- **Micro-creators (<50K)**: 8-12%
- **Mid-tier (50K-500K)**: 4-8%
- **Large creators (500K+)**: 2-5%
- **Branded content**: 2-4%

### LinkedIn
- **Overall average**: 2.0-4.0%
- **Document/carousel posts**: 3.5-6.0%
- **Video posts**: 2.5-4.5%
- **Text-only (with hook)**: 2.0-3.5%
- **Image posts**: 1.5-2.5%
- **Articles**: 0.5-1.5%
- **Newsletter open rates**: 30-50%
- **Company pages**: 1.0-2.0%
- **Personal profiles**: 3.0-6.0%

### X/Twitter
- **Overall average**: 0.5-1.0%
- **Threads**: 1.0-2.5%
- **Video tweets**: 0.8-1.5%
- **Image tweets**: 0.5-1.2%
- **Text-only**: 0.3-0.8%
- **Polls**: 1.5-3.0%
- **Spaces (live audio)**: engagement varies widely

### Facebook
- **Overall average**: 0.5-1.0%
- **Reels**: 1.5-3.0%
- **Video posts**: 0.8-1.5%
- **Image posts**: 0.5-1.0%
- **Link posts**: 0.2-0.5%
- **Groups**: 1.5-3.5% (significantly higher engagement)
- **Organic reach rate (page)**: 2-5% of followers

### YouTube
- **Overall average**: 2.0-4.0% (likes + comments per view)
- **Subscriber click-through rate**: 4-10%
- **Non-subscriber CTR**: 2-6%
- **Average view duration**: 50-60% of video (good benchmark)
- **Like rate**: 3-5% of views
- **Comment rate**: 0.5-1.5% of views

### Pinterest

- **Save rate**: 0.1-0.3%

- **Idea Pins engagement**: 1.0-2.5%
- **Video Pins**: 0.8-1.5%
- **Standard Pins**: 0.2-0.5%
- **Monthly active reach growth**: 5-15% for consistent pinners

## Best Posting Times by Platform

Times in local audience timezone. Always verify with platform-specific analytics.

### Instagram
- **Best days**: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Best times**: 7-8 AM, 11 AM-1 PM, 7-9 PM
- **Worst times**: 3-5 AM, late Sunday evening
- **Reels**: Tuesday-Thursday, 9 AM and 12 PM
- **Stories**: 7-9 AM (morning routine), 12-2 PM (lunch), 7-9 PM (wind-down)

### TikTok
- **Best days**: Tuesday, Thursday, Friday
- **Best times**: 7-9 AM, 12-3 PM, 7-11 PM
- **Peak engagement**: 7-9 PM
- **Note**: TikTok FYP algorithm is less time-sensitive than other platforms

### LinkedIn
- **Best days**: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Best times**: 7-8 AM, 12 PM, 5-6 PM
- **Worst times**: Weekends, after 8 PM
- **B2B optimal**: Tuesday-Thursday 7-10 AM

### X/Twitter
- **Best days**: Monday-Thursday
- **Best times**: 8-10 AM, 12-1 PM
- **News/trending content**: Post quickly, time-sensitivity matters most
- **B2B**: Weekday mornings; B2C: Evenings and weekends also work

### Facebook
- **Best days**: Wednesday, Thursday, Friday
- **Best times**: 9-11 AM, 1-3 PM
- **Worst times**: Early morning (before 7 AM), late night
- **Groups**: Evenings 7-9 PM often see highest activity

### YouTube
- **Best upload days**: Thursday, Friday (pre-weekend)
- **Best upload times**: 2-4 PM (indexes before evening viewing)
- **Shorts**: Morning and lunch hours
- **Live streams**: Evening hours, 7-9 PM

### Pinterest
- **Best days**: Saturday, Sunday, Friday
- **Best times**: 8-11 PM, 2-4 PM
- **Seasonal**: Pin 30-45 days before seasonal events
- **Fresh Pins**: Prioritized; schedule throughout the week

## Posting Frequency Guidelines

### Recommended Posting Cadence
| Platform        | Minimum     | Optimal       | Maximum       |
|-----------------|-------------|---------------|---------------|

| Instagram Reels | 3x/week     | 5-7x/week     | 2x/day         |
| Instagram Stories| Daily       | 3-7/day       | 10-15/day      |
| TikTok          | 3x/week     | 1-2x/day      | 3-4x/day       |
| LinkedIn        | 2x/week     | 3-5x/week     | 1-2x/day       |
| X/Twitter       | 1x/day      | 3-5x/day      | 10-15x/day     |
| Facebook        | 3x/week     | 5-7x/week     | 1-2x/day       |
| YouTube Long    | 1x/week     | 1-2x/week     | 3x/week        |
| YouTube Shorts  | 3x/week     | 5-7x/week     | 2x/day         |

### Quality vs. Quantity Rule
- Consistency beats volume; never sacrifice quality for frequency
- Algorithm favors accounts that post on a regular schedule
- Better to post 3 high-quality pieces than 7 mediocre ones

## Content Format Performance

### Instagram Format Ranking (by reach and engagement, 2025-2026)
1. **Reels (15-60s)**: Highest reach (2-3x feed posts), 2.5-4.5% engagement
2. **Carousel posts (5-10 slides)**: Strong saves and shares, 1.8-3.2% engagement
3. **Collaborative posts**: Doubled audience reach
4. **Single image (strong visual)**: 0.8-1.5% engagement
5. **Stories (interactive stickers)**: High DM and reply engagement
6. **Static text/quote graphics**: Declining reach, 0.5-1.0%
7. **Link posts**: Lowest organic reach

### TikTok Format Ranking
1. **Original trending audio + hook (15-60s)**: Highest FYP potential
2. **Stitch/Duet with trending creators**: Piggyback reach
3. **Story-driven content (60-180s)**: Strong watch time
4. **Photo carousels**: Growing format, 2-4% engagement
5. **Live streams**: High real-time engagement, conversion for commerce
6. **Series content**: Encourages follow for more

### LinkedIn Format Ranking
1. **Document/PDF carousels**: 3.5-6.0% engagement, highest saves
2. **Short-form video (30-90s)**: Growing rapidly, 2.5-4.5%
3. **Text posts with strong hook**: 2.0-3.5%, algorithm-friendly
4. **Polls**: High engagement but lower quality interactions
5. **Image + text**: 1.5-2.5%
6. **Newsletter**: Consistent reach via notifications
7. **External link posts**: Penalized by algorithm, 0.5-1.0%

### YouTube Format Comparison
| Metric              | Long-form (8-15 min) | Shorts (<60s)    |
|---------------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Subscriber growth   | Higher quality subs  | Faster growth     |
| Revenue potential   | Higher RPM ($3-15)   | Lower RPM ($0.02-0.10) |
| Discoverability     | Search + suggested   | Shorts feed       |
| Watch time credit   | Full credit          | Limited credit    |

## Audience Growth Benchmarks

### Healthy Follower Growth Rates (Monthly)
| Stage                | Monthly Growth | Notes                          |
|----------------------|----------------|--------------------------------|
| New account (0-1K)   | 10-30%         | Fastest growth phase           |
| Growing (1K-10K)     | 5-15%          | Content-quality dependent      |
| Established (10K-50K)| 3-8%           | Consistency matters most       |
| Large (50K-500K)     | 2-5%           | Healthy sustained growth       |
| Major (500K+)        | 1-3%           | Viral moments drive spikes     |

### Red Flags
- Sudden spikes without viral content (potential bot followers)
- Follower-to-engagement ratio below 1% (audience quality issue)
- Unfollower rate exceeding 2% per month (content mismatch)
- Engagement dropping while followers grow (shadow ban or bot issue)

### Growth Levers by Priority
1. Consistent posting schedule with high-quality content
2. Reels/short-form video (algorithmic boost for reach)
3. Collaborations and cross-promotion
4. Community engagement (comments, DMs, replies)
5. Hashtag and SEO optimization (platform search)
6. Paid promotion of top-performing organic content
7. Cross-platform content repurposing

## Social Media Tool Comparison

### Buffer
- **Best for**: Small businesses, solopreneurs, simplicity
- **Pricing**: Free (3 channels), $6/channel/month (Essentials)
- **Strengths**: Clean UI, easy scheduling, AI assistant, Start Page (link-in-bio)
- **Weaknesses**: Limited analytics on lower tiers, no social listening
- **Platforms**: Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, LinkedIn, X, Pinterest, YouTube, Mastodon

### Hootsuite
- **Best for**: Enterprise teams, multi-brand management
- **Pricing**: $99/month (Professional), $249/month (Team)
- **Strengths**: Comprehensive analytics, ad management, social listening, team workflows
- **Weaknesses**: Expensive, complex UI, steeper learning curve
- **Platforms**: All major platforms, including Threads

### Later
- **Best for**: Visual-first brands, Instagram-heavy strategies
- **Pricing**: Free (limited), $25/month (Starter)
- **Strengths**: Visual planner, Linkin.bio, best-time-to-post, UGC tools, AI captions
- **Weaknesses**: Weaker X/Twitter support, analytics require higher tier
- **Platforms**: Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, LinkedIn, X, Pinterest, YouTube

### Sprout Social
- **Best for**: Mid-market to enterprise, agencies, teams needing CRM
- **Pricing**: $249/month (Standard), $399/month (Professional)
- **Strengths**: Best-in-class analytics, social CRM, listening, advocacy, AI workflows
- **Weaknesses**: Most expensive option, overkill for small teams
- **Platforms**: All major platforms

### Metricool
- **Best for**: Budget-conscious teams, analytics-focused, agencies
- **Pricing**: Free (1 brand), $22/month (Starter)
- **Strengths**: Competitor analysis, ad analytics, excellent reporting, affordable
- **Weaknesses**: Smaller user community, fewer integrations
- **Platforms**: Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, LinkedIn, X, Pinterest, YouTube, Twitch

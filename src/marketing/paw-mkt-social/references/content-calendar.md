## Content Pillar Framework

### What Are Content Pillars?
Content pillars are 4-6 core themes that all your social content aligns to. They ensure strategic variety while maintaining brand coherence.

### The 5-Pillar Model

#### Pillar 1: Educate (30-35% of content)
- **Purpose**: Establish authority and provide value
- **Formats**: How-tos, tutorials, frameworks, tips, myth-busting
- **Examples**: "5 ways to...", step-by-step guides, industry explainers
- **KPI**: Saves, shares, link clicks

#### Pillar 2: Inspire (15-20% of content)
- **Purpose**: Motivate and create emotional connection
- **Formats**: Success stories, case studies, transformations, quotes
- **Examples**: Client wins, before/after, milestone celebrations
- **KPI**: Likes, comments, story reactions

#### Pillar 3: Entertain (15-20% of content)
- **Purpose**: Build relatability and shareability
- **Formats**: Memes, behind-the-scenes, trends, humor, relatable content
- **Examples**: Day-in-the-life, industry humor, trending audio
- **KPI**: Shares, reach, new followers

#### Pillar 4: Engage (15-20% of content)
- **Purpose**: Drive conversation and community
- **Formats**: Questions, polls, AMAs, challenges, UGC features
- **Examples**: "What do you think about...", this-or-that, hot takes
- **KPI**: Comments, DMs, poll participation

#### Pillar 5: Promote (10-15% of content)
- **Purpose**: Drive business outcomes
- **Formats**: Product showcases, offers, launches, CTAs, testimonials
- **Examples**: Product features, limited offers, demo videos, sales events
- **KPI**: Link clicks, conversions, revenue

### Content Ratio Rule
Follow the **80/20 rule**: 80% value-driven content (pillars 1-4), 20% promotional (pillar 5).
For early-stage accounts building audience, shift to 90/10.

## Hashtag Research Framework

### Hashtag Strategy Tiers

#### Tier 1: Niche-Specific (40% of hashtags)
- **Volume**: 10K-100K posts
- **Purpose**: Reach your exact target audience
- **Example**: #SaaSMarketing, #PlantBasedRecipes, #FitnessForMoms
- **Discovery**: Search your niche topics, check competitor hashtags

#### Tier 2: Industry/Broad (30% of hashtags)
- **Volume**: 100K-1M posts
- **Purpose**: Appear in broader category feeds
- **Example**: #DigitalMarketing, #HealthyEating, #HomeWorkout
- **Discovery**: Instagram Explore, TikTok Discover, industry blogs

#### Tier 3: Trending/Timely (20% of hashtags)
- **Volume**: Varies (current momentum matters)
- **Purpose**: Ride trending waves for discovery
- **Example**: #2026Trends, #SpringVibes, #MondayMotivation
- **Discovery**: Platform trending sections, Google Trends, social listening

#### Tier 4: Branded (10% of hashtags)
- **Volume**: You build this from zero
- **Purpose**: Collect UGC, build community, track mentions
- **Example**: #YourBrandName, #YourCampaignName, #YourCommunityName
- **Discovery**: Create them yourself, promote in CTAs

### Platform-Specific Hashtag Guidelines
| Platform   | Optimal Count | Strategy Notes                              |
|------------|---------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Instagram  | 5-15          | Mix tiers; place in caption, not comments   |
| TikTok     | 3-5           | Trend-heavy; niche + trending combo         |
| LinkedIn   | 3-5           | Professional; industry and topic specific   |
| X/Twitter  | 1-2           | Only trending or campaign-specific          |
| YouTube    | 3-5           | In description; focus on search terms       |
| Pinterest  | 2-5           | Keyword-focused; treated like search terms  |

### Hashtag Research Process
1. **Seed list**: Brainstorm 20-30 terms your audience would search
2. **Platform search**: Enter each term, note related hashtags suggested
3. **Competitor audit**: Document top 5 competitors' hashtag sets
4. **Volume check**: Categorize by post volume into tiers above
5. **Performance test**: Use different sets for 2-4 weeks, track reach
6. **Refine**: Keep top performers, replace underperformers monthly

### Hashtag Sets (Pre-Built Rotation)
Create 5-8 hashtag sets, one per content pillar or topic, and rotate them:
- **Set A (Education)**: 10-15 hashtags focused on your educational content
- **Set B (Inspiration)**: 10-15 hashtags for motivational content
- **Set C (Promotion)**: 10-15 hashtags for product/service content
- **Set D (Community)**: 10-15 hashtags for engagement content
- **Set E (Seasonal)**: 10-15 hashtags updated monthly for trends

## UGC Collection & Curation Workflow

### Step 1: Create a UGC Ecosystem
- **Branded hashtag**: Promote one clear hashtag for submissions
- **Submission form**: Link in bio or website page for UGC uploads
- **Email trigger**: Post-purchase email requesting photos/videos
- **Incentives**: Feature spotlights, discounts, contests, ambassador programs
- **In-product prompts**: Packaging inserts, app notifications

### Step 2: Monitor & Collect
- **Daily monitoring**: Check branded hashtag, mentions, tags
- **Tools**: Later (UGC collection), Sprout Social, manual saves
- **Screenshot + save**: Archive all potential UGC to a shared folder
- **DM outreach**: Contact creators of great organic mentions

### Step 3: Secure Permission
- **Always get written permission** before reposting
- **DM template**: "Hi [name]! We love your [post/photo/video] featuring [product]. Would you mind if we shared it on our page? We'll tag you and give full credit."
- **Legal**: Maintain a permission log with dates and screenshots of approval
- **Rights agreement**: For paid UGC, use a content license agreement

### Step 4: Curate & Organize
- **Rating system**: Rate UGC 1-5 stars for quality
- **Categorize by**: Product, content type, platform, theme, campaign
- **Content library**: Store in organized folders (Google Drive, Notion, Airtable)
- **Metadata**: Tag with creator name, date, permission status, usage rights

### Step 5: Publish & Credit
- **Always credit the creator**: Tag in post, mention in caption
- **Edit minimally**: Maintain authenticity (slight crop/filter is acceptable)
- **Contextualize**: Add brand messaging around UGC naturally
- **Cross-platform**: Repurpose UGC across channels where rights allow

### UGC Quality Guidelines
- Well-lit, clear imagery (not blurry or dark)
- Product/service clearly visible
- Authentic and not overly staged
- Positive sentiment and brand alignment
- Meets platform minimum resolution requirements

## Social Media Crisis Response Playbook

### Crisis Severity Levels

#### Level 1: Low (Monitor)
- **Examples**: Single negative comment, minor complaint, isolated mention
- **Response time**: Within 4-8 hours
- **Action**: Community manager responds with empathy and solution
- **Escalation**: Not required unless repeated

#### Level 2: Medium (Respond)
- **Examples**: Multiple complaints about same issue, negative review going viral, influencer criticism
- **Response time**: Within 1-2 hours
- **Action**: Acknowledge issue publicly, take conversation to DMs, loop in team lead
- **Escalation**: Notify marketing manager and relevant department

#### Level 3: High (Act)
- **Examples**: Product safety concern, data breach, employee misconduct going viral, brand boycott, legal threat
- **Response time**: Within 30 minutes (acknowledgment)
- **Action**: Pause all scheduled content, draft official statement, convene crisis team
- **Escalation**: Executive team, legal, PR, all hands on deck

### Crisis Response Steps

#### Immediate (0-1 hour)
1. **Pause scheduled content**: Stop all auto-scheduled posts immediately
2. **Assess**: Determine severity level and scope
3. **Document**: Screenshot everything, start a crisis log
4. **Acknowledge** (Level 2-3): Post brief acknowledgment that you are aware
5. **Assemble**: Notify relevant team members and stakeholders

#### Short-term (1-24 hours)
1. **Draft response**: Craft official statement (approved by legal if Level 3)
2. **Respond publicly**: Post official statement across relevant platforms
3. **Monitor**: Track sentiment, volume, and spread in real-time
4. **Engage**: Respond to individual comments with consistent messaging
5. **Internal updates**: Keep all team members aligned on messaging

#### Recovery (24-72 hours)
1. **Follow-up**: Update stakeholders on resolution progress
2. **Transparency**: Share what happened, what you are doing, and what changes you are making
3. **Resume content**: Gradually resume normal posting (test tone carefully)
4. **Outreach**: Contact key complainants directly to resolve
5. **Document lessons**: Create post-mortem report

### Crisis Response Templates

#### Acknowledgment Template
"We're aware of [issue] and take this seriously. We're looking into it and will share an update as soon as possible. Thank you for your patience."

#### Resolution Template
"Thank you for bringing [issue] to our attention. After investigation, [explain what happened]. We've [action taken]. We're committed to [future prevention]. We appreciate your understanding."

#### Apology Template
"We sincerely apologize for [specific issue]. This does not reflect our values or standards. We've [immediate action taken] and are implementing [systemic changes] to prevent this from happening again."

### Do's and Don'ts

#### Do
- Respond quickly, even if just to acknowledge
- Show empathy and take responsibility
- Take heated discussions to private channels
- Keep consistent messaging across all team members
- Monitor 24/7 during active crisis
- Document everything for post-mortem

#### Don't
- Delete negative comments (unless truly abusive or spam)
- Argue with individuals publicly
- Make promises you cannot keep
- Use humor during a serious crisis
- Blame customers or external parties
- Go silent and hope it blows over

## Approval & Publishing Workflow

### Content Approval Process
1. **Creator drafts content** (copy + visuals) in content calendar tool
2. **Self-review**: Check against brand guidelines, pillar alignment, CTA
3. **Peer review**: Second team member reviews for errors and tone
4. **Manager approval**: Final sign-off for sensitive or promotional content
5. **Schedule**: Queue in scheduling tool at optimal time
6. **Publish**: Auto-publish or manual publish as needed
7. **Monitor**: Check engagement within first 30-60 minutes, respond to comments

### Content Calendar Tools
- **Notion**: Best for flexible editorial calendars and team collaboration
- **Airtable**: Excellent for database-style content tracking with views
- **Google Sheets**: Simple, free, accessible to all team members
- **Trello/Asana**: Kanban-style workflow (Draft > Review > Approved > Published)
- **Platform-native**: Use Buffer, Later, or Hootsuite built-in calendars

### Naming Convention for Content Files
```
[Date]_[Platform]_[Pillar]_[Format]_[Topic]
Example: 2026-03-15_IG_Educate_Carousel_SEOTips
Example: 2026-03-15_TT_Entertain_Reel_BehindTheScenes
```

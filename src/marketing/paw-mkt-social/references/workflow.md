# Social Media Marketing Workflow

This reference defines the standard workflow, phase structure, diagnostic questions, platform-specific considerations, and deliverable format for all organic social media marketing work.

---

## Workflow Overview

The Social Media Marketing Workflow is a 6-phase lifecycle that takes social strategy from audience audit through content production, publishing, and performance iteration. Every phase has entry conditions, key activities, deliverables, and exit conditions.

The core principle: **Platform-native strategy first, content second.** No social content gets produced without a documented platform strategy, audience mapping, content pillars, and success metrics. Cross-posting generic content is wasted effort.

### Phase Summary

| Phase | Name | Purpose | Typical Duration |
|-------|------|---------|-----------------|
| 1 | Platform Strategy & Audit | Audit current presence, select platform mix, define native strategy per platform | 3-5 days |
| 2 | Content Strategy & Calendar | Define pillars, format mix, posting cadence, and 30-day content calendar | 3-5 days |
| 3 | Content Creation & Templates | Produce caption templates, hashtag sets, asset specs, and repeatable content formats | 2-5 days |
| 4 | Publishing & Engagement | Execute posting schedule, manage community engagement, respond to DMs and comments | Ongoing |
| 5 | Analytics & Optimization | Track KPIs, identify top-performing content, adjust strategy based on data | Weekly reviews |
| 6 | Growth & Scaling | Implement follower acquisition tactics, cross-promotion, collaboration plays | Monthly cycles |

---

## Diagnostic Questions

Ask these before producing any social media recommendation. Recommendations without this data are guesses.

1. **Current state**: Which platforms have active accounts? What is the current follower count, engagement rate, and posting frequency on each?
2. **Audience**: Where does the target audience spend time? What demographics and psychographics define them? What content do they engage with?
3. **Competitive landscape**: What are 3-5 competitors or comparable brands doing on social? What content formats and cadences are working for them?
4. **Resources**: Who creates content? What is the available time, budget, and tooling for social media? Can the team produce video?
5. **Brand context**: Is there an existing brand voice, visual identity, or SOSTAC plan? What tone is appropriate for this audience?
6. **Goals**: Is the primary goal awareness (reach, impressions), engagement (comments, shares, saves), traffic (link clicks), or conversion (leads, sales)?
7. **History**: What content has performed best and worst in the past 90 days? What has been tested?

If the user cannot answer questions 1-2, recommend an audience research phase before building a content calendar. Social strategy without audience understanding is shouting into a void.

## Phase 1: Platform Strategy & Audit

**Purpose**: Evaluate the current social presence, select the right platform mix based on audience presence, and define a platform-native strategy for each channel.

### Entry Conditions
- Brand context or SOSTAC plan available (or user provides equivalent context)
- User has articulated social media goals or a marketing objective

### Key Activities
1. **Audit existing presence**: Review each active platform for follower count, engagement rate (last 30 days), posting frequency, content mix, bio/profile optimization, and link-in-bio setup.
2. **Assess platform fit**: Map the target audience to platform demographics. Not every brand belongs on every platform. Prioritize 2-3 platforms for focused execution over 5+ with diluted effort.
3. **Define platform roles**: Each platform serves a distinct purpose in the strategy:
   - Instagram: visual brand storytelling, product showcasing, community building via Stories and DMs
   - TikTok: discovery and reach through short-form video, trend participation, authenticity
   - LinkedIn: thought leadership, B2B lead generation, industry authority
   - X/Twitter: real-time conversation, industry commentary, customer support
   - YouTube: long-form education, tutorials, brand documentaries
   - Pinterest: evergreen discovery, product inspiration, traffic driver
   - Reddit: niche community engagement, authentic participation (never market directly)
4. **Set platform-specific KPIs**: Different platforms optimize for different metrics. Define primary and secondary KPIs per platform.
5. **Identify quick wins**: Profile optimization, bio updates, link-in-bio tools, pinned post strategy.

### Deliverables
- Platform audit summary with current metrics per channel
- Platform strategy brief: selected platforms, role of each, KPIs per platform
- Profile optimization checklist

### Exit Conditions
- 2-3 primary platforms selected with clear strategic rationale
- Platform-specific KPIs defined
- Profile optimization items identified

## Phase 2: Content Strategy & Calendar

**Purpose**: Define content pillars, format priorities, posting cadence, and build a 30-day content calendar that balances value delivery with audience growth.

### Entry Conditions
- Platform strategy is defined (Phase 1 complete)
- Brand voice and positioning context available

### Key Activities
1. **Define content pillars** (4-6 maximum):
   - Each pillar maps to an audience need or business objective
   - Example mix: 30% educate, 25% entertain, 15% inspire, 15% promote, 15% connect
   - Every piece of content should map to exactly one pillar

2. **Select format priorities** per platform:
   - Instagram: Reels (reach) > Carousels (saves) > Stories (engagement) > Static posts (authority)
   - TikTok: Short-form video only; hook in first 1.5 seconds
   - LinkedIn: Text posts (reach) > Document carousels (saves) > Short video > Articles
   - X/Twitter: Threads (depth) > Single tweets (frequency) > Quote tweets (community)

3. **Set posting cadence**: Consistency matters more than volume.
   - Minimum viable cadence per platform (adjust based on resources)
   - Instagram: 4-7 Reels/week, daily Stories
   - TikTok: 5-7 videos/week
   - LinkedIn: 3-5 posts/week
   - X/Twitter: 1-3 per day

4. **Build 30-day content calendar**:
   - Assign each post to: date, platform, pillar, format, hook/caption direction, CTA, hashtag set
   - Include seasonal hooks, industry events, and trending topic slots
   - Build in 20% flex for real-time and trending content

5. **Define hashtag strategy**:
   - 3-tier system: branded hashtags, niche hashtags (10K-500K posts), broad hashtags (500K+)
   - Platform-specific: Instagram uses 5-15, TikTok uses 3-5, LinkedIn uses 3-5

### Deliverables
- Content pillar framework with pillar-to-goal mapping
- 30-day content calendar (per platform)
- Hashtag strategy document with curated sets per pillar

### Exit Conditions
- Content pillars defined with clear audience connection
- 30-day calendar populated for all primary platforms
- Hashtag sets curated and organized by pillar

## Phase 3: Content Creation & Templates

**Purpose**: Produce reusable content templates, caption frameworks, and asset specifications that enable consistent, efficient content production.

### Entry Conditions
- Content strategy and calendar are defined (Phase 2 complete)
- Brand voice and visual identity guidelines available

### Key Activities
1. **Create caption templates** per content pillar:
   - Hook line (stops the scroll): question, bold statement, statistic, or pattern interrupt
   - Body: value delivery in 2-4 short paragraphs
   - CTA: specific action (save, share, comment, click link)
   - Provide 3-5 caption templates per pillar

2. **Define asset specifications**:
   - Image dimensions per platform (feed, Stories, Reels cover)
   - Video specifications: aspect ratio, resolution, duration targets
   - Carousel templates: cover slide hook, content slides, CTA slide
   - Brand elements: fonts, colors, logo placement rules

3. **Build repeatable content formats**:
   - Series concepts (weekly recurring themes)
   - Template structures (listicles, before/after, day-in-the-life, tutorial, myth-busting)
   - UGC prompts and repost frameworks

4. **Create engagement response templates**:
   - Comment response guidelines by sentiment
   - DM response templates for common inquiries
   - Community management escalation triggers

### Deliverables
- Caption template library (organized by pillar and platform)
- Asset specification guide
- Repeatable content format playbook
- Engagement response template set

### Exit Conditions
- At least 3 caption templates per pillar created
- Asset specs documented for all primary platforms
- At least 5 repeatable content formats defined

## Phase 4: Publishing & Engagement

**Purpose**: Execute the content calendar consistently, manage community engagement, and maintain brand presence through active social participation.

### Entry Conditions
- Content templates and calendar are ready (Phase 3 complete)
- Content production pipeline is active

### Key Activities
1. **Execute posting schedule**: Publish content per the calendar. Optimal posting times vary by platform and audience — start with industry benchmarks, then adjust based on analytics.
2. **Community engagement**: Respond to comments within 1-2 hours during business hours. Engage with audience content (like, comment, share) for 15-30 minutes per platform daily.
3. **Story and real-time content**: Use Stories, live sessions, and real-time posts to maintain presence between scheduled content.
4. **Monitor mentions and tags**: Track brand mentions, tagged content, and relevant conversations for engagement and UGC opportunities.
5. **Cross-promote**: Share content across platforms with native adaptation — never identical cross-posts.

### Deliverables
- Published content per calendar
- Engagement log (weekly summary of response rate and sentiment)
- UGC library (saved repurposable user content)

### Exit Conditions
- Calendar adherence above 80% for the period
- Average response time under 2 hours during business hours
- Engagement metrics tracked and logged

## Phase 5: Analytics & Optimization

**Purpose**: Measure performance against KPIs, identify top-performing content patterns, and optimize strategy based on data.

### Entry Conditions
- At least 30 days of consistent posting data available
- Analytics tools connected and tracking

### Key Activities
1. **Track platform KPIs**:
   - Reach and impressions (awareness)
   - Engagement rate: (likes + comments + shares + saves) / reach
   - Follower growth rate (net new followers / total followers)
   - Click-through rate on link content
   - Saves and shares (strongest algorithm signals)

2. **Identify content patterns**:
   - Top 5 performing posts: what do they have in common? (Format, pillar, hook type, posting time)
   - Bottom 5 performing posts: what went wrong? (Weak hook, wrong format, poor timing, irrelevant topic)
   - Format performance ranking: which content types drive the most reach, engagement, and saves?

3. **Benchmark against competitors**: Compare engagement rates, posting frequency, and content types with 3-5 comparable accounts.

4. **Optimize and adjust**:
   - Double down on formats and pillars that outperform
   - Retire or reduce underperforming content types
   - Adjust posting times based on audience activity data
   - Refresh hashtag sets based on reach contribution

### Deliverables
- Monthly analytics report with KPI tracking
- Content performance analysis (top/bottom performers with pattern insights)
- Strategy adjustment recommendations

### Exit Conditions
- Monthly report produced with actionable insights
- At least 2 optimization actions identified and implemented
- Calendar updated to reflect performance learnings

## Phase 6: Growth & Scaling

**Purpose**: Implement follower acquisition tactics, collaboration strategies, and cross-promotion plays to accelerate audience growth beyond organic content performance.

### Entry Conditions
- Consistent posting cadence established (Phases 1-5 running)
- Analytics showing stable or growing engagement trends

### Key Activities
1. **Collaboration plays**: Partner with complementary accounts for cross-promotion, joint lives, takeovers, or co-created content.
2. **UGC campaigns**: Launch branded hashtag challenges, customer spotlight series, or community contests to generate authentic third-party content.
3. **Comment and community strategy**: Strategically engage on high-visibility posts in the niche. Add value — never self-promote in comments.
4. **Cross-platform funnel**: Drive audiences between platforms (TikTok discovery → Instagram community → email list → conversion).
5. **Trend and moment marketing**: Monitor trending topics, sounds, and formats for timely participation that drives discovery.
6. **Influencer micro-collaborations**: Partner with nano/micro-influencers for authentic reach in target communities. Route larger campaigns to `paw-mkt-influencer`.

### Deliverables
- Growth playbook with prioritized acquisition tactics
- Collaboration outreach templates and target list
- UGC campaign brief
- Cross-platform funnel map

### Exit Conditions
- At least 3 growth tactics active and tracked
- Collaboration pipeline established with 5+ targets
- Month-over-month follower growth rate improving

## Ethics: Authenticity Over Manipulation

Social media builds relationships. Manipulation destroys them.

### Do
- Represent the brand honestly — no fabricated testimonials, fake engagement, or misleading metrics
- Disclose partnerships and sponsored content per FTC guidelines
- Respect community norms on each platform — what works on TikTok is inappropriate on LinkedIn
- Use engagement tactics that provide genuine value — not engagement bait without substance
- Credit original creators and sources

### Do Not
- Buy followers, likes, or engagement — inflated metrics mislead stakeholders and tank algorithm credibility
- Use engagement pods or reciprocal engagement schemes
- Fabricate urgency, scarcity, or social proof
- Automate comments or DMs with generic messages
- Appropriate cultural moments or social issues for brand promotion without genuine connection

Before implementing any social tactic, ask: **Would the audience feel well-served if they understood exactly how this content was created and distributed?** If the answer is no, revise the approach.

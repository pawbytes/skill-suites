# Quick-Reference Table

Marketing challenge mapped to relevant principle and specific tactics. Use for rapid diagnostic in any situation.

| Marketing Challenge | Primary Principle | Supporting Principle | Specific Tactics |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low landing page conversion | Social proof | Loss aversion | Add specific testimonial above fold; name what visitors lose by not acting; simplify to one CTA |
| High cart or signup abandonment | Status quo bias | Loss aversion | Abandonment email naming specific items + objection handling; risk reversal (free trial, guarantee) |
| Pricing feels expensive | Anchoring | Decoy pricing | Show problem cost before product cost; add a higher-priced tier; reframe in ROI terms |
| Customers say "let me think about it" | Status quo bias | Commitment-consistency | Make cost of inaction concrete; reference prior commitments ("You already told us this is a priority") |
| Low trial-to-paid conversion | Peak-end rule | Loss aversion | Compress path to aha moment; trial-end email naming specific things that disappear; celebrate progress invested |
| High churn in first 30 days | Peak-end rule | BJ Fogg (ability) | Redesign onboarding to get to value faster; add milestone emails; reduce friction on first key action |
| Weak email open rates | Curiosity gap | Loss aversion | Rewrite subjects as questions with implied loss or specific curiosity gap; test behavioral send times |
| Slow feature adoption | BJ Fogg (prompt) | Commitment-consistency | Add contextual in-app prompts at moment of relevant motivation; trigger behavioral email on prior action |
| Ad copy not converting | Confirmation bias | Loss aversion | Mirror audience's existing beliefs; switch to loss framing; use their exact language from reviews/interviews |
| Referral participation is low | BJ Fogg (ability) | Reciprocity | Trigger referral ask immediately after positive peak moment; provide pre-written share copy; one-click mechanism |
| Newsletter unsubscribes rising | Reciprocity | Paradox of choice | Increase value-to-promotion ratio; add preference center; reduce email frequency option |
| Weak differentiation from competitors | First principles | JTBD | Challenge every assumption; identify the emotional and social job competitors are failing to do |
| Low webinar attendance | Social proof | Scarcity | Add "X people already registered" counter; frame as limited capacity; send loss-framed reminder |
| Proposals not closing | Anchoring | Commitment-consistency | Open proposals with problem cost (anchor); reference prior conversations and agreed-on goals (consistency) |
| Cold outreach not getting replies | Reciprocity | Availability heuristic | Lead with genuine value (insight, resource) before any ask; use specific, vivid opening line about their situation |
| Cancellations increasing | Peak-end rule | Commitment-consistency | Redesign cancellation flow to highlight invested progress; offer pause instead of cancel; improve the ending experience |
| High trial signup, low activation | BJ Fogg (ability) | Tiny habits | Reduce steps to first value moment; anchor first action to existing workflow; celebrate first completion |
| Low annual plan uptake | Rule of 100 | Mental accounting | Show savings in concrete dollar amount ("Save $240/year"); frame as prepaid at a discount, not a commitment |
| Content not generating leads | JTBD | Reciprocity | Audit whether content addresses the actual job readers are trying to do; increase value before the gate |
| Long sales cycle not progressing | Commitment-consistency | Status quo bias | Small commitment asks between touchpoints; make cost of inaction compounding and visible |

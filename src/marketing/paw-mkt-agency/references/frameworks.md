## 1. RACE Framework (Reach, Act, Convert, Engage)

### Overview
- Created by Smart Insights; used in 100+ countries.
- Customer lifecycle framework covering the full journey from awareness to advocacy.
- Sometimes called PRACE to include the planning stage upfront.
- Complements SOSTAC well: SOSTAC defines the overall plan; RACE structures the tactical execution.

### Stage 1: Plan (The "P" in PRACE)
- Define goals, ICPs (Ideal Customer Profiles), and KPIs before executing.
- Conduct competitor and market research.
- Perform keyword research and audience analysis.
- Establish budget parameters and measurement framework.
- Map existing assets and identify gaps.

### Stage 2: Reach — Build Awareness and Visibility
**Goal**: Drive qualified traffic and grow share of voice.

**Key Tactics**:
- SEO topic clusters and digital PR to grow organic reach.
- Discovery ads, video ads, and social amplification.
- Partnerships, communities, and influencer programs.
- Content marketing for top-of-funnel awareness.

**KPIs**:
- Unique visitors, impressions, reach
- Share of voice (SOV) vs. competitors
- Cost per thousand impressions (CPM)
- Organic search visibility index
- Social media reach and follower growth rate
- Referral traffic volume

**Channel Mix by Stage**:
| Channel | Role in Reach |
|---|---|
| SEO | Long-term organic visibility |
| Paid Social | Targeted awareness campaigns |
| Display / Programmatic | Broad awareness, retargeting seeds |
| Content Marketing | Thought leadership, blog posts, infographics |
| PR / Digital PR | Earned media, backlinks, brand mentions |
| Influencer Marketing | Audience access, social proof |

### Stage 3: Act — Encourage Interaction and Engagement
**Goal**: Drive micro-conversions and engagement that move prospects toward purchase.

**Key Tactics**:
- Lead magnets, webinars, and interactive content (quizzes, calculators).
- Landing pages optimized for conversions.
- Smart retargeting to educational value pages.
- Email capture and nurture sequence entry.
- Social media engagement (comments, DMs, community).

**KPIs**:
- Pages per session, time on site, bounce rate
- Lead magnet download rate
- Email subscription rate
- Blog engagement (scroll depth, comments, shares)
- Social engagement rate (likes, comments, shares / reach)
- Micro-conversion rate (add to cart, create account, start trial)

### Stage 4: Convert — Turn Leads into Customers
**Goal**: Drive macro-conversions (sales, sign-ups, deals closed).

**Key Tactics**:
- Compelling CTAs with clear value propositions.
- Social proof: testimonials, case studies, reviews, guarantees.
- Automated email nurturing and follow-up through CRM.
- Conversion rate optimization (CRO): A/B testing, landing page optimization.
- Sales enablement content (comparison pages, ROI calculators, demos).

**KPIs**:
- Conversion rate (by channel, by campaign)
- Cost per acquisition (CPA) / Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- Revenue / transactions
- Average order value (AOV)
- Cart abandonment rate (ecommerce)
- Sales cycle length (B2B)
- Lead-to-customer conversion rate

### Stage 5: Engage — Drive Retention and Advocacy
**Goal**: Increase customer lifetime value and turn customers into advocates.

**Key Tactics**:
- Onboarding email sequences and welcome journeys.
- Loyalty programs and exclusive offers.
- Customer success / support excellence.
- Community building (forums, user groups, social communities).
- Referral programs, UGC campaigns, review solicitation.
- Personalized cross-sell and upsell campaigns.

**KPIs**:
- Customer lifetime value (CLV)
- Repeat purchase rate / retention rate
- Net Promoter Score (NPS)
- Referral rate and referral revenue
- Customer satisfaction score (CSAT)
- Churn rate
- Social advocacy metrics (UGC volume, brand mentions)

### RACE + SOSTAC Integration
| SOSTAC Phase | RACE Application |
|---|---|
| Situation Analysis | Audit current performance across all RACE stages |
| Objectives | Set SMART goals per RACE stage |
| Strategy | Define audience segments and value propositions for each stage |
| Tactics | Select specific channels and tactics for Reach, Act, Convert, Engage |
| Action | Create implementation plan with deliverables mapped to RACE stages |
| Control | Set up KPI dashboards tracking each RACE stage |

## 2. See-Think-Do-Care Framework (Google)

### Overview
- Developed by Avinash Kaushik at Google.
- Audience-intent-based framework that maps content and channels to customer mindset.
- Prevents the mistake of only targeting "ready to buy" audiences.

### The Four Audiences

**See (Largest Addressable Qualified Audience)**
- Who: Everyone who *could* buy but is not actively considering.
- Intent: None. They are not looking for your product.
- Content: Brand awareness, entertainment, inspiration, educational content.
- Channels: YouTube, display, social media, content marketing, PR.
- Measurement: Reach, impressions, brand awareness lift, video views.
- Example: A running shoe brand creating content about health and fitness lifestyle.

**Think (Considering, Not Yet Committed)**
- Who: People who have a need and are researching options.
- Intent: Informational. Comparing, evaluating, learning.
- Content: How-to guides, comparison articles, webinars, product demos, reviews.
- Channels: SEO, content marketing, email nurture, retargeting, YouTube.
- Measurement: Engagement, email sign-ups, content downloads, search rankings.
- Example: "Best running shoes for marathon training" comparison guide.

**Do (Ready to Act)**
- Who: People ready to purchase or convert.
- Intent: Transactional. Looking to buy, sign up, or commit.
- Content: Product pages, pricing pages, free trials, demos, promotions, CTAs.
- Channels: Paid search, shopping ads, retargeting, email offers, direct.
- Measurement: Conversion rate, revenue, CPA, ROAS.
- Example: "Buy Nike Pegasus 41 — Free Shipping" landing page with clear CTA.

**Care (Existing Customers)**
- Who: Current customers with 2+ transactions or active subscriptions.
- Intent: Loyalty, support, community.
- Content: Loyalty rewards, exclusive content, product tips, community, support.
- Channels: Email, in-app, community platforms, customer success.
- Measurement: CLV, retention rate, NPS, repeat purchase rate.
- Example: Members-only running community with training plans and gear discounts.

### Implementation Matrix
| Dimension | See | Think | Do | Care |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Budget Share | 15-25% | 25-35% | 30-40% | 10-15% |
| Content Type | Awareness | Educational | Transactional | Retention |
| Primary Metric | Reach | Engagement | Conversion | LTV |
| Typical Channels | Social, Video, PR | SEO, Content, Email | PPC, Retarget, Direct | Email, Community |
| Attribution | View-through | Multi-touch | Last-click | Cohort |

## 3. Growth Loops vs. Funnels

### The Funnel Model
- Linear: Awareness -> Interest -> Desire -> Action (AIDA) or TOFU -> MOFU -> BOFU.
- Each stage loses a percentage of prospects (leaky bucket).
- Requires constant top-of-funnel investment to sustain growth.
- Best for: Product launches, new market entry, campaign planning, sales-led organizations.

### Growth Loops (Flywheel / Compounding Growth)
- Non-linear: Output of one user action becomes the input for acquiring/activating new users.
- Self-reinforcing: Growth compounds rather than leaking.
- Reduces dependency on paid acquisition over time.

### Types of Growth Loops

**Viral Loop**:
- User creates content or invites others -> New users sign up -> They create/invite -> Repeat.
- Example: Dropbox referral program (give storage, get storage).
- Key metric: Viral coefficient (K-factor). K > 1 = exponential growth.

**Content Loop**:
- Users create content -> Content indexed by search engines -> New users discover content -> They create content -> Repeat.
- Example: Pinterest (users pin content -> SEO traffic -> new users -> more pins).
- Key metric: Organic traffic growth rate, UGC volume.

**Paid Loop**:
- Revenue from customers -> Reinvested into ads -> Ads acquire new customers -> Revenue -> Repeat.
- Example: Ecommerce brand reinvesting 20% of revenue into Meta Ads.
- Key metric: LTV:CAC ratio (target: 3:1 or higher for sustainable loop).

**Sales Loop**:
- Happy customers -> Referrals / case studies -> Credibility attracts new customers -> Repeat.
- Example: B2B SaaS using customer success stories to close new deals.
- Key metric: Referral rate, case study conversion impact.

### When to Use Each
| Scenario | Use Funnel | Use Growth Loop |
|---|---|---|
| New product, no traction | Yes | Not yet |
| Established product with network effects | Support | Primary |
| Sales-led B2B with long cycles | Primary | Support |
| Product-led growth (PLG) model | Support | Primary |
| Ecommerce with repeat purchase | Hybrid | Paid loop |
| Content/community platform | Support | Content loop |

### How to Identify Growth Loops in a Business
1. Map the customer journey from acquisition to activation to monetization.
2. Identify where output from one stage could feed back into an earlier stage.
3. Look for: user-generated content, referral incentives, viral mechanics, data network effects.
4. Ask: "Does each new customer make the product more valuable for the next customer?"
5. If no natural loop exists, engineer one (referral program, affiliate program, content program).

## 4. Bullseye Framework for Channel Prioritization

### Overview
- Created by Gabriel Weinberg and Justin Mares (Traction book).
- Systematic approach to finding the channel(s) that will drive your growth.
- Tests 19 traction channels to find the "bullseye" — the one channel that works best right now.

### The 19 Traction Channels
1. Viral Marketing
2. Public Relations (PR)
3. Unconventional PR / Stunts
4. Search Engine Marketing (SEM / PPC)
5. Social and Display Ads
6. Offline Ads (TV, Radio, Print, Billboards)
7. Search Engine Optimization (SEO)
8. Content Marketing
9. Email Marketing
10. Engineering as Marketing (free tools, calculators, widgets)
11. Targeting Blogs / Niche Media
12. Business Development / Partnerships
13. Sales
14. Affiliate Programs
15. Existing Platforms (marketplaces, app stores, social platforms)
16. Trade Shows / Events
17. Offline Events / Meetups
18. Speaking Engagements
19. Community Building

### The Three-Ring Process

**Outer Ring: Brainstorm (All 19 Channels)**
- For each of the 19 channels, brainstorm at least one tactic.
- Do not filter or judge at this stage; capture all ideas.
- Include: What would a test look like? What is the expected outcome? How would you measure it?

**Middle Ring: Rank & Test (Top 6)**
- Rank all ideas by potential impact and feasibility.
- Select 6 channels for cheap, fast tests (1-2 weeks, minimal budget).
- Design a minimum viable test (MVT) for each:
  - Budget: $100-$500 per channel test
  - Timeline: 1-2 weeks
  - Success metric: Defined upfront (e.g., CPA below $X, conversion rate above Y%)

**Inner Ring: Focus (Top 1-3)**
- Analyze test results.
- Double down on the 1-3 channels showing the most promise.
- Invest significant resources (budget, team, time) into optimizing these channels.
- Revisit the outer ring every 3-6 months as the business evolves.

### Traction Testing Template
| Channel | Test Description | Budget | Timeline | Success Metric | Result | Next Step |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Content Marketing | Publish 4 SEO articles targeting buyer keywords | $2,000 | 4 weeks | 500+ organic visits/mo after 60 days | Pending | — |
| Meta Ads | Run 3 ad sets targeting ICP with lead magnet | $500 | 2 weeks | CPA below $25 | Pending | — |
| Partnerships | Co-webinar with complementary SaaS tool | $0 | 3 weeks | 50+ qualified leads | Pending | — |

## 5. ICE Scoring for Experiment Prioritization

### Overview
- Created by Sean Ellis (growth hacking pioneer) in the early 2010s.
- Fast, practical method for ranking marketing experiments and initiatives.
- Turns subjective debates into structured judgments.
- Used in weekly or biweekly planning cycles to maintain momentum.

### The Three Dimensions

**Impact (1-10)**: How much will this move the needle on our target metric?
- 10: Transformative impact on primary KPI (e.g., 2x conversion rate)
- 7-9: Significant impact, clear improvement expected
- 4-6: Moderate impact, incremental improvement
- 1-3: Minimal impact, nice-to-have

**Confidence (1-10)**: How sure are we that this will achieve the expected impact?
- 10: Strong data / evidence from similar past experiments
- 7-9: Good supporting data, analogous case studies
- 4-6: Reasonable hypothesis, some indirect evidence
- 1-3: Pure gut feeling, novel/untested idea

**Ease (1-10)**: How easy is this to implement?
- 10: Can do in a few hours, no dependencies
- 7-9: A few days, minimal resources needed
- 4-6: 1-2 weeks, moderate resources / coordination required
- 1-3: Multiple weeks, significant resources, complex dependencies

### Scoring Formula
```
ICE Score = (Impact + Confidence + Ease) / 3
```
Alternative (multiplicative):
```
ICE Score = Impact x Confidence x Ease
```
The additive version (average) is more commonly used in marketing contexts as it is less punitive for low scores in one dimension.

### ICE Scoring Example — Marketing Experiments
| Experiment | Impact | Confidence | Ease | ICE Score | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A/B test homepage headline | 8 | 7 | 9 | 8.0 | 1st |
| Launch referral program | 9 | 5 | 4 | 6.0 | 3rd |
| Add exit-intent popup | 7 | 8 | 8 | 7.7 | 2nd |
| Redesign pricing page | 8 | 6 | 3 | 5.7 | 4th |
| Test TikTok ads | 6 | 3 | 7 | 5.3 | 5th |

### Best Practices
- Score as a team to reduce individual bias. Average team scores.
- Re-score monthly as confidence increases with new data.
- Complement with RICE (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) when reach varies significantly between experiments.
- Use ICE for rapid weekly prioritization; use more detailed models (NPV, payback) for large investments.
- Document assumptions behind each score for future reference.

## 6. AARRR Pirate Metrics

### Overview
- Created by Dave McClure (500 Startups).
- Five-stage framework for measuring and optimizing the customer lifecycle.
- Acronym: Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, Referral.
- Maps directly to marketing activities and experiments.

### The Five Stages

**Acquisition**: How do users find us?
- Channels: SEO, SEM, social media, content, PR, partnerships, ads.
- Key Metrics: Traffic by source, cost per visit, channel conversion rate.
- Marketing Activities: Campaign launches, content creation, ad management, SEO optimization.

**Activation**: Do users have a great first experience?
- Definition: The "aha moment" — when a user first experiences core product value.
- Key Metrics: Sign-up rate, onboarding completion rate, time to value, activation rate.
- Marketing Activities: Onboarding email sequences, welcome tutorials, quick-start guides, UX copy.

**Retention**: Do users come back?
- Definition: Users return and continue engaging over time.
- Key Metrics: DAU/MAU ratio, cohort retention curves, churn rate, session frequency.
- Marketing Activities: Email re-engagement campaigns, push notifications, content updates, loyalty programs.

**Revenue**: How do we make money?
- Definition: Users convert to paying customers and generate revenue.
- Key Metrics: ARPU, LTV, conversion to paid rate, MRR/ARR, average order value.
- Marketing Activities: Pricing page optimization, upsell campaigns, checkout optimization, promotional offers.

**Referral**: Do users tell others?
- Definition: Users actively recommend the product.
- Key Metrics: Referral rate, viral coefficient (K-factor), NPS, referral revenue.
- Marketing Activities: Referral programs, UGC campaigns, review solicitation, affiliate programs.

### AARRR Mapped to Agency Services
| Pirate Stage | Primary Agency Services |
|---|---|
| Acquisition | SEO, PPC, Social Media, Content Marketing, PR |
| Activation | CRO, Email Onboarding, UX/UI, Landing Pages |
| Retention | Email Marketing, Content, Community, Social |
| Revenue | CRO, Email Automation, PPC Remarketing |
| Referral | Referral Programs, Influencer, Community, PR |

## 7. Customer Journey Mapping

### Purpose
- Visualize the complete customer experience from first touch to advocacy.
- Identify gaps, friction points, and opportunities at each stage.
- Align marketing, sales, and customer success around a shared understanding.

### Journey Mapping Process

**Step 1: Define Personas**
- Create 2-4 primary buyer personas based on research (not assumptions).
- Include: Demographics, psychographics, goals, pain points, information sources, objections.

**Step 2: Map Journey Stages**
- Typical stages: Awareness -> Consideration -> Decision -> Onboarding -> Usage -> Advocacy.
- For each stage, document:
  - **Touchpoints**: Where does the customer interact with us?
  - **Actions**: What is the customer doing?
  - **Thoughts**: What are they thinking?
  - **Emotions**: How are they feeling? (Frustration? Excitement? Confusion?)
  - **Pain Points**: Where are they struggling?
  - **Opportunities**: Where can we improve the experience?

**Step 3: Identify Moments of Truth**
- Critical touchpoints that disproportionately influence the customer's decision or loyalty.
- **First Moment of Truth**: First impression (website, ad, social post).
- **Second Moment of Truth**: Product/service experience.
- **Third Moment of Truth (Zero Moment of Truth)**: Post-experience sharing (reviews, referrals, social posts).

**Step 4: Map Content to Journey Stages**
| Journey Stage | Content Types | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Blog posts, social content, videos, infographics | Educate, inspire |
| Consideration | Comparison guides, case studies, webinars, whitepapers | Build trust, differentiate |
| Decision | Demos, free trials, pricing pages, testimonials, ROI calculators | Remove objections, convert |
| Onboarding | Welcome emails, tutorials, quick-start guides | Activate, deliver value fast |
| Usage | Tips, best practices, feature updates, community | Retain, deepen engagement |
| Advocacy | Referral programs, review requests, UGC campaigns | Amplify, grow organically |

**Step 5: Identify Gaps and Priorities**
- Where are customers dropping off? (Analytics data)
- Where is the experience inconsistent? (Customer feedback)
- Which touchpoints have the highest impact on conversion/retention?
- Prioritize improvements using ICE scoring.

## 8. Marketing Maturity Model

### Overview
- Assessment framework to evaluate how sophisticated a business's marketing operations are.
- Helps agencies set appropriate recommendations based on where the client actually is.
- Derived from CMMI (Capability Maturity Model Integration) principles adapted for marketing.

### The Five Stages

**Stage 1: Ad Hoc / Reactive**
- Characteristics: No documented strategy, reactive campaigns, inconsistent execution, no analytics.
- Marketing is driven by gut feeling and ad-hoc requests.
- No defined KPIs, no regular reporting, no marketing technology stack.
- Team: Usually 1 person or a business owner doing marketing part-time.
- Agency Recommendation: Start with foundational strategy (SOSTAC), implement basic analytics (GA4), establish brand guidelines, create a content calendar.

**Stage 2: Repeatable / Emerging**
- Characteristics: Some documented processes, basic analytics in place, occasional campaigns.
- Marketing activities are somewhat consistent but not data-driven.
- Basic CRM in place, email marketing started, social media presence established.
- Team: 1-3 people with some marketing skills.
- Agency Recommendation: Formalize strategy, implement marketing automation, establish KPI dashboard, create documented playbooks for recurring activities.

**Stage 3: Defined / Managed**
- Characteristics: Documented strategy, regular campaigns, consistent reporting, defined KPIs.
- Processes standardized across the organization.
- Marketing technology stack in place (CRM, email, analytics, social management).
- Team: Dedicated marketing team (3-8 people) with defined roles.
- Agency Recommendation: Optimize existing channels, implement advanced analytics (attribution modeling, cohort analysis), expand channel mix, A/B testing program.

**Stage 4: Measured / Quantitative**
- Characteristics: Data-driven decisions, multi-channel attribution, continuous optimization, advanced personalization.
- Marketing operations run with clear SLAs and predictable outcomes.
- Advanced martech stack with integration layer (CDP, data warehouse).
- Team: Full marketing department (8-20+) with specialists.
- Agency Recommendation: Implement growth loops, predictive analytics, dynamic personalization, incrementality testing, expand to advanced channels.

**Stage 5: Optimized / Continuous Improvement**
- Characteristics: AI-augmented operations, real-time optimization, test-and-learn culture embedded, marketing as a strategic growth engine.
- Full attribution model, marketing mix modeling (MMM), incrementality testing.
- Highly integrated tech stack with real-time data flows.
- Team: Large, cross-functional marketing organization with center of excellence.
- Agency Recommendation: Serve as strategic advisor, co-innovate on experimental channels, provide specialized expertise for advanced initiatives, act as innovation lab.

|---|---|---|
| Strategy & Planning | | |

| Technology & Tools | | |
| Content & Creative | | |
| Channel Execution | | |
| Team & Skills | | |

Score interpretation:
- 1.0-1.9: Ad Hoc (Stage 1)
- 2.0-2.4: Emerging (Stage 2)
- 2.5-3.4: Defined (Stage 3)

## 9. Budget Allocation Models

### The 70/20/10 Rule
Originally adapted from learning and development research; famously used by Coca-Cola as "NOW / NEXT / NEW."

**70% — Proven Performance (NOW)**
- Fund channels and tactics with clear benchmarks and predictable outcomes.
- Known audience behavior, tested creative, proven ROI.
- Examples: Paid search (brand + high-intent keywords), SEO (established strategy), email marketing (proven sequences), proven social ad formats.

**20% — Growth-Ready Opportunities (NEXT)**
- Fund channels showing promise but needing more investment to scale.
- Lower-risk opportunities that could potentially migrate into the core 70%.
- Examples: Short-form video strategy gaining traction, new audience segments showing positive signals, emerging platform with early results (e.g., Threads, Reddit ads).

**10% — Experimental Bets (NEW)**
- Fund truly novel experiments, new channels, creative formats, or approaches.
- Accept that most will fail; the few that succeed could become future 20% or 70%.
- Examples: AI-generated video campaigns, new platform launches, experimental partnership formats, guerrilla marketing tactics.

**Application Tips**:
- Rebalance monthly based on performance data, not annually based on assumption.
- When 20% experiments prove out, graduate them to the 70% bucket.
- Protect the 70%: never starve proven channels to fund unproven experiments.
- This framework has been shown to improve CAC stability by 25-30% across quarters.

### Budget Allocation by Business Stage

**Startup / Pre-Revenue ($0-$500K revenue)**
- Marketing budget: 15-25% of revenue (or allocated from funding).
- Focus: Product-market fit signals, initial traction channel discovery (Bullseye).
- Allocation:
  - 40% Content & SEO (long-term organic foundation)
  - 30% Paid acquisition testing (Meta, Google, LinkedIn)
  - 15% Community & partnerships (low-cost, high-trust)
  - 15% Brand & creative (website, positioning, basic assets)

**Growth Stage ($500K-$5M revenue)**
- Marketing budget: 10-20% of revenue.
- Focus: Scale proven channels, expand channel mix, build team.
- Allocation:
  - 35% Paid acquisition (scale winning channels)
  - 25% Content & SEO (compound organic growth)
  - 15% Email & automation (nurture, retention, upsell)
  - 15% Social media & community
  - 10% Experimental (new channels, partnerships)

**Scale Stage ($5M-$50M revenue)**
- Marketing budget: 8-15% of revenue.
- Focus: Optimize efficiency, diversify channels, build brand moat.
- Allocation:
  - 30% Paid acquisition (optimized, multi-channel)
  - 20% Content & SEO (authority building, topic leadership)
  - 15% Email & CRM (lifecycle marketing, personalization)
  - 15% Brand building (PR, events, thought leadership)
  - 10% Social & community
  - 10% Innovation & experiments

**Enterprise ($50M+ revenue)**
- Marketing budget: 5-10% of revenue.
- Focus: Brand dominance, market share defense, operational efficiency.
- Allocation:
  - 25% Paid acquisition (programmatic, search, social)
  - 20% Brand & PR (awareness, reputation, executive thought leadership)
  - 15% Content & SEO (comprehensive topic coverage)
  - 15% CRM & lifecycle (retention, expansion, loyalty)
  - 10% Events & partnerships
  - 10% Analytics, attribution, MarTech optimization
  - 5% Innovation lab / experimental

### Channel Mix Optimization Process
1. **Establish Baseline**: Track current spend, conversions, and CPA per channel.
2. **Calculate Channel ROI**: Revenue or value generated divided by total cost (media + management).
3. **Identify Diminishing Returns**: Plot CPA vs. spend volume per channel. Look for where CPA rises as spend increases.
4. **Reallocate at the Margins**: Shift budget from channels with rising CPA to channels with flat or declining CPA.
5. **Test & Learn**: Run controlled tests (geo-split, holdout groups) to validate channel impact.
6. **Review Quarterly**: Adjust allocation based on seasonality, competitive moves, and test results.

## 10. Framework Selection Guide

Use this decision tree to select the right framework for the task at hand.

| If the client needs... | Use this framework |
|---|---|
| Full lifecycle marketing plan | RACE (integrated with SOSTAC) |
| Audience-intent content strategy | See-Think-Do-Care |
| Channel discovery and prioritization | Bullseye Framework |
| Experiment prioritization | ICE Scoring |
| Product-led growth metrics | AARRR Pirate Metrics |
| Self-reinforcing growth strategy | Growth Loops |
| Understanding customer experience | Customer Journey Mapping |
| Assessing marketing sophistication | Marketing Maturity Model |
| Budget planning and allocation | 70/20/10 + Business Stage Model |
| Overall strategic marketing plan | SOSTAC (primary skill framework) |

### Combining Frameworks (Recommended Stack)
For a comprehensive agency engagement, layer frameworks as follows:

1. **Marketing Maturity Assessment** — Understand where the client is today.
2. **SOSTAC** — Build the strategic plan (Situation, Objectives, Strategy, Tactics, Action, Control).
3. **RACE** — Structure execution across the customer lifecycle.
4. **See-Think-Do-Care** — Map content and messaging to audience intent.
5. **Bullseye** — Prioritize channels through rapid testing.
6. **ICE** — Prioritize experiments and optimizations within each channel.
7. **AARRR** — Track metrics across the full customer lifecycle.
8. **70/20/10** — Allocate budget with structured risk management.
9. **Customer Journey Mapping** — Continuously improve the experience.
10. **Growth Loops** — Identify and engineer compounding growth mechanisms.

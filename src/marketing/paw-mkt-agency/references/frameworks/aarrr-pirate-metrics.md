# AARRR Pirate Metrics

## Overview
- Created by Dave McClure (500 Startups).
- Five-stage framework for measuring and optimizing the customer lifecycle.
- Acronym: Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, Referral.
- Maps directly to marketing activities and experiments.

## The Five Stages

**Acquisition**: How do users find us?
- Channels: SEO, SEM, social media, content, PR, partnerships, ads.
- Key Metrics: Traffic by source, cost per visit, channel conversion rate.
- Marketing Activities: Campaign launches, content creation, ad management, SEO optimization.

**Activation**: Do users have a great first experience?
- Definition: The "aha moment" — when a user first experiences core product value.
- Key Metrics: Sign-up rate, onboarding completion rate, time to value, activation rate.
- Marketing Activities: Onboarding email sequences, welcome tutorials, quick-start guides, UX copy.

**Retention**: Do users come back?
- Definition: Users return and continue engaging over time.
- Key Metrics: DAU/MAU ratio, cohort retention curves, churn rate, session frequency.
- Marketing Activities: Email re-engagement campaigns, push notifications, content updates, loyalty programs.

**Revenue**: How do we make money?
- Definition: Users convert to paying customers and generate revenue.
- Key Metrics: ARPU, LTV, conversion to paid rate, MRR/ARR, average order value.
- Marketing Activities: Pricing page optimization, upsell campaigns, checkout optimization, promotional offers.

**Referral**: Do users tell others?
- Definition: Users actively recommend the product.
- Key Metrics: Referral rate, viral coefficient (K-factor), NPS, referral revenue.
- Marketing Activities: Referral programs, UGC campaigns, review solicitation, affiliate programs.

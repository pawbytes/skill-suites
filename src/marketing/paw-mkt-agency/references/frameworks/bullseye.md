# Bullseye Framework for Channel Prioritization

## Overview
- Created by Gabriel Weinberg and Justin Mares (Traction book).
- Systematic approach to finding the channel(s) that will drive your growth.
- Tests 19 traction channels to find the "bullseye" — the one channel that works best right now.

## The 19 Traction Channels
1. Viral Marketing
2. Public Relations (PR)
3. Unconventional PR / Stunts
4. Search Engine Marketing (SEM / PPC)
5. Social and Display Ads
6. Offline Ads (TV, Radio, Print, Billboards)
7. Search Engine Optimization (SEO)
8. Content Marketing
9. Email Marketing
10. Engineering as Marketing (free tools, calculators, widgets)
11. Targeting Blogs / Niche Media
12. Business Development / Partnerships
13. Sales
14. Affiliate Programs
15. Existing Platforms (marketplaces, app stores, social platforms)
16. Trade Shows / Events
17. Offline Events / Meetups
18. Speaking Engagements
19. Community Building

## The Three-Ring Process

**Outer Ring: Brainstorm (All 19 Channels)**
- For each of the 19 channels, brainstorm at least one tactic.
- Do not filter or judge at this stage; capture all ideas.
- Include: What would a test look like? What is the expected outcome? How would you measure it?

**Middle Ring: Rank & Test (Top 6)**
- Rank all ideas by potential impact and feasibility.
- Select 6 channels for cheap, fast tests (1-2 weeks, minimal budget).
- Design a minimum viable test (MVT) for each:
  - Budget: $100-$500 per channel test
  - Timeline: 1-2 weeks
  - Success metric: Defined upfront (e.g., CPA below $X, conversion rate above Y%)

**Inner Ring: Focus (Top 1-3)**
- Analyze test results.
- Double down on the 1-3 channels showing the most promise.
- Invest significant resources (budget, team, time) into optimizing these channels.
- Revisit the outer ring every 3-6 months as the business evolves.

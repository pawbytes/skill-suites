# Customer Journey Mapping

## Purpose
- Visualize the complete customer experience from first touch to advocacy.
- Identify gaps, friction points, and opportunities at each stage.
- Align marketing, sales, and customer success around a shared understanding.

## Journey Mapping Process

**Step 1: Define Personas**
- Create 2-4 primary buyer personas based on research (not assumptions).
- Include: Demographics, psychographics, goals, pain points, information sources, objections.

**Step 2: Map Journey Stages**
- Typical stages: Awareness -> Consideration -> Decision -> Onboarding -> Usage -> Advocacy.
- For each stage, document:
  - **Touchpoints**: Where does the customer interact with us?
  - **Actions**: What is the customer doing?
  - **Thoughts**: What are they thinking?
  - **Emotions**: How are they feeling? (Frustration? Excitement? Confusion?)
  - **Pain Points**: Where are they struggling?
  - **Opportunities**: Where can we improve the experience?

**Step 3: Identify Moments of Truth**
- Critical touchpoints that disproportionately influence the customer's decision or loyalty.
- **First Moment of Truth**: First impression (website, ad, social post).
- **Second Moment of Truth**: Product/service experience.
- **Third Moment of Truth (Zero Moment of Truth)**: Post-experience sharing (reviews, referrals, social posts).

**Step 4: Map Content to Journey Stages**
| Journey Stage | Content Types | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Blog posts, social content, videos, infographics | Educate, inspire |
| Consideration | Comparison guides, case studies, webinars, whitepapers | Build trust, differentiate |
| Decision | Demos, free trials, pricing pages, testimonials, ROI calculators | Remove objections, convert |
| Onboarding | Welcome emails, tutorials, quick-start guides | Activate, deliver value fast |
| Usage | Tips, best practices, feature updates, community | Retain, deepen engagement |
| Advocacy | Referral programs, review requests, UGC campaigns | Amplify, grow organically |

**Step 5: Identify Gaps and Priorities**
- Where are customers dropping off? (Analytics data)
- Where is the experience inconsistent? (Customer feedback)
- Which touchpoints have the highest impact on conversion/retention?
- Prioritize improvements using ICE scoring.

# Budget Allocation Models

## The 70/20/10 Rule
Originally adapted from learning and development research; famously used by Coca-Cola as "NOW / NEXT / NEW."

**70% — Proven Performance (NOW)**
- Fund channels and tactics with clear benchmarks and predictable outcomes.
- Known audience behavior, tested creative, proven ROI.
- Examples: Paid search (brand + high-intent keywords), SEO (established strategy), email marketing (proven sequences), proven social ad formats.

**20% — Growth-Ready Opportunities (NEXT)**
- Fund channels showing promise but needing more investment to scale.
- Lower-risk opportunities that could potentially migrate into the core 70%.
- Examples: Short-form video strategy gaining traction, new audience segments showing positive signals, emerging platform with early results (e.g., Threads, Reddit ads).

**10% — Experimental Bets (NEW)**
- Fund truly novel experiments, new channels, creative formats, or approaches.
- Accept that most will fail; the few that succeed could become future 20% or 70%.
- Examples: AI-generated video campaigns, new platform launches, experimental partnership formats, guerrilla marketing tactics.

**Application Tips**:
- Rebalance monthly based on performance data, not annually based on assumption.
- When 20% experiments prove out, graduate them to the 70% bucket.
- Protect the 70%: never starve proven channels to fund unproven experiments.
- This framework has been shown to improve CAC stability by 25-30% across quarters.

## Budget Allocation by Business Stage

**Startup / Pre-Revenue ($0-$500K revenue)**
- Marketing budget: 15-25% of revenue (or allocated from funding).
- Focus: Product-market fit signals, initial traction channel discovery (Bullseye).
- Allocation:
  - 40% Content & SEO (long-term organic foundation)
  - 30% Paid acquisition testing (Meta, Google, LinkedIn)
  - 15% Community & partnerships (low-cost, high-trust)
  - 15% Brand & creative (website, positioning, basic assets)

**Growth Stage ($500K-$5M revenue)**
- Marketing budget: 10-20% of revenue.
- Focus: Scale proven channels, expand channel mix, build team.
- Allocation:
  - 35% Paid acquisition (scale winning channels)
  - 25% Content & SEO (compound organic growth)
  - 15% Email & automation (nurture, retention, upsell)
  - 15% Social media & community
  - 10% Experimental (new channels, partnerships)

**Scale Stage ($5M-$50M revenue)**
- Marketing budget: 8-15% of revenue.
- Focus: Optimize efficiency, diversify channels, build brand moat.
- Allocation:
  - 30% Paid acquisition (optimized, multi-channel)
  - 20% Content & SEO (authority building, topic leadership)
  - 15% Email & CRM (lifecycle marketing, personalization)
  - 15% Brand building (PR, events, thought leadership)
  - 10% Social & community
  - 10% Innovation & experiments

**Enterprise ($50M+ revenue)**
- Marketing budget: 5-10% of revenue.
- Focus: Brand dominance, market share defense, operational efficiency.
- Allocation:
  - 25% Paid acquisition (programmatic, search, social)
  - 20% Brand & PR (awareness, reputation, executive thought leadership)
  - 15% Content & SEO (comprehensive topic coverage)
  - 15% CRM & lifecycle (retention, expansion, loyalty)
  - 10% Events & partnerships
  - 10% Analytics, attribution, MarTech optimization
  - 5% Innovation lab / experimental

## Channel Mix Optimization Process
1. **Establish Baseline**: Track current spend, conversions, and CPA per channel.
2. **Calculate Channel ROI**: Revenue or value generated divided by total cost (media + management).
3. **Identify Diminishing Returns**: Plot CPA vs. spend volume per channel. Look for where CPA rises as spend increases.
4. **Reallocate at the Margins**: Shift budget from channels with rising CPA to channels with flat or declining CPA.
5. **Test & Learn**: Run controlled tests (geo-split, holdout groups) to validate channel impact.
6. **Review Quarterly**: Adjust allocation based on seasonality, competitive moves, and test results.

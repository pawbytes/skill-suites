# Guerrilla Marketing Playbooks

> Step-by-step playbooks for executing high-impact guerrilla marketing and growth hacking tactics. Each playbook includes prerequisites, timeline, execution steps, measurement, and common pitfalls.

---

## Playbook 1: Product Hunt Launch (30-Day Prep)

### Goal
Launch on Product Hunt, achieve Top 5 Product of the Day, and convert traffic into signups/revenue.

### Prerequisites
- Working product (or convincing demo/beta).
- Landing page with clear value proposition and signup/trial flow.
- Analytics and conversion tracking in place.
- Email list or supporter network of at least 100 people (300-500 preferred).

### Phase 1: Preparation (Day -30 to Day -14)

**Day -30 to -28: Positioning**
1. Write your one-sentence value proposition. Test it on 10 people outside your team. If they cannot repeat it back, simplify.
2. Define your Product Hunt tagline (max 60 characters). Format: "[Verb] [benefit] [for whom]".
3. Identify your primary category and topics on Product Hunt.
4. Research the top 20 launches in your category from the last 6 months. Note their taglines, descriptions, upvote counts, and comment patterns.

**Day -27 to -21: Asset Creation**
1. Create the Product Hunt gallery images (1270x760px recommended):
   - Image 1: Hero shot showing the product in action.
   - Image 2: Key features overview.
   - Image 3: Before/after or problem/solution.
   - Image 4: Social proof (logos, metrics, testimonials).
   - Image 5: Pricing or call to action.
2. Record a demo video (90-120 seconds):
   - First 10 seconds: the problem.
   - Next 60 seconds: the solution in action.
   - Final 20 seconds: results and call to action.
3. Write the Product Hunt description:
   - Paragraph 1: What it is and who it is for.
   - Paragraph 2: Key features and differentiators.
   - Paragraph 3: Why now / what is new.
   - Include a "Maker's comment" draft with your personal story.

**Day -21 to -14: Community Building**
1. Engage on Product Hunt daily: upvote, comment, and support other launches.
2. Connect with 10-20 active Product Hunt community members. Genuine engagement, not transactional.
3. Identify and reach out to 3-5 potential "hunters" (people with large followings on PH).
4. Begin teasing the launch on social media (X, LinkedIn): share behind-the-scenes content.
5. Start a "launch supporters" list via email or community (Slack, Discord).

### Phase 2: Pre-Launch (Day -14 to Day -1)

**Day -14 to -7:**
1. Finalize all Product Hunt page assets.
2. Send preview link to 10-20 trusted supporters for feedback.
3. Load-test your product and landing page. Launch day traffic can spike 10-100x.
4. Prepare response templates for common Product Hunt comments:
   - "What makes this different from [competitor]?"
   - "How does pricing work?"
   - "What is the roadmap?"
   - "Can it do [specific feature]?"
5. Set up a dedicated Slack/Discord channel for launch day coordination.

**Day -7 to -3:**
1. Send "launch is coming" email to your supporter list with the date and time.
2. Brief supporters on engagement guidelines:
   - "Engage authentically -- upvote if you genuinely like it, leave a real comment."
   - "Product Hunt detects and penalizes coordinated/fake engagement."
   - "Do NOT directly ask for upvotes. Share the page and let people decide."
3. Schedule social media posts for launch day (X, LinkedIn, Instagram, communities).

**Day -1:**
1. Final check: all assets uploaded, page reviewed, product stable.
2. Send "launching tomorrow" reminder to supporters with exact time.
3. Get sleep. Launch day is a marathon.

### Phase 3: Launch Day (Day 0)

**Timing**: Launch at 00:01 AM Pacific Time. The PH day resets at midnight PT.

**Hour 0-2 (Midnight - 2 AM PT):**
1. Publish the Product Hunt page.
2. Post your Maker's Comment immediately -- personal, authentic, tell the story.
3. Notify inner circle supporters via direct message/email.
4. Post on X/Twitter and LinkedIn with native content (no link in main post on LinkedIn; add link in first comment).

**Hour 2-6:**
1. Respond to every comment on Product Hunt within 30 minutes. Detailed, helpful responses.
2. Monitor social media mentions and engage.
3. Share progress updates with supporters ("We're #3 right now!").
4. Post in relevant communities (Slack, Discord, Reddit) where you have established presence.

**Hour 6-12:**
1. Continue responding to all comments.
2. Share a "mid-day update" on social media with a key insight or user story.
3. Engage with other launches happening the same day (goodwill + visibility).

**Hour 12-24:**
1. Maintain engagement on all comments.
2. Post a "final hours" update on social media.
3. Thank early supporters publicly.
4. Start collecting email addresses from PH visitors who hit your landing page.

### Phase 4: Post-Launch (Day +1 to +30)

1. **Day +1**: Publish a launch retrospective on your blog/social: what happened, metrics, lessons.
2. **Day +1-7**: Follow up with every person who commented on Product Hunt. Offer personal onboarding, a demo, or exclusive access.
3. **Day +3-7**: Repurpose launch content: LinkedIn post about the experience, X thread with lessons, blog post with data.
4. **Day +7-14**: Analyze conversion data. What percentage of PH visitors signed up? What was the cost per acquisition (if any paid promotion)?
5. **Day +14-30**: Convert launch momentum into sustained growth. Feature the Product Hunt badge on your site. Continue engaging with the PH community.

### Measurement
- Upvotes and ranking.
- Website traffic from Product Hunt (track via UTM: `?ref=producthunt`).
- Signups/trials from PH traffic.
- Revenue attributed to launch.
- Email subscribers captured.
- Social media follower growth.
- Backlinks earned (Product Hunt page itself is a high-DA backlink).

### Common Pitfalls
- Asking for upvotes directly (PH penalizes this).
- Launching on Monday or Friday (lower traffic).
- Not responding to comments (kills momentum).
- Treating PH as a one-day event instead of a campaign.
- No conversion funnel -- traffic arrives but has nowhere to go.
- Product instability on launch day.

## Playbook 2: Viral LinkedIn Post

### Goal
Create a LinkedIn post that achieves 50K+ impressions and drives meaningful engagement and followers.

### Prerequisites
- Active LinkedIn profile with at least 300 connections in your target audience.
- A genuine insight, story, or data point to share.
- Understanding of the 2026 LinkedIn algorithm (see best-practices.md section 3.3).

### Step 1: Choose Your Format (Pick One)

**Format A -- The "Hard Truth" Formula:**
```
Unpopular opinion:
[Industry belief] is overrated.

I have seen people with [X] fail because of [Y].

What actually works is:
- A
- B
- C

Learned this the hard way.
```

**Format B -- Mini-Story to Lesson:**
```
[Short personal anecdote -- 3-5 lines]
[The turning point or insight]
[The lesson, stated clearly]
[Question to the audience]
```

**Format C -- Data-Backed Contrarian Take:**
```
[Surprising statistic or data point]
[Why most people interpret this wrong]
[What the data actually means]
[Actionable takeaway]
[Invite discussion]
```

**Format D -- Document Carousel (Highest Dwell Time):**
- Slide 1: Bold hook statement or question.
- Slides 2-8: One key point per slide, large text, minimal design.
- Final slide: Summary + CTA (follow, comment, share).

### Step 2: Write the Hook (First 2 Lines)

The first 150 characters determine whether someone clicks "see more." Rules:
- Open with a specific number, surprising statement, or personal confession.
- Create a gap between what the reader expects and what you are about to say.
- Never start with "I'm excited to announce" or "I'm thrilled to share" (instant scroll-past).

**Good hooks**:
- "I lost $50K on a marketing campaign. Here's what it taught me."
- "We grew from 0 to 10,000 users without spending a dollar on ads."
- "95% of landing pages make this mistake. Is yours one of them?"

**Bad hooks**:
- "Great article on marketing trends!" (no value)
- "Thoughts?" (lazy engagement bait)
- "I'm excited to announce our new feature!" (nobody cares about your announcement)

### Step 3: Write the Body

- Short paragraphs (1-3 lines maximum).
- Use line breaks between every paragraph for mobile readability.
- Include specific numbers, names, and details (specificity = credibility).
- End with a genuine question that invites a range of answers.
- Do NOT include external links in the post body (60% reach penalty). Put links in the first comment.
- Length: 800-1,500 characters for text posts. 8-12 slides for carousels.

### Step 4: Optimize for Algorithm

- Post Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9 AM in your audience's primary timezone.
- Respond to every comment within the first 60 minutes. Each response counts as engagement.
- Ask follow-up questions in your replies to extend comment threads.
- Do not edit the post in the first 2 hours (some evidence editing resets distribution).
- Do not use more than 3 hashtags. Zero hashtags is fine.
- Do not tag more than 2-3 people (tagging many people looks like spam).

### Step 5: Amplify

- Share the post link with 5-10 colleagues/friends and ask them to leave genuine comments (not "great post" -- real thoughts).
- Repost a condensed version in relevant LinkedIn groups.
- Cross-post a modified version on X/Twitter (different format, same insight).
- If the post performs well, create a follow-up post within 48 hours that builds on the conversation.

### Measurement
- Impressions (target: 10K+ for a good post, 50K+ for viral).
- Comments (target: 50+ genuine comments).
- Profile views (should spike 5-10x after a viral post).
- Follower growth (target: 100-500 new followers per viral post).
- Inbound messages or connection requests from target audience.
- Click-throughs on comment link (if you placed a URL in comments).

## Playbook 3: Content Hijack / Newsjacking

### Goal
Insert your brand into a trending conversation to capture outsized attention with minimal production cost.

### Prerequisites
- Monitoring infrastructure: Google Alerts, X/Twitter lists, industry RSS feeds, social listening tool.
- Ability to create and publish content within 2-6 hours.
- Clear understanding of your brand's connection to trending topics.

### Step 1: Monitor (Always On)

Set up monitoring for:
- Industry keywords and competitor names (Google Alerts, Mention, Brandwatch).
- Trending topics on X, LinkedIn, Reddit, and Hacker News.
- Competitor launches, outages, price changes, and PR moments.
- Cultural events, award shows, viral moments with potential brand relevance.
- Government announcements, regulation changes, and economic data relevant to your industry.

### Step 2: Evaluate (5-Minute Decision)

When a trend appears, ask:
1. Does this connect naturally to our brand or expertise? (If forced, skip.)
2. Can we add genuine value to the conversation? (Data, insight, humor, a tool.)
3. Is the timing right? (Breaking = best. 24+ hours old = too late.)
4. Is there any reputational risk? (Tragedy, politics, sensitive topics = proceed with extreme caution or skip.)
5. Can we produce quality content within 2-4 hours? (If not, skip.)

**Decision matrix**: If 4+ answers are "yes," proceed. If fewer than 4, skip.

### Step 3: Create (2-4 Hours Maximum)

**Content formats for newsjacking (fastest to slowest)**:
1. **Social post with hot take** (30 minutes): Quick, opinionated, text-only.
2. **Data visualization or infographic** (1-2 hours): Pull relevant data, create a simple chart or graphic.
3. **Short blog post or article** (2-3 hours): 500-800 words, publish on your blog and cross-post.
4. **Tool or calculator** (3-6 hours): If the trend involves numbers, build a quick calculator.
5. **Video or audio reaction** (1-3 hours): Record a quick take, minimal editing.

**Content rules for newsjacking**:
- Lead with your insight, not your product.
- Reference the trend explicitly (use the trending hashtag or keyword).
- Add something the audience cannot get elsewhere: data, expertise, a contrarian take, a useful tool.
- Include a subtle but clear connection to your brand. The audience should discover your brand through the value you provide.

### Step 4: Distribute (Immediately After Creating)

1. Post on your primary social channel first (whichever has the most relevant audience).
2. Cross-post adapted versions on 2-3 other channels within 1 hour.
3. Share in relevant communities (Slack, Discord, Reddit, Facebook Groups) where you have existing credibility.
4. Email to relevant journalists or newsletter writers who are covering the trend.
5. If the content gains traction, boost with minimal paid spend ($50-$200) to accelerate.

### Step 5: Measure

- Social engagement: impressions, shares, comments.
- Website traffic spike correlated with publishing time.
- Brand mentions and sentiment during and after the trend.
- Leads or signups attributed to the content (via UTM tracking).
- Media pickups or journalist mentions.

## Playbook 4: Cold Outreach at Scale

### Goal
Generate qualified leads through personalized cold outreach at scale, achieving 5%+ reply rates.

### Prerequisites
- Clear ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) definition.
- Value proposition that solves a specific, urgent problem.
- Email infrastructure: warmed sending domains, proper SPF/DKIM/DMARC configuration.
- Data enrichment tools (Apollo, Clearbit, LinkedIn Sales Navigator).
- AI personalization capability (Claude, GPT-4, or dedicated AI SDR tool).

### Step 1: Build Your Prospect List

1. Define your ICP: company size, industry, role, technology, funding stage, geography.
2. Build a list of 500-2,000 prospects using LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Apollo, or similar.
3. Enrich each prospect with: company news, recent LinkedIn posts, tech stack, hiring activity, funding events.
4. Segment the list into 3-5 sub-segments based on likely pain points.

### Step 2: Craft Personalization Layers

**Layer 1 (Minimum)**: Company + role context.
**Layer 2 (Good)**: Recent company news or prospect's LinkedIn activity.
**Layer 3 (Great)**: Specific pain point inferred from job postings, reviews, or public statements.
**Layer 4 (Best)**: Mutual connections, shared experiences, or specific content the prospect created.

Use AI to generate personalized opening lines for each prospect:
```
Prompt: "Given the following prospect data [data], write a personalized
opening line that references something specific about their situation.
Do not use generic phrases like 'I noticed you work at.' Be specific
and genuine. Maximum 2 sentences."
```

**Human review**: Spot-check 10-20% of AI-generated personalization. AI hallucinates 3-8% of the time (references nonexistent roles, incorrect company details).

### Step 3: Write the Sequence

**Email 1 (Day 1) -- Value-First**:
```
Subject: [Specific, relevant question -- 4-7 words]

[Personalized opening line referencing their specific context]

[1-2 sentences connecting their situation to a problem you solve]

[Value offer: a resource, insight, or tool -- NOT a demo request]

[Soft CTA: "Would it be useful if I shared [resource]?"]
```

**Email 2 (Day 4) -- Follow-Up with Value**:
```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

[Reference to Email 1 briefly]

[New value: a case study, data point, or insight relevant to their specific situation]

[Slightly stronger CTA: "Would a 15-minute call to discuss [specific topic] be worth your time?"]
```

**Email 3 (Day 8) -- Social Proof**:
```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

[Brief reference to previous emails]

[Social proof: "We helped [similar company] achieve [specific result]"]

[Direct CTA: "I'd love to show you how. Any time work this week?"]
```

**Email 4 (Day 14) -- Breakup**:
```
Subject: Re: [original subject]

[Acknowledge you have not heard back -- no guilt]

[One final value statement]

[Respectful close: "If timing isn't right, no worries. I'll follow your work and reach out if something changes."]
```

### Step 4: Multi-Channel Touchpoints

Between emails, add LinkedIn touchpoints:
- Day 2: Send LinkedIn connection request with a personalized note (not a pitch).
- Day 6: Like or comment on their recent LinkedIn post with a genuine, substantive comment.
- Day 10: Share a relevant article or resource via LinkedIn DM (only if connected).

### Step 5: Optimize and Iterate

- Track open rates, reply rates, and positive reply rates per segment.
- A/B test subject lines (send 50 with version A, 50 with version B).
- A/B test opening lines, CTAs, and value offers.
- Drop segments or approaches with reply rates below 2% after 200+ sends.
- Double down on segments or approaches with reply rates above 5%.

### Benchmarks (2026)
- Generic cold email: <1% reply rate.
- Basic personalization (name + company): 2-4% reply rate.
- AI-personalized with 2+ layers: 4-12% reply rate.
- Hyper-personalized with 3+ layers: 15-42% reply rate.

## Playbook 5: Community Infiltration (Ethical, Value-First)

### Goal
Build genuine credibility in target communities so that your brand becomes the trusted recommendation when members need your solution.

### Prerequisites
- Genuine expertise in the community's domain.
- Patience (this is a 2-3 month minimum investment before any brand mentions).
- A person (founder, team member) willing to be the face of community engagement.

### Phase 1: Research and Join (Week 1-2)

1. Identify 3-5 communities where your target audience is most active:
   - Reddit subreddits (check r/[industry], r/[role], r/[tool-category]).
   - Facebook Groups (search for groups with 1K-50K members in your niche).
   - Slack/Discord communities (directories: slofile.com, top.gg, disboard.org).
   - Indie Hackers, Hacker News, Product Hunt Discussions.
   - Niche forums and Q&A sites (Quora, Stack Overflow, industry-specific).
2. Lurk for one week. Read rules, culture, common questions, and top contributors.
3. Identify the top 5 recurring questions or pain points in each community.
4. Note the 10 most active and respected members. Study their communication style.

### Phase 2: Contribute Value (Week 3-8)

1. Answer 3-5 questions per week in each community. Prioritize questions where you can provide genuine, detailed, helpful answers.
2. Share frameworks, templates, or tools freely. No gating, no email capture.
3. Start conversations by asking thoughtful questions (not promotional ones).
4. Engage with other members' posts: add to their answers, share relevant experiences.
5. **The 90/10 Rule**: 90% of your activity should be pure value with zero brand mention. 10% can be tangentially brand-related (mentioning your experience building the product, sharing data from your work).

**Content to share**:
- Detailed how-to answers with step-by-step instructions.
- Templates, checklists, and frameworks relevant to the community's needs.
- Data and insights from your own work (anonymized if needed).
- Honest opinions and recommendations (including competitors when they are the better fit).

**Content to avoid**:
- Links to your product in top-level posts.
- "Check out my new tool" posts (instant credibility loss).
- Promotional language or marketing speak.
- Generic one-line comments ("Great question!" "Thanks for sharing!").

### Phase 3: Establish Authority (Week 8-12)

1. By now, community members should recognize your name.
2. Begin creating original content for the community: guides, analyses, case studies.
3. When someone asks a question that your product solves, mention it naturally:
   - "We actually built [product] to solve exactly this. Happy to share more if helpful."
   - "I've been working on this problem and created [product] -- here's how it works: [brief explanation]."
4. Respond to DMs and questions about your product with genuine helpfulness (not a sales pitch).
5. Offer free access, beta invites, or extended trials to community members.

### Phase 4: Sustain (Ongoing)

1. Continue contributing value even after establishing credibility.
2. Share product updates as build-in-public updates, not announcements.
3. Ask for feedback from the community. Implement suggestions publicly.
4. Become a moderator or community leader if the opportunity arises.

### Measurement
- Karma / reputation score in the community.
- DMs or mentions requesting your product.
- Referral traffic from community platforms (via UTM tracking).
- Signups with community-attributed source.
- Qualitative: are community members recommending your product to others?

## Playbook 6: Competitive Takedown (Alternative & Comparison Content)

### Goal
Capture search traffic from people evaluating competitors and convert them to your product.

### Prerequisites
- Clear understanding of your competitive advantages and honest disadvantages.
- Keyword research showing search volume for "[Competitor] alternative" and "[Competitor] vs [Your Brand]" terms.
- Landing page capability.

### Step 1: Identify Target Competitors

1. List your top 5-10 competitors.
2. Check search volume for:
   - "[Competitor] alternative" / "[Competitor] alternatives"
   - "[Competitor] vs [anything]"
   - "switch from [Competitor]"
   - "[Competitor] pricing" / "[Competitor] review"
3. Prioritize competitors with the highest search volume and weakest existing comparison content.

### Step 2: Create Comparison Pages

**Page structure for "[Your Brand] vs [Competitor]"**:

```
H1: [Your Brand] vs [Competitor]: Honest Comparison [Year]

Section 1: Quick Summary
  - 3-4 sentence overview of both products.
  - "Best for" statement for each.

Section 2: Feature Comparison Table
  - Side-by-side table of key features.
  - Use checkmarks, x marks, and "partial" indicators.
  - Be honest about competitor strengths.

Section 3: Pricing Comparison
  - Side-by-side pricing tiers.
  - Total cost of ownership analysis.

Section 4: Pros and Cons
  - Honest pros and cons for both products.
  - Competitor pros should be real. Credibility is everything.

Section 5: Customer Reviews
  - Aggregate ratings from G2, Capterra, TrustRadius.
  - Selected quotes from real reviews.

Section 6: Who Should Choose What
  - "Choose [Your Brand] if you need..."
  - "Choose [Competitor] if you need..."
  - This section builds massive trust.

Section 7: Switching Guide
  - How to migrate from competitor.
  - Offer migration assistance.

CTA: Start a free trial / See a demo
```

**Page structure for "[Competitor] Alternatives"**:

```
H1: Top [N] [Competitor] Alternatives [Year]

Section 1: Why Look for Alternatives
  - Common pain points with the competitor.

Section 2: Quick Comparison Table
  - Table of all alternatives with key differentiators.
  - Your product listed but not always #1 (credibility).

Section 3: Detailed Review of Each Alternative
  - 200-400 words per alternative.
  - Your product gets the most detailed review but is positioned fairly.

Section 4: How to Choose
  - Decision framework based on use case.

CTA: Try [Your Brand] free
```

### Step 3: SEO Optimization

- Target exact-match keywords in H1 and title tag.
- URL structure: `/vs/[competitor]` or `/alternatives/[competitor]`.
- Add schema markup (FAQ schema, comparison schema).
- Build internal links from blog posts mentioning the competitor.
- Update pages quarterly as products change.

### Step 4: Promote

- Bid on competitor brand keywords in Google Ads, pointing to comparison pages.
- Share comparison content in communities where users discuss the competitor.
- Create social content summarizing the comparison.
- Pitch the comparison page to review sites and directories.

### Step 5: Maintain

- Update every quarter or when either product changes significantly.
- Monitor competitor pages that compare to you. Respond to inaccuracies.
- Track rankings for comparison keywords monthly.

## Playbook 7: Zero-Budget Launch (Waitlist + Beta + Referral)

### Goal
Launch a product with zero marketing budget by leveraging waitlists, beta programs, and referral mechanics.

### Phase 1: Waitlist (Pre-Product)

1. Create a landing page with:
   - Compelling headline describing the problem you solve.
   - Brief description of the solution (4-6 sentences).
   - Email capture form: "Join the waitlist for early access."
   - Social proof (if available): "Join 500+ others waiting."
2. Add a referral mechanic:
   - After signup: "Share your unique link to move up the list."
   - Show their position: "You are #247 in line."
   - Reward: "Refer 3 friends to get priority access."
3. Promote the waitlist page via:
   - Personal social media posts (X, LinkedIn).
   - Relevant communities where you have credibility.
   - Direct outreach to 50-100 target users.
   - Build-in-public updates about the product you are building.
4. Send weekly updates to the waitlist: development progress, sneak peeks, and surveys.

### Phase 2: Beta Program (Pre-Launch)

1. Invite the top 50-100 waitlist members (highest referrals, most engaged).
2. Create a private community (Slack, Discord) for beta testers.
3. Give beta testers a mission: "Help us make this great before we launch publicly."
4. Collect feedback aggressively. Implement visible changes based on feedback.
5. Ask beta testers to write reviews, testimonials, and case studies.
6. Ask: "Would you be willing to share this with others who might benefit?"

### Phase 3: Public Launch

1. Use the Product Hunt Playbook (Playbook 1) for launch day.
2. Invite the full waitlist with exclusive early-bird pricing or features.
3. Activate beta testers as launch advocates -- they have social proof and ownership.
4. Leverage all testimonials, reviews, and case studies collected during beta.
5. Implement a referral program from day one (see below).

### Referral Mechanics

| Mechanic | How It Works |
|---|---|
| Double-sided incentive | Both referrer and referred get a benefit (credit, extended trial, free month). |
| Milestone rewards | Refer 1 friend = X. Refer 5 = Y. Refer 10 = Z. Escalating rewards drive volume. |
| Leaderboard | Public ranking of top referrers. Competition drives engagement. |
| Social sharing | Pre-written messages + one-click share to X, LinkedIn, email. |
| In-product prompts | Show referral CTA after moments of delight (completed task, achieved result). |

## Playbook 8: Partnership Hacks (Co-Marketing, Cross-Promotion, Integration Directories)

### Goal
Leverage partner audiences and ecosystems to grow without paid acquisition.

### Tactic A: Newsletter Swaps

1. Identify 10-20 newsletters with audiences that overlap yours but are not competitive.
2. Pitch a swap: "I'll mention your newsletter to my [X] subscribers, you mention mine to yours."
3. Write a dedicated paragraph (not just a one-line mention) recommending the partner newsletter.
4. Track signups via unique UTM links.
5. Start with smaller newsletters (1K-10K subscribers) to build a track record, then approach larger ones.

### Tactic B: Co-Created Content

1. Partner with a complementary brand to co-create a resource:
   - Joint webinar (each brand brings their audience).
   - Co-authored ebook or guide (split the writing and the leads).
   - Joint research report (each brand contributes data).
   - Co-branded template or tool (each brand adds their expertise).
2. Both brands promote through their channels.
3. Gate the resource with a shared lead capture form (leads go to both partners).
4. Follow up with leads independently.

### Tactic C: Integration Directories

1. Build integrations with tools your audience already uses (Slack, Zapier, HubSpot, Salesforce, etc.).
2. List your product in partner marketplaces and directories:
   - Zapier App Directory.
   - Slack App Directory.
   - HubSpot App Marketplace.
   - Salesforce AppExchange.
   - Chrome Web Store.
   - VS Code Marketplace (for developer tools).
3. Optimize your listing:
   - Keyword-rich title and description.
   - Screenshots and demo video.
   - Reviews and ratings (ask happy users to review).
4. Each listing is a permanent, free acquisition channel with high-intent traffic.

### Tactic D: Cross-Promotion Bundles

1. Partner with 3-5 complementary products to create a bundle:
   - "The [Category] Starter Pack: [Product A] + [Product B] + [Product C] for [discounted price]."
2. Each product promotes the bundle to their audience.
3. The combined audience reach is 3-5x any individual product.
4. AppSumo, StackSocial, and similar platforms facilitate bundle deals.
5. Use bundles for launch events, holiday promotions, or industry conferences.

### Tactic E: Affiliate Micro-Networks

1. Recruit 10-20 micro-influencers or niche bloggers with small but engaged audiences (1K-10K followers).
2. Offer generous commissions: 20-40% recurring for SaaS, $50-$200 per referral for services.
3. Provide each affiliate with:
   - Unique tracking links.
   - Pre-written content (social posts, email copy, review templates).
   - Product access and training so they can speak authentically.
4. 20 affiliates x 2,000 followers each = 40,000 reach for zero upfront cost.
5. Pay only for results: no risk, pure performance.

### Measurement for All Partnership Tactics

| Metric | Tracking Method |
|---|---|
| Referral traffic | UTM parameters per partner |
| Signups from partners | Unique landing pages or promo codes per partner |
| Revenue attributed | Referral/affiliate tracking links |
| Cost per acquisition | Commission/cost / signups |
| Partner satisfaction | Quarterly check-in and feedback |
| Pipeline from partnerships | CRM attribution |

## General Playbook Principles

### Before Every Playbook

1. Read brand context and SOSTAC plan. Ground tactics in strategy.
2. Check risk tolerance. Conservative brands use conservative plays.
3. Set clear success metrics before starting.
4. Assign a single owner for execution.
5. Define a kill criteria: at what point do you stop if results are not materializing?

### After Every Playbook

1. Document results in `./.pawbytes/marketing-suites/brands/{brand-slug}/operations/guerrilla/performance/` (or `campaigns/{type}-{slug}/guerrilla/performance/` if campaign-tied).
2. Calculate ROI: (revenue or value generated - cost) / cost.
3. Identify what to repeat, what to iterate, and what to abandon.

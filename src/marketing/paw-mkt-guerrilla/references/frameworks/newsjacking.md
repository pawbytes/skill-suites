# Content Hijack / Newsjacking

### Goal
Insert your brand into a trending conversation to capture outsized attention with minimal production cost.

### Prerequisites
- Monitoring infrastructure: Google Alerts, X/Twitter lists, industry RSS feeds, social listening tool.
- Ability to create and publish content within 2-6 hours.
- Clear understanding of your brand's connection to trending topics.

### Step 1: Monitor (Always On)

Set up monitoring for:
- Industry keywords and competitor names (Google Alerts, Mention, Brandwatch).
- Trending topics on X, LinkedIn, Reddit, and Hacker News.
- Competitor launches, outages, price changes, and PR moments.
- Cultural events, award shows, viral moments with potential brand relevance.
- Government announcements, regulation changes, and economic data relevant to your industry.

### Step 2: Evaluate (5-Minute Decision)

When a trend appears, ask:
1. Does this connect naturally to our brand or expertise? (If forced, skip.)
2. Can we add genuine value to the conversation? (Data, insight, humor, a tool.)
3. Is the timing right? (Breaking = best. 24+ hours old = too late.)
4. Is there any reputational risk? (Tragedy, politics, sensitive topics = proceed with extreme caution or skip.)
5. Can we produce quality content within 2-4 hours? (If not, skip.)

**Decision matrix**: If 4+ answers are "yes," proceed. If fewer than 4, skip.

### Step 3: Create (2-4 Hours Maximum)

**Content formats for newsjacking (fastest to slowest)**:
1. **Social post with hot take** (30 minutes): Quick, opinionated, text-only.
2. **Data visualization or infographic** (1-2 hours): Pull relevant data, create a simple chart or graphic.
3. **Short blog post or article** (2-3 hours): 500-800 words, publish on your blog and cross-post.
4. **Tool or calculator** (3-6 hours): If the trend involves numbers, build a quick calculator.
5. **Video or audio reaction** (1-3 hours): Record a quick take, minimal editing.

**Content rules for newsjacking**:
- Lead with your insight, not your product.
- Reference the trend explicitly (use the trending hashtag or keyword).
- Add something the audience cannot get elsewhere: data, expertise, a contrarian take, a useful tool.
- Include a subtle but clear connection to your brand. The audience should discover your brand through the value you provide.

### Step 4: Distribute (Immediately After Creating)

1. Post on your primary social channel first (whichever has the most relevant audience).
2. Cross-post adapted versions on 2-3 other channels within 1 hour.
3. Share in relevant communities (Slack, Discord, Reddit, Facebook Groups) where you have existing credibility.
4. Email to relevant journalists or newsletter writers who are covering the trend.
5. If the content gains traction, boost with minimal paid spend ($50-$200) to accelerate.

### Step 5: Measure

- Social engagement: impressions, shares, comments.
- Website traffic spike correlated with publishing time.
- Brand mentions and sentiment during and after the trend.
- Leads or signups attributed to the content (via UTM tracking).
- Media pickups or journalist mentions.

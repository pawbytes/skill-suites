# Community Infiltration (Ethical, Value-First)

### Goal
Build genuine credibility in target communities so that your brand becomes the trusted recommendation when members need your solution.

### Prerequisites
- Genuine expertise in the community's domain.
- Patience (this is a 2-3 month minimum investment before any brand mentions).
- A person (founder, team member) willing to be the face of community engagement.

### Phase 1: Research and Join (Week 1-2)

1. Identify 3-5 communities where your target audience is most active:
   - Reddit subreddits (check r/[industry], r/[role], r/[tool-category]).
   - Facebook Groups (search for groups with 1K-50K members in your niche).
   - Slack/Discord communities (directories: slofile.com, top.gg, disboard.org).
   - Indie Hackers, Hacker News, Product Hunt Discussions.
   - Niche forums and Q&A sites (Quora, Stack Overflow, industry-specific).
2. Lurk for one week. Read rules, culture, common questions, and top contributors.
3. Identify the top 5 recurring questions or pain points in each community.
4. Note the 10 most active and respected members. Study their communication style.

### Phase 2: Contribute Value (Week 3-8)

1. Answer 3-5 questions per week in each community. Prioritize questions where you can provide genuine, detailed, helpful answers.
2. Share frameworks, templates, or tools freely. No gating, no email capture.
3. Start conversations by asking thoughtful questions (not promotional ones).
4. Engage with other members' posts: add to their answers, share relevant experiences.
5. **The 90/10 Rule**: 90% of your activity should be pure value with zero brand mention. 10% can be tangentially brand-related (mentioning your experience building the product, sharing data from your work).

**Content to share**:
- Detailed how-to answers with step-by-step instructions.
- Templates, checklists, and frameworks relevant to the community's needs.
- Data and insights from your own work (anonymized if needed).
- Honest opinions and recommendations (including competitors when they are the better fit).

**Content to avoid**:
- Links to your product in top-level posts.
- "Check out my new tool" posts (instant credibility loss).
- Promotional language or marketing speak.
- Generic one-line comments ("Great question!" "Thanks for sharing!").

### Phase 3: Establish Authority (Week 8-12)

1. By now, community members should recognize your name.
2. Begin creating original content for the community: guides, analyses, case studies.
3. When someone asks a question that your product solves, mention it naturally:
   - "We actually built [product] to solve exactly this. Happy to share more if helpful."
   - "I've been working on this problem and created [product] -- here's how it works: [brief explanation]."
4. Respond to DMs and questions about your product with genuine helpfulness (not a sales pitch).
5. Offer free access, beta invites, or extended trials to community members.

### Phase 4: Sustain (Ongoing)

1. Continue contributing value even after establishing credibility.
2. Share product updates as build-in-public updates, not announcements.
3. Ask for feedback from the community. Implement suggestions publicly.
4. Become a moderator or community leader if the opportunity arises.

### Measurement
- Karma / reputation score in the community.
- DMs or mentions requesting your product.
- Referral traffic from community platforms (via UTM tracking).
- Signups with community-attributed source.
- Qualitative: are community members recommending your product to others?

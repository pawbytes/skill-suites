# Competitive Takedown (Alternative & Comparison Content)

### Goal
Capture search traffic from people evaluating competitors and convert them to your product.

### Prerequisites
- Clear understanding of your competitive advantages and honest disadvantages.
- Keyword research showing search volume for "[Competitor] alternative" and "[Competitor] vs [Your Brand]" terms.
- Landing page capability.

### Step 1: Identify Target Competitors

1. List your top 5-10 competitors.
2. Check search volume for:
   - "[Competitor] alternative" / "[Competitor] alternatives"
   - "[Competitor] vs [anything]"
   - "switch from [Competitor]"
   - "[Competitor] pricing" / "[Competitor] review"
3. Prioritize competitors with the highest search volume and weakest existing comparison content.

### Step 2: Create Comparison Pages

**Page structure for "[Your Brand] vs [Competitor]"**:

```
H1: [Your Brand] vs [Competitor]: Honest Comparison [Year]

Section 1: Quick Summary
  - 3-4 sentence overview of both products.
  - "Best for" statement for each.

Section 2: Feature Comparison Table
  - Side-by-side table of key features.
  - Use checkmarks, x marks, and "partial" indicators.
  - Be honest about competitor strengths.

Section 3: Pricing Comparison
  - Side-by-side pricing tiers.
  - Total cost of ownership analysis.

Section 4: Pros and Cons
  - Honest pros and cons for both products.
  - Competitor pros should be real. Credibility is everything.

Section 5: Customer Reviews
  - Aggregate ratings from G2, Capterra, TrustRadius.
  - Selected quotes from real reviews.

Section 6: Who Should Choose What
  - "Choose [Your Brand] if you need..."
  - "Choose [Competitor] if you need..."
  - This section builds massive trust.

Section 7: Switching Guide
  - How to migrate from competitor.
  - Offer migration assistance.

CTA: Start a free trial / See a demo
```

**Page structure for "[Competitor] Alternatives"**:

```
H1: Top [N] [Competitor] Alternatives [Year]

Section 1: Why Look for Alternatives
  - Common pain points with the competitor.

Section 2: Quick Comparison Table
  - Table of all alternatives with key differentiators.
  - Your product listed but not always #1 (credibility).

Section 3: Detailed Review of Each Alternative
  - 200-400 words per alternative.
  - Your product gets the most detailed review but is positioned fairly.

Section 4: How to Choose
  - Decision framework based on use case.

CTA: Try [Your Brand] free
```

### Step 3: SEO Optimization

- Target exact-match keywords in H1 and title tag.
- URL structure: `/vs/[competitor]` or `/alternatives/[competitor]`.
- Add schema markup (FAQ schema, comparison schema).
- Build internal links from blog posts mentioning the competitor.
- Update pages quarterly as products change.

### Step 4: Promote

- Bid on competitor brand keywords in Google Ads, pointing to comparison pages.
- Share comparison content in communities where users discuss the competitor.
- Create social content summarizing the comparison.
- Pitch the comparison page to review sites and directories.

### Step 5: Maintain

- Update every quarter or when either product changes significantly.
- Monitor competitor pages that compare to you. Respond to inaccuracies.
- Track rankings for comparison keywords monthly.

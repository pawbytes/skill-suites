# Viral LinkedIn Post

### Goal
Create a LinkedIn post that achieves 50K+ impressions and drives meaningful engagement and followers.

### Prerequisites
- Active LinkedIn profile with at least 300 connections in your target audience.
- A genuine insight, story, or data point to share.
- Understanding of the 2026 LinkedIn algorithm (see best-practices.md section 3.3).

### Step 1: Choose Your Format (Pick One)

**Format A -- The "Hard Truth" Formula:**
```
Unpopular opinion:
[Industry belief] is overrated.

I have seen people with [X] fail because of [Y].

What actually works is:
- A
- B
- C

Learned this the hard way.
```

**Format B -- Mini-Story to Lesson:**
```
[Short personal anecdote -- 3-5 lines]
[The turning point or insight]
[The lesson, stated clearly]
[Question to the audience]
```

**Format C -- Data-Backed Contrarian Take:**
```
[Surprising statistic or data point]
[Why most people interpret this wrong]
[What the data actually means]
[Actionable takeaway]
[Invite discussion]
```

**Format D -- Document Carousel (Highest Dwell Time):**
- Slide 1: Bold hook statement or question.
- Slides 2-8: One key point per slide, large text, minimal design.
- Final slide: Summary + CTA (follow, comment, share).

### Step 2: Write the Hook (First 2 Lines)

The first 150 characters determine whether someone clicks "see more." Rules:
- Open with a specific number, surprising statement, or personal confession.
- Create a gap between what the reader expects and what you are about to say.
- Never start with "I'm excited to announce" or "I'm thrilled to share" (instant scroll-past).

**Good hooks**:
- "I lost $50K on a marketing campaign. Here's what it taught me."
- "We grew from 0 to 10,000 users without spending a dollar on ads."
- "95% of landing pages make this mistake. Is yours one of them?"

**Bad hooks**:
- "Great article on marketing trends!" (no value)
- "Thoughts?" (lazy engagement bait)
- "I'm excited to announce our new feature!" (nobody cares about your announcement)

### Step 3: Write the Body

- Short paragraphs (1-3 lines maximum).
- Use line breaks between every paragraph for mobile readability.
- Include specific numbers, names, and details (specificity = credibility).
- End with a genuine question that invites a range of answers.
- Do NOT include external links in the post body (60% reach penalty). Put links in the first comment.
- Length: 800-1,500 characters for text posts. 8-12 slides for carousels.

### Step 4: Optimize for Algorithm

- Post Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9 AM in your audience's primary timezone.
- Respond to every comment within the first 60 minutes. Each response counts as engagement.
- Ask follow-up questions in your replies to extend comment threads.
- Do not edit the post in the first 2 hours (some evidence editing resets distribution).
- Do not use more than 3 hashtags. Zero hashtags is fine.
- Do not tag more than 2-3 people (tagging many people looks like spam).

### Step 5: Amplify

- Share the post link with 5-10 colleagues/friends and ask them to leave genuine comments (not "great post" -- real thoughts).
- Repost a condensed version in relevant LinkedIn groups.
- Cross-post a modified version on X/Twitter (different format, same insight).
- If the post performs well, create a follow-up post within 48 hours that builds on the conversation.

### Measurement
- Impressions (target: 10K+ for a good post, 50K+ for viral).
- Comments (target: 50+ genuine comments).
- Profile views (should spike 5-10x after a viral post).
- Follower growth (target: 100-500 new followers per viral post).
- Inbound messages or connection requests from target audience.
- Click-throughs on comment link (if you placed a URL in comments).

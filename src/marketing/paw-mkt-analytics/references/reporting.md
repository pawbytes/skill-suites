# Reporting

Create reports that lead with insight, not data. Every data point answers "So what?" and "Now what?"

## Report Types and Cadence

| Report | Frequency | Length | Audience |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daily monitor | Daily | 5 min check | Marketing team |
| Weekly snapshot | Weekly | 1-2 pages | Marketing lead |
| Monthly deep-dive | Monthly | 5-10 pages | Leadership |
| Quarterly review | Quarterly | 10-15 pages | Executive |
| Annual planning | Annually | 15-20 pages | C-suite, board |

## Report Structure

Every report follows:
1. **Executive Summary** — 3-5 bullets: what happened, so what, now what
2. **KPI Scorecard** — metric, target, actual, vs-target %, trend
3. **Key Insights** — 3-5 findings with evidence
4. **Channel Performance** — per-channel highlights
5. **What Worked and What Did Not**
6. **Recommendations** — specific, prioritized actions
7. **Appendix**

## Storytelling with Data

Lead with insight, not data. "Organic traffic grew 23% because our pillar content strategy is working" beats "Sessions: 45,231."

Use comparisons: vs target, vs prior period, vs benchmark. Annotate trend lines with actions taken.

## Actionable Insights Format

For every insight:
- **FINDING** — what data shows
- **CONTEXT** — comparison to benchmark or target
- **CAUSE** — root cause or hypothesis
- **ACTION** — specific recommendation with owner and deadline
- **IMPACT** — expected outcome

Save to `./.pawbytes/marketing-suites/brands/{brand-slug}/analytics/reports/`

**Templates:**
- `weekly-snapshot-{YYYY-MM-DD}.md`
- `monthly-report-{YYYY-MM}.md`

**Report Template Structure:**
- Period
- Executive Summary

- Key Insights (using FINDING/CONTEXT/CAUSE/ACTION/IMPACT format)

**Purpose:** Content strategy, production, distribution, and measurement reference.

## 1. Content Strategy

### Hub-and-Spoke (Topic Cluster) Model
- **Hub (Pillar page)**: Comprehensive guide covering a broad topic (2,000-4,000 words). Example: "Complete Guide to Email Marketing"
- **Spokes (Cluster pages)**: Specific subtopic articles (1,000-2,000 words each) that link back to the hub. Example: "How to Write Welcome Emails", "Email Deliverability Guide"
- **Internal linking**: Every spoke links to the hub. Hub links to all spokes. Spokes cross-link where relevant.
- **Why it works**: Builds topical authority (Google rewards comprehensive topic coverage), creates clear site architecture, improves internal linking

### Content Pillars Methodology
Choose 3-5 core content pillars aligned with:
1. What your audience needs to learn
2. What your product/service solves
3. What you have genuine expertise in

**Example for a project management SaaS:**
- Pillar 1: Team productivity and collaboration
- Pillar 2: Project planning and execution
- Pillar 3: Remote work management
- Pillar 4: Agile and Scrum methodology

### Content Prioritization (ICE Framework)
Score each content idea 1-10 on:
- **Impact**: How much traffic/conversions/authority will this drive?
- **Confidence**: How sure are we this will work? (Data backing, competitor validation)
- **Ease**: How hard is it to create? (Resources, expertise, time)

ICE Score = (I + C + E) / 3. Prioritize highest scores.

### Content Gap Analysis
1. List your top 5 competitors
2. Pull their top-performing content (Ahrefs Content Gap, SEMrush)
3. Identify keywords they rank for that you don't
4. Identify topics they cover that you haven't
5. Prioritize gaps by search volume and business relevance

## 3. AI Content Production (2025)

### AI-Assisted Content Workflow
```
1. Research (AI + human)
   → AI: Keyword research, competitor content analysis, outline suggestions
   → Human: Strategic direction, unique angle, experience to add

2. Outline (AI draft → human refine)
   → AI: Generate structured outline from keyword and intent analysis
   → Human: Restructure, add unique sections, ensure differentiation

3. First Draft (AI)
   → AI: Generate draft following outline, maintaining consistent voice
   → Human: Review for accuracy, add examples, inject expertise

4. Human Enhancement (Critical)
   → Add: Original data, personal experience, expert quotes, proprietary insights
   → Add: Real screenshots, custom graphics, original examples
   → Remove: Generic statements, filler, anything that sounds templated
   → Verify: Every factual claim, statistic, and recommendation

5. Edit and Publish
   → Human: Final edit for voice, flow, and quality
   → SEO: Optimize title, meta description, headings, internal links
```

### E-E-A-T Compliance with AI Content
- **Author attribution**: Every AI-assisted piece should have a named human author who reviewed and enhanced it
- **Experience signals**: Add first-hand experience that AI cannot provide — real examples, screenshots, personal anecdotes
- **Expert review**: For YMYL topics, have a subject matter expert review and sign off
- **Disclosure**: Consider a disclosure like "Researched with AI assistance, reviewed by [Expert Name]" — not required but builds trust
- **Quality test**: Would a reader know this was AI-assisted? If yes, it needs more human enhancement.

### When AI Hurts Content Quality
- Generic, surface-level articles on competitive topics (you're competing against human expertise)
- YMYL (Your Money Your Life) content without expert verification
- Content requiring original research, interviews, or proprietary data
- Brand voice content that requires deep understanding of company culture
- Any content published without human review and enhancement

## 4. Content Distribution

### The 80/20 Rule
Spend 20% of time creating content, 80% distributing it. Most content fails not because it's bad but because no one sees it.

### Distribution Channels Playbook
| Channel | How to Use | When |
|---------|-----------|------|
| Email newsletter | Send to subscribers, segment by interest | Every piece of content |
| LinkedIn | Publish native post (not just link), share key insights | B2B, thought leadership |
| X/Twitter | Thread summarizing key points, link in final tweet | Tech, media, marketing audiences |
| Reddit | Share in relevant subreddits where you're an active member | When genuinely helpful to the community |
| Facebook Groups | Share in relevant groups with context, not just link drops | Niche communities |
| Hacker News | Submit original research, tools, contrarian takes | Tech, startup audiences |
| Product Hunt | Free tools, calculators, templates | Product launches |
| Syndication | Republish on Medium, LinkedIn Articles (canonical back to original) | Expanding reach |
| Paid promotion | Boost top-performing content on Meta/LinkedIn | High-value pieces, lead magnets |
| Email outreach | Notify people mentioned or quoted in the content | Research, roundups, mentions |
| Internal | Share with sales team, customer success, partners | Every piece of content |

### Content Repurposing Matrix
One long-form piece → 10+ derivative pieces:
```
Blog Post (2,000 words)
  → LinkedIn post (key insight, 200-300 words)
  → X/Twitter thread (5-10 tweets)
  → Email newsletter section
  → Instagram carousel (8-10 slides)
  → TikTok/Reels video (60-90 seconds, key takeaways)
  → YouTube video (5-10 min deep-dive)
  → Podcast talking point
  → Infographic (data and framework visualization)
  → SlideShare/PDF download
  → Quora/Reddit answer (linking to full post)
  → Guest post angle (different angle, same research)
```

## 5. Content Measurement

### Leading vs Lagging Metrics
| Leading (Track Weekly) | Lagging (Track Monthly/Quarterly) |
|-----------------------|----------------------------------|
| Organic impressions (Search Console) | Organic traffic (GA4) |
| Keyword rankings (position tracking) | Revenue attributed to content |
| Backlinks acquired | Leads generated from content |
| Social shares and engagement | Email subscribers from content |
| Time on page, scroll depth | Content-influenced pipeline |
| Email open/click rates | Brand search volume growth |

### Content ROI Framework
```
Content ROI = (Revenue from Content - Cost of Content) / Cost of Content × 100

Revenue from Content:
  - Direct: Last-touch attribution to content pages
  - Assisted: Content appears in conversion path (GA4 conversion paths)
  - Influenced: Pipeline where contact engaged with content (CRM tracking)

Cost of Content:
  - Writer time/cost (salary or freelancer rate)
  - Editor time
  - Design (graphics, images)
  - Distribution cost (ads, tools)
  - Tool subscriptions (SEO, CMS, analytics)
```

### Content Performance Scoring
Score content monthly:
- **Traffic score**: Organic sessions vs target
- **Engagement score**: Time on page, scroll depth, bounce rate
- **Conversion score**: Leads or signups attributed
- **Link score**: Referring domains acquired
- **Social score**: Shares, comments, saves

Flag content scoring below threshold for update, merge, or pruning.

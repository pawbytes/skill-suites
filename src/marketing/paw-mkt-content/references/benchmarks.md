**Purpose:** Data-driven benchmarks for content performance, costs, production, and channel effectiveness. Use these to set realistic targets, evaluate performance, and justify content investments.

| < 500 | 50-150 | 0-2 | News updates, product announcements |

### Optimal Length by Content Type
| Content Type | Recommended Length | Reason |
|-------------|-------------------|--------|
| How-to tutorial | 1,500-2,500 words | Enough depth for step-by-step without overwhelming |
| Listicle ("Best X") | 2,000-3,500 words | Thoroughness wins; more items = more keywords |
| Comparison ("X vs Y") | 1,500-2,500 words | Covers both sides with clear recommendation |
| Ultimate guide | 3,000-5,000 words | Comprehensive enough to be the definitive resource |
| Case study | 1,200-2,000 words | Focused on results; concise storytelling |
| Original research | 2,000-4,000 words | Data presentation plus analysis and takeaways |
| Glossary / definition | 500-1,000 words | Answer quickly; depth is not expected |
| Product update | 300-800 words | Brief and functional; announcement format |
| Opinion / thought leadership | 800-1,500 words | Sharp perspective does not need padding |

## 6. Gated vs Ungated Content Performance

### Gated Content Benchmarks

| Metric | Average | Good | Excellent |
|--------|---------|------|-----------|
| Landing page conversion rate | 10-20% | 20-35% | 35-50% |
| Cost per lead (organic) | $15-50 | $5-15 | < $5 |
| Cost per lead (paid traffic) | $30-100 | $15-30 | < $15 |
| Lead-to-MQL rate | 5-15% | 15-25% | 25%+ |
| Download-to-read rate | 30-50% | 50-70% | 70%+ |

### When to Gate vs Ungate

| Gate This | Ungate This |
|-----------|-------------|
| Ebooks and whitepapers (high effort, unique value) | Blog posts and articles (SEO value) |
| Templates and toolkits (high utility) | Infographics (shareability matters) |
| Original research reports (exclusive data) | Case study summaries (sales enablement) |
| Webinar recordings (event scarcity) | Product comparisons (buyer intent, needs SEO) |
| Multi-part email courses (sequential value) | How-to guides (builds trust, drives organic) |

### Hybrid Approach (Recommended)
- Ungate the first 30-50% of valuable content
- Gate the full version, template, or advanced section
- Example: "Read the top 5 strategies here. Download all 25 strategies with templates."
- Typical conversion lift: 20-40% higher than full-gate, with 60-80% of the SEO value of ungated

## 8. AI-Generated vs Human Content Performance

### Head-to-Head Performance Comparison (2025 Data)

| Metric | AI-Only Content | Human-Only Content | AI + Human (Best Practice) |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Avg organic traffic (month 6) | 60-80% of human baseline | Baseline (100%) | 110-130% of baseline |
| Time on page | 15-25% lower | Baseline | Comparable to baseline |
| Bounce rate | 5-15% higher | Baseline | Comparable to baseline |
| Backlinks acquired | 40-60% fewer | Baseline | 90-120% of baseline |
| Social shares | 30-50% fewer | Baseline | Comparable to baseline |
| Production speed | 3-5x faster | Baseline | 2-3x faster |
| Production cost | 50-70% lower | Baseline | 30-50% lower |
| E-E-A-T signals | Weak | Strong | Strong |
| Factual accuracy (unedited) | 85-92% | 95-99% | 97-99% |

### Key Findings (2025-2026)
- AI-only content can rank for low-competition keywords but struggles for competitive terms
- Google has not penalized AI content explicitly, but AI content that lacks E-E-A-T signals underperforms
- The largest performance gap is in backlink acquisition: editors and journalists link to original thinking, not AI summaries
- AI + human collaboration produces the best ROI when AI handles research, structure, and first drafts while humans add expertise, original data, and editorial polish
- Content detection tools are unreliable (60-75% accuracy) but reader perception of quality matters more than detection

### Where AI Content Performs Best
- High-volume, low-competition informational queries
- Product descriptions and feature pages at scale
- Social media post variations and repurposing
- Email subject line and CTA testing variants
- Internal documentation and knowledge base articles
- Meta descriptions and title tag optimization

### Where Human Content is Essential
- Thought leadership and opinion pieces
- Original research and data analysis
- Customer stories requiring real interviews
- YMYL content (health, finance, legal)
- Brand-critical pages (homepage, about, mission)
- Content targeting competitive keywords with high domain authority competitors

## How to Use These Benchmarks

1. **Set baseline expectations**: Use "Average" columns to calibrate new campaigns. Do not expect "Excellent" results immediately.
2. **Identify underperformance**: If metrics fall below "Average" for 3+ months, investigate and optimize.
3. **Justify investment**: Use cost-per-piece and ROI data to build business cases for content programs.
4. **Benchmark against yourself**: Track your own metrics monthly and compare quarter-over-quarter improvement rather than only comparing to industry averages.
5. **Adjust for context**: B2B benchmarks typically show lower volume but higher per-lead value. B2C benchmarks show higher volume with lower per-conversion value. Niche industries will have lower absolute numbers but potentially higher conversion rates.
6. **Update annually**: These benchmarks reflect 2025-2026 data. Content marketing benchmarks shift as algorithms, tools, and audience behaviors evolve.

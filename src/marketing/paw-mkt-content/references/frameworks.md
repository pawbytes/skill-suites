**Purpose:** Proven frameworks, templates, and workflows for planning, creating, distributing, and scoring content at scale.

## 1. Topic Cluster Model

### What It Is
A strategic approach to organizing site content around broad "pillar" topics, supported by tightly related "cluster" articles that interlink to build topical authority.

### Structure
```
                    [Pillar Page]
                   /   |    |    \
                  /    |    |     \
          [Cluster] [Cluster] [Cluster] [Cluster]
            |   \      |         |        /   |
          [Sub] [Sub] [Sub]    [Sub]   [Sub] [Sub]
```

### Pillar Page Requirements
- **Length**: 3,000-5,000+ words
- **Scope**: Comprehensive coverage of a broad topic
- **Format**: Ultimate guide, definitive resource, or complete playbook
- **Links out**: To every cluster page in the topic group
- **CTA**: Lead magnet or conversion action relevant to the whole topic
- **Update cadence**: Quarterly review, semi-annual refresh

### Cluster Content Requirements
- **Length**: 1,200-2,500 words
- **Scope**: One specific subtopic or question within the pillar
- **Links back**: Must link to the pillar page (contextual, not forced)
- **Cross-links**: Link to 2-3 sibling cluster pages where relevant
- **Intent match**: Each cluster targets a distinct keyword/search intent

### How to Map a Topic Cluster

**Step 1: Choose the pillar topic**
- Must align with a core product/service offering
- Must have sufficient search volume (1,000+ monthly searches)
- Must be broad enough to support 8-15 subtopics

**Step 2: Identify cluster subtopics**
- Use keyword research (Ahrefs, SEMrush, Google Keyword Planner)
- Mine "People Also Ask" and "Related Searches" in Google
- Analyze competitor content structure
- Survey customers for their questions

**Step 3: Map search intent per cluster**
- Informational: "What is X", "How does X work"
- Navigational: "X tool", "X platform"
- Commercial: "Best X for Y", "X vs Z"
- Transactional: "Buy X", "X pricing", "X free trial"

**Step 4: Build the internal linking architecture**
- Every cluster links to the pillar (mandatory)
- Pillar links to every cluster (mandatory)
- Clusters cross-link where contextually relevant (recommended)
- Use descriptive anchor text, not "click here"

### Example: SaaS Project Management Tool
```
Pillar: "The Complete Guide to Project Management" (4,000 words)
  |
  |-- Cluster: "Agile vs Waterfall: Which Method Is Right for Your Team?" (1,800 words)
  |-- Cluster: "How to Create a Project Timeline in 7 Steps" (1,500 words)
  |-- Cluster: "10 Best Project Management Templates for 2026" (2,200 words)
  |-- Cluster: "Project Risk Management: A Practical Framework" (1,600 words)
  |-- Cluster: "How to Run an Effective Sprint Retrospective" (1,400 words)
  |-- Cluster: "Resource Allocation: Balancing Workloads Across Teams" (1,700 words)
  |-- Cluster: "Project Management for Remote Teams: Complete Playbook" (2,000 words)
  |-- Cluster: "Gantt Charts Explained: When and How to Use Them" (1,300 words)
  |-- Cluster: "Project Management Software Comparison 2026" (2,500 words)
  |-- Cluster: "How to Write a Project Brief That Keeps Teams Aligned" (1,200 words)
```

### Measuring Cluster Performance
- Track pillar page rankings for head terms (monthly)
- Track cluster rankings for long-tail terms (monthly)
- Monitor internal link click-through using GA4 events
- Measure topical authority improvement via total organic impressions for the cluster

## 2. Content Funnel Mapping

### Funnel Stages and Content Alignment

#### TOFU (Top of Funnel) -- Awareness
**Goal**: Attract strangers, build brand awareness, earn trust
**Buyer mindset**: "I have a problem" or "I'm curious about this topic"
**Search intent**: Informational

| Content Type | Example | Gating |
|-------------|---------|--------|
| Blog posts | "What Is Content Marketing?" | Ungated |
| Infographics | "The State of Remote Work 2026" | Ungated |
| Social media content | LinkedIn carousels, Twitter threads | Ungated |
| Podcasts | Industry trend discussions | Ungated |
| YouTube videos | Tutorials, explainers | Ungated |
| Original research | "We Analyzed 10,000 Blog Posts" | Ungated (gate the full report) |
| Quizzes/assessments | "What's Your Marketing Maturity Score?" | Partially gated (results via email) |

**Metrics**: Traffic, impressions, social shares, backlinks, brand mentions

#### MOFU (Middle of Funnel) -- Consideration
**Goal**: Nurture interest, educate on solutions, position your brand
**Buyer mindset**: "I'm exploring solutions to my problem"
**Search intent**: Commercial investigation

| Content Type | Example | Gating |
|-------------|---------|--------|
| Whitepapers | "The ROI of Marketing Automation" | Gated |
| Ebooks | "Complete Guide to ABM Strategy" | Gated |
| Webinars | "How [Company] Increased Retention 40%" | Gated (registration) |
| Comparison guides | "HubSpot vs Salesforce vs Pipedrive" | Ungated |
| Case studies | "How [Client] Grew Revenue 3x" | Ungated or lightly gated |
| Email nurture series | 5-part educational drip | Email subscriber |
| Templates/toolkits | "Content Calendar Template Pack" | Gated |

**Metrics**: Leads generated, email signups, webinar registrations, content downloads, email engagement

#### BOFU (Bottom of Funnel) -- Decision
**Goal**: Convert prospects into customers, remove objections
**Buyer mindset**: "I'm ready to choose a solution"
**Search intent**: Transactional

| Content Type | Example | Gating |
|-------------|---------|--------|
| Product demos | Interactive or video walkthroughs | Ungated or request-based |
| Free trials/freemium | Hands-on product experience | Account creation |
| ROI calculators | "Calculate Your Content Marketing ROI" | Ungated (capture optional) |
| Customer testimonials | Video and written success stories | Ungated |
| Pricing pages | Transparent pricing with comparisons | Ungated |
| Implementation guides | "Getting Started in 30 Minutes" | Ungated |
| Vendor comparison sheets | Feature-by-feature competitive breakdown | Ungated |

**Metrics**: Trials started, demos booked, proposals sent, revenue attributed

### Content Mix by Funnel Stage
- **Ideal ratio**: 60% TOFU / 25% MOFU / 15% BOFU
- Adjust based on maturity: early-stage companies may need more TOFU; established brands can shift toward MOFU/BOFU
- Every piece of content should have a clear "next step" pushing the reader deeper into the funnel

## 3. Skyscraper Technique 2.0

### Original Concept (Backlinko, Brian Dean)
1. Find content with lots of backlinks
2. Create something significantly better
3. Reach out to people linking to the inferior piece

### Updated for the AI-Era (2025-2026)

**Step 1: Find link-worthy content**
- Use Ahrefs Content Explorer: filter by referring domains > 50, published in last 2 years
- Search your target keyword and sort by backlinks
- Identify the top 3-5 most-linked pieces

**Step 2: Improve with genuine differentiation**
AI can replicate generic "longer and more detailed" content. You must differentiate with:
- **Original data**: Run surveys, analyze proprietary datasets, scrape public data
- **Expert contributions**: Interview 5-10 industry experts, include named quotes
- **Visual assets**: Custom charts, diagrams, infographics (not stock images)
- **Recency**: Include 2025-2026 statistics, trends, and examples
- **Interactivity**: Calculators, quizzes, self-assessment tools
- **First-hand experience**: Real examples from your own work, not hypothetical

**Step 3: Strategic outreach (updated)**
- **Broken link approach**: Find sites linking to outdated/dead versions of the original content
- **Resource page targeting**: Find curated resource pages in your niche
- **HARO/Connectively/Quoted**: Offer yourself as an expert source referencing your content
- **Digital PR**: Pitch the original data angle to industry publications
- **Social proof first**: Get initial traction via social media, newsletters, and communities before outreaching

### What "Better" Means in 2026
| Dimension | What AI Can Do | What Requires Humans |
|-----------|---------------|---------------------|
| Length and coverage | Easily replicated | -- |
| Data and statistics | Can compile existing data | Original research and surveys |
| Design and visuals | Basic graphics | Custom infographics, branded design |
| Expert quotes | Can fabricate (bad) | Real interviews with named sources |
| Timeliness | Can scrape recent data | Contextualized industry analysis |
| Interactivity | Basic widgets | Custom tools and calculators |

## 4. Content Repurposing Framework

### The 1-to-20+ Model
One pillar content piece should generate at minimum 20 derivative assets across channels.

### Pillar-to-Derivative Workflow
```
PILLAR PIECE (e.g., 3,000-word blog post or 30-min video)
  |
  |-- SHORT-FORM WRITTEN
  |     |-- LinkedIn post (key insight, 150-300 words)
  |     |-- LinkedIn article (repurposed angle, 800-1,200 words)
  |     |-- Twitter/X thread (8-12 tweets, key takeaways)
  |     |-- Twitter/X single tweet (most surprising stat or insight)
  |     |-- Reddit post (tailored to subreddit, add discussion question)
  |     |-- Quora answer (address specific question with content excerpt)
  |
  |-- EMAIL
  |     |-- Newsletter feature (200-400 word summary + link)
  |     |-- Dedicated email blast (for high-value pieces)
  |     |-- Nurture sequence inclusion (reference in relevant drip)
  |
  |-- VIDEO
  |     |-- YouTube long-form (8-15 min deep-dive)
  |     |-- YouTube Shorts (3-5 clips, 30-60 seconds each)
  |     |-- TikTok/Reels (3-5 clips, trending format)
  |     |-- Loom walkthrough (for technical/how-to content)
  |
  |-- AUDIO
  |     |-- Podcast episode (discuss the topic with added commentary)
  |     |-- Podcast guest pitch (offer the topic angle to other shows)
  |     |-- Audio version of blog post (for accessibility and reach)
  |
  |-- VISUAL
  |     |-- Infographic (key data and frameworks visualized)
  |     |-- Instagram carousel (8-10 slides, key points)
  |     |-- Pinterest pin (vertical infographic or checklist)
  |     |-- SlideShare/PDF deck (framework presented as slides)
  |
  |-- GATED ASSETS
  |     |-- Checklist/template download (extracted from content)
  |     |-- Worksheet/workbook (interactive version of the framework)
  |     |-- Mini-course module (content restructured as a lesson)
```

### Repurposing Timeline
| Timeframe | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Day 0 | Publish pillar piece |
| Day 0-1 | Share on social (LinkedIn post, Twitter thread) |
| Day 1-3 | Send newsletter, email blast |
| Day 3-7 | Create short-form video clips |
| Week 2 | Publish YouTube video, infographic |
| Week 2-3 | Syndicate to Medium, LinkedIn Articles |
| Week 3-4 | Submit to Reddit, Quora, communities |
| Month 2 | Record podcast episode on the topic |
| Month 2-3 | Create gated asset (template, worksheet) |
| Quarter | Update and re-promote with fresh data |

### Platform-Specific Adaptation Rules
- **LinkedIn**: Lead with a personal hook or contrarian take. Write natively, do not just paste the blog intro.
- **Twitter/X**: Start the thread with the most surprising data point. Each tweet should stand alone.
- **YouTube**: Open with the problem statement in the first 10 seconds. Add a custom thumbnail.
- **Instagram**: Design carousel slides that tell a visual story. Minimal text per slide.
- **Email**: Lead with why the reader should care. Include one clear CTA.
- **Reddit**: Provide genuine value in the post itself. Do not just drop a link.
- **Podcast**: Add personal stories, tangents, and opinions not in the written piece.

## 5. Content Scoring Model

### Quality Rubric (Score 1-10 per dimension)

| Dimension | 1-3 (Poor) | 4-6 (Average) | 7-8 (Good) | 9-10 (Excellent) |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|------------|------------------|
| **Depth** | Surface-level, no new insight | Covers basics adequately | Thorough with useful detail | Comprehensive with expert-level insight |
| **Originality** | Rehashed from other sources | Some unique framing | Original angle or data | First-of-its-kind research or perspective |
| **Accuracy** | Contains errors or outdated info | Mostly correct, basic sources | Well-sourced, verified claims | Expert-reviewed, primary sources cited |
| **Readability** | Disorganized, poor flow | Readable but unremarkable | Well-structured, scannable | Engaging, clear, enjoyable to read |
| **Visual quality** | No images or stock only | Basic custom graphics | Professional design, charts | Interactive, custom illustrations, branded |
| **Actionability** | Theoretical only | Some advice given | Step-by-step guidance | Templates, tools, and executable frameworks |

### Engagement Potential Score
- Search volume of target keyword (0-3 points)
- Social shareability of topic (0-2 points)
- Controversy or novelty factor (0-2 points)
- Visual/interactive potential (0-1 point)
- Timeliness/trending relevance (0-2 points)
- **Total: 0-10 points**

### SEO Value Score
- Keyword difficulty vs. domain authority match (0-3 points)
- Search intent alignment (0-2 points)
- Internal linking opportunity (0-1 point)
- Backlink acquisition potential (0-2 points)
- Content gap (competitors have not covered well) (0-2 points)
- **Total: 0-10 points**

### Conversion Potential Score
- Proximity to purchase decision (0-3 points)
- Natural product/service integration (0-2 points)
- Lead capture opportunity (0-2 points)
- Sales enablement value (0-1 point)
- Retargeting audience value (0-2 points)
- **Total: 0-10 points**

### Composite Content Score
```
Composite Score = (Quality * 0.30) + (Engagement * 0.20) + (SEO * 0.30) + (Conversion * 0.20)
```

| Composite Score | Action |
|----------------|--------|
| 8.0-10.0 | Publish immediately, promote aggressively, invest in repurposing |
| 6.0-7.9 | Publish with standard promotion |
| 4.0-5.9 | Revise before publishing or deprioritize |
| Below 4.0 | Do not publish; rethink the topic or angle |

## 6. Editorial Calendar Framework

### Monthly Planning Structure

#### Content Mix: 70/20/10 Rule
- **70%**: Proven content types (SEO blog posts, how-to guides, listicles) -- low risk, steady traffic
- **20%**: Innovative content (new formats, new channels, new angles) -- moderate risk, growth potential
- **10%**: Experimental content (bold ideas, unusual formats, provocative takes) -- high risk, breakout potential

#### Monthly Content Calendar Template
```
Week 1:
  - Mon: Publish SEO blog post #1 (TOFU)
  - Tue: Social distribution of blog post
  - Wed: Newsletter send
  - Thu: Publish quick-hit content (checklist, template)
  - Fri: Community engagement (Reddit, Quora, forums)

Week 2:
  - Mon: Publish SEO blog post #2 (MOFU)
  - Tue: Social distribution
  - Wed: Repurpose Week 1 content (video, carousel)
  - Thu: Guest post or collaboration outreach
  - Fri: Content performance review

Week 3:
  - Mon: Publish pillar/long-form content
  - Tue-Wed: Aggressive distribution across all channels
  - Thu: Publish case study or customer story (BOFU)
  - Fri: Internal content sharing (sales enablement)

Week 4:
  - Mon: Publish SEO blog post #3 (TOFU)
  - Tue: Social distribution
  - Wed: Content audit and refresh older articles
  - Thu: Plan next month's editorial calendar
  - Fri: Monthly performance report and retrospective
```

#### Quarterly Planning Cycle
- **Month 1 of quarter**: Execute core calendar, gather data
- **Month 2 of quarter**: Optimize based on Month 1 data, launch experimental content
- **Month 3 of quarter**: Plan next quarter, finalize upcoming editorial calendar, conduct content audit

### Assignment Workflow
```
1. Content strategist identifies topic and keyword (Week -3)
2. SEO review and content brief created (Week -2)
3. Writer assigned with brief, deadline, and examples (Week -2)
4. First draft submitted (Week -1)
5. Editorial review and feedback (Week -1)
6. Revisions completed (Week 0)
7. Final edit, SEO optimization, design assets (Week 0)
8. Publish and distribute (Week 0)
9. Performance tracking begins (Week 0+)
```

## 7. Case Study Framework

### STAR Template (Situation-Task-Action-Result)

**Situation** (10-15% of word count)
- Who is the customer? (Industry, size, role of decision-maker)
- What was their context before engaging with you?
- What specific challenge or pain point did they face?
- Include a quantifiable "before" metric if possible

**Task** (10-15% of word count)
- What did the customer need to accomplish?
- What were their specific goals or KPIs?
- What constraints or requirements existed?
- Why was this problem urgent or important?

**Action** (30-40% of word count)
- What solution did you implement?
- Walk through the process step by step
- Highlight specific features, strategies, or approaches used
- Include timeline and milestones
- Mention any obstacles overcome during implementation

**Result** (30-40% of word count)
- Quantify outcomes with specific numbers and percentages
- Compare "before" vs "after" metrics
- Include timeframe for results (e.g., "within 90 days")
- Add customer quote validating the results
- Mention unexpected benefits or secondary outcomes
- Include forward-looking statement (ongoing impact, future plans)

### Data-Driven Storytelling Rules
- Lead with the most impressive number in the headline ("How [Company] Increased Revenue 340% in 6 Months")
- Include at least 3 quantified results
- Use charts/graphs to visualize before-and-after comparisons
- Add a pull quote from the customer near each major result
- Include the customer's photo, logo, and name (with permission)
- End with a CTA relevant to the reader's likely situation

## 9. AI Content Workflow

### When to Use AI for Content

| Use Case | AI Suitability | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| First drafts of standard blog posts | High | Always requires human editing |
| Content outlines and structure | High | AI excels at organizing information |
| Meta descriptions and title variations | High | Generate 10 options, pick the best |
| Social media post variations | High | Create platform-specific versions quickly |
| Email subject line brainstorming | High | A/B test AI-generated options |
| Repurposing content across formats | Medium-High | Needs human review for platform nuance |
| Competitive content analysis | Medium | Good for summarization, misses strategic nuance |
| Original thought leadership | Low | AI lacks genuine perspective and experience |
| Customer stories and interviews | Low | Requires real human interaction |
| Brand voice content (About, Mission) | Low | Must authentically represent the company |
| YMYL content (health, finance, legal) | Very Low | Requires expert human authorship and review |

### Human-AI Collaboration Model
```
Phase 1: AI Research and Ideation
  - AI generates topic ideas from seed keywords
  - AI summarizes competitor content
  - AI drafts initial outline
  - HUMAN reviews, selects, and refines direction

Phase 2: AI Drafting
  - AI writes first draft from approved outline
  - AI generates multiple headline options
  - AI suggests internal linking opportunities
  - HUMAN reviews draft for accuracy and voice

Phase 3: Human Enhancement (Non-Negotiable)
  - ADD: Personal experience, anecdotes, and real examples
  - ADD: Original data, screenshots, and proprietary insights
  - ADD: Expert quotes from real people (with attribution)
  - ADD: Nuanced opinions and genuine perspective
  - REMOVE: Generic filler, vague claims, unsupported statistics
  - REMOVE: Repetitive or obvious statements
  - VERIFY: Every fact, statistic, link, and recommendation

Phase 4: Human Final Review
  - Read the entire piece aloud (catches awkward phrasing)
  - Check: Does this sound like it was written by our brand?
  - Check: Would an expert in this field find this credible?
  - Check: Does this add something new to the conversation?
  - Check: Would I personally share this with a colleague?
```

### AI Content Quality Control Checklist
```
BEFORE PUBLISHING -- MANDATORY CHECKS:

[ ] Factual accuracy: Every statistic, claim, and recommendation verified
[ ] Source attribution: All data points have credible, current sources
[ ] No hallucinations: No fabricated quotes, studies, or statistics
[ ] No plagiarism: Run through plagiarism checker (Copyscape, Originality.ai)
[ ] Brand voice: Reads naturally in our established tone and style
[ ] Original value: Contains at least 30% original insight, data, or examples
[ ] E-E-A-T signals: Named author, experience signals, expert review if YMYL
[ ] No AI artifacts: No "as an AI", "certainly", "delve", or robotic transitions
[ ] Human readability: Scans well, logical flow, no run-on AI paragraphs
[ ] Competitive differentiation: Offers something the top 3 ranking pages do not
```

## 10. Conversion Copywriting for Landing and Pricing Pages

Content marketing includes the pages that convert -- landing pages, pricing pages, and product pages. These require a different discipline than blog writing: every word serves conversion.

### 10.1 Page Structure

Use this sequence for any landing or pricing page. Not every section is mandatory -- cut what does not serve the specific audience and offer.

| # | Section | Purpose | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Hero** | Make the promise immediately clear | Headline + subhead + primary CTA. Above fold. |
| 2 | **Social Proof Bar** | Instant credibility | Logos, customer count, ratings. Reduces bounce. |
| 3 | **Problem / Stakes** | Agitate the pain | Make them feel the cost of not solving it. |
| 4 | **Solution / How It Works** | Show the mechanism | Brief, benefit-led. 3-step process or feature highlights. |
| 5 | **Proof** | Remove doubt | Case study results, testimonials, stats. Specific beats vague. |
| 6 | **Objection Handling** | Pre-empt hesitation | FAQ, "Who is this for / not for," risk reversal. |
| 7 | **CTA Block** | Close the conversion | Repeat the offer. Reinforce the primary CTA with urgency or guarantee. |

### 10.2 CTA Formula

```
[Action verb] + [What they get] + [Optional qualifier]
```

**Examples:**
- `Start your free trial` -- simple, clear
- `Get my free SEO audit` -- possessive first-person, specificity
- `Start saving hours today` -- outcome-framed
- `Try {Product} free for 14 days` -- specificity + risk reduction
- `Book a 20-minute strategy call` -- sets expectation

**Weak CTAs to replace:** "Submit," "Click here," "Learn more," "Get started" (too vague), "Sign up" (implies commitment without communicating value).

Always pair the CTA with a micro-copy line beneath it: `No credit card required`, `Cancel anytime`, `Join 3,400 teams`.

### 10.3 Headline Frameworks

The headline is the highest-leverage element -- if it fails, nothing else matters.

**Proven patterns:**
- **Outcome + timeframe**: "Double your qualified leads in 90 days"
- **Eliminate the negative**: "Close the books in minutes, not hours"
- **Specific social proof**: "How 1,200 agencies run client reporting without spreadsheets"
- **For/For/Not**: "For freelancers who want agency-level results without agency-level overhead"
- **Contrarian**: "Stop chasing leads. Let your best customers bring the next ones."

**Tests to run:** Benefit-led vs. problem-led; specific number vs. no number; question vs. statement.

### 10.4 Pricing Page Specific Rules

Pricing pages are the highest-intent, highest-anxiety page. Apply:

- **Lead with value, not price.** The tier name and headline should describe the outcome, not just the features.
- **Anchor high.** Lead with the most premium tier (or show it first) to frame lower tiers as the reasonable choice.
- **Highlight the recommended tier.** "Most popular" or "Best for teams" label on one tier increases conversion.
- **Translate features into outcomes.** "Unlimited users" -> "Everyone on the team included, no per-seat upsells."
- **Handle objections on the page.** Place FAQ directly below the pricing table. Common questions: "What happens at end of trial?", "Can I change plans?", "Is my data safe?"
- **Money-back guarantee or free trial removes the biggest risk.** State it prominently near the CTA.

### 10.5 Copywriting Output Format

When producing landing or pricing page copy, always deliver:

1. **Full page copy draft** -- every section, in order, with headlines, subheads, bullets, CTAs, and micro-copy.
2. **Annotations** -- brief notes explaining the strategic choice for key sections (why this headline angle, why this proof placement, which objection is being handled where).
3. **2-3 headline alternatives** -- so the user can select the direction or A/B test.
4. **CTA alternatives** -- at least 2-3 options with different angles (benefit, urgency, social proof).

Save to `./.pawbytes/marketing-suites/brands/{brand-slug}/content/copy/landing-page-{page-name}-{YYYY-MM-DD}.md`.

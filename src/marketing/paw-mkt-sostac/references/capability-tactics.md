# Capability: Tactics Planning

**Outcome:** Translate strategy into prioritized, budgeted channel and content plan.

**Output:** `./.pawbytes/marketing-suites/brands/{brand-slug}/sostac/04-tactics.md`

**Phase Question:** "Details of strategy"

## Frameworks Applied

Load from `./references/frameworks-index.csv` (filter: `sostac_phase=Tactics`):
- AARRR Pirate Metrics
- ICE Scoring
- PIE Framework
- Hub-Hero-Help Content Model
- Pillar-Cluster SEO
- TOFU/MOFU/BOFU
- 7P Marketing Mix
- 70/20/10 Budget Rule

**CRITICAL:** Always read `./references/frameworks/situational-router.md` in full during Phase 4 — it contains the 3-layer routing logic and all 9 playbooks.

## Research Work

### 1. Run the Situational Router
Using Phase 1-3 data, determine:
- Brand maturity (Pre-launch / Launch / Growth / Scale)
- AARRR priority gap (which stage has biggest leak)
- Recommended playbook from 9 options

**Don't ask — calculate it from the data.**

### 2. Build the Channel Plan
For the recommended playbook, research what's actually working in this category:
- Competitor ad activity
- Content strategies
- Channel presence from auto-discovery

Recommend specific channels with reasoning tied to strategy.

### 3. ICE Score the Top 15 Tactics
Score each tactic on:
- **Impact** (on OKRs)
- **Confidence** (evidence it works for this situation)
- **Ease** (execution feasibility)

Present as ranked backlog.

### 4. Design the Content Strategy
Based on playbook's funnel weight, map specific content types to funnel stages. Provide concrete content ideas, not generic categories. Use competitor content gaps and customer language from Phase 1.

### 5. Draft 2-3 Campaign Concepts
Named campaigns with hooks, channels, expected outcomes. Draw from competitor weaknesses and customer pain points.

### 6. Build the Budget Allocation
70/20/10 split mapped to ICE-prioritized tactic list. Show the math.

## Presentation Format

> "Based on your [maturity stage] and the [AARRR gap], here's my tactical plan:"

**Recommended Playbook** — what it is and why it fits

**Top 10 tactics ranked by ICE** with rationale for each score

**Channel plan** — which channels, what role each plays, expected contribution

**Content strategy** — specific content ideas mapped to funnel stages

**Campaign concepts** — 2-3 named campaigns with hooks

**Budget allocation** — where the money goes and why

**Key decisions for user** — budget envelope, team capacity, channel preferences

## Validation Questions

- Budget constraints and team capacity
- Channel preferences or restrictions ("we don't do TikTok")
- Campaign concept preferences

## Success Criteria

- Playbook matches brand maturity and AARRR gap
- Every tactic serves the positioning and target segment from Strategy
- ICE scores have clear reasoning
- Budget allocation follows 70/20/10 with rationale
- Content ideas are specific, not generic
- Campaigns have hooks tied to customer pain points

## Tactics Must Connect to Strategy

Check every tactic against:
- Does it serve the target segment?
- Does it reinforce the positioning?
- Does it close the AARRR gap identified?

If a tactic fails any check, either adjust it or remove it.

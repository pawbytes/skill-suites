# Objective-and-Task Budgeting

**What it is:** Builds the budget by defining the objective, then costing the tasks required to achieve it — bottom-up, evidence-based.

**SOSTAC Phase:** Action

**Step-by-step:**
1. State the specific objective (e.g., "Generate 500 MQLs in Q3")
2. Define required tasks (e.g., 10 blog posts, 4 paid campaigns, 2 webinars, 1 lead magnet)
3. Cost each task using market rates or internal capacity
4. Sum = budget request. Justify with the objective.

**Why superior to % of revenue:** % of revenue is backwards — it sizes the budget to past performance, not future goals. Objective-and-task links spend directly to outcomes.

**Common cost benchmarks (indicative, 2024-2025):**
| Item | Indicative Range |
|---|---|
| Blog post (freelance, 1,000-1,500 words) | £200–£600 |
| SEO platform (e.g., Semrush, Ahrefs) | £100–£400/month |
| Paid media management fee | 10–15% of ad spend |
| Email platform (mid-tier) | £50–£300/month |
| Video production (basic brand) | £1,500–£8,000 |
| Marketing automation platform | £200–£1,500/month |

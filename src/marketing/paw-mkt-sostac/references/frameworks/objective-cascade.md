# Objective Cascade Method

**What it is:** Works backwards from a business revenue goal through marketing contribution to channel-level sub-objectives.

**SOSTAC Phase:** Objectives

**Cascade logic:**
```
Business goal: £2M revenue
→ Marketing contribution: 60% = £1.2M from marketing-driven channels
→ Average deal value: £2,000 → need 600 customers
→ Close rate: 25% → need 2,400 sales-qualified leads
→ MQL-to-SQL rate: 50% → need 4,800 MQLs
→ Conversion rate from traffic: 3% → need 160,000 sessions
→ Channel split: 50% organic (80k), 30% paid (48k), 20% email/referral (32k)
```

**Common conversion benchmarks (use as starting point, validate with client data):**
- Website visitor → lead: 1–5% (B2B), 1–3% (e-commerce)
- Lead → MQL: 20–40%
- MQL → SQL: 30–50%
- SQL → close: 20–30% (B2B), varies widely in B2C

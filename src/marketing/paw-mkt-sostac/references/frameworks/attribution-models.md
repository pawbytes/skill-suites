# Attribution Models Comparison

**What it is:** Attribution models assign conversion credit to touchpoints in the customer journey. Different models produce different insights and incentivise different channel investments.

**SOSTAC Phase:** Control

| Model | Logic | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **First-touch** | 100% credit to first interaction | Long sales cycles, awareness investment |
| **Last-touch** | 100% credit to last interaction | Short cycles, direct response |
| **Linear** | Equal credit to all touches | Balanced multi-channel view |
| **Time-decay** | More credit to recent touches | Short-cycle, sales-led |
| **Position-based (U-shaped)** | 40% first, 40% last, 20% split middle | B2B, where first touch and close matter most |
| **W-shaped** | 30% first, 30% lead creation, 30% close, 10% other | Complex B2B with defined pipeline stages |
| **Data-driven** | ML assigns credit based on actual path data | High data volume, mature analytics |

**Selection guide:** Use last-touch for early-stage businesses with limited data. Move to position-based once multi-touch data is available. Consider data-driven only with 10k+ monthly conversions and robust analytics infrastructure.

**Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM):** For budgets above ~£500k/year, MMM provides econometric analysis of channel contribution independent of cookies/click tracking. Mention as future-state capability.

# Capability: Strategy Development

**Outcome:** Define who to target, how to position, and where growth comes from.

**Output:** `./.pawbytes/marketing-suites/brands/{brand-slug}/sostac/03-strategy.md`

**Phase Question:** "How do we get there?"

## Frameworks Applied

Load from `./references/frameworks-index.csv` (filter: `sostac_phase=Strategy`):
- STP (Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning)
- Moore's Positioning Statement
- Ansoff Matrix
- Porter's Generic Strategies
- Blue Ocean Strategy
- Value Proposition Canvas
- Online Value Proposition (OVP)
- Customer Journey Mapping

## Research Work

Using Situation + Objectives:

### 1. Propose Segmentation
Based on customer data from reviews, forums, competitor positioning. Identify 3-5 distinct segments. Score each on attractiveness (size, growth, fit, profitability, competitive intensity).

### 2. Recommend Target Segments
Present top 1-2 recommended segments with reasoning. Show why these give best return on strategic focus.

### 3. Build Competitive Positioning Map
Identify two axes that matter most to buyers. Map all players. Identify white space.

### 4. Draft Positioning Statement
Using Moore's formula, present 2-3 options with different angles. Explain trade-offs.

### 5. Determine Ansoff Direction
Recommend where growth should come from. Show why.

### 6. Draft Value Proposition Canvas
Map customer jobs/pains/gains against product pain relievers/gain creators. Identify mismatches.

### 7. Map Customer Journey
Using research data, map full journey from trigger to advocacy. Identify 2-3 biggest drop-off risks and recommend interventions.

## Presentation Format

> "Here's my strategic recommendation based on everything we know:"

**Recommended target segments** with scoring and reasoning

**Positioning map** showing competitive white space

**2-3 positioning statement options** with trade-offs

**Customer journey map** with drop-off risks and recommended fixes

**Strategic phasing** — what to prioritize first, second, third

**Key strategic decision point** — 1-2 choices needing user input (e.g., "premium vs accessible positioning")

## Validation Questions

- Confirm segment prioritization matches internal knowledge of best customers
- Choose between positioning options
- Validate journey map against actual customer behavior

## Success Criteria

- Segments are specific, not generic ("VP Engineering at 50-200 person SaaS companies" not "businesses")
- Positioning is differentiated — if it applies to 3 competitors, it's not positioning
- Ansoff direction matches resources and risk tolerance
- Journey map identifies specific intervention points
- Strategy directly connects to Situation findings and Objectives

## Strategy vs Tactics Check

**Strategy answers:** WHO (segments), HOW (positioning), WHERE (growth direction)
**Tactics answer:** WHAT (channels), WHEN (timing), HOW MUCH (budget)

If you're recommending channels, that's tactics. Keep strategy at the positioning and targeting level.

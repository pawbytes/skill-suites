# Spokesperson Preparation

> Key messages framework, interview preparation, and handling difficult questions.

## Key Messages Framework

For every media opportunity, prepare:

**Core message**: 1 sentence -- the single most important takeaway

**Supporting messages**: Maximum 3, each with proof (data, example, or story)

**Proof points**: Stats, case studies, third-party validation

**Bridging phrases**:
- "What's important to understand is..."
- "The real story here is..."
- "What our customers tell us is..."
- "Let me put that in context..."

**Off-limits topics** with suggested redirects

## Interview Preparation

**Before**:
- Research journalist and outlet
- Review recent articles
- Prepare 3-5 key messages, practice each in under 30 seconds

**During**:
- Lead with strongest message
- Bridge to redirect
- Provide specific examples and data
- Speak in quotable sentences
- Pause before difficult questions

**After**:
- Send thank-you email within 2 hours
- Provide any promised follow-up materials immediately
- Share the published piece and tag the journalist

## Handling Difficult Questions

| Question Type | Technique |
|---|---|
| Hostile or loaded | Reframe premise, then bridge: "I'd characterize it differently. What's actually happening is..." |
| Speculative | Decline, offer facts: "I can't speculate, but what I can tell you is..." |
| Off-topic | Acknowledge, redirect: "That's fair. What's most relevant here is..." |
| "No comment" trap | Never say it: "We're not ready to discuss that yet, but here's what we can share..." |
| Competitor comparison | Focus on strengths: "I can't speak for them, but what our customers value is..." |

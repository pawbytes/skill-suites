# Crisis Statement Templates

> Part of the **Media Pitch Templates & Frameworks** collection.

### 10.1 Initial Holding Statement (Within 1-2 Hours)

Use when the crisis has been detected but facts are still being gathered.

```
[Company Name] Statement Regarding [Brief Description of Situation]

We are aware of [brief, factual description of the situation]. We take this matter
seriously and are actively investigating.

[If people are affected]: Our immediate priority is [the safety / support / well-being]
of [those affected].

We are working to understand the full scope of this situation and will provide an
update by [specific time -- commit to a deadline, typically within 4-8 hours].

For questions, please contact [Name] at [Email] / [Phone].
```

Key rules for holding statements:
- Acknowledge the situation. Do not ignore or minimize.
- Express seriousness without admitting fault (legal may need to review).
- Commit to a specific time for the next update. Meet that deadline.
- Do not speculate, assign blame, or provide details you cannot confirm.

### 10.2 Full Response Statement (When Facts Are Clear)

```
[Company Name] Statement on [Situation]

On [date], [factual description of what happened -- be specific and honest].

[IMPACT]: [Who was affected and how. Be specific about scope.]

[ACCOUNTABILITY]: [Take responsibility where appropriate. "We fell short of our
standards" or "This was caused by [specific cause]." Avoid passive voice and
corporate euphemisms.]

[ACTION TAKEN]: [What has been done so far to address the immediate issue.]

[PREVENTION]: [Specific steps being taken to prevent recurrence. Be concrete:
"We have implemented X" not "We are reviewing our processes."]

[SUPPORT]: [What is being offered to those affected. Specific remedies, not
vague promises.]

We will provide further updates as [additional information becomes available /
our review is completed / specific milestone]. Our next update will be on [date].

[Spokesperson Name], [Title]
[Company Name]
```

### 10.3 Update Statement (During Ongoing Crisis)

```
Update: [Company Name] Statement on [Situation] -- [Date, Time]

This is an update to our statement issued on [previous date].

[NEW INFORMATION]: [What has been learned since the last update. Facts only.]

[PROGRESS]: [What actions have been completed since the last update.]

[NEXT STEPS]: [What is being done next, with timeline.]

[ONGOING SUPPORT]: [Remind affected parties of available resources/remedies.]

Our next update will be on [date/time] or sooner if significant new information
becomes available.

[Spokesperson Name], [Title]
[Company Name]
```

### 10.4 Resolution Statement (Crisis Resolved)

```
[Company Name] Final Update on [Situation]

Following our investigation into [brief description], we want to share our
findings and the steps we've taken.

[ROOT CAUSE]: [Clear, honest explanation of what caused the situation.]

[FULL SCOPE]: [Complete picture of impact -- who was affected, extent of damage.]

[REMEDIATION]: [Everything that has been done to address the situation and support
those affected.]

[SYSTEMIC CHANGES]: [Specific, permanent changes implemented to prevent recurrence.
Be concrete and verifiable.]

[COMMITMENT]: [Forward-looking statement about standards and accountability.]

We recognize that trust is earned through action, not words. We are committed to
[specific ongoing measures] and will [reporting mechanism for accountability,
e.g., publish a follow-up report in 90 days].

To those affected: [Direct, empathetic message. Thank them for patience. Reiterate
support resources.]

[CEO/Spokesperson Name], [Title]
[Company Name]
```

### Crisis Communication Principles

1. **Speed over perfection**: A holding statement within 1-2 hours beats a perfect
   statement 12 hours later. Fill the information vacuum before others do.
2. **Specificity over vagueness**: "We are reviewing our processes" means nothing.
   "We have hired an independent auditor and will publish results in 30 days" builds trust.
3. **Empathy before facts**: Lead with care for those affected. Facts and details come
   second.
4. **One spokesperson**: All public statements come from one designated person. Mixed
   messages amplify crisis.
5. **Commit to timelines**: Promise the next update by a specific time and meet that
   deadline, even if the update is "we are still investigating."
6. **Assume public**: Every internal message, every DM, every email may become public.
   Write accordingly.
7. **No "no comment"**: Always provide something. "We are actively investigating and
   will share details by [time]" is always better than silence.
8. **Post-crisis follow-through**: Announce changes and follow through visibly. Broken
   promises after a crisis are worse than the crisis itself.

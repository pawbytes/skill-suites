### 6.1 The Message Triangle

For every media opportunity, prepare three layers:

```
            [CORE MESSAGE]
         (1 sentence -- the single
        most important takeaway)
           /              \
    [SUPPORTING          [SUPPORTING
     MESSAGE 1]           MESSAGE 2]
    (with proof)          (with proof)
         |                     |
    [SUPPORTING MESSAGE 3]
         (with proof)
```

- **Core message**: One sentence. If the journalist remembers only one thing, this is it.
- **Supporting messages**: Maximum 3. Each backed by a proof point (data, case study,
  third-party validation, or specific example).
- **Proof points**: Statistics, customer stories, analyst quotes, awards, third-party
  data. Never make a claim without a proof point.

### 6.2 Bridging Phrases

When the conversation drifts off-message, use bridging phrases to redirect:

| Situation | Bridging Phrase |
|---|---|
| Off-topic question | "That's an important area. What's most relevant to what we're seeing is..." |
| Hostile framing | "I'd characterize it differently. What's actually happening is..." |
| Speculative question | "I can't speculate on that, but what I can tell you is..." |
| Competitor comparison | "I can't speak for them, but what our customers value is..." |
| Confidential topic | "We're not ready to discuss that yet, but here's what we can share..." |
| Negative premise | "Let me put that in context..." |
| Rumor | "The facts are..." |

### 6.3 Q&A Preparation Document

For every media interview, prepare:

```markdown
## Interview Prep: [Topic] -- [Date]

### Journalist
- Name: [Full name]
- Outlet: [Publication]
- Beat: [Their focus area]
- Recent articles: [Links to 3-5 recent pieces]
- Style notes: [Conversational? Adversarial? Deep-dive? Quick-hit?]

### Our Objectives
1. [Primary message we want to convey]
2. [Secondary message]
3. [Tertiary message]

### Anticipated Questions & Approved Responses

**Q: [Most likely question]**
A: [Approved response -- written in conversational language, under 30 seconds spoken]

**Q: [Second likely question]**
A: [Approved response]

**Q: [Potentially difficult question]**
A: [Approved response with bridging phrase]

**Q: [Potentially difficult question #2]**
A: [Approved response]

### Off-Limits Topics
- [Topic 1]: Redirect with "[bridging phrase] + [approved message]"
- [Topic 2]: Redirect with "[bridging phrase] + [approved message]"

### Key Data Points
- [Stat 1 -- memorize this]
- [Stat 2]
- [Stat 3]
```

### 6.4 Interview Best Practices

**Before**:
- Research the journalist and outlet. Read their last 5 articles.
- Practice key messages aloud, each in under 30 seconds.
- Prepare 2-3 anecdotes that illustrate key points.
- Test audio/video setup for virtual interviews.

**During**:
- Lead with your strongest message within the first 60 seconds.
- Speak in quotable sentences. Short, declarative, specific.
- Use bridging phrases to redirect, never say "no comment."
- Provide specific examples and data rather than generalities.
- Pause before answering difficult questions. Silence is acceptable.
- If you do not know the answer, say so and offer to follow up.

**After**:
- Send a thank-you email within 2 hours.
- Provide any promised follow-up materials immediately.
- Share the published piece and tag the journalist.

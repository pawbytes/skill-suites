## 1. Digital PR for SEO

### 1.1 Why Digital PR Is the Top Link Building Strategy

Backlinks remain the second strongest ranking signal after content relevance in 2026.
A single editorial link from a DA 80+ news publication outweighs hundreds of links from
low-authority directories or link farms. Sites running consistent digital PR campaigns
earn 3-5x more referring domains per quarter than those relying on traditional outreach
alone (BuzzStream State of Digital PR 2026).

Key advantages of PR-driven link building over other methods:

- **Editorial context**: Links appear within genuine news coverage, not sponsored posts
  or guest blogs. Google values editorial signals.
- **High domain authority**: Tier-1 and Tier-2 outlets typically carry DA 70-95, which
  traditional link building rarely reaches.
- **Natural anchor text**: Journalists link using their own phrasing, producing the
  diverse anchor profile search engines prefer.
- **Scalability**: A single data study can earn 20-100+ links from one campaign, while
  manual outreach yields 1 link per effort.
- **Compounding returns**: Newsworthy assets continue to earn passive links long after
  the initial campaign.

### 1.2 PR-Driven Link Building Tactics

**Data Journalism / Original Research**

The highest-ROI digital PR tactic in 2025-2026. Original data studies earn a median of
24 referring domains per campaign, with top campaigns exceeding 100+ (Fractl/Moz data).
Process:

1. Identify a question journalists and the target audience want answered.
2. Gather data: survey (500-1,000+ respondents), proprietary data analysis, public data
   compilation, or Freedom of Information requests.
3. Find the surprising, counterintuitive, or debate-settling finding.
4. Package as a branded report with methodology, key findings, and visualizations.
5. Create assets: full report, summary blog post, infographic, social graphics, press
   release.
6. Pitch leading with the most surprising finding. Offer exclusive early access to Tier 1.

Survey platforms and costs: Pollfish ($1-3/response), SurveyMonkey Audience ($2-5),
Prolific ($1-4), Cint ($1-5). Budget $500-$5,000 for a robust study.

**Reactive PR / Newsjacking**

Speed is everything. First-hour responses to breaking news earn 60%+ higher placement
rates than responses sent after the first day (Digital Applied 2026). Framework:

1. Monitor: Google Alerts, Feedly, X lists, industry newsletters, Google Trends.
2. Assess fit: Does the brand have genuine expertise? Real value to add?
3. React fast: Draft expert commentary within 1-2 hours.
4. Pitch: Email journalists covering the story with pre-written, quotable commentary.
5. Follow up: If the story has legs, publish a longer analysis and pitch as follow-up.

**Expert Commentary**

Position spokespeople as go-to sources for journalists covering their beat. This builds
a recurring relationship where journalists reach out proactively. Prepare a stable of
2-3 pre-approved experts with distinct domains. Maintain a "rapid response" document
with pre-cleared quotes on common industry topics that can be adapted in under 30
minutes.

**Digital PR Campaign Formats**

| Format | Median Links | Cost Range | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Data study / survey | 20-50 | $2,000-$15,000 | 4-8 weeks |
| Interactive tool / calculator | 30-80 | $5,000-$25,000 | 6-12 weeks |
| Expert roundup (10-20 experts) | 10-25 | $500-$2,000 | 2-4 weeks |
| Newsjacking commentary | 3-10 | $0-$500 | Hours-1 day |
| Annual index / ranking | 25-100 | $3,000-$20,000 | 4-8 weeks |
| Visual asset / infographic | 5-20 | $500-$3,000 | 1-3 weeks |

### 1.3 Link Quality Assessment

Not all PR links are equal. Assess quality using:

- **Domain Authority / Domain Rating**: DA 70+ is excellent, DA 50-69 is good, DA 30-49
  is acceptable for niche relevance.
- **Dofollow vs nofollow**: Dofollow passes link equity. Many top-tier outlets use
  nofollow, but nofollow links from major publications still carry brand value and
  referral traffic.
- **Placement quality**: In-content editorial links >> sidebar/footer >> author bio.
- **Topical relevance**: A DA 50 link from a relevant industry publication often
  outperforms a DA 80 link from a general outlet for topical authority.
- **Anchor text**: Natural, brand-name or descriptive anchors are ideal. Avoid
  optimized exact-match anchors, which look manipulative.

### 1.4 PR-SEO Coordination

Coordinate with SEO strategy to maximize link value:

- **Target page alignment**: Pitch stories that naturally link to priority SEO pages,
  not just the homepage.
- **Anchor text distribution**: Let journalists choose naturally. Do not request
  specific anchors -- this damages relationships and violates editorial standards.
- **Topical authority**: Consistent coverage in a niche signals expertise to search
  engines. 10 links from relevant niche outlets can outperform 1 link from a
  general Tier-1 outlet for topical ranking.
- **Content synergy**: Data studies and interactive tools serve as linkable assets for
  broader link building campaigns beyond the initial PR push.

## 2. Journalist Relations

### 2.1 How Journalists Find Sources (2025-2026)

Based on Muck Rack's State of Journalism surveys and Cision's Global State of the
Media reports:

- **73% of journalists** say they are pitched too many irrelevant stories. Relevance
  to their beat is the number one factor in deciding whether to open a pitch.
- **68% prefer email** as the primary pitch channel. X/Twitter is a distant second for
  initial contact. LinkedIn DMs are gaining traction (used by ~30% of journalists).
- **52% of journalists** now use AI tools for research, fact-checking, or draft
  assistance, but editorial standards and human judgment remain dominant.
- **60%+ of journalists** spend less than 1 minute deciding whether to read a pitch
  beyond the subject line and first sentence.
- **Personalization matters**: Pitches that reference the journalist's recent work see
  2-3x higher response rates than generic blasts.

Source discovery channels journalists use:

1. Their existing source network and Rolodex
2. Social media (X/Twitter, LinkedIn)
3. Google search and Google News
4. Journalist request platforms (HARO, Qwoted, Source of Sources)
5. PR pitches and press releases
6. Industry events and conferences

### 2.2 Pitch Preferences

What journalists want in a pitch:

- **Short subject lines**: 6-10 words. Include the news hook, not the company name.
  Subject lines under 36 characters perform best (Propel Media Barometer Q4 2025).
- **Brief body**: 150-300 words maximum. Lead with the story, not the company.
- **Data and specifics**: Concrete numbers, percentages, and findings. Not superlatives
  or vague claims.
- **Ready-to-use assets**: High-resolution images, charts, data tables, pre-written
  quotes. Make coverage easy.
- **Clear ask**: What are you offering? Exclusive data, interview, expert quote, demo?
- **No attachments on cold pitch**: Embed everything in the email body or link out.
  Attachments lower open rates and trigger spam filters.

What journalists do not want:

- Mass-blast pitches with no personalization
- "Just checking in" follow-ups that add no new value
- Embargoed stories they never agreed to receive
- Phone calls unless previously arranged
- Pitches on their personal social media
- Jargon-heavy corporate language

### 2.3 Building Journalist Relationships

Long-term relationships are the single most effective way to earn consistent coverage.
Framework:

**Phase 1 -- Pre-pitch (2-4 weeks before first pitch)**:

- Follow the journalist on X/Twitter and LinkedIn.
- Read their last 10-15 articles to understand their beat, angle preferences, and
  publication style.
- Engage genuinely: Share their articles, leave thoughtful comments, quote their work
  in your own content.
- Note what stories they cover, what sources they cite, and what angles they favor.

**Phase 2 -- First contact (the pitch)**:

- Reference a specific recent article with a genuine, non-generic compliment.
- Offer something valuable: data, expert access, exclusive information.
- Keep it short and make the journalist's job easy.
- Accept "no" gracefully. Not every pitch fits.

**Phase 3 -- Post-coverage relationship maintenance**:

- Thank them. Share the article across your channels and tag them.
- Continue engaging with their work on social media.
- Send relevant tips or data even when you do not need coverage.
- Become a reliable resource, not just a pitch sender.
- Check in quarterly with a genuine note, not a pitch.

### 2.4 Embargoes and Exclusives

**Embargoes**: An agreement that the journalist can access the news early but cannot
publish until a specified date/time. Rules:

- Only offer embargoes for genuinely significant news (funding rounds, major product
  launches, original research).
- Get explicit agreement before sharing embargoed material.
- If a journalist declines the embargo, do not send the material.
- Honor the embargo date absolutely. Breaking an embargo destroys trust permanently.
- Send a reminder 24 hours before the embargo lifts.

**Exclusives**: Offering one journalist/outlet the story before anyone else. Rules:

- Exclusives yield the highest placement rates (25-40%) but limit reach.
- Choose the outlet whose audience best matches your target.
- Be explicit: "We are offering this exclusively to [Outlet]. Is [Journalist] interested?"
- Once accepted, honor it. Do not pitch the same story elsewhere before publication.
- After exclusive publication, you can pitch the story more broadly as a follow-up.

### 2.5 Pitch Timing

| Strategy | When to Use | Lead Time |
|---|---|---|
| Newsjacking | Breaking news relevant to brand expertise | Hours, same day |
| Seasonal / holiday | Predictable annual hooks | 4-8 weeks |
| Product launch | New product, feature, or service | 2-4 weeks |
| Data / research | Original study or survey results | 2-3 weeks |
| Trend commentary | Emerging industry trend | 1-2 weeks |
| Embargo | Major news, exclusive access | 1-2 weeks before release |
| Long-lead print | Magazine features, annual roundups | 3-6 months |

Best days to pitch: Tuesday through Thursday, 8:00-10:00 AM in the journalist's local
timezone. Avoid Monday mornings (inbox overload), Friday afternoons (weekend), and days
with major news events that will dominate the cycle.

Propel Media Barometer data (Q4 2025) shows pitch open rates are highest on Tuesday
(38%) and Wednesday (36%), with response rates peaking on Wednesday and Thursday.

## 3. HARO / Connectively / Qwoted

### 3.1 Platform Landscape (2025-2026)

The journalist source-request ecosystem has evolved significantly. HARO (acquired by
Cision, rebranded to Connectively, then relaunched as HARO under new ownership in 2025)
now competes with several mature alternatives.

**Platform Comparison**:

| Platform | Request Volume | Quality (DR 80+) | Best For | Free Tier | Paid From |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| HARO (relaunched) | High | Medium | Broad coverage, all industries | Yes (limited) | $19/mo |
| Qwoted | Very high | Highest (most DR 80+ queries) | Business, finance, tech | Yes (limited) | $149/mo |
| Source of Sources (SOS) | Medium | High (36% follow links) | SEO-focused link building | Yes | Free |
| Featured.com | Medium | Medium-high | Automated matching, convenience | No | ~$100/mo |
| Terkel | Medium | Medium | Quick expert quotes | Yes | Free |
| ProfNet (Cision) | Medium | High | Enterprise, established brands | No | Part of Cision suite |
| X/Twitter (#journorequest) | Variable | Variable | Fast, informal requests | Yes | Free |
| LinkedIn | Growing | Variable | B2B, business press | Yes | Free |

Key insight from BuzzStream's 2026 study of 7 journalist request platforms:
- Source of Sources delivers the most follow links (36%), ideal for SEO impact.
- Qwoted dominates for volume and quality, with the highest share of DR 80+ queries.
- HARO has the highest cross-platform overlap, meaning many queries also appear elsewhere.
- X/Twitter and LinkedIn have 82% outlet overlap, making it redundant to monitor both.

### 3.2 Optimizing Response Success Rates

Average HARO/Connectively response-to-placement rate is 5-15%, depending on quality,
speed, and niche relevance. Top responders achieve 15-25% by following these practices:

**Speed is critical**: Respond within 1-2 hours of the query going live. First
responders receive priority. Many journalists select sources within the first 3 hours.
The 60%+ higher placement rate for first-hour responses is consistent across platforms.

**Response structure** (keep under 200 words):

1. **Lead with credentials**: "I'm [Name], [Title] at [Company]. I've [specific
   experience relevant to the query]."
2. **Answer directly**: 2-4 quotable sentences that directly address the journalist's
   question. Write in a voice that can be copied verbatim.
3. **Add a unique angle**: Data point, contrarian take, first-hand experience, or
   specific example. Generic answers get ignored.
4. **Include assets**: Headshot (high-res), bio (2-3 sentences), website URL.
5. **Professional signature**: Name, title, company, email, phone.

**Optimization tactics**:

- Set up keyword alerts across all platforms. Do not rely on browsing.
- Prepare boilerplate bios and credential paragraphs for each expert.
- Keep a "quote bank" of pre-approved commentary on common industry topics.
- Track wins and follow up with journalists who use your quotes -- this often leads to
  direct outreach for future stories.
- Respond to queries even in adjacent niches if you have genuine expertise.
- Avoid queries from anonymous sources or vague publications.

### 3.3 Common Mistakes

- Responding with a sales pitch instead of answering the journalist's question.
- Ignoring the stated word count or format preferences.
- Failing to include credentials or proof of expertise.
- Responding to queries outside your genuine area of expertise.
- Sending responses hours or days after the query was posted.
- Not including a headshot and bio (journalists skip responses without them).

## 4. Thought Leadership

### 4.1 Byline Articles

Byline articles (op-eds, contributed pieces, guest columns) position executives as
industry authorities while earning backlinks and brand exposure. Best practices:

**Where to publish**:

- Tier-1 business press: Forbes Councils, Entrepreneur, Inc., Fast Company (competitive,
  often pay-to-play via contributor programs).
- Industry publications: Target 3-5 publications your audience reads. These are
  often more influential within the niche than general business press.
- LinkedIn Articles / Newsletters: Native distribution indexed by Google. Lower barrier
  but high reach for B2B audiences.

**Writing guidelines**:

- Lead with a strong, specific opinion. Bland thought leadership gets ignored.
- Support every claim with data, examples, or first-hand experience.
- Write in first person. Authentic voice outperforms corporate tone.
- 800-1,200 words for most publications. Check submission guidelines.
- Include 1-2 specific, actionable takeaways the reader can implement.
- Do not make it about your product. Mention your company once in the bio.

**Submission process**:

1. Research the publication's contributor guidelines and recent articles.
2. Pitch the editor with a 200-word summary of your proposed angle.
3. If accepted, deliver on deadline with clean copy.
4. Promote the published piece across all channels.
5. Repurpose the core ideas into social posts, newsletter content, and speaking topics.

### 4.2 Conference Speaking

Speaking positions build authority and create networking opportunities that lead to media
coverage. Progression:

1. **Start local**: Industry meetups, local business events, webinars. Build a talk and
   refine delivery.
2. **Move to virtual**: Webinars, virtual summits, podcast guest appearances. Wider
   reach, lower barrier.
3. **Target regional**: Regional industry conferences, university guest lectures.
4. **Aim national/international**: Major industry conferences, keynotes. Typically
   requires a track record of prior speaking and/or significant industry standing.

**Speaking pitch essentials**:

- Propose a specific talk title and 3-5 key takeaways (not "I'd love to speak").
- Include speaker bio, headshot, previous speaking experience (videos if available).
- Explain why this topic is timely and relevant to the conference audience.
- Offer to customize the talk to the event's theme.
- Apply 6-12 months in advance for major conferences.

### 4.3 Podcast Guesting

Podcasts offer 30-60 minutes of unfiltered exposure, direct trust transfer from host to
guest, and evergreen discoverability via search and podcast directories.

**Target selection**:

- Identify podcasts your target audience listens to, not just the biggest shows.
- Check guest history to ensure your topic is fresh for that show.
- Aim for 2-4 appearances per month to build momentum.

**Pitch structure**:

1. Reference a specific episode with a genuine, non-generic compliment.
2. Propose a topic with 3 specific talking points (not "I'd love to come on").
3. Include 2-sentence bio with relevant credentials.
4. Mention previous podcast appearances (links if available).
5. Offer to promote the episode to your audience.

**Post-appearance**:

- Share clips across social media. Short-form video clips (30-90 seconds) perform
  especially well on LinkedIn and Instagram.
- Repurpose key insights into written content.
- Thank the host and maintain the relationship.

### 4.4 LinkedIn Thought Leadership

LinkedIn is the highest-ROI organic platform for B2B thought leadership in 2025-2026.
Founder posts outperform brand pages 5-10x in reach and engagement.

**Content cadence**:

- 3-5 posts per week from founder/executive personal accounts.
- 1-2 long-form articles or newsletter editions per month.
- Mix: personal stories (highest engagement), data/frameworks (highest saves),
  industry commentary (highest shares), behind-the-scenes (highest trust).

**Best practices**:

- Write in first person with authentic voice. No corporate speak.
- Use line breaks liberally. Dense paragraphs kill mobile engagement.
- Lead with a hook in the first 2 lines (before "see more" fold).
- Include a clear opinion or stance. Neutral posts get ignored.
- Engage in comments on journalist and industry leader posts for visibility.
- Carousels and document posts earn 2-3x more saves than text-only.

## 5. Crisis Communications

### 5.1 The Detect-Assess-Respond-Recover (DARR) Framework

**Detect**:

- Continuous monitoring via Google Alerts, social listening tools (Brandwatch, Mention,
  Brand24), and direct reports from employees/customers.
- Set up real-time alerts for brand name, key personnel, product names, and industry
  crisis keywords.
- Establish internal escalation channels (Slack, phone tree) so front-line staff can
  flag issues immediately.

**Assess**:

- Within 30 minutes of detection, the crisis lead should have a preliminary assessment:
  - What happened? What are the known facts?
  - Who is affected? How many people?
  - What is the current public narrative?
  - What tier is this? (Minor / Moderate / Major)
  - Is there legal, safety, or regulatory exposure?

| Tier | Definition | Response Time | Decision Authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 - Minor | Negative review, isolated complaint, minor social post | 24 hours | Comms lead |
| Tier 2 - Moderate | Viral complaint, negative press article, trending topic | 4-8 hours | Comms + decision maker |
| Tier 3 - Major | Product failure, data breach, legal issue, safety concern | 1-2 hours | Full crisis team |

**Respond**:

- Use the AIRR sub-framework:
  - **Acknowledge**: Within 1-2 hours. Confirm awareness. Do not speculate or blame.
  - **Investigate**: Gather facts internally. Document everything. Brief crisis team.
  - **Respond**: Full public statement when facts are clear. Own the mistake. Explain
    the fix. State prevention measures. Be specific.
  - **Recover**: Follow up with affected parties. Monitor sentiment. Rebuild trust.

**Recover**:

- Post-mortem with all stakeholders when the immediate crisis has passed.
- Publish a transparent follow-up if the crisis was public.
- Monitor sentiment for 30-90 days. One crisis often reveals others.
- Update the crisis plan based on lessons learned.
- Rebuild trust through consistent positive action, not through messaging alone.

### 5.2 Social Media Crisis Management

The "Golden 15 Minutes" rule: In 2025-2026, social media crises can escalate from a
single post to national news in under 60 minutes. The first 15 minutes after a brand
becomes aware of a potential crisis are critical for setting the narrative.

Key principles:

- **Do not delete negative posts** (unless they violate platform policies). Deleted
  posts get screenshotted and shared as evidence of a cover-up.
- **Respond publicly first**, then move to DM for individual resolution.
- **Pause all scheduled content** immediately. A cheerful promotional post during a
  crisis looks tone-deaf.
- **One voice**: All responses should come from a single approved account or
  spokesperson. Mixed messages amplify confusion.
- **Monitor in real time**: Assign a team member to track mentions continuously during
  an active crisis.
- **Assume everything is screenshotted**: Every response, every DM, every internal
  communication may become public.

### 5.3 Reputation Repair

Post-crisis reputation repair follows a predictable timeline:

| Phase | Timeline | Actions |
|---|---|---|
| Immediate | Days 1-7 | Transparent communication, direct outreach to affected parties |
| Short-term | Weeks 2-4 | Visible corrective action, progress updates |
| Medium-term | Months 1-3 | Positive story generation, community investment |
| Long-term | Months 3-12 | Consistent delivery, trust metrics monitoring |

Brands that respond within the first hour and take visible corrective action recover
to pre-crisis sentiment levels 2.5x faster than those that delay (Onclusive 2026).

A February 2025 case study: a major fast-food chain lost an estimated $800 million in
market value after a food safety video went viral -- their PR team did not respond for
six hours. The damage was caused not by the incident alone but by the silence.

## 6. AI Impact on PR

### 6.1 How AI Is Changing PR (2025-2026)

AI adoption in PR is now mainstream. Muck Rack's State of AI in PR report found that
75% of PR professionals use generative AI in their daily tasks. Key areas:

- **Media monitoring**: AI-powered tools (Meltwater, Onclusive, Brandwatch) can process
  millions of mentions in real time, detect sentiment shifts, and flag emerging crises
  faster than human monitoring.
- **Draft assistance**: AI accelerates first drafts of press releases, pitch emails,
  media lists, and social copy. Time savings: 30-50% on routine writing tasks.
- **Media list building**: AI tools (BuzzStream ListIQ, Muck Rack AI) can identify
  relevant journalists based on recent coverage, beat analysis, and social signals.
- **Pitch personalization at scale**: AI can analyze a journalist's last 20 articles
  and suggest personalized angles. But human review is mandatory.
- **Trend identification**: AI can scan thousands of news sources to identify emerging
  trends for newsjacking opportunities.

### 6.2 AI Detection and Journalist Skepticism

Journalists are increasingly skeptical of AI-generated pitches:

- **47% of journalists** say they can detect AI-generated pitches and are less likely
  to respond to them (Cision 2025 Global State of the Media).
- AI-generated pitches lack the specificity, personality, and genuine understanding of
  the journalist's beat that human-crafted pitches convey.
- Some publications now use AI detection tools on submitted byline articles.

**Best practice**: Use AI for research, drafting, and efficiency. Always edit AI outputs
to inject genuine personalization, specific details, and authentic voice. Never send
an unedited AI draft to a journalist.

### 6.3 AI and the Future of Earned Media

New dynamics to account for:

- **AI search citations**: AI assistants (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews)
  now cite sources from news coverage. Brands mentioned in authoritative media coverage
  are more likely to be cited in AI-generated answers. This creates a new value layer
  for PR beyond traditional SEO.
- **AI-optimized press releases**: Structure press releases with clear facts, data
  points, and entity names that AI systems can easily parse and cite.
- **Verification premium**: As AI-generated content floods the internet, journalists
  place a premium on verified, original data and authentic human expertise.
  First-hand experience and proprietary data become even more valuable.

## 7. Digital PR Campaigns

### 7.1 What Earns Links in 2025-2026

Based on BuzzStream's State of Digital PR 2026 and industry campaign analysis:

**High performers**:

- Original data studies with surprising findings (median 24+ links)
- Interactive tools and calculators (high embed/link value)
- Annual indices and rankings (recurring, evergreen campaigns)
- Reactive expert commentary on breaking news (fast turnaround)
- Local/regional data breakdowns (city/state data earns local coverage)

**Declining effectiveness**:

- Generic infographics without original data
- Celebrity-focused studies with no genuine connection to the brand
- Rehashed industry surveys with predictable findings
- "We analyzed X tweets/posts" studies (oversaturated format)

### 7.2 Data Study Best Practices

**Methodology that earns coverage**:

- Minimum 500 respondents for a survey (1,000+ for national claims).
- Clear methodology section with margin of error.
- Segmented data by demographics, region, or other newsworthy dimensions.
- Findings that challenge assumptions or reveal hidden patterns.
- Timely topic connected to current news cycle or cultural moment.

**Packaging for journalists**:

- Lead with 3-5 headline-worthy statistics.
- Create visualizations (charts, maps, infographics) that journalists can embed or
  screenshot.
- Provide a full methodology section for credibility.
- Write a press release with the most surprising finding as the lede.
- Prepare spokesperson quotes contextualizing the data.
- Offer exclusive early access to Tier 1 outlets.

**Distribution**:

1. Tier 1 exclusive (1-2 outlets, 1 week before public release).
2. Tier 2 targeted pitch (20-30 journalists, day of public release).
3. Public launch: press release, blog post, social campaign.
4. Tier 3 follow-up: niche and regional outlets with localized angles.
5. Ongoing: Pitch new angles as the data relates to emerging news.

### 7.3 Interactive Content

Interactive tools (calculators, quizzes, maps, comparison tools) earn links because:

- Journalists can embed or link to them as resources for readers.
- They provide ongoing utility beyond the news cycle.
- They earn passive links over time as people discover and share them.
- They differentiate from standard blog content.

Examples of high-performing interactive PR assets:

- Cost-of-living calculators with city-by-city data
- Industry benchmark comparison tools
- Interactive maps showing regional data
- Self-assessment quizzes tied to research findings

Budget for interactive content ranges from $5,000-$25,000 depending on complexity,
but the link-building ROI often exceeds 10x the investment over 12 months.

### 7.4 Newsjacking Playbook

Timing windows for effective newsjacking:

| Window | Response Type | Success Rate |
|---|---|---|
| 0-2 hours | Expert quote to journalist | Highest |
| 2-6 hours | Short commentary + pitch | High |
| 6-24 hours | Short analysis blog + pitch | Medium |
| 1-3 days | Deep analysis / data response | Medium (for "day 2" stories) |
| 3+ days | Too late for most stories | Low |

Only newsjack when:

- The brand has genuine expertise on the topic.
- The commentary adds real value (not just "we agree").
- The topic aligns with brand values and positioning.
- The spokesperson can be available for follow-up interviews.

## 8. Measurement & Attribution

### 8.1 Core PR Metrics (2025-2026)

The industry is moving away from vanity metrics (AVE, impressions) toward business
impact metrics. Prioritize:

**Tier 1 -- Business impact metrics**:

- Referral traffic from earned media (GA4)
- Backlink quality and quantity (Ahrefs, Moz)
- Domain authority growth attributable to PR
- Lead generation from coverage (UTM-tagged links)
- Share of voice vs competitors

**Tier 2 -- Coverage quality metrics**:

- Real article readership (not impressions -- use Onclusive, CoverageBook, or
  similar platforms that track actual readers)
- Placement tier (Tier 1/2/3)
- Sentiment (positive/neutral/negative)
- Message pull-through (did key messages appear in coverage?)
- Social shares of coverage

**Tier 3 -- Activity metrics**:

- Pitch volume and response rate
- HARO response rate and win rate
- Media list growth
- Journalist relationship pipeline
- Campaign output cadence

### 8.2 Reporting Cadence

- **Per campaign**: Placements by tier, estimated reach, backlinks earned (count + DA
  range), referral traffic, social shares, sentiment, cost per placement.
- **Monthly**: Media mentions trend, new PR backlinks, referral traffic, share of voice,
  sentiment, HARO stats, journalist relationship pipeline.
- **Quarterly**: Domain authority impact, coverage quality trends, PR ROI vs other
  channels, competitive benchmarking, strategy adjustments.

### 8.3 Coverage Quality Benchmarks (CoverageBook 2025)

From CoverageBook's analysis of 6 million pieces of global coverage:

- 15% of coverage in 2024 came from DA 90+ sites (up from 1.5% in 2020, a 10x increase).
- 67% of coverage receives zero social shares. Only 19% receives 10+ shares.
- PR teams achieving DA 90+ coverage are in the top 15% of performers.
- High-DA coverage correlates strongly with referral traffic and SEO impact.

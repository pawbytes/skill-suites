## 1. Pitch Success Rates

### 1.1 Response Rates by Pitch Type

| Pitch Type | Open Rate | Response Rate | Placement Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cold pitch (no prior relationship) | 30-45% | 1-3% | 0.5-1.5% | Personalization lifts response by 47% |
| Warm pitch (prior engagement) | 50-65% | 10-20% | 5-12% | Relationship quality is primary driver |
| Warm pitch (established relationship) | 65-80% | 15-30% | 10-20% | Ongoing relationship with journalist |
| Exclusive offer | 60-75% | 25-40% | 20-35% | Highest conversion, limits reach |
| Embargo pitch | 55-70% | 20-35% | 15-30% | Requires trust and established rapport |
| Data study pitch | 40-55% | 8-15% | 5-10% | Strength of data determines response |
| Expert commentary (reactive) | 45-60% | 15-30% | 10-25% | Speed is critical; first-hour premium |
| Newsjacking pitch | 50-65% | 15-25% | 10-20% | Must reach journalist within 2 hours |

Sources: Digital Applied 2026, Propel Media Barometer Q4 2025, BuzzStream 2026,
industry composite data.

### 1.2 Factors That Increase Response Rates

| Factor | Impact on Response Rate | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Personalized opening (specific article reference) | +47% vs generic | Backlink Grid 2026 |
| Short subject line (under 36 characters) | +18% vs long subject | Propel Barometer Q4 2025 |
| Sent Tuesday-Thursday | +23% vs Monday/Friday | Backlink Grid 2026 |
| Sent 8-10 AM journalist timezone | +15% vs afternoon/evening | Propel Barometer |
| Includes specific data points | +35% vs no data | Industry composite |
| Follow-up at Day 3-4 | +22% of all positive responses come from follow-ups | Backlink Grid 2026 |
| Previous journalist interaction | +200-400% vs cold | Industry composite |

### 1.3 Pitch Volume Benchmarks

| Metric | Benchmark | Context |
|---|---|---|
| Pitches per campaign | 30-50 targeted | Quality over quantity |
| Pitches per journalist per quarter | Maximum 4-6 | More than this damages relationships |
| Follow-ups per pitch | Maximum 2 | Beyond 2 is counterproductive |
| Media list size (per campaign) | 30-50 contacts | 50-100 for major launches |
| Pitch-to-placement ratio (cold) | 30:1 to 50:1 | 30-50 pitches for 1 placement |
| Pitch-to-placement ratio (warm) | 5:1 to 10:1 | Relationships matter |

## 3. Distribution Costs & Results

### 3.1 Wire Service Pricing (2026)

| Service | Base Price | Coverage | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| PR Newswire (Cision) | $350-$1,500 (US) | National/global, AP feed | Enterprise, compliance, broad reach |
| Business Wire (Berkshire) | $400-$1,500 (US) | National/global, financial | IR, SEC compliance, earnings |
| GlobeNewswire | $350-$1,200 | National/global | Tech, mid-market |
| EIN Presswire | $100-$250 | National, niche targeting | Budget, startups, trade press |
| Send2Press | $99-$399 | National, targeted industry | Small business, targeted reach |
| Newswire.com | $149-$699 | National, digital-first | Digital-native distribution |
| Pressonify.ai | $29-$99 | AI-optimized, citation tracking | AI search optimization |

Notes:
- Premium add-ons (multimedia, geographic targeting, industry targeting) can add
  $200-$2,000+ per release on major wires.
- PR Newswire and Business Wire both cost $1,000+ minimum for meaningful national
  distribution with multimedia.
- Budget wires ($100-$500) are effective for trade press and niche distribution.
- Wire distribution should always be paired with direct journalist outreach.

### 3.2 Distribution Results Benchmarks

| Method | Avg Pickups | Avg Backlinks | Cost per Placement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Targeted email pitch (30-50 contacts) | 3-8 | 2-5 | $0 (time only) |
| Premium wire (PR Newswire, Business Wire) | 50-200 syndications | 10-30 | $30-$100 |
| Budget wire (EIN, Send2Press) | 20-80 syndications | 5-15 | $10-$40 |
| Digital PR agency campaign | 10-50 earned placements | 15-60 | $200-$1,000 |

Important distinction: Wire "pickups" are mostly automated syndications (low value).
Earned placements from targeted pitching are editorially chosen (high value). A single
earned Tier-1 placement is worth more than 100 wire syndications.

## 7. Crisis Timeline Benchmarks

### 7.1 Response Time Standards

| Phase | Best Practice | Industry Average | Worst Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| Crisis detection | 15-30 minutes | 1-2 hours | 6+ hours |
| Internal assessment | 30-60 minutes | 2-4 hours | 12+ hours |
| Holding statement (public) | 1-2 hours | 4-8 hours | 24+ hours |
| Full response statement | 4-8 hours | 12-24 hours | 48+ hours |
| Stakeholder notification | 2-4 hours | 8-12 hours | 24+ hours |

### 7.2 Crisis Impact Benchmarks

| Metric | Fast Response (<2 hrs) | Delayed Response (6+ hrs) | No Response (24+ hrs) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sentiment recovery time | 1-2 weeks | 4-8 weeks | 3-6 months |
| Stock price impact (public co) | -2 to -5% | -5 to -15% | -10 to -30%+ |
| Customer trust recovery | 70-85% within 30 days | 40-60% within 90 days | <30% within 6 months |
| Media cycle duration | 1-3 days | 1-2 weeks | 2-4+ weeks |
| Social media amplification | Contained | 5-10x viral spread | Uncontrolled spiral |

Fast response brands recover to pre-crisis sentiment levels 2.5x faster than those
that delay beyond 6 hours (Onclusive 2026).

### 7.3 Crisis Preparedness Checklist Benchmarks

| Preparedness Element | % of Companies With This |
|---|---|
| Written crisis communication plan | 62% |
| Designated crisis spokesperson | 55% |
| Pre-approved holding statements | 35% |
| Crisis simulation / drill (annual) | 28% |
| Real-time social monitoring | 45% |
| Stakeholder notification system | 40% |
| Post-crisis review process | 30% |

Companies with comprehensive crisis plans recover 40% faster and suffer 30% less
reputational damage than those without (Onclusive 2026).

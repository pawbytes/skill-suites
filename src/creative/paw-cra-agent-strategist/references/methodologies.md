# Strategic Methodologies Reference

Industry-standard frameworks and methodologies for research, content strategy, script writing, and copy creation. Use these to ground recommendations in proven approaches.

---

## Competitor Analysis Frameworks

Choose the right framework based on the decision you're trying to make.

### Quick Selection Guide

| Situation | Recommended Framework |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Fast competitive overview | SWOT Analysis |
| Entering a new market | Porter's Five Forces |
| Too many competitors to track | Strategic Group Analysis |
| Repositioning your brand | Perceptual Mapping |
| Need quantitative comparison | Competitive Profile Matrix |
| Understanding operational advantages | Value Chain Analysis |
| Facing high uncertainty | Scenario Planning |

---

### SWOT Analysis (Done Right)

**Purpose:** Quick competitive diagnostic focusing on actionable insights.

**The Mistake:** Generic platitudes like "Strong brand" or "Good customer service."

**The Right Way:**
- Make it competitor-focused and specific
- Include measurable comparisons
- Every finding should inform a decision

**Template:**
```markdown
## SWOT: {Competitor Name}

### Strengths
- {Specific strength with evidence}
- {Specific strength with evidence}

### Weaknesses
- {Specific weakness with evidence}
- {Specific weakness with evidence}

### Opportunities (for us)
- {Gap they're leaving open}
- {Market shift we can exploit}

### Threats (from them)
- {Specific competitive risk}
- {Specific competitive risk}

### Strategic Implications
- What this means for our positioning
- What this means for our content strategy
```

**Example:**
- Strength: 47 integrations vs. our 12
- Weakness: Setup takes 3 weeks vs. our 24 hours; 38% of reviews mention poor support
- Opportunity: Rebranding upmarket leaves SMB customers underserved
- Threat: Well-funded, aggressive marketing

### Porter's Five Forces

**Purpose:** Understand industry structure and whether a market is attractive.

**The Five Forces:**
1. **Threat of new entrants** — How easy is it for new competitors to enter?
2. **Bargaining power of suppliers** — How much leverage do suppliers have?
3. **Bargaining power of buyers** — How much leverage do customers have?
4. **Threat of substitutes** — What alternatives exist?
5. **Rivalry among existing competitors** — How intense is the competition?

**Template:**
```markdown
## Porter's Five Forces: {Industry}

### Threat of New Entrants
- **Rating:** {Low/Medium/High}
- **Evidence:** {barriers to entry, capital requirements, regulations}

### Bargaining Power of Suppliers
- **Rating:** {Low/Medium/High}
- **Evidence:** {supplier concentration, switching costs}

### Bargaining Power of Buyers
- **Rating:** {Low/Medium/High}
- **Evidence:** {buyer concentration, price sensitivity, alternatives}

### Threat of Substitutes
- **Rating:** {Low/Medium/High}
- **Evidence:** {alternative solutions, switching costs}

### Rivalry Among Competitors
- **Rating:** {Low/Medium/High}
- **Evidence:** {number of competitors, industry growth rate, differentiation}

### Strategic Implications
- Is this market structurally attractive?
- Where are the structural weaknesses we can exploit?
```

**Modern Note:** Add a sixth force for platform effects and network dynamics.

### Strategic Group Analysis

**Purpose:** Find white space in crowded markets by mapping competitors on strategic dimensions.

**How It Works:**
1. Pick two dimensions that matter (price vs. features, enterprise vs. SMB, simple vs. powerful)
2. Plot every competitor on a 2×2 matrix
3. Look for clusters (strategic groups)
4. Look for empty spaces (white space)

**Template:**
```markdown
## Strategic Group Map: {Industry}

**Dimension X:** {e.g., Product Complexity (Simple ↔ Complex)}
**Dimension Y:** {e.g., Target Customer (Freelancers ↔ Enterprise)}

### Quadrant Analysis

**Top-Left (Complex + Freelancer):**
- {Competitors in this space}
- **Assessment:** {crowded/empty/emerging}

**Top-Right (Complex + Enterprise):**
- {Competitors in this space}
- **Assessment:** {crowded/empty/emerging}

**Bottom-Left (Simple + Freelancer):**
- {Competitors in this space}
- **Assessment:** {crowded/empty/emerging}

**Bottom-Right (Simple + Enterprise):**
- {Competitors in this space}
- **Assessment:** {crowded/empty/emerging}

### White Space Opportunity
- **Location:** {which quadrant is empty}
- **Why it exists:** {market dynamics}
- **Our positioning:** {how we capture this space}
```

### Perceptual Mapping

**Purpose:** Understand how customers perceive brands vs. how we want to be perceived.

**Process:**
1. Choose two attributes customers care about (affordable vs. premium, simple vs. powerful)
2. Analyze reviews/surveys to understand actual perception
3. Plot brands on the map
4. Identify gaps between intended and actual positioning

**Template:**
```markdown
## Perceptual Map: {Category}

**Dimension X:** {Attribute 1}
**Dimension Y:** {Attribute 2}

### Our Intended Position
- Where we want to be perceived

### Our Actual Position (from customer data)
- Where customers actually see us
- Evidence: {review quotes, survey responses}

### Competitor Positions
- {Competitor}: {position on map}

### Perception Gap Analysis
- **Gap:** {difference between intended and actual}
- **Cause:** {why the gap exists}
- **Fix:** {messaging/positioning changes needed}
```

### Competitive Profile Matrix (CPM)

**Purpose:** Quantitative comparison across critical success factors.

**Process:**
1. Identify critical success factors (6-10 factors)
2. Assign weights to each factor (total = 1.0)
3. Rate each company on each factor (1-4 scale: 1=major weakness, 4=major strength)
4. Multiply rating × weight for weighted score
5. Sum for total competitive strength

**Template:**
```markdown
## Competitive Profile Matrix: {Category}

| Success Factor | Weight | Us (rating) | Us (score) | {Comp A} (rating) | {Comp A} (score) | {Comp B} (rating) | {Comp B} (score) |
|----------------|--------|-------------|------------|-------------------|------------------|-------------------|------------------|
| Product Quality | 0.15 | 4 | 0.60 | 3 | 0.45 | 3 | 0.45 |
| Price | 0.10 | 2 | 0.20 | 3 | 0.30 | 4 | 0.40 |
| Brand Reputation | 0.15 | 3 | 0.45 | 4 | 0.60 | 2 | 0.30 |
| Customer Service | 0.20 | 4 | 0.80 | 2 | 0.40 | 3 | 0.60 |
| Innovation | 0.15 | 3 | 0.45 | 4 | 0.60 | 2 | 0.30 |
| Distribution | 0.10 | 2 | 0.20 | 4 | 0.40 | 3 | 0.30 |
| Marketing | 0.15 | 3 | 0.45 | 4 | 0.60 | 3 | 0.45 |
| **TOTAL** | **1.00** | — | **3.15** | — | **3.35** | — | **2.80** |

### Key Insights
- Our competitive advantage: {highest-scoring factors}
- Our vulnerability: {lowest-scoring factors}
- Priority improvements: {factors with biggest gap to competitors}
```

## Content Strategy Frameworks

### Content Pillar Strategy

**Purpose:** Build topical authority through pillar content supported by cluster content.

**What It Is:**
- **Pillar Content:** Comprehensive, authoritative content covering a broad topic
- **Cluster Content:** Specific, detailed content covering subtopics
- **Internal Linking:** Cluster content links back to pillar, pillar links to clusters

**Selecting Pillars:**
1. Align with business expertise and audience needs
2. Broad enough to support multiple cluster pieces
3. Specific enough to build clear authority
4. Typically 3-5 pillars for most brands

**Template:**
```markdown
## Content Pillar Structure

### Pillar 1: {Pillar Name}
**Description:** {What this pillar covers}
**Audience:** {Who this serves}
**Pillar Content:** {Main comprehensive piece}

**Cluster Topics:**
- {Subtopic 1} → {content piece}
- {Subtopic 2} → {content piece}
- {Subtopic 3} → {content piece}

### Pillar 2: {Pillar Name}
...

### Pillar 3: {Pillar Name}
...
```

## Video Script Frameworks

### The 7-Part Retention Framework

**Core Principle:** YouTube rewards percentage of video watched, not total watch time. Structure scripts to keep viewers throughout.

**The Framework:**

| Part | Timing | Purpose | Example |
|------|--------|---------|---------|
| **1. Hook** | 0-8 sec | Pattern interrupt, break scroll momentum | "Everything you've been told about [topic] is backwards." |
| **2. Anti-Hook** | 8-15 sec | Acknowledge skepticism, build trust | "I know that sounds like clickbait. I thought the same thing until I saw the data." |
| **3. Promise** | 15-30 sec | Specific outcome + capability | "By the end, you'll have a complete framework you can use immediately." |
| **4. Preview + Open Loops** | 30-60 sec | Create curiosity gaps | "There's one technique at the 6-minute mark that changed everything for me." |
| **5. Core Content** | 60-80% | Deliver value with pattern interrupts every 45-90 sec | See Pattern Interrupt techniques below |
| **6. Value Bomb** | 60-70% mark | Your single best insight placed before drop-off point | The insight that changes everything |
| **7. Soft CTA** | Final 10% | Natural next step, not begging | "Next week I'm covering X. Subscribe if you want to see it." |

### Hook Formulas

**The Contradiction Hook:**
```
"Everyone says [common advice]. But the data shows [opposite]."
```

**The Specific Number Hook:**
```
"After analyzing [specific number] [things], I found [unexpected insight]."
```

**The Identity Hook:**
```
"If you're a [identity] who struggles with [problem], this changes everything."
```

**The Mistake Hook:**
```
"I made this mistake for [time period]. It cost me [specific consequence]."
```

### Pattern Interrupt Techniques

Use every 45-90 seconds to re-engage wandering attention:

- **"Here's what most people miss"** pivot — signals insider knowledge
- **5-10 second personal story** — humanizes content
- **Direct address** — "Now, you might be thinking..."
- **Visual change** — different angle, location, b-roll
- **Energy shift** — speed up or slow down deliberately
- **Unexpected question** — reframe the conversation

### Value Bomb Placement

**Why 60-70%:** This is where viewers typically drop off. Placing your best insight here keeps them watching through the exit zone.

**Wrong:** Front-load best content → viewers leave after getting value
**Wrong:** Save for conclusion → 70% never reach it
**Right:** Place at 60-70% mark → maximum viewers see your best insight

## Copywriting Formulas

### AIDA (Classic Funnel Framework)

**Best For:** Landing pages, sales pages, long-form ads

| Stage | Purpose | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **Attention** | Grab with compelling headline | "Is Your Website Invisible to 95% of Potential Customers?" |
| **Interest** | Build engagement with facts/stories | "Studies show page one captures 92% of all web traffic..." |
| **Desire** | Paint the transformation | "Imagine qualified leads finding your site every day..." |
| **Action** | Clear, direct CTA | "Book Your Free SEO Audit Today" |

---

### PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)

**Best For:** Email sequences, social ads, problem-aware audiences

| Stage | Purpose | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **Problem** | State the core problem clearly | "Struggling to Get Approved for Google AdSense?" |
| **Agitate** | Emotionally amplify the pain | "Rejection after rejection costs real revenue. Every day without ads is lost passive income. The vague 'low-value content' messages feel impossible..." |
| **Solution** | Present as the obvious answer | "Our AdSense Approval Service audits, edits, and optimizes with a 95% success rate." |

---

### BAB (Before-After-Bridge)

**Best For:** Case studies, video sales letters, service pages

| Stage | Purpose | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **Before** | Paint the painful current state | "Your blog is a ghost town. Sporadic posts generate zero leads." |
| **After** | Paint the transformed future | "Your blog is your top sales rep, attracting ideal clients who trust you before they call." |
| **Bridge** | Explain how you get them there | "Our Content Marketing service provides the strategy and writing to make that transition." |

---

### The 4 C's (Clarity + Connection)

**Best For:** Value propositions, service descriptions, subject lines

| C | Question | Example |
|---|----------|---------|
| **Clear** | Is it immediately understandable? | "You need a fast website that converts visitors." |
| **Concise** | Is every word necessary? | "Custom websites with 2-second load times." |
| **Compelling** | Does it create desire? | "Turn visitors into customers with blazing-fast design." |
| **Credible** | Is there proof? | "Average load time under 2 seconds. See our Portfolio." |

## Trend Analysis Methodology

### Signal Detection Process

1. **Broad Scan** — Search "{industry} trends 2026", monitor platform trend reports
2. **Platform-Specific** — TikTok Creative Center, Instagram Trend Reports, YouTube Trends
3. **Cultural Signals** — What's entering mainstream? What's declining? What's niche but growing?
4. **Relevance Filter** — Which trends fit brand voice? Align with audience? Have staying power?

### Trend Classification

| Category | Definition | Action |
|----------|------------|--------|
| **Fad** | Quick spike, short lifespan (<3 months) | Jump on quickly if aligned, don't over-invest |
| **Trend** | Sustained growth (6-18 months) | Plan content series, build expertise |
| **Movement** | Long-term shift (2+ years) | Build brand authority, create cornerstone content |
| **Declining** | Past peak, losing relevance | Avoid or use ironically |

### Trend Report Structure
```markdown
# Trend Analysis: {Brand/Industry}

## Executive Summary
{2-3 key insights}

## Emerging Trends
### {Trend 1}
- **What it is:** {description}
- **Why it matters:** {relevance to audience}
- **How to leverage:** {specific content ideas}
- **Velocity:** {Growing/Stable/Declining}
- **Classification:** {Fad/Trend/Movement}

## Declining Trends
- {Trend to avoid and why}

## Platform-Specific Insights
### TikTok
- {trending formats, sounds, hooks}

### Instagram
- {trending formats, aesthetics}

### YouTube
- {trending formats, topics}

## Priority Matrix
| Trend | Relevance | Ease | Impact | Priority |
|-------|-----------|------|--------|----------|
| {trend} | {H/M/L} | {Easy/Med/Hard} | {H/M/L} | {1-5} |
```

## Choosing the Right Approach

When in doubt, ask: **"What decision am I trying to make?"**

- **Repositioning?** → Perceptual Mapping
- **Entering new market?** → Porter's Five Forces + Strategic Group Analysis
- **Need to benchmark?** → Competitive Profile Matrix
- **Facing uncertainty?** → Scenario Planning
- **Writing video scripts?** → 7-Part Retention Framework
- **Writing copy?** → AIDA (general), PAS (pain-aware), BAB (transformation)
- **Building content strategy?** → Content Pillar framework
- **Analyzing trends?** → Signal Detection + Classification

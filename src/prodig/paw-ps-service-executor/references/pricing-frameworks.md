# Pricing Frameworks

## Purpose

Match pricing models to service types and market position. Design pricing that captures value while remaining attractive to buyers.

## Pricing Models

### 1. Fixed Project Pricing

**Best for:** Defined scope, clear deliverables, predictable timeline

```text
Structure:
- Single price for entire project
- Payment terms (e.g., 50% upfront, 50% on completion)
- Clear scope boundaries

Advantages:
- Easy for client to budget
- Clear expectations
- Simple proposal process

Risks:
- Scope creep without change orders
- Underestimating complexity
- Client delays impact margins
```

**Example:**
```text
Website Redesign Package: $8,500
Payment: 50% ($4,250) to start, 50% ($4,250) on launch
Timeline: 8 weeks
```

### 2. Tiered Pricing

**Best for:** Serving multiple client segments with one service concept

```text
Structure:
- 3 tiers: Good, Better, Best (or DIY, DWY, DFY)
- Each tier offers clear value progression
- Middle tier is often the target

Psychology:
- Anchors high tier to make middle feel reasonable
- Low tier serves budget-conscious or DIY clients
- Clear upgrade path
```

**Example:**
```text
Content Strategy Package

Starter ($1,500)
- Content audit
- 3-month content calendar
- Topic suggestions

Standard ($4,500) ← Target Tier
- Everything in Starter
- Content pillars development
- 12 blog post outlines
- 2 revision rounds

Premium ($9,500)
- Everything in Standard
- Full blog post drafts (12)
- Email sequence (5 emails)
- Social media content calendar
- 4 strategy calls
```

### 3. Retainer Pricing

**Best for:** Ongoing services, predictable revenue, long-term relationships

```text
Structure:
- Monthly fee for defined scope
- Rolling contract (month-to-month) or term (3-6-12 months)
- Clear scope per month

Advantages:
- Predictable revenue
- Deeper client relationships
- Easier capacity planning

Risks:
- Scope creep over time
- Need clear rollover policy
- Client may want more than scoped
```

**Example:**
```text
Marketing Partner Retainer: $5,000/month

Monthly Scope:
- Up to 20 hours execution
- 2 strategy calls (60 min each)
- Priority email response (within 4 hours)
- Monthly performance report

Terms:
- 3-month minimum commitment
- Unused hours do not roll over
- Additional hours: $250/hour
```

### 4. Value-Based Pricing

**Best for:** High-impact services where outcome value far exceeds delivery cost

```text
Structure:
- Price based on value created for client
- Often percentage of revenue or savings
- Requires understanding client economics

Advantages:
- Captures true value
- Aligns incentives
- Premium positioning

Risks:
- Requires client trust
- May need outcome guarantees
- Harder to predict revenue
```

**Example:**
```text
Conversion Rate Optimization

Pricing: 20% of first-year revenue lift
Minimum engagement: $10,000

Example:
Current revenue: $1M/year
After optimization: $1.3M/year
Lift: $300,000
Fee: $60,000 (paid over 6 months)
```

### 5. Performance Pricing

**Best for:** Services with measurable, attributable outcomes

```text
Structure:
- Base fee + performance bonus
- Tied to specific metrics
- Shared risk/reward

Advantages:
- Client confidence (skin in the game)
- Potential for higher earnings
- Clear success definition

Risks:
- Need attribution clarity
- External factors affect results
- Cash flow variability
```

**Example:**
```text
Google Ads Management

Base: $2,000/month
Performance bonus: 10% of ad spend saved vs. previous agency
Guaranteed: If CPA doesn't improve by 20% in 3 months, full refund
```

## Pricing Calculation Methods

### Cost-Plus (Baseline)

```text
Hourly rate: $X
Hours to deliver: Y
─────────────────────────
Minimum price: $X × Y

Add margin for:
- Client communication (20% buffer)
- Revisions (10-20% buffer)
- Profit margin (30-50%)
```

### Value-Anchored (Target)

```text
Client outcome value: $X
Willingness to pay: 10-25% of outcome value
─────────────────────────
Target price: $X × (10-25%)

Example:
Client will make $100K from this project
They'd happily pay $10K–$25K for it
Price accordingly
```

### Market-Referenced (Validation)

```text
Competitor pricing: Research what others charge
Differentiation: Where you command premium or need to discount
Positioning: Budget / Mid-market / Premium

Decision: Price at, above, or below market based on positioning
```

## Pricing Psychology

### Anchoring

```text
Show highest tier first
Makes middle tier feel reasonable

Example:
Premium: $15,000
Standard: $7,500 ← Looks like a deal
Basic: $3,000
```

### Good-Better-Best

```text
Three options optimal
Most choose middle
Position your target tier as middle

Good: Sufficient but limited
Better: Full solution (target)
Best: Premium with extras
```

### Charm Pricing

```text
$4,997 instead of $5,000
Psychological perception of significantly less
Use for consumer-facing or lower-priced services

For premium B2B services, round numbers often signal quality
$15,000 not $14,997
```

### Payment Options

```text
Single payment: Simplest, often discounted
Payment plan: Higher total, lower barrier
(e.g., 3 payments of $1,750 vs. $4,997 single)

Options:
- Full pay: $4,997 (save $250)
- 3-pay: $1,750/month
```

## Pricing Communication

### How to Present Pricing

```markdown
## Investment

**[Service Name]** — $[Price]

**Payment Options:**
- Pay in full: $[Amount] (save $[X])
- Payment plan: $[X]/month for [N] months

**What's Included:**
- [Deliverable 1]
- [Deliverable 2]
- [Deliverable 3]
- [Support element]

**Total Value:** $[X] (itemized)
**Your Investment:** $[Y]

**Money-Back Guarantee:** [If applicable]
```

### Addressing Price Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Too expensive" | Reframe on value: "Let's look at what this returns for you..." |
| "Can I get a discount?" | Offer payment plan or scope reduction, not discount |
| "Competitor is cheaper" | Differentiate on outcome, not price |
| "Not in budget" | Explore phased approach or scaled-down version |
| "Need to think about it" | Clarify decision factors, offer time-sensitive bonus |

## Pricing Tiers by Service Type

### Consulting/Advisory

| Tier | Price Range | Characteristics |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| Hourly | $150–$500/hr | Ad-hoc, no commitment |
| Project | $2K–$50K | Defined scope, deliverable |
| Retainer | $2K–$25K/mo | Ongoing access, recurring |
| Equity | Varies | Skin in the game, long-term |

### Done-For-You Services

| Tier | Price Range | Characteristics |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| Template/DIY | $500–$2K | Productized asset |
| Done-With-You | $2K–$10K | Coaching + guidance |
| Done-For-You | $5K–$100K+ | Full execution |

### Retainer Services

| Tier | Price Range | Typical Scope |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| Lite | $1K–$3K/mo | Limited hours, async mostly |
| Standard | $3K–$10K/mo | Full scope, regular calls |
| Premium | $10K–$50K+/mo | Full team, priority, custom |

## Pricing Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Pricing too low | Attracts wrong clients, undervalues work | Calculate value delivered, price accordingly |
| No scope boundaries | Scope creep erodes margins | Define what's NOT included |
| Single option | No comparison, harder decision | Offer 2-3 tiers |
| Hiding price | Trust issues, wasted conversations | Be transparent |
| Discounting easily | Devalues service | Offer payment plans instead |
| Ignoring market | Out of step with expectations | Research competitors |

## Pricing Review Checklist

Before finalizing pricing:

- [ ] Calculated minimum viable price (cost-plus with margin)
- [ ] Researched competitor pricing
- [ ] Estimated value to client
- [ ] Considered tier options
- [ ] Defined payment terms
- [ ] Established scope boundaries
- [ ] Prepared response to common objections
- [ ] Tested price with trusted advisors (optional)

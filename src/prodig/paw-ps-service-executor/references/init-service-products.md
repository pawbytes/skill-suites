## Service Products Overview

Service products transform expertise into packaged offerings with clear scope, delivery models, and outcomes. Unlike traditional consulting (time-for-money), productized services have defined boundaries, repeatable processes, and scalable delivery.

## Core Characteristics

### What Makes a Service "Productized"

| Traditional Service | Productized Service |
|--------------------|---------------------|
| Time-based billing | Outcome-based pricing |
| Vague scope | Clear boundaries |
| Custom every time | Repeatable process |
| Creator-dependent | Team-deliverable |
| Hard to scale | Scalable model |
| "I do consulting" | "Here's what you get" |

### Key Elements

Every productized service needs:

1. **Clear Promise** — Specific outcome the client receives
2. **Defined Scope** — What's included and what's not
3. **Delivery Process** — How the service is fulfilled
4. **Deliverables** — Tangible outputs the client keeps
5. **Pricing Model** — How much and why

## Service Categories

### 1. Done-For-You (DFY)

Full execution by the service provider.

```
Characteristics:
- Provider does all the work
- Client receives completed outcome
- Highest perceived value
- Highest delivery effort

Examples:
- "We build your website"
- "We manage your ads"
- "We write your content"

Pricing: Premium (reflects full execution)
```

### 2. Done-With-You (DWY)

Collaborative execution with client involvement.

```
Characteristics:
- Shared work between provider and client
- Client participates in process
- Builds client capability
- Medium delivery effort

Examples:
- "Coaching + implementation"
- "Strategy sessions + homework"
- "We guide, you execute"

Pricing: Mid-range (time + expertise)
```

### 3. Consulting/Advisory

Expert guidance without execution.

```
Characteristics:
- Advice and direction
- Client executes
- High expertise, low execution
- Repeatable through frameworks

Examples:
- "Strategic roadmap"
- "Expert audit"
- "Advisory retainer"

Pricing: Variable (expertise-based)
```

### 4. Templates & Tools

Self-service products with no direct service.

```
Characteristics:
- Product, not service
- No customization
- Scale without effort
- Support may be included

Examples:
- "Template pack"
- "Swipe file"
- "Training program"

Pricing: Low (high scale)
```

## Packaging Framework

### Step 1: Name the Service

Use outcome-driven naming:
- Bad: "Marketing Consulting"
- Good: "90-Day Launch Accelerator"
- Bad: "SEO Services"
- Good: "First Page Domination Program"

### Step 2: Define the Promise

One sentence: "You will [outcome] within [timeframe]"

Example: "You will have a fully functional sales funnel live within 30 days."

### Step 3: Specify the Client

Who this is for:
- Role/industry
- Pain point
- Stage of business
- Budget range

Who this is NOT for:
- Clear exclusions

### Step 4: List Deliverables

Tangible items the client receives:
- Documents
- Templates
- Built systems
- Training

### Step 5: Define Scope

What's included:
- [Specific deliverable]
- [Specific process]
- [Support elements]

What's NOT included:
- [Adjacent services]
- [Ongoing work]
- [Common assumptions]

### Step 6: Structure Delivery

Phases with clear milestones:
- Phase 1: Discovery (Week 1-2)
- Phase 2: Design (Week 3-4)
- Phase 3: Delivery (Week 5-8)

### Step 7: Set Pricing

Based on:
- Value delivered
- Market positioning
- Effort required
- Client budget

## Delivery Models

| Model | Best For | Timeline | Pricing |
|-------|----------|----------|---------|
| Project | Fixed scope | 2-12 weeks | Fixed fee |
| Retainer | Ongoing | Monthly | Recurring |
| Sprint | Intensive | 1-5 days | Fixed + premium |
| Milestone | Complex | Phased | Tied to milestones |

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Vague scope | Scope creep | List every deliverable |
| No boundaries | Client expectations diverge | Define what's excluded |
| Time-based pricing | Limits revenue | Price on value |
| Creator-dependent | Can't scale | Document process |
| Too many options | Decision paralysis | 3 tiers maximum |

## Success Metrics

Track these for every service:
- Delivery time vs. estimate
- Revision rounds required
- Client satisfaction score
- Profit margin per project
- Repeat/referral rate

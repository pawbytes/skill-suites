# Idea Expansion

Transform raw concepts into rich idea sets with themes, variations, and angles worth exploring.

## When to Use

- User shares a vague notion ("I want to create something about productivity")
- User has a partial idea that needs fleshing out
- User presents a "what if" question
- User wants to explore the full possibility space around a concept

## Input

Gather from conversation:
- The seed idea or notion
- User's excitement level and interest areas
- Any constraints or preferences mentioned
- Domain or product family hints (course, template, SaaS, service)

## Expansion Process

### Step 1: Acknowledge the Kernel

Before expanding, identify and validate what's compelling about the seed:

- "The interesting thing here is..."
- "What's exciting about this is..."
- "The core insight I hear is..."

This builds rapport and ensures we expand in the right direction.

### Step 2: Generate Variations

Apply expansion techniques:

| Technique | Prompt | Output |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Dimension stretching** | "What if we made this [bigger/smaller/faster/slower]?" | Scale variations |
| **Audience shifting** | "Who else might need this?" | Audience variations |
| **Problem reframing** | "What if the real problem is X instead of Y?" | Problem variations |
| **Analogy mapping** | "This is like X for Y. What else fits?" | Metaphor variations |
| **Constraint removal** | "If anything were possible, what would this be?" | Boundary-pushing variations |
| **Hybridization** | "What if we combined this with Z?" | Fusion ideas |

### Step 3: Find Themes

Cluster variations into thematic groups:

```
Theme: "Speed & Efficiency"
- Idea A: Quick-win templates
- Idea B: Automation-focused approach
- Idea C: Time-boxed methodology

Theme: "Depth & Mastery"
- Idea D: Comprehensive course
- Idea E: Certification program
- Idea F: Coaching component
```

### Step 4: Shape Into Concepts

Turn promising variations into concrete concepts:

```markdown
## Concept: [Name]

**Core premise:** One-sentence description
**Target audience:** Who this serves
**Key value:** What makes it valuable
**Format hint:** Course/template/tool/service
**Differentiator:** What makes it unique
```

## Output

Create an idea set:

```markdown
# Idea Expansion: [Topic]

## Seed Idea
[Original notion from user]

## Core Insight
[What's compelling about the seed]

## Variations Explored
### Theme 1: [Theme Name]
- **Idea A:** [Description]
- **Idea B:** [Description]

### Theme 2: [Theme Name]
- **Idea C:** [Description]
- **Idea D:** [Description]

## Standout Concepts
### [Concept Name]
- Premise: [One sentence]
- Audience: [Who]
- Format: [Type]
- Why promising: [Reason]

## Next Angles to Explore
- [ ] Angle 1: [Description]
- [ ] Angle 2: [Description]
```

## Quality Standard

Expansion is complete when:
- [ ] At least 3 themes identified
- [ ] 6-10 distinct variations generated
- [ ] 2-3 concepts shaped with enough detail to evaluate
- [ ] User has expressed preference or excitement about at least one direction

## Example Dialogue

**User:** "I'm thinking about something with AI and writing..."

**Discovery Agent:** "Ooh, AI and writing — there's rich territory there! Let me make sure I'm tracking with you: what's exciting about that combination? Is it about helping people write faster, better, or something else entirely?"

[User clarifies]

**Discovery Agent:** "Got it. Let me riff on a few directions..."

[Generates variations across themes like speed, quality, creativity, skill-building]

"Some of these might feel more 'you' than others — what's sparking interest?"

## Common Pitfalls

- **Rushing to filter** — Let ideas breathe before judging
- **Staying too abstract** — Ground ideas in concrete form
- **Missing the kernel** — Don't lose what excited the user initially
- **Generating without shaping** — Variations need to become concepts

## Completion Trigger

When the user expresses clear excitement about 1-3 concepts:
- Confirm what resonates
- Summarize the concept(s)
- Ask: "Want to explore this further or pressure-test it with some questions?"
- Route to research or strategy when ready

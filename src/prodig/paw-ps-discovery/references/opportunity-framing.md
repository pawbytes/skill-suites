# Opportunity Framing

Recast ideas into structured product opportunities with clear value propositions.

## When to Use

- User has a concept that needs clearer articulation
- User wants to see an idea as a market opportunity
- User needs help communicating a concept's value
- Transitioning from brainstorming to evaluation

## Input

Gather from conversation:
- The concept or idea to frame
- What the user finds compelling about it
- Who might want this (even if vague)
- What problem it solves or desire it fulfills

## Framing Process

### Step 1: Capture the Essence

Distill the idea to its core:

- "In one sentence, this is..."
- "The heart of this is..."
- "What makes this special is..."

### Step 2: Apply Opportunity Statement Format

Transform into a structured opportunity:

```markdown
## Opportunity: [Name]

**For** [target audience]
**Who** [need/problem/situation]
**[Product name]** is a [product type]
**That** [key benefit/differentiator]
**Unlike** [alternatives/status quo]
**Our product** [unique advantage]
```

### Step 3: Explore Framing Variations

Different frames reveal different opportunities:

| Frame | Question | Reveals |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Problem frame** | What pain does this solve? | Urgency and motivation |
| **Desire frame** | What aspiration does this fulfill? | Emotional appeal |
| **Outcome frame** | What result does this create? | Value and ROI |
| **Identity frame** | Who does this help them become? | Transformation |
| **Simplification frame** | What complexity does this remove? | Ease and convenience |

Try multiple frames and see which resonates:

```markdown
### Frame 1: Problem-First
For busy founders who struggle to write consistently,
Writing OS is a guided system that makes publishing feel automatic,
Unlike scattered tips and tools,
Our system provides a complete workflow from idea to published.

### Frame 2: Identity-First
For aspiring thought leaders who want to be recognized in their field,
Writing OS is a reputation-building engine that turns your expertise into content,
Unlike hiring ghostwriters,
Our system captures your unique voice and perspective.
```

### Step 4: Pressure-Test the Frame

Evaluate the opportunity statement:

- Is the audience specific enough?
- Is the problem/desire real and felt?
- Is the benefit compelling?
- Is the differentiator defensible?
- Is this actually unique?

## Output

Create framed opportunity statements:

```markdown
# Opportunity Framing: [Concept]

## Core Essence
[One-sentence distillation]

## Primary Opportunity Statement
**For** [audience]
**Who** [need/problem]
**[Name]** is a [type]
**That** [benefit]
**Unlike** [alternatives]
**Our product** [advantage]

## Alternative Frames
### [Frame Type]
[Opportunity statement]

### [Frame Type]
[Opportunity statement]

## Frame Comparison
| Frame | Strengths | Weaknesses | Best For |
|-------|-----------|------------|----------|
| [Type] | [Pros] | [Cons] | [Context] |

## Recommended Frame
[Which frame resonates most and why]
```

## Quality Standard

Framing is complete when:
- [ ] Core essence captured in one sentence
- [ ] Full opportunity statement articulated
- [ ] At least 2 alternative frames explored
- [ ] Frame pressure-tested with questions
- [ ] User agrees the framing captures their vision

## Example Dialogue

**User:** "I want to create a course about being more productive, but not the usual tips stuff."

**Discovery Agent:** "Love that you want to differentiate. What makes your approach different from the usual productivity content?"

[User explains their angle]

**Discovery Agent:** "So it sounds like the essence is... [distills]. Let me frame this as an opportunity and see if it lands..."

[Creates opportunity statement]

"How does that feel? Does that capture what makes your approach different?"

## Completion Trigger

When the user agrees the framing captures their vision:
- Confirm the primary frame
- Ask: "Want to explore other directions with this frame, or ready to see if this opportunity is real?"
- Route to research for validation or strategist for definition

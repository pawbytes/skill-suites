# Part 3: Skills Directory

## Chapter 7: Growth & Conversion Skills

Growth and conversion skills focus on optimizing the customer journey after acquisition, turning visitors into customers, retaining existing customers, and leveraging satisfied users to drive new growth. These three skills form a powerful revenue optimization engine.

---

### 7.1 CRO Specialist

**Skill ID:** `paw-mkt-cro`

#### What It Does

The CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization) Specialist delivers evidence-based recommendations to improve conversion rates across all critical user touchpoints. Unlike generic optimization advice, this skill follows a rigorous priority framework that addresses foundational issues before tactical refinements. Every recommendation is structured with clear effort levels and expected impact.

The specialist audits live pages, analyzes user behavior data, and produces actionable optimization plans with Quick Wins, High-Impact Changes, and Test Hypotheses.

#### Optimization Areas

| Area | Description | Key Focus |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| **Landing Pages** | Homepage, dedicated landing pages, pricing pages, feature pages | Above-the-fold optimization, headline effectiveness, CTA placement |
| **Signup Flows** | Registration, trial signup, checkout processes | Friction reduction, field optimization, step analysis |
| **Forms** | Lead capture, contact forms, multi-step forms | Field reduction, input optimization, validation |
| **Popups & Modals** | Exit intent, lead magnets, announcement bars | Timing, targeting, copy, design |
| **Paywalls** | Upgrade prompts, subscription gates | Value communication, plan comparison, timing |
| **Onboarding** | First-use experience, activation flows | Aha moment achievement, step reduction, empty states |

#### When to Use It / Trigger Phrases

Activate this skill when you hear:

- "Our landing page isn't converting"
- "We need to improve our signup flow"
- "Run a CRO audit on this page"
- "Optimize our conversion rate"
- "A/B test our homepage"
- "Reduce form abandonment"
- "Improve our checkout flow"
- "Our onboarding has drop-off"

#### Priority Framework Explained

The CRO Specialist follows a strict priority framework. This ensures foundational issues are addressed before surface-level optimizations:

| Priority | Focus Area | Why It Matters |
|----------|------------|----------------|
| **1. Value Proposition Clarity** | Can visitors understand what you do in 5 seconds? | The most common reason pages fail to convert |
| **2. Headline Effectiveness** | Does the headline lead with customer outcomes? | Headlines are the most-read element on any page |
| **3. CTA Placement & Copy** | Is the primary action clear and compelling? | Confusing CTAs kill conversions |
| **4. Visual Hierarchy** | Can scanners extract the core message? | 80% of visitors scan, they don't read |
| **5. Trust Signals** | Is there proof this works? | Skepticism is the default state |
| **6. Objection Handling** | Are the top 5 objections addressed? | Unaddressed concerns prevent action |
| **7. Friction Points** | Is anything making conversion difficult? | Every friction point costs conversions |

**Key Principle:** A weak value proposition cannot be fixed by a better CTA button color. Work through priorities in order.

#### Evidence-Based Methodology

The skill requires diagnostic data before making recommendations:

**Quantitative Data Sources:**
- Funnel analytics (where users drop off)
- Heatmaps (where users click, scroll, ignore)
- Form analytics (which fields cause abandonment)
- Session recordings (watch 20-50 non-converting sessions)

**Qualitative Data Sources:**
- Exit surveys ("What stopped you from signing up?")
- Post-signup surveys (ask 3-7 days after conversion)
- Support ticket analysis (common objections)
- User interviews (5-7 recent converters)

**Output Structure:**

Every CRO recommendation is delivered in this format:

1. **Quick Wins** - Same-day changes, no developer required
2. **High-Impact Changes** - 1-5 day effort, significant lift expected
3. **Test Hypotheses** - A/B test candidates with sample size guidance
4. **Copy Alternatives** - 2-3 headline/CTA variants with rationale

#### Example: Landing Page Optimization

**Scenario:** A SaaS company's landing page has a 2% conversion rate with 10,000 monthly visitors from paid search.

**CRO Specialist Analysis:**

**Priority 1 Assessment - Value Proposition:**
> Current headline: "The Leading Project Management Platform"
> 
> Problem: Reads like a company tagline. No specific outcome.
> 
> Quick Win: "Cut project completion time by 40% without adding headcount"

**Priority 3 Assessment - CTA:**
> Current CTA: "Sign Up"
> 
> Problem: Generic, doesn't describe what happens next.
> 
> Quick Win: "Start My Free Trial" - Clear, action-oriented, no ambiguity.

**Priority 5 Assessment - Trust Signals:**
> Current state: Three testimonials without photos, all anonymous
> 
> Problem: Zero credibility. Anonymous testimonials hurt more than help.
> 
> High-Impact Change: Replace with 3 attributed testimonials (name, photo, company, specific outcome).

**Test Hypothesis Generated:**
> "If we change the headline from tagline-style to outcome-focused with specific numbers, then conversion rate will improve by 15-25% because cold traffic from paid search needs immediate clarity on what they'll gain."

**Expected Results:**
- Quick wins implemented within 24 hours: +10-15% lift
- High-impact changes within 1 week: +20-30% lift
- Testing program over 90 days: +50-100% cumulative improvement

### 7.2 Retention & Churn Prevention Specialist

**Skill ID:** `paw-mkt-retention`

#### What It Does

The Retention & Churn Prevention Specialist prevents customer churn through systematic diagnosis, intervention design, and recovery programs. This skill addresses both voluntary churn (customers who choose to leave) and involuntary churn (payment failures), with a focus on the highest-recovery-rate opportunities first.

The specialist designs cancel flows, payment recovery sequences, health scoring systems, and win-back campaigns. Every intervention is grounded in actual customer behavior signals and the brand's economics.

#### Retention Areas

| Area | Purpose | Typical Recovery Rate |
|------|---------|----------------------|
| **Cancel Flows** | Intervene at the moment of cancel intent | 25-35% save rate |
| **Payment Recovery (Dunning)** | Recover failed payments automatically | 50-60% recovery rate |
| **Win-Back Campaigns** | Re-engage churned customers | 10-15% reactivation rate |
| **Health Scoring** | Predict and prevent churn proactively | 20-40% at-risk save rate |
| **Proactive Retention** | Reach out before cancel intent forms | Varies by trigger |

#### When to Use It / Trigger Phrases

Activate this skill when you hear:

- "Our churn rate is too high"
- "Design a cancel flow"
- "We're losing too many customers to failed payments"
- "Create a win-back campaign"
- "Build a customer health score"
- "Improve our retention rate"
- "Customers are leaving after month one"
- "Our dunning emails aren't working"

#### Churn Diagnosis Methodology

Before designing any retention intervention, the specialist diagnoses what type of churn is happening:

**Types of Churn:**

| Type | Definition | Primary Cause | Recovery Approach |
|------|------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Voluntary (active)** | Customer deliberately cancels | Value perception, missing features, competitor, price | Cancel flow, proactive retention, win-back |
| **Involuntary (passive)** | Payment fails and account lapses | Expired card, insufficient funds, bank block | Dunning sequences, Smart Retry, card updater |
| **Implicit** | Customer stops using but doesn't cancel | Disengagement, poor onboarding, habit not formed | Health scoring, proactive outreach |
| **Contractual expiry** | Fixed-term contract ends without renewal | No renewal conversation, poor ongoing value | QBRs, renewal playbooks, expansion offers |

**Critical Insight:** Involuntary churn represents 30-50% of all SaaS churn and has the highest recovery rate. Address it first.

**Churn Rate Benchmarks:**

| Business Type | Monthly Churn Target | Annual Churn Target |
|---------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| SMB SaaS | Under 2% | Under 22% |
| Mid-market SaaS | Under 1% | Under 12% |
| Enterprise SaaS | Under 0.5% | Under 6% |
| Consumer subscription | Under 5% | Under 46% |

#### Example: Cancel Flow Design

**Scenario:** A B2B SaaS company has 4% monthly churn. Exit survey data shows 40% cite "too expensive" and 30% cite "not using it enough."

**Cancel Flow Architecture:**

```
Cancel button --> [Pause Gate] --> [Exit Survey (4-6 reasons)] --> [Dynamic Offer (reason-matched)]
  --> Accept: confirm save, update billing/plan
  --> Decline: [Confirmation Page] graceful exit (data export, community, support)
  --> [Post-Cancel Sequence] win-back emails start
```

**Exit Survey Options:**

| Reason | Data Use | Dynamic Offer |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| It's too expensive | Trigger discount or pause offer | 20-30% discount for 3 months OR plan downgrade |
| I'm not using it enough | Trigger usage coaching + pause | Free onboarding session + pause option |
| It's missing a feature I need | Trigger roadmap acknowledgment | Share timeline, offer pause until feature ships |
| I'm switching to a competitor | Trigger differentiation | Feature comparison + one month free |
| I had a technical issue | Escalate to support | Immediate support + account credit |
| Something else | Route to text field | Manager callback offer |

**Offer Hierarchy (lowest cost to highest):**

1. **Pause** - Costs nothing, preserves relationship
2. **Downgrade** - Reduces revenue but retains customer
3. **Time-limited discount** - 3-month incentive
4. **Free extension** - 1 month free
5. **Feature access** - Unlock higher plan feature

**Critical Rules:**
- Show ONE best-matched offer, not all offers at once
- Never use dark patterns (hidden cancel buttons, guilt language)
- If they decline the offer, complete cancellation immediately
- Keep customer data for 30-90 days post-cancellation for reactivation

**Expected Results:**
- Cancel flow impression rate: 80%+ of cancellers see the flow
- Survey completion rate: 60-70%
- Save rate: 25-35% of intending cancellers
- Pause adoption rate: 10-20%

### 7.3 Referral & Partnership Specialist

**Skill ID:** `paw-mkt-referral`

#### What It Does

The Referral & Partnership Specialist designs programs that turn customers, affiliates, and partners into scalable acquisition channels. This skill delivers sustainable programs with economics that work from day one, including customer referral programs, affiliate structures, strategic partnerships, and co-marketing campaigns.

Every program is grounded in the brand's LTV economics and optimized for both participation and conversion.

#### Program Types

| Type | How It Works | Best For |
|------|--------------|----------|
| **Customer Referral** | Existing customers refer friends for rewards | All products with satisfied customers |
| **Affiliate Program** | Third-party promoters earn commission on sales | Products with margin for 15-30% commission |
| **Strategic Partnerships** | Co-marketing with complementary brands | B2B SaaS, integrated products |
| **Co-Marketing Campaigns** | Joint content, events, or promotions | Audience expansion, credibility building |
| **Reseller Partnerships** | Partners sell on your behalf | Enterprise sales, geographic expansion |

#### When to Use It / Trigger Phrases

Activate this skill when you hear:

- "Create a referral program"
- "Set up an affiliate program"
- "We need word-of-mouth growth"
- "Design a refer-a-friend program"
- "Build a partner program"
- "How do we incentivize customers to share?"
- "Create a commission structure for partners"
- "Launch a customer advocacy program"

#### K-Factor and Economics

**Viral Coefficient (K-Factor):**

K = i x c (invites sent per user x conversion rate per invite)

| K-Factor | Classification | Growth Effect |
|----------|----------------|---------------|
| Below 0.2 | Weak | Negligible contribution |
| 0.2 - 0.5 | Decent supplement | 20-50% additional growth |
| 0.5 - 1.0 | Strong referral engine | Significant acquisition channel |
| Above 1.0 | Viral growth | Self-sustaining exponential growth |

**Levers to Increase K-Factor:**

1. **Increase invites sent:** Prompt at value moments, make sharing effortless, remind of unclaimed rewards
2. **Increase conversion per invite:** Stronger incentive for referred, personalized landing pages, reduce signup friction
3. **Reduce time-to-invite:** Shorten time between signup and first referral prompt

**Referral Economics:**

| Product Price | Recommended Total Incentive | Typical Split | % of Price |
|---------------|----------------------------|---------------|------------|
| Under $50 | $10-$15 | $5/$5 or $5/$10 | 20-30% |
| $50-$100 | $15-$25 | $10/$10 or $10/$15 | 15-25% |
| $100-$500 | $25-$75 | $25/$25 or $25/$50 | 10-20% |
| SaaS ($20-$50/mo) | 1 free month each | Equal | ~1 month revenue |
| SaaS ($50-$200/mo) | 1-2 free months or 20-30% off | Varies | 1-2 months revenue |

**Rule of Thumb:** Keep total referral cost (both sides) at 10-25% of customer LTV.

#### Example: Two-Sided Referral Program

**Scenario:** A SaaS product with $500 LTV and 5% monthly churn wants to launch a referral program.

**Program Design:**

**Type Selection:** Two-sided program (both referrer and referred get rewarded)
- Reason: Two-sided programs have 30-50% higher conversion rates than one-sided

**Incentive Structure:**
- Referrer: $25 account credit per successful referral
- Referred: $25 off first month
- Total cost: $50 per acquisition (10% of LTV - sustainable)

**Mechanics:**
- Unique referral links: `app.company.com/ref/username`
- Attribution window: 30 days
- Trigger: Credit applied when referred user converts to paid

**Referral Program Page Elements:**
1. Headline: "Give $25, Get $25"
2. How it works: 3 visual steps
3. Unique link: One-click copy
4. Share buttons: Email, SMS, WhatsApp, Twitter, LinkedIn
5. Progress tracker: Referrals sent, pending, completed, rewards earned

**Success Metrics:**
- Participation rate target: 5-10% of customers actively refer
- K-factor target: 0.3-0.5 within 6 months
- Referred customer LTV: 16-25% higher than non-referred
- Program ROI: 3-5x

**Launch Sequence:**
1. Month 1: Technical validation, flow optimization (expect 1-2% participation)
2. Month 2: A/B testing incentives and copy (expect 2-4% participation)
3. Month 3: Channel expansion, prompt optimization (expect 3-5% participation)
4. Month 6: Tiered rewards, gamification (expect 5-8% participation)

## Chapter 8: Launch & Promotion Skills

Launch and promotion skills orchestrate how products and features enter the market. From major product launches to influencer campaigns, these skills coordinate multiple channels for maximum impact and convert attention into lasting relationships.

### 8.1 Product Launch Specialist

**Skill ID:** `paw-mkt-launch`

#### What It Does

The Product Launch Specialist plans and executes coordinated product launches across owned, rented, and borrowed channels. This skill delivers phased launch timelines, Product Hunt strategy, launch content suites, and post-launch activation plans grounded in brand positioning and audience intelligence.

Every launch converts attention into relationships - turning rented and borrowed reach into owned email subscribers and product signups.

#### Launch Types and Tiers

**Launch Categories:**

| Category | Description | Full ORB Activation? |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|
| **New Product** | First public release of entirely new product | Yes - full treatment |
| **Major Feature** | Significant capability expansion | Yes - full treatment |
| **Minor Feature** | Useful addition to existing workflows | Partial - blog + email + social |
| **Patch/Fix** | Bug fixes, performance improvements | Minimal - changelog + tweet |
| **Category Entry** | Entering new market or segment | Yes - full treatment + new positioning |
| **Partnership** | Joint launch with partner brand | Yes - coordinated cross-brand |

**Launch Tiers:**

| Tier | Scope | Channel Activation | Content Suite |
|------|-------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Tier 1 (Major)** | New product, major feature, category entry | Full ORB activation, Product Hunt, press | Complete launch suite |
| **Tier 2 (Minor)** | Monthly feature or improvement | Blog, email, 3-5 social posts | Scaled treatment |
| **Tier 3 (Patch)** | Weekly/biweekly updates | Changelog, one social post | Minimal |

**Key Principle:** The best companies don't just launch once. They launch again and again. Build a repeatable announcement cadence from day one.

#### When to Use It / Trigger Phrases

Activate this skill when you hear:

- "We're launching a new product"
- "Plan our go-to-market strategy"
- "Create a Product Hunt launch plan"
- "We need a launch checklist"
- "Announce our new feature"
- "Coordinate our launch day"
- "Build buzz before launch"
- "We're coming out of beta"

#### ORB Channel Strategy

Every launch operates across three channel types:

**Owned Channels** (brand controls directly):
- Email list (highest conversion channel in a launch)
- Blog/website
- In-product notifications
- Community (if exists)
- SMS/push notifications (if active)

**Rented Channels** (platform-dependent, algorithm-controlled):
- X/Twitter (threads, product announcements)
- LinkedIn (company page + founder posts)
- Instagram (feed posts, Stories, Reels)
- TikTok (if audience is present)
- YouTube (if brand has video presence)
- Product Hunt

**Borrowed Channels** (leveraging other people's audiences):
- Guest posts and editorial placements
- Podcast appearances (book 6-8 weeks ahead)
- Influencer seeding
- Press coverage
- Partner email blasts and co-marketing
- Reddit/Hacker News/Indie Hackers

**Core Principle:** Convert rented and borrowed attention into owned relationships. Every launch tactic should have a path to email opt-in, community join, or product signup.

**ORB Channel Plan Template:**

| Channel | Type | Tactic | Timing | Owner | Goal |
|---------|------|--------|--------|-------|------|
| Email list | Owned | Announcement + sequence | Launch day | Marketing | Signups/activations |
| Blog | Owned | Launch post | T-1 day (embargo) | Content | SEO + social proof |
| X/Twitter | Rented | Launch thread | Launch day 8am | Social | Shares + traffic |
| LinkedIn | Rented | Founder announcement | Launch day 9am | Founder | B2B reach |
| Product Hunt | Rented | Submission | Launch day 12:01am PT | PM/founder | Votes + directory traffic |
| Press/media | Borrowed | Targeted pitches | T-2 weeks outreach | PR | Coverage + backlinks |
| Podcast guesting | Borrowed | Pre-booked appearances | T-6 to T-2 weeks | Founder | Awareness + list growth |
| Influencer seeding | Borrowed | Early access for review | T-3 weeks | Marketing | Testimonials + posts |

#### Five-Phase Timeline

| Phase | Timing | Goal | Key Actions |
|-------|--------|------|-------------|
| **1. Internal Launch** | T-8 to T-6 weeks | Validate core experience | 10-30 internal testers, fix P0/P1 bugs, lock messaging |
| **2. Alpha Launch** | T-6 to T-4 weeks | Controlled external validation | 50-200 trusted users, personal invitations, collect testimonials |
| **3. Beta Launch** | T-4 to T-2 weeks | Build buzz, generate social proof | Open waitlist, teaser campaign, influencer seeding, draft launch content |
| **4. Early Access** | T-2 weeks to T-1 day | Build final momentum | Public early access, finalize Product Hunt assets, brief supporters |
| **5. Full Launch** | Launch Day | Maximum coordinated push | All channels activated, Product Hunt submission, press embargo lifts |

**Gating Criteria:**

- Phase 1 to 2: Core experience stable, messaging locked, launch date confirmed
- Phase 2 to 3: 60-70% alpha activation, 3-5 testimonials collected
- Phase 3 to 4: 10-30 testimonials ready, all launch content drafted
- Phase 4 to 5: All content scheduled, team briefed, supporter email drafted

#### Product Hunt Playbook

**When Product Hunt Works Best:**
- Product serves makers, founders, developers, designers, or early adopters
- Can mobilize 100+ genuine supporters to vote on launch day
- Has a clear "wow" demo moment that works in a GIF
- Team can engage comments actively for 12+ hours

**Pre-Launch Preparation (T-4 to T-2 weeks):**

1. **Hunter Selection:**
   - Ideal: 1,000+ followers, active in relevant category
   - Options: Self-hunt (acceptable), recruit community member, reach out to top hunters

2. **Gallery Preparation:**
   - Thumbnail: 240x240px, shows product not abstract design
   - Screenshots: 5-8 at 1270x952px, show product in use
   - Demo GIF: 3-8 seconds showing core value moment
   - Tagline: 260 characters max, lead with benefit

3. **Supporter List:**
   - Target: 50-200 confirmed supporters
   - Send personal outreach explaining what, why, when, and the simple action

**Launch Day Execution:**

| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| 12:01am PT | Submit to Product Hunt |
| Within 5 minutes | Post maker's first comment (personal story, problem, who it's for) |
| 9am PT | Send supporter email with Product Hunt link |
| Throughout day | Respond to every comment within 30 minutes |
| 9-10am PT | Post in relevant communities (not "go vote" - ask for feedback) |

**Product Hunt Milestones:**

| Milestone | What It Unlocks |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Top 10 of the day | Newsletter inclusion (100k+ subscribers) |
| Top 5 of the day | Homepage feature, 500-5,000 visitor spike |
| Product of the Day | Golden Kitty eligibility, strong SEO |
| Product of the Week | Weekly digest feature, major social proof |

#### Example: Tier 1 Launch Planning

**Scenario:** A new B2B SaaS project management tool is launching in 8 weeks. The company has 2,000 email subscribers and an active presence on LinkedIn and Twitter.

**Launch Classification:** Tier 1 - New Product (full ORB activation)

**Phased Timeline:**

**Phase 1 - Internal Launch (T-8 to T-6 weeks):**
- Deploy to staging environment
- Invite 20 internal testers (team, advisors, investors)
- Run structured feedback sessions
- Fix P0/P1 bugs
- Finalize positioning: "Project management for teams that hate project management"
- Begin briefing podcast hosts (book 3-5 appearances)

**Phase 2 - Alpha Launch (T-6 to T-4 weeks):**
- Send personal invitations to 100 trusted users from waitlist
- Set expectations: early access in exchange for honest feedback
- Monitor activation rates
- Collect 5 testimonials
- Brief 3 journalists under embargo

**Phase 3 - Beta Launch (T-4 to T-2 weeks):**
- Open public waitlist
- Launch teaser campaign on LinkedIn and Twitter
- Seed product to 10 micro-influencers in productivity space
- Draft all launch content (blog, email, social, Product Hunt)
- Create Product Hunt gallery assets

**Phase 4 - Early Access (T-2 weeks to T-1 day):**
- Open early access publicly
- Finalize Product Hunt submission (identify hunter, prepare first comment)
- Email beta users with launch day briefing
- Schedule all social posts
- Final QA pass on signup and core workflow

**Phase 5 - Full Launch (Launch Day - Tuesday):**
- 12:01am PT: Submit to Product Hunt
- 6am PT: Post launch thread on Twitter
- 8am PT: Post founder announcement on LinkedIn
- 9am PT: Send email to 2,000 subscribers with Product Hunt link
- 9am-6pm PT: Engage every Product Hunt comment within 30 minutes
- Throughout day: Post in relevant communities with genuine context

**Success Metrics:**
- Product Hunt: Top 5 of the day
- Email: 25% open rate, 5% click rate
- Signups: 500 new trial signups
- Press: 2-3 media mentions
- Social: 500+ engagements across platforms

### 8.2 PR & Media Relations Specialist

**Skill ID:** `paw-mkt-pr`

#### What It Does

The PR & Media Relations Specialist delivers media relations, press releases, journalist outreach, crisis communications, and reputation management. All recommendations are grounded in the brand's SOSTAC plan and current PR best practices.

The skill emphasizes quality over quantity in media lists - 30-50 targeted contacts beat 500 generic blasts.

#### PR Areas

| Area | Purpose | Key Deliverables |
|------|---------|------------------|
| **Press Releases** | Announce newsworthy events | Structured release, distribution strategy |
| **Journalist Outreach** | Build media relationships | Targeted pitches, follow-up sequences |
| **Crisis Communications** | Protect brand reputation | Holding statements, response plans |
| **Reputation Management** | Monitor and shape perception | Sentiment tracking, response protocols |
| **Thought Leadership** | Position executives as experts | Bylined articles, speaking opportunities |
| **Media Kits** | Support journalist coverage | Company background, executive bios, assets |

#### When to Use It / Trigger Phrases

Activate this skill when you hear:

- "Write a press release"
- "Pitch this story to journalists"
- "Handle a PR crisis"
- "Build relationships with reporters"
- "Get media coverage"
- "Respond to negative press"
- "Create a media kit"
- "Position our CEO as a thought leader"

#### Pitch Creation Methodology

**Every pitch must answer:**
1. Why this journalist?
2. Why now?
3. Why should their audience care?

**Pitch Structure:**

```
Subject: [Specific, newsworthy angle - not generic]

Hi [First name],

[Personalized opening referencing their recent work]

[Why this matters now - the news hook]

[Your story in 2-3 sentences]

[Offer: exclusive access, data, interview]

[Clear CTA]

[Signature]
```

**Pitch Best Practices:**
- Keep under 150 words
- Lead with the news, not the company
- Offer something exclusive (data, interview, early access)
- Follow up once after 3-5 days, then move on
- Never send an unedited AI draft to a journalist

**Distribution Strategy:**

| Method | Best For | Cost |
|--------|----------|------|
| Targeted pitch (email) | Tier 1-2, exclusive/embargo stories | Free |
| Wire service (PR Newswire, Business Wire) | Broad distribution, compliance | $400-$2,000+ |
| Niche wire (EIN Presswire) | Trade press, budget distribution | $100-$500 |
| Press page | Always-on availability, SEO | Free |

**Strategy:** Targeted pitch for high-value stories. Wire only when broad distribution or compliance requires it.

#### Crisis Response Framework: AIRR

| Phase | Action | Timing | Key Principle |
|-------|--------|--------|---------------|
| **Acknowledge** | Confirm awareness, use holding statement | 1-2 hours | Do not speculate or assign blame |
| **Investigate** | Gather facts, document everything | Ongoing | Brief crisis team, prepare timeline |
| **Respond** | Full public response when facts are clear | 4-8 hours | Own the mistake, explain the fix |
| **Recover** | Follow up, post-mortem, monitor sentiment | Ongoing | Rebuild trust through action |

**Crisis Tiers:**

| Tier | Definition | Response Time | Decision Authority |
|------|------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Tier 1 - Minor** | Negative review, isolated complaint | 24 hours | Comms lead |
| **Tier 2 - Moderate** | Viral complaint, negative press | 4-8 hours | Comms + decision maker |
| **Tier 3 - Major** | Product failure, data breach, legal/safety | 1-2 hours | Full crisis team |

**Crisis Communication Priorities:**

1. Affected customers (email, in-app) - First priority
2. All customers (email, website) - Same day
3. Employees (internal comms) - Before external
4. Media (press statement) - After affected parties
5. Partners/investors (direct) - Same day

#### Example: Press Release and Media Outreach

**Scenario:** A B2B SaaS company is announcing a $5M Series A funding round.

**Press Release Structure:**

```
HEADLINE: [Company] Raises $5M Series A to Transform Project Management for Remote Teams
Subheadline: Funding led by [VC Firm], with participation from [Notable Angel]

SAN FRANCISCO, CA — [Date] — [Company], the project management platform built for distributed teams, today announced $5 million in Series A funding led by [VC Firm].

[Lead paragraph: Who, what, when, where, why in 2-3 sentences]

[Second paragraph: Supporting detail, context, significance]

[Quote from CEO: Vision and why this matters]

[Quote from investor: Why they invested]

[Body: Additional details, data, background]

[CTA: Where to learn more]

### About [Company]
[Boilerplate: 2-3 sentences]

### Media Contact
[Name] | [Email] | [Phone]
```

**Media Outreach Plan:**

**Tier 1 Targets (exclusive/embargo):**
- 5-10 journalists who cover SaaS, productivity tools, remote work
- Offer 48-hour embargo with exclusive interview access
- Pitch angle: "The story behind [specific insight or data point]"

**Tier 2 Targets (announcement day):**
- 20-30 additional relevant journalists
- Pitch after embargo lifts with the press release and offer for interview
- Focus on industry-specific angles (B2B, remote work, productivity)

**Tier 3 Targets (syndication):**
- Wire service for broad distribution
- Industry newsletters and roundup publications

**Timing:**
- T-5 days: Pitch Tier 1 journalists under embargo
- T-2 days: Confirm coverage, provide additional materials
- T-1 day: Final check with journalists
- Launch day: Release goes live, embargo lifts, Tier 2 pitches sent
- T+1 day: Follow up with any interested parties

### 8.3 Paid Advertising Specialist

**Skill ID:** `paw-mkt-paid-ads`

#### What It Does

The Paid Advertising Specialist plans and manages paid media campaigns across Google, Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok, and programmatic channels. Recommendations are grounded in brand positioning, competitive research, and performance data.

Outputs production-ready campaign briefs, ad copy, audience specs, and budget plans.

#### Platforms Covered

| Platform | Best For | Key Strengths |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| **Google Ads** | High-intent search, remarketing | Captures active demand, measurable ROAS |
| **Meta (Facebook/Instagram)** | Awareness, consideration, DTC | Massive audience, sophisticated targeting |
| **LinkedIn Ads** | B2B, professional audiences | Precise professional targeting |
| **TikTok Ads** | Awareness, younger demographics | Viral potential, creative-first |

### 8.4 Influencer Marketing Specialist

**Skill ID:** `paw-mkt-influencer`

#### What It Does

The Influencer Marketing Specialist identifies influencers, manages outreach campaigns, builds ambassador and UGC programs, and designs creator affiliate partnerships. The skill delivers actionable strategies grounded in the brand's SOSTAC plan and the modern creator economy.

Key principle: 80% of budget on nano/micro creators delivers more total engagement than one macro creator.

#### Campaign Types

| Type | Description | Best For | Timeline |
|------|-------------|----------|----------|
| **Sponsored Content** | Single/multi-post paid partnerships | Awareness, traffic | 2-4 weeks |
| **Product Reviews** | Ship product for honest review | High-consideration products | 2-4 weeks |
| **Tutorials/How-To** | Creator demonstrates usage | Complex products, SaaS | 2-4 weeks |
| **UGC Programs** | Creator content for brand use | Authentic content at scale | Ongoing |
| **Brand Ambassador** | Long-term partnerships (3-12 months) | Deeper brand integration | 3-12 months |
| **Affiliate Campaigns** | Commission-based performance | Conversion focus | Ongoing |
| **Account Takeovers** | Creator runs brand account | Audience growth | 1-2 days |

#### When to Use It / Trigger Phrases

Activate this skill when you hear:

- "Find influencers for our brand"
- "Launch an influencer campaign"
- "Build a creator program"
- "Partner with content creators"
- "Create a UGC program"
- "Set up brand ambassadors"
- "Run influencer outreach"
- "Find TikTok/Instagram creators"

#### Creator Tier Strategy

| Tier | Followers | Engagement Rate | Cost Range | Best Use |
|------|-----------|-----------------|------------|----------|
| **Nano** | 1K-10K | 4-10% (highest) | $50-$400 | Authentic advocacy, UGC, tight budgets |
| **Micro** | 10K-100K | 2-4% | $200-$1,500 | Targeted reach, strong engagement |
| **Mid-Tier** | 100K-500K | 1.5-2.5% | $1,500-$5,000 | Balance of reach and engagement |
| **Macro** | 500K-1M | 1-1.5% | $5,000-$30,000 | Mass awareness |
| **Mega** | 1M+ | 0.5-1% | $15,000-$200,000+ | Brand moments, mass reach |

**Strategic Recommendation:**

For a $25,000 budget:
- Option A: One macro creator at $25K = ~500K reach, 0.9% engagement
- Option B: 20 nano/micro creators at $1K each = ~2.5M reach, 8% engagement

Option B delivers 5x the reach and 8x the engagement. Volume over vanity.

#### Example: Micro-Influencer Campaign

**Scenario:** A DTC skincare brand with a $10,000 budget wants to launch an influencer campaign for a new moisturizer launch.

**Campaign Strategy:**

**Campaign Type:** Sponsored content + UGC rights

**Tier Selection:** Micro-influencers (10K-100K)
- Reason: Highest engagement rates, authentic advocacy, cost-efficient

**Platform:** Instagram + TikTok
- Reason: Skincare category performs best on visual platforms

**Creator Selection Criteria:**
- Follower count: 10K-75K
- Engagement rate: Minimum 3% (above platform average)
- Content style: Skincare routines, authentic reviews, educational
- Audience demographics: 25-45, skincare-interested
- Brand alignment: Clean beauty values, sustainability

**Campaign Structure:**

| Deliverable | Quantity | Cost Per Creator | Total |
|-------------|----------|------------------|-------|
| 10 micro-influencers | - | $750-$1,000 each | $7,500-$10,000 |
| 1 Reel per creator | 10 Reels | Included | - |
| 3 Stories per creator | 30 Stories | Included | - |
| 6-month content license | - | Included | - |

**Deliverables Per Creator:**
- 1 Instagram Reel (30-60 seconds) showcasing product in routine
- 3 Instagram Stories (product unboxing, application, results)
- Authentic review approach (not scripted)
- UGC rights for brand use in paid ads (6 months)

**Creator Brief Elements:**
- Key messages (not script - allow creative freedom)
- Product USPs to highlight
- Hashtags to include
- Tag @brand account
- FTC disclosure (#ad or #sponsored)
- Dos and don'ts

**Attribution Methods:**
1. Unique discount code per creator (SKIN10-creatorname)
2. UTM-tagged link in bio
3. Post-purchase survey ("How did you hear about us?")

**Success Metrics:**
- Reach: 500,000+ impressions
- Engagement: 3%+ average rate
- Code redemptions: 200+ uses
- Sales attributed: $15,000+ (1.5x ROAS minimum)
- UGC assets: 10+ pieces for paid ads

**Timeline:**
- Week 1: Creator identification and outreach
- Week 2: Negotiate terms, ship product
- Week 3-4: Content creation
- Week 5: Content goes live
- Week 6: Performance reporting

**Long-term Opportunity:**
- Top-performing creators become brand ambassadors (6-month retainer)
- UGC content used in paid social campaigns

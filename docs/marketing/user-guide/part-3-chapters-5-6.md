## Chapter 5: Planning & Strategy Skills

### 5.1 SOSTAC Marketing Plan Builder

#### What It Does

The SOSTAC Marketing Plan Builder is your strategic planning foundation. It creates comprehensive marketing plans through a proven 6-phase framework that ensures nothing is overlooked—from understanding your current situation to measuring your results.

Think of it as hiring a senior marketing strategist who shows up prepared, having already researched your market, competitors, and opportunities before the first meeting.

#### The 6 Phases Explained

| Phase | Purpose | What You Get |
|-------|---------|--------------|
| **S - Situation** | Where are we now? | Market analysis, competitive landscape, SWOT/TOWS, internal audit |
| **O - Objectives** | Where do we want to be? | SMART goals, OKRs, KPIs with targets and timelines |
| **S - Strategy** | How do we get there? | Target segments, positioning, value proposition, channel selection |
| **T - Tactics** | What tactics do we use? | Channel plans, content themes, campaign structures, budget allocation |
| **A - Action** | How do we execute? | Implementation roadmap, RACI matrix, timelines, resource allocation |
| **C - Control** | How do we measure? | Measurement framework, dashboards, review cadences, optimization loops |

#### When to Use It

- Starting a new brand or product
- Annual marketing planning
- Entering a new market
- Launching a significant campaign
- When marketing feels disconnected from business goals

#### Trigger Phrases

- "Help me create a marketing plan"
- "I need a SOSTAC plan"
- "Let's do situation analysis"
- "Build me a marketing strategy"
- "I need marketing objectives"

#### What You Get (Deliverables)

Each phase produces a structured document saved to your brand workspace:

```
brands/{your-brand}/sostac/
├── README.md                # Phase completion tracker
├── 00-auto-discovery.md     # Research findings
├── 01-situation.md          # Current state analysis
├── 02-objectives.md         # Goals and KPIs
├── 03-strategy.md           # Strategic direction
├── 04-tactics.md            # Tactical plan
├── 05-action.md             # Implementation plan
├── 06-control.md            # Measurement framework
└── plan-summary.md          # Executive summary
```

#### Research-First Approach Explained

Unlike traditional planning where you're asked endless questions, the SOSTAC skill follows a **Research-Recommend-Validate** pattern:

1. **Research First**: The skill researches your market, competitors, and industry before making recommendations
2. **Analyze & Recommend**: It applies relevant frameworks to produce concrete recommendations with evidence
3. **Present Findings**: You receive 3-5 key insights with strategic implications
4. **Validate & Refine**: Only 2-4 targeted questions to fill genuine gaps
5. **Save and Advance**: Documents are saved after your confirmation

This means you spend less time answering questions and more time making decisions.

#### Real-World Example Walkthrough

**Scenario**: You're launching a new SaaS product for project management.

**Phase 1 - Situation**: The skill researches your competitors (Asana, Monday, Notion), analyzes the market size ($5B+ and growing 12% annually), identifies your strengths (AI-powered automation), and maps opportunities (underserved small business segment).

**Phase 2 - Objectives**: Based on the situation, it recommends specific targets: "Acquire 500 paying customers in 6 months with CAC under $150 and 85% retention rate."

**Phase 3 - Strategy**: It defines your positioning ("AI-powered project management for small teams who hate complexity"), selects target segments (startups with 5-20 employees), and chooses primary channels (content marketing, product-led growth).

**Phase 4 - Tactics**: You get detailed channel plans with budget allocation, content themes mapped to funnel stages, and campaign structures.

**Phase 5 - Action**: A week-by-week implementation roadmap with clear owners and dependencies.

**Phase 6 - Control**: Dashboard designs, weekly metrics to track, and optimization triggers.

### 5.2 Product Marketing Context Builder

#### What It Does

This skill creates the deep positioning document that makes all your marketing feel native and authentic. It captures the exact language your customers use, the objections they have, and the proof points that convince them.

Think of it as building the "brain" that all other marketing skills reference—ensuring every blog post, email, and ad speaks the same language.

#### The 12-Section Positioning Document

| Section | What It Contains |
|---------|------------------|
| 1. Product Overview | What you sell, for whom, and why |
| 2. Category Definition | How you position against alternatives |
| 3. Target Audience | Demographics, firmographics, psychographics |
| 4. Buyer Personas | 2-4 detailed profiles with goals and pain points |
| 5. Jobs to Be Done | What customers "hire" your product to accomplish |
| 6. Value Proposition | Clear articulation of unique value |
| 7. Differentiation | Competitive advantages with proof |
| 8. Objection Handling | Common objections and responses |
| 9. Customer Language | **Verbatim phrases customers use** (critical!) |
| 10. Proof Points | Specific numbers, case studies, testimonials |
| 11. Messaging Framework | Key messages by persona and funnel stage |
| 12. Brand Voice | Tone, style, and language guidelines |

#### When to Use It

- After completing SOSTAC Strategy phase
- Before launching any marketing campaigns
- When messaging feels generic or off-brand
- Before creating content, ads, or sales materials
- When entering new markets or segments

#### Trigger Phrases

- "Help me with positioning"
- "Define my target audience"
- "What's my differentiation"
- "Build my ICP"
- "Create buyer personas"
- "I need a messaging framework"

#### What You Get

A comprehensive `product-marketing-context.md` file that serves as the strategic intelligence layer for all downstream marketing execution.

#### How It Connects to Other Skills

Every marketing specialist reads this document before creating any content:

- **Content Marketing**: Uses customer language and messaging framework for blog posts
- **Email Marketing**: Applies objection handling to email sequences
- **Paid Ads**: Uses proof points in ad copy
- **Sales Enablement**: References differentiation for battle cards
- **PR**: Pulls positioning for press releases

#### Example: Building Positioning from Scratch

**Input**: "I have a new AI writing tool for marketers."

**Process**:
1. Skill checks for existing SOSTAC plan and extracts what's available
2. Identifies gaps (usually customer language and proof points)
3. Asks focused questions: "What do your best customers say when they recommend you?"
4. Builds the 12-section document with your input

**Output**:
> **Customer Language (Section 9)**:
> - "It writes like me but faster"
> - "Finally, AI that doesn't sound like a robot"
> - "I shipped a week's worth of content in a day"
>
> **Key Objections (Section 8)**:
> - "Will it sound like me?" → Show the voice training feature
> - "Is it worth $99/month?" → Break down to $3.30/day for 10+ hours saved

#### When to Use It

- Building organic traffic
- Establishing thought leadership
- Creating lead magnets
- Planning content strategy
- Writing specific content pieces

#### Trigger Phrases

- "Write a blog post about..."
- "Create a content strategy"
- "I need a lead magnet"
- "Build an editorial calendar"
- "Write a case study"
- "Help with content marketing"

#### The Content Strategy Workflow

1. **Ground in Strategy**: Read SOSTAC and product marketing context
2. **Define Pillars**: 3-5 content pillars aligned to positioning
3. **Map to Funnel**: TOFU (awareness), MOFU (consideration), BOFU (conversion)
4. **Create Calendar**: 90-day editorial calendar with specific topics
5. **Produce Content**: Draft publication-ready pieces
6. **Plan Distribution**: 80/20 rule (20% creation, 80% distribution)

#### Editorial Calendar Creation

The skill creates structured calendars with:

```
Content Calendar Structure:
├── Pillar (e.g., "AI for Marketing")
│   ├── Blog post 1 (TOFU - Awareness)
│   ├── Blog post 2 (MOFU - Consideration)
│   └── Lead magnet (BOFU - Conversion)
```

Each entry includes:
- Target keyword (SEO focus)
- Funnel stage
- Content format
- Publishing date
- Distribution channels
- CTA/next step

#### Distribution Planning

Every piece includes a distribution plan:

| Channel | Action | Timing |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Email | Newsletter feature | Publish day |
| LinkedIn | Personal post + company page | Publish day + Day 1 |
| Twitter/X | Thread from key insights | Day 1 |
| Newsletter | Dedicated send | Day 3 |
| Repurpose | Create 3-5 social posts | Week 1 |

#### Example: 90-Day Content Plan

**Scenario**: SaaS tool for project management targeting small teams.

**Content Pillars Defined**:
1. "Productivity for Small Teams" (40% of content)
2. "AI in Project Management" (30%)
3. "Team Collaboration" (30%)

**Sample Calendar Output**:

| Week | Piece | Pillar | Funnel | Keyword Target |
|------|-------|--------|--------|----------------|
| 1 | "10 Time-Wasters Killing Small Teams" | Productivity | TOFU | "small team productivity" |
| 2 | "How AI is Changing Project Management" | AI | TOFU | "AI project management" |
| 3 | "Case Study: Startup Scales with [Product]" | Productivity | MOFU | "project management case study" |
| 4 | "The Ultimate Guide to Team Collaboration" | Collaboration | MOFU | "team collaboration guide" |

#### Sequence Design Methodology

The skill follows a structured approach:

1. **Define the Journey**: What's the subscriber's starting point and destination?
2. **Map the Steps**: How many emails, what timing, what purpose each?
3. **Write the Content**: Subject lines, body copy, CTAs
4. **Plan the Triggers**: Entry conditions, timing, exit conditions

#### Deliverability Considerations

The skill ensures technical compliance:

- **Authentication**: SPF, DKIM, DMARC setup guidance
- **Warming**: Gradual volume increase for new domains
- **List Hygiene**: Bounce handling, sunset policies
- **Content**: Spam trigger avoidance, image-to-text ratio
- **Engagement**: Re-engagement sequences for inactive subscribers

**Structure Frameworks**:
- Tension-Twist-Payoff - Build tension, deliver twist, satisfying end

2. **Curiosity Gap**: Create information gap
